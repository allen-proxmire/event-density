### 3.4 N-c: the chain-contribution construction (the load-bearing step)

#### 3.4.1 Setup

V1 is the **vacuum-response kernel**: $K_\mathrm{vac}(x, x')$ is defined as the linear response of the effective vacuum at $x$ to a chain perturbation at $x'$. By construction, V1 is a **response kernel** — distinct from the *unperturbed* vacuum two-point function $W(x, x') = \langle 0 | \hat{\phi}(x) \hat{\phi}(x') | 0 \rangle$, which is a different object measuring intrinsic vacuum correlations.

A response kernel is sourced by perturbations. The source for V1 is **chain bandwidth content**: chain perturbations $\delta P_\mathrm{chain}(x')$ are bandwidth-coupled to the effective vacuum at the chain's commitment events, and the kernel $K_\mathrm{vac}$ propagates the perturbation across the substrate.

This sourcing structure is implicit in Arc Q.8 effective-vacuum factorisation: the effective vacuum at any spacetime point is determined by the bandwidth content of all chains in its support (see [`vacuum_and_zero_point.md`](../arc-Q/14_Q8_memo_01_zeropoint_vacuum_classification.md) where the multi-rule-type vacuum factorisation operates). B.2 makes this explicit.

#### 3.4.2 The chain-contribution sum

Let $\{\gamma_K\}_{K=1}^N$ index the chains in the support of $V_\mathrm{vac}(x)$. Each chain $\gamma_K$ is a worldline parameterised by its proper time $\tau_K$; commitment events along $\gamma_K$ occur at indices $n = 1, 2, \ldots$ with proper-time values $\tau_K^{(n)}$.

A chain perturbation $\delta b_K(\tau_K^{(m)})$ at commitment index $m$ on chain $K$ produces a perturbation in the chain's bandwidth content at later commitment events $n \geq m$ via the chain's *forward* bandwidth-update rule:
$$
\delta b_K(\tau_K^{(n)}) = \sum_{m \leq n} U_K(n, m) \cdot \delta b_K^\mathrm{source}(\tau_K^{(m)}),
$$
where $U_K(n, m)$ is the chain's forward propagator. **By P11 irreversibility, $U_K(n, m) = 0$ for $n < m$**: the propagator vanishes for backward propagation.

The chain's contribution to the effective vacuum at spacetime point $x$ is given by the bandwidth content at the commitment event nearest $x$ (or, more generally, by the integrated bandwidth-coupling at all commitment events whose vacuum-coupling support reaches $x$). Schematically:
$$
\delta V_\mathrm{vac}^{(K)}(x) = \sum_n \mathcal{F}_K(x; \tau_K^{(n)}) \cdot \delta b_K(\tau_K^{(n)}),
$$
where $\mathcal{F}_K(x; \tau)$ is the vacuum-coupling form-factor (specific functional form is INHERITED, not relevant to direction question).

#### 3.4.3 Composition: V1 is forward-cone-only

Combining the chain-update rule with the vacuum-contribution rule, the kernel relating a perturbation at $x'$ to a vacuum response at $x$ is:
$$
K_\mathrm{vac}(x, x') = \sum_K \sum_{n \geq m} \mathcal{F}_K(x; \tau_K^{(n)}) \cdot U_K(n, m) \cdot \mathcal{F}_K^\dagger(x'; \tau_K^{(m)}),
$$
with the $n \geq m$ constraint enforced by $U_K(n, m) = 0$ for $n < m$.

Now: when does $\tau_K^{(n)} \geq \tau_K^{(m)}$ correspond to $(x - x')$ in the forward causal cone?

For chain $K$ with timelike worldline $\gamma_K$ (P02 + P13 + chain-causality), the proper-time parameter $\tau_K$ is monotone increasing along the *forward* time orientation of the chain. P11 supplies this orientation: increasing $\tau_K$ corresponds to later commitment events, decreasing $\tau_K$ to earlier. **All chains share this orientation** — there is no "anti-chain" running backward in time, because P11 forbids un-commitment for *every* chain in the inventory. P11 is universal across the chain ensemble.

Therefore, for any chain $K$ contributing to V1, the constraint $\tau_K^{(n)} \geq \tau_K^{(m)}$ (forward chain dynamics) maps to $(x_n^K - x_m^K)$ being in the forward causal cone of $\gamma_K$ — and since $\gamma_K$ is timelike with universal forward orientation, this maps to $(x - x')$ being in the forward causal cone in any frame in which $\gamma_K$'s velocity is forward-pointing, i.e., **in every Lorentz frame consistent with the universal P11-supplied time orientation**.

Conclusion:
$$
K_\mathrm{vac}(x, x') \neq 0 \;\Rightarrow\; (x - x') \text{ is in the forward causal cone}.
$$

**V1 has forward-cone-only support: V1 is RETARDED.**

#### 3.4.4 Symmetric V1 (BC3) is NOT a consistent construction

For a symmetric V1 to arise from chain contributions, the kernel sum would need to admit terms with $\tau_K^{(n)} < \tau_K^{(m)}$, i.e., *backward* chain propagation. By P11, $U_K(n, m) = 0$ for $n < m$. There is no such term in the chain-contribution sum.

A symmetric V1 cannot be constructed from forward-only chain dynamics. To achieve BC3, V1 would need a *non-chain-sourced* component — a kernel-level contribution that bypasses chain dynamics. We address this in §3.4.5.

#### 3.4.5 Could V1 have non-chain-sourced contributions?

V1 is **the response kernel**, defined as the linear response of the effective vacuum to chain perturbations. By definition, V1 acts on chain perturbations and propagates them to the vacuum. Non-chain-sourced contributions to V1 would not be "response" in the technical sense — they would be intrinsic vacuum correlations (Wightman-type), measured by $W(x, x')$ rather than $K_\mathrm{vac}$.

The Wightman function $W(x, x')$ is **not the response kernel V1**. They are distinct objects:
- $W(x, x')$: unperturbed correlator; symmetric (or has the standard $W(x, x') = W^*(x', x)$ structure).
- $K_\mathrm{vac}(x, x')$: response kernel; what Arc N forces to exist with finite-width and Lorentz-scalar form.

In continuum QFT, both are derivable from the same underlying field theory; the retarded Green's function $G_R$ and the Wightman function $W$ are related by $G_R(x, y) = i \theta(t_x - t_y) [W(x, y) - W(y, x)]$. Both can coexist; the retarded Green's function is built from the symmetric Wightman correlator via a $\theta$-factor restriction.

This is precisely the **CR continuum-approximation framing**: at primitive level, V1 (the response kernel) is forced retarded by chain-contribution structure; at continuum approximation, both $G_R$ (retarded), $G_F$ (Feynman), and $W$ (Wightman) are admissible Green's functions of the underlying wave equation. **No contradiction at either level.**

**Conclusion of §3.4.5: V1 has no non-chain-sourced contribution as a response kernel. The symmetric BC3 construction cannot be supplied from elsewhere.**

This is structurally equivalent to UV-FIN's framing: primitive-level FORCED structural property, continuum-approximation effective machinery, no contradiction. **CR satisfied.**
