## 3. Proof sketch

The structural argument that closes the theorem (B.2 §3.4 in full; summary here):

**Step 1 — V1 is a response kernel.**
Per Theorem N1 (Arc N stage N.2 §2), V1 is the vacuum-response kernel: the linear response of the effective vacuum at $x$ to a chain perturbation at $x'$. This is *distinct* from the unperturbed two-point Wightman correlator $W(x, x') = \langle 0 | \hat{\phi}(x) \hat{\phi}(x') | 0 \rangle$, which measures intrinsic vacuum correlations. V1 is the *response*; $W$ is the *unperturbed correlator*. They are different objects at both primitive and continuum levels.

**Step 2 — Response kernels require sourced contributions.**
A response kernel propagates perturbations through the substrate. By the Q.8 effective-vacuum factorisation (Arc Q stage Q.8 closure), the source of effective-vacuum response at any spacetime point is the bandwidth content of all chains in its support; the vacuum is *bandwidth-sourced* by the chain ensemble. There is no non-chain-sourced contribution to V1 *as a response kernel*; non-chain contributions are intrinsic-correlator content, which lives in $W$, not V1.

**Step 3 — Chain bandwidth dynamics are forward-only along proper time.**
- Primitive 11 (commitment-irreversibility, primitive-given): commitment events are non-reversible; a committed event is a fact of the chain's past relative to subsequent commitments.
- Primitive 02 (chain): worldline $\gamma_K$ admits a parameterisation by proper time $\tau_K$.
- Primitive 13 (proper-time intervals): finite intervals between commitment events along $\gamma_K$, ordered by P11.
- Primitive 04 (bandwidth update rule): bandwidth content $b_K(\tau_K)$ is updated forward at each commitment event.

Jointly: the chain forward-propagator $U_K(n, m) = 0$ for $n < m$. Bandwidth at $\tau_K^{(n)}$ depends on chain history $\{\tau_K^{(m)} : m \leq n\}$; never on future $\{\tau_K^{(m)} : m > n\}$.

**Step 4 — Forward-only chain dynamics produce forward-cone-only V1 support.**
The chain-contribution sum to V1 takes the form
$$
K_\mathrm{vac}(x, x') = \sum_K \sum_{n \geq m} \mathcal{F}_K(x; \tau_K^{(n)}) \cdot U_K(n, m) \cdot \mathcal{F}_K^\dagger(x'; \tau_K^{(m)}),
$$
with the $n \geq m$ constraint enforced by Step 3. For each chain $K$ with timelike worldline $\gamma_K$, the chain-internal proper-time ordering $\tau_K^{(n)} \geq \tau_K^{(m)}$ maps to $(x_n^K - x_m^K)$ being in the forward causal cone of $\gamma_K$. **All chains share the same time orientation** (P11 is universal across the chain ensemble), so the chain-summed kernel inherits forward-cone-only support globally.

**Step 5 — Therefore V1 is RETARDED.**
$K_\mathrm{vac}(x, x') \neq 0 \Rightarrow (x - x')$ is in the forward causal cone.

**Step 6 — Symmetric and advanced kernels require backward chain dynamics, which P11 forbids.**
Per B.3 §2–4: symmetric V1 demands non-zero $U_K(n, m)$ for $n < m$ on at least some terms; this is forbidden by Step 3. Advanced V1 demands non-zero $U_K(n, m)$ for $n < m$ on all terms; forbidden globally. Hybrid variants demand non-zero backward content on partial subsets; forbidden by the same construction. **No alternative is constructible at primitive level.**

**Step 7 — PDE T-symmetry does not rescue alternatives.**
The KG-class / Dirac / Cl(3,1) PDE structure is direction-neutral; it propagates whatever direction is supplied at the kernel-construction level. PDE T-symmetry permits multiple Green's functions but does not select among them. Selection is structural, supplied by primitive-level chain dynamics.

**∴ Theorem 18.** V1 retardation is FORCED at primitive level uniquely. The microscopic arrow of time is structurally established at the kernel level.
