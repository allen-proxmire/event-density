## 2. GRH — Gauge-field-as-Rule-Type Hypothesis

### 2.1 Statement

**GRH (formal):** The gauge connection A_μ that appears in the Stage R.1 / R.3 minimal coupling D_μ = ∂_μ + (iq/ℏ)A_μ is the participation measure of a specific Case-P rule-type τ_gauge with internal Lorentz representation (1/2, 1/2) (vector) and L3 interface content requiring local gauge invariance A_μ → A_μ + ∂_μα.

Equivalently: A_μ is not an abstract structural connection imposed by symmetry; it is **itself an ED rule-type's participation content**, with its own four-band partition, its own bandwidth, its own commitment dynamics.

### 2.2 Why GRH is the natural closure point

Stage M.1.2 established that a Case-P rule-type with gauge-invariant L3 interface is **automatically massless via MR-P** — the gauge constraint forbids the m²A_μA^μ term. Three properties make GRH the natural closure:

(i) **Gauge field is structurally Case P with vector representation.** A_μ transforms as a four-vector under Lorentz — this is the (1/2, 1/2) representation. It is integer-spin (s = 1), so Case P. Statistics-class assignment matches.

(ii) **Minimal coupling forces the existence of A_μ.** Stage R.1's `kg_minimal_coupling_and_current.md` derived A_μ as the connection compelled by local-phase-invariance of the participation measure. Without A_μ, local U(1) invariance cannot hold. So *some* object with the structure of A_μ is FORCED at primitive level.

(iii) **MR-P naturally identifies that object as a massless rule-type.** If A_μ is treated as a participation measure, MR-P's gauge-invariance route makes it automatically massless. The conditional "if GRH then massless gauge rule-type exists" becomes unconditional "minimal coupling forces a massless gauge rule-type to exist."

GRH closes the loop: the gauge connection forced by R.1 minimal coupling is the participation measure that occupies the M.1.2 massless slot via MR-P.

### 2.3 What GRH commits to

Affirmative GRH closure entails:

- A_μ has its own b_K_gauge with four-band decomposition.
- A_μ has its own commitment dynamics (Primitive 11) along the gauge rule-type's worldlines, if any. (Lightlike worldlines may make "worldline" degenerate — see §2.5.)
- A_μ couples to other rule-types (the ones carrying charge q) via the existing minimal-coupling structure.
- Promotes M.1.2's F-GRH-1 and F-GRH-2 to unconditional FORCED.

### 2.4 What GRH leaves open

- The **specific gauge group** (U(1) vs SU(N) vs combinations) is NOT fixed by GRH. GRH only asserts that *whatever* gauge structure is present is realised as a participation rule-type. → Q.2.
- The **specific charge spectrum** (which rule-types carry which charges) is rule-type data per Lever L2/L3. NOT fixed by GRH. → Q.2 + empirical.
- Whether **non-Abelian** gauge fields require structural extensions beyond the Abelian R.1 minimal-coupling derivation. → Q.2.
- Whether the **gauge field's bandwidth amplitude** is constrained (e.g., quantised). → Q.7 second-quantisation scoping.
- Whether the **graviton** can be brought into the same framework as a (1, 1) tensor representation rule-type. → likely SPECULATIVE / out of Arc Q scope.

### 2.5 Lightlike-worldline subtlety

The gauge rule-type carries massless content, hence its worldline is lightlike. Primitive 13's proper-time relational timing degenerates on lightlike worldlines (proper-time interval = 0). This means:

- Stage R.1's commitment-rate construction needs reformulation for lightlike rule-types.
- The four-band decomposition along γ_K may need to be re-anchored using a lightlike affine parameter rather than τ.
- Bandwidth content is still well-defined (b is a scalar field on the event manifold), but the per-worldline accounting that worked for massive rule-types needs adjustment.

This is a real structural issue — not a blocker, but a refinement Q.1 must address.

### 2.6 Status of GRH

**CANDIDATE — to be evaluated in Stage Q.1.** Arc Q's first major deliverable. Expected closure: affirmative with lightlike-refinement detail filled in.

## 3. Major structural targets

### 3.1 Q.1 — Gauge-rule-type structure (GRH evaluation)

Closes GRH. Reformulates Stage R.1 minimal coupling with A_μ explicitly treated as a Case-P (1/2, 1/2) rule-type. Resolves lightlike-worldline subtleties. Expected closure: affirmative + clean structural reformulation.

### 3.2 Q.2 — Gauge-group derivation attempts

Asks whether ED primitives + R.2 taxonomy constrain the **gauge-group choice**:
- Is U(1) the unique structurally minimal choice from local-phase-invariance arguments?
- Is SU(N) gauge structure reachable by extending the rule-type's internal index space?
- Is the Standard-Model SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) primitive-derivable, partially-derivable, or fully empirical?

**Expected verdict:** mostly empirical. ED primitives almost certainly do not force the SM gauge group (this would be a massive structural claim and there is no current mechanism in hand). The question is whether ED *constrains* gauge-group choice at all, or accepts it as full empirical inheritance.

### 3.3 Q.3 — Interaction vertices and coupling constants

Derives interaction structure between rule-types:
- Vertex structure from R.2.4 Fierz-bilinear basis.
- Coupling-constant inheritance vs. structural derivation.
- Tree-level interaction Lagrangians for QED-analogue + QCD-analogue.

**Expected verdict:** vertices structurally classified by Fierz basis; coupling values empirical.

### 3.4 Q.4 — Higgs-like symmetry breaking and mass generation

Asks whether ED has a primitive-level analogue of the Higgs mechanism:
- Spontaneous symmetry breaking from rule-type interaction structure.
- VEV-like nonzero bandwidth content as a structural attractor.
- Yukawa-like couplings from Fierz interactions.

**Expected verdict:** speculative. Arc M found no primitive Higgs analogue. Arc Q may identify an SSB mechanism in multi-chain dynamics (commitment-induced phase transitions), but specific Higgs-VEV-style mass generation is more likely empirical than structural.

### 3.5 Q.5 — Radiative corrections and g − 2 deviations

Loop-level corrections to Dirac g = 2:
- Arc R produces g = 2 structurally. QED's g − 2 ≈ α/π + … requires loop-level / second-quantised content.
- Whether ED's primitive structure supports loop integrals or whether these are inherited from QFT machinery.

**Expected verdict:** loop-level structure is inherited via Arc Q's second-quantisation layer (Q.7). g − 2 is a numerical prediction of QFT; ED inherits its structural form via the Q.7 framework but does not predict α numerically.

### 3.6 Q.6 — Generations, flavour, and mixing

Three-generation structure of fermions; CKM/PMNS mixing; CP violation:
- Whether a rule-type-level mechanism produces the count of three generations.
- Whether flavour-mixing matrices are structurally constrained or fully empirical.

**Expected verdict:** likely fully empirical. Arc M found no generation mechanism. Arc Q may probe cosmological-boundary-condition routes but these are SPECULATIVE.

### 3.7 Q.7 — Second quantisation and field-level composition

Multi-chain participation as a quantum field:
- Promotion of single-chain Ψ to operator-valued Ψ̂ on a Fock space.
- Creation/annihilation operators from chain-creation / chain-destruction events.
- Anti-commutation for Case R / commutation for Case P FORCED by R.2.5 spin-statistics.

**Expected verdict:** structurally FORCED at the form level (R.2.5 already gives the (anti-)commutation choice). Arc Q.7 assembles this into the standard QFT framework. Likely the most positive-content target in Arc Q.

### 3.8 Q.8 — Vacuum structure and zero-point participation

ED's analogue of the QFT vacuum:
- Zero-point bandwidth content as a primitive-level structure.
- Vacuum-energy regularisation issues (UV cutoff, renormalisation).
- Whether ED's vacuum is structurally distinct from QFT's vacuum.

**Expected verdict:** mixed. ED's bandwidth-field structure may give a natural finite "vacuum" via primitive-level UV cutoff (e.g., minimal commitment scale), but this is SPECULATIVE pending Q.7 closure.

### 5.2 Likely inherited

- **Q.2 (Gauge group):** SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) is almost certainly empirical inheritance. ED primitives admit gauge structure; specific group is rule-type / phenomenological data.
- **Q.5 (Coupling values, including α):** Numerical coupling constants are inherited.
- **Q.5 (Radiative-correction values):** g − 2 ≈ α/π + … is inherited from QED machinery once Q.7 supplies the loop-level structure.

### 5.3 Frontier research

- **Q.4 (Higgs analogue):** SPECULATIVE. May identify an SSB mechanism in commitment-induced phase transitions; full Higgs-VEV equivalence unlikely.
- **Q.6 (Generation count + mixing):** SPECULATIVE. No mechanism in hand; cosmological-boundary-condition routes possible but speculative.

### 5.4 May require new primitives

If Arc Q surfaces structural content that cannot be derived from Primitives 01–13 as written, candidate extensions could include:

- A **multi-chain-content primitive** explicitly accounting for joint participation across many chains beyond the two-chain individuation pairing.
- A **vacuum primitive** if zero-point structure cannot be fitted into existing primitives.
- A **generation primitive** (most speculative) if a structural mechanism for three generations is identified.

These are speculative. Arc Q should attempt closure with existing primitives first; only propose new primitives if structurally forced.

### 5.5 Honest verdict distribution (working prior)

For Arc Q overall:

## 7. Framing

### 7.1 Arc Q is the largest arc in ED

Eight structural substages, each comparable in scope to a single Arc-M sub-memo. Multi-month research program. Integrates everything from Phase-1 through Arc M and pushes into territory where ED's primitives are visibly thin (gauge group, generations, Higgs).

### 7.2 Arc Q may produce partial or negative results

Honest framing — Arc M closed H1-dominant; Arc Q is expected to produce a mix:

- **Form-level FORCED** content (gauge-rule-type, vertex classification, second quantisation, vacuum structure).
- **Empirical inheritance** demarcated (gauge-group choice, coupling values, mass values, generation count).
- **Speculative content** flagged (Higgs analogue, generation mechanism).

The arc's **goal is to map the structural frontier honestly**, not to force predictions ED's primitives cannot support.

### 7.3 Arc Q's value

Even if Arc Q closes mostly-inheritance, the value is:

(a) Confirming that ED's primitive stack is **consistent with QFT structure** at the form level (gauge, second quantisation, Fierz interactions).
(b) Cleanly demarcating which QFT content ED predicts vs. inherits, paralleling Arc M's clean separation for mass.
(c) Identifying what (if anything) requires new primitives beyond 01–13.
(d) Producing the foundation for downstream platform-bridge work and empirical retrodiction.

### 7.4 What Arc Q does not aim to do

- Predict the SM gauge group from primitives (likely impossible).
- Predict α, sin²θ_W, or other coupling constants (inherited).
- Predict generation count or fermion mass spectrum (Arc M closed; no mechanism).
- Reproduce specific QFT calculations from scratch (Arc Q uses QFT machinery via Q.7 inheritance).
