## 2. Decomposition into Sub-Features

U3 packages five structurally distinct sub-claims, each requiring independent structural status determination.

### 2.1 The five sub-features

- **(F1) Existence of a strongly continuous time-translation representation.** Time translations $U_t : P_K(x, s) \mapsto P_K(x, s - t)$ are well-defined unitary operators on the U2 Hilbert space, forming a strongly continuous one-parameter group. Primitive 13 supplies time-translation symmetry; Primitive 03 supplies the homogeneity that makes the group property meaningful; the U2 inner product supplies the unitarity check.

- **(F2) Uniqueness of the self-adjoint generator (Stone's theorem).** By Stone's theorem applied to F1's group, there exists a unique self-adjoint operator $\hat{H}$ on the U2 Hilbert space such that $U_t = \exp(-i \hat{H} t / \hbar)$. The generator is the Hamiltonian. This is the temporal analog of U5's F2.

- **(F3) Linearity of the evolution equation.** The induced evolution $i\hbar \, \partial_t P_K = \hat{H} P_K$ is linear in $P_K$. Linearity follows from the linearity of the unitary representation $U_t$ (which acts linearly on Hilbert-space elements by definition of "operator on a Hilbert space"). This sub-feature is automatic given F1 + F2.

- **(F4) First-order-in-time structure.** The evolution equation is first-order in $\partial_t$, not second-order ($\partial_t^2$, Klein–Gordon-like) or higher. First-order structure follows directly from the operator-exponential form $U_t = \exp(-i \hat{H} t / \hbar)$: differentiating once in $t$ yields $i\hbar \, \partial_t U_t = \hat{H} U_t$, which is first-order. This sub-feature is automatic given F2.

- **(F5) Hamiltonian form of the generator (compatibility with U4).** The unique generator $\hat{H}$ produced by F2 coincides with the Hamiltonian operator whose form U4 derives — i.e., $\hat{H} = \hbar^2 |\hat{p}|^2/(2m) + V(\hat{x})$ in the non-relativistic single-particle scope. The two arcs (U3 and U4) must produce *the same operator*, not two operators that happen to share a name. Compatibility is the structural-coherence check that closes the Framing-1 conditionality U4 carried.

The arc has one load-bearing item (F5). F1 and F2 close cleanly via established techniques (Stone's theorem on Primitive 13 + U2). F3 and F4 are automatic given F1 + F2.

The arc shape is therefore **anticipated cleaner than U4 and methodologically parallel to U5** — the same Stone's-theorem skeleton applied to a different symmetry group (time translations rather than spatial translations).

### 2.3 The F5 load-bearing question, isolated

F5 is the genuinely substantive structural question of this arc. The U4 derivation produced a specific self-adjoint operator $\hat{H}_{U4} = \hbar^2|\hat{p}|^2/(2m) + V(\hat{x})$ via Galilean Lie algebra integration (acting on the U5 momentum operator). The U3 derivation will produce a self-adjoint operator $\hat{H}_{U3}$ via Stone's theorem applied to time-translation symmetry. The F5 question is whether $\hat{H}_{U3} = \hat{H}_{U4}$ as operators on the U2 Hilbert space.

Three possible outcomes:

1. **F5 closes uniquely (anticipated).** The two operators are identical. U3 promotes to FORCED; U4 promotes to FORCED-unconditional retroactively.
2. **F5 closes conditionally.** The two operators agree up to an additive constant (a vacuum-energy zero-point shift) or an additive Hermitian operator from outside U4's scope. The verdict is PARTIAL with the residual content the additive-shift freedom.
3. **F5 fails.** The two operators are structurally inequivalent. U3 verdict is FREE; U4's Framing-1 conditionality stands.

The natural prior, by Stone's-theorem uniqueness, is outcome (1). The arc must verify this.

### 2.4 Discrete vs continuum regime split

The discrete-vs-continuum split applies to U3 in the same way it applied to U5:

- **Discrete regime.** Time translations on the participation graph are well-defined as graph automorphisms preserving the time-axis structure of Primitive 13. Stone's theorem applies on the discrete U2 Hilbert space. F1–F5 close in this regime first.
- **Continuum regime, flat-spacetime baseline.** Standard time-translation symmetry on $\mathbb{R}_t$. Stone's theorem applies on the continuum U2 Hilbert space. The U2-Continuum conformal gauge acts trivially on the time-translation generator (the conformal rescaling is spatial-only at the description level, leaving time-translation generator invariant) — a check parallel to U5's gauge-compatibility check.
- **Continuum regime, curved-spacetime baseline.** Time-translation symmetry is local rather than global (Killing-vector field at each point). This is anticipated to remain within the gravitational-sector arc's outlook (Phase-3) rather than within Phase-1.

The Phase-1 scope (non-relativistic, flat spacetime) addresses the first two regimes. The third is out of scope.

## 3. Falsifier Inventory

The arc is **closed FORCED** if all five sub-features (F1–F5) close uniquely under the available structural inputs.

The arc is **closed PARTIAL** if F1–F4 close but F5 admits an additive-shift or related residual freedom.

The arc is **closed FREE** if any of F1–F5 admits a primitive-consistent alternative that produces a structurally distinct dynamical equation.

### 3.1 The seven specific falsifiers

- **(Fal-1) Non-unitary time evolution.** Time translations exist on the U2 Hilbert space but fail to preserve the U2 inner product. Physical interpretation: probability would not be conserved under time evolution. Targets: F1. Source: primitive-level (Primitive 13 + U2). Evidence that would count: a structural argument that the U2 inner product is *not* time-translation-invariant — e.g., the U2 measure carries an explicit time-dependent weight function or the participation-measure components rescale under time translation.

- **(Fal-2) Multiple inequivalent generators.** The time-translation group admits two or more inequivalent self-adjoint generators on the U2 Hilbert space. Stone's theorem fails. Physical interpretation: there would be no unique Hamiltonian; energy would be structurally ambiguous. Targets: F2. Source: primitive-level (would require Stone's theorem itself to fail, which is mathematically excluded). Evidence: nothing realistic; this falsifier is dispatched by the mathematical theorem itself.

- **(Fal-3) Non-linear evolution.** The induced evolution equation is non-linear in $P_K$ (e.g., a Gross-Pitaevskii-like nonlinearity). Physical interpretation: superposition would not be preserved under time evolution; quantum mechanics's linearity principle would fail. Targets: F3. Source: U2-dependent (linear unitary representation requires the Hilbert-space structure). Evidence: a structural argument that the time-translation operators are not linear on Hilbert-space elements — which would contradict the definition of "operator on a Hilbert space."

- **(Fal-4) Higher-order-in-time evolution.** The evolution equation is second-order or higher in $\partial_t$ (e.g., Klein–Gordon-like $\partial_t^2 \psi = -\hat{H}^2 \psi$, or some other higher-order form). Physical interpretation: the Schrödinger equation's first-order character would be replaced by a relativistic-like second-order structure. Targets: F4. Source: U4-dependent (the non-relativistic scope condition is what selects first-order over second-order). Evidence: a structural argument that the operator-exponential form $U_t = \exp(-i\hat{H}t/\hbar)$ does not apply — which would require Stone's theorem itself to fail.

- **(Fal-5) Generator incompatible with U4 kinetic structure.** The U3-derived generator $\hat{H}_{U3}$ is not equal to the U4-derived operator $\hat{H}_{U4} = \hbar^2|\hat{p}|^2/(2m) + V(\hat{x})$. Physical interpretation: the time-translation generator and the kinetic-plus-potential operator are different observables; energy and the generator-of-time-translations are decoupled. Targets: F5. Source: U4-dependent (this is the load-bearing compatibility check). Evidence: a structural argument that the two Stone-theorem applications (spatial translations for U4, time translations for U3) produce inequivalent operators — for instance, if Galilean Lie algebra integration produces an operator that is not time-translation-conjugate.

- **(Fal-6) Non-self-adjoint generator.** The time-translation generator exists but is not self-adjoint (only Hermitian or symmetric on a non-dense domain). Physical interpretation: energy would not be a physical observable; eigenvalues need not be real; time evolution would not be unitary. Targets: F2. Source: primitive-level (would require Stone's theorem to fail). Evidence: a structural argument that the time-translation group is not strongly continuous on a dense domain — which would contradict the standard properties of $L^2$ translations.

- **(Fal-7) Non-continuous time-translation representation.** Time translations are well-defined for a discrete set of times but do not admit continuous-parameter extension. Physical interpretation: time would be fundamentally discrete at the structural level; no infinitesimal time-evolution generator would exist. Targets: F1. Source: primitive-level (Primitive 13 supplies continuous time, but a more conservative reading might restrict it to discrete time). Evidence: a primitive-level argument that the time axis is discrete rather than continuous — a question about Primitive 13's interpretation rather than Stone's theorem.

### 3.3 The load-bearing falsifier, isolated

**Fal-5 is the substantive falsifier of the arc.** Its dispatch requires showing that the Stone's-theorem application to time-translation symmetry produces the *same* self-adjoint operator as the Galilean-integration construction in U4. Two structural arguments are anticipated:

1. **Galilean-algebra closure argument.** The Galilean Lie algebra contains $\hat{H}$ as the time-translation generator and $\hat{p}$, $\hat{x}$, $\hat{K}$ as the spatial-symmetry generators. The algebra's commutator structure forces the time-translation generator to coincide with the operator U4 derives via Galilean integration. The compatibility is a property of the Galilean group, not a separate commitment.

2. **Stone-theorem uniqueness argument.** Stone's theorem produces a *unique* self-adjoint generator from a strongly continuous unitary group. If the U4 operator $\hat{H}_{U4}$ is the generator of *any* strongly continuous unitary group on the U2 Hilbert space, and if U3's time-translation group is itself strongly continuous and unitary, then the two generators must coincide if and only if the two groups coincide. Operator equality reduces to group equality.

The Memo 03 task will be to make one (or both) of these arguments primitive-level rigorous.
