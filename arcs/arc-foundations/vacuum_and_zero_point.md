### 5.2 Argument for UV-FIN promotion to FORCED

Consider an arbitrary multi-chain participation integral 𝒜 representing some multi-loop QFT diagram in the continuum approximation:

  𝒜 = ∫ d⁴k₁ d⁴k₂ … (integrand involving propagators, vertices, polarisation tensors).

In the continuum, large-k regions of integration produce UV divergences when integrand grows or fails to fall fast enough. In ED's primitive-level realisation, the same physical content corresponds to a sum over discrete multi-chain commitment-event configurations:

  𝒜_ED = Σ_{discrete configurations} (primitive-level amplitudes).

**Three-step argument:**

(i) **Each summand is finite.** A primitive-level configuration involves a finite number of micro-events (Primitive 01 finiteness per event-region) with bounded participation amplitudes (Primitive 04 boundedness). Each amplitude is a finite real or complex number.

(ii) **The configuration sum has a finite number of terms per finite event-region.** Primitive 01 discreteness + Primitive 13 finite proper-time intervals mean only finitely many micro-events fit into a finite spacetime region. The configuration count is finite per region.

(iii) **Bounded total amplitude.** Bandwidth conservation (Primitive 04 + flux-continuity) bounds the total amplitude sum; even as the region grows, the contribution from any specific configuration class is bounded.

Combining (i)–(iii): **𝒜_ED is finite for any finite spacetime region, and its limit as region → infinite spacetime exists or diverges only at the rate physically expected (e.g., density of states proportional to volume).** UV divergences of the conventional form (∫ d⁴k k² → ∞ from large-k) **do not arise structurally** — they are artefacts of the continuum integral over-extending beyond the actual finite primitive-level domain.
