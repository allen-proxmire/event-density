## 1. Evaluation Frame

### 1.1 Definition of REFUTED in Phase-3

A curvature-coupling structure is REFUTED at primitive level iff it violates at least one of:

(a) **C1 Lorentz / general covariance:** the structure cannot be expressed as a generally-covariant combination of event-manifold quantities; it picks out a preferred frame, coordinate system, or violates general covariance under diffeomorphisms.

(b) **C2 Spin-statistics preservation:** the structure violates Stage R.2.5's spin-statistics theorem $\eta = (-1)^{2s}$ at finite curvature — e.g., produces apparent η-violations or Pauli-exclusion violations under curvature-dependent modulation.

(c) **C3 UV-FIN preservation:** the structure violates Theorem Q3's UV-FIN — e.g., curvature-modified kernels with insufficient short-time decay that amplify high-frequency contributions and break primitive-level finiteness.

(d) **Locality (Primitive 11):** the structure violates commitment-event locality at the primitive level.

(e) **Phase-2 / Arc N / Theorem GR1 violation:** the structure violates a FORCED structural theorem under curved-spacetime extension.

A structure that satisfies all five conditions is NOT REFUTED at Stage GR.3. REFUTED structures are eliminated from the catalogue's admissibility class; NOT-REFUTED structures pass to Stage GR.4 for cosmological-implications evaluation.

### 1.2 Sub-case structure

Some catalogue items admit multiple specific functional forms; sub-cases of a broader item may be REFUTED while other sub-cases remain admissible. Stage GR.3 records sub-case structure where relevant — paralleling the Stage N.3 methodology where, e.g., V3 power-law was REFUTED for $\alpha \geq 2$ but admissible for $\alpha < 2$.

For Theorem GR1 (FORCED V1 with Synge world function), the FORCED status applies to the *class* of finite-width Synge-world-function kernels. Limit sub-cases at zero width and infinite width are REFUTED — bounding the FORCED class in curved spacetime exactly parallel to the Stage N.3 V1-δ / V1-∞ bounding in flat space.

### 1.3 Expected outcome

Per Phase-3 opening §7.1 + Stage GR.2 hand-off:

- 2–4 primary items REFUTED (concentrated in C2-violating sub-cases).
- 2–3 sub-cases of FORCED items REFUTED (bounding the FORCED admissibility classes).
- Remainder NOT-REFUTED.

This memo confirms the prior with **3 primary REFUTED + 3 sub-case REFUTED + 13 NOT-REFUTED + GR-4A as NOT REFUTED but SPECULATIVE**.

## 2. Primary REFUTED Candidates — Detailed Analysis

The three primary REFUTED candidates flagged in Stages GR.1 + GR.2 receive detailed analysis here.

### 2.1 GR-1E — Curvature-dependent Case-P/R distinctions REFUTED by C2

**Catalogue claim (GR.1 §2.5).** The exchange-statistics dichotomy $\eta(\tau) \in \{+1, -1\}$ from Stage R.2.1 becomes curvature-dependent in curved spacetime: effective $\eta_\mathrm{eff}(x) = \pm 1 + \delta_\eta(R(x))$ at finite curvature.

**Refutation analysis.** Stage R.2.5 establishes the spin-statistics theorem $\eta = (-1)^{2s}$ as **unconditional FORCED** at primitive level via the chain: exchange dichotomy → π_1(Q_2) = ℤ_2 → exchange-rotation generator identity → Cl(3,1) frame → MB closure. The forcing argument depends on:

- The exchange operation $E_{AB}$ involutive structure (Primitive-level, metric-independent).
- $\pi_1(Q_2) = \mathbb{Z}_2$ topology in 3+1D (depends on spatial dimensionality, not metric).
- The Cl(3,1) frame (algebraic structure, metric-independent at the level of γ-matrix anticommutation).

None of these depends on spacetime curvature. The π_1(Q_2) topological theorem holds in any 3+1D spacetime with locally-Minkowski tangent structure (the tangent-space dimensionality is what enters, and tangent spaces are flat by construction at every event). The Cl(3,1) frame algebra is metric-independent.

Therefore curvature-induced modifications of Case-P/R distinctions would violate the spin-statistics theorem at finite curvature — directly contradicting Stage R.2.5's unconditional FORCED status.

**Refuted sub-cases.** All curvature-induced Case-P/R modifications. No admissible sub-cases survive — the spin-statistics theorem applies pointwise at every event regardless of curvature, leaving no room for curvature-induced statistics-class shifts.

**Verdict:** **GR-1E REFUTED unconditionally by C2.**

**Constraint violated:** C2 (spin-statistics preservation).

**Primitive dependencies of refutation:** Stage R.2.1 exchange dichotomy; Stage R.2.3 π_1(Q_2) = ℤ_2; Stage R.2.4 Cl(3,1) frame; Stage R.2.5 spin-statistics theorem; Primitive 06 (general covariance preservation of locally-Minkowski tangent structure).

### 2.2 GR-1D — Curvature-dependent Fierz-class shifts REFUTED in TYPE-mixing sub-cases by C2

**Catalogue claim (GR.1 §2.4).** For Case-R rule-types, the L3 Fierz-class element $\Gamma_\tau$ becomes curvature-dependent: $\Gamma_\tau^\mathrm{curved}(x) = \Gamma_\tau^\mathrm{flat} + \gamma_\tau \cdot R(x) \cdot \mathbb{1} + \cdots$ or analogous combinations involving Riemann-tensor contractions.

**Refutation analysis.** Arc M Theorem M3 §6 established that the Fierz class structurally distinguishes mass-term TYPES (Dirac mass for $\Gamma = \mathbb{1}$, Majorana for charge-conjugation bilinear, pseudoscalar for $\Gamma = \gamma^5$). The TYPE classification is structural — Stage R.2.5 spin-statistics applies per TYPE and per rule-type's inherent statistics class.

Curvature-induced Fierz shifts that **mix TYPES** — e.g., a curvature term that turns a pure Dirac-mass rule-type into a partial Majorana-mass rule-type at finite curvature — would violate the structural TYPE classification. Specifically, in a region of finite curvature, a rule-type that is structurally Dirac at $R = 0$ would become structurally Dirac+Majorana at $R \neq 0$, changing its primitive-level identity.

The Fierz TYPE is rule-type data (Lever L3 from Primitive 07) — set per rule-type at the structural level, not modulated by spacetime curvature. Curvature-induced TYPE shifts would require rule-type data to depend on curvature, contradicting Primitive 07's rule-type classification as a discrete primitive-level structure.

**Refuted sub-cases.** Curvature shifts producing Dirac↔Majorana, Dirac↔pseudoscalar, or any cross-TYPE mixing. *Same-TYPE* curvature corrections (e.g., pure-Dirac rule-type with curvature-modified Dirac mass coefficient $\sigma_\tau \to \sigma_\tau(R)$) remain admissible — these are within-class refinements, not TYPE-mixing.

**Verdict:** **GR-1D in TYPE-mixing sub-cases REFUTED by C2.** Same-TYPE curvature corrections remain admissible (subsumed under GR-1B σ_τ curvature shift).

**Constraint violated:** C2 (Fierz TYPE preservation as a corollary of spin-statistics theorem).

**Primitive dependencies of refutation:** Stage R.2.4 Cl(3,1) Fierz basis; Stage R.2.5 spin-statistics; Arc M Theorem M3 §6 (Fierz as TYPE classifier); Primitive 07 L3.

### 2.3 GR-3B — Curvature-dependent commitment thresholds REFUTED in Case-R sub-cases by C2

**Catalogue claim (GR.1 §4.2).** Primitive 10's individuation threshold becomes curvature-dependent: $\Theta_K(x) = \Theta_K|_\mathrm{flat} \cdot h(R(x))$.

**Refutation analysis.** This is the curved-spacetime analogue of Stage N.3 §2.2's N2-B Case-R refutation. For Case-R rule-types, Primitive 10 enforces vanishing-on-coincidence: two same-type Case-R chains cannot coincide at the same event-manifold point (Pauli exclusion at structural level).

Curvature-modulated thresholds in the form $h(R(x))$ that introduce a *delay* between bandwidth-threshold-crossing and individuation status (parallel to N2-B hysteresis) violate Pauli exclusion in regions of finite curvature: two Case-R chains at the same point in a high-curvature region could coexist within the curvature-modulated delay window, violating the spin-statistics theorem at finite curvature.

Crucially, the distinction between **quantitative** threshold modulation (admissible) and **qualitative** delay (REFUTED) — parallel to Stage N.3 §4.5 N2-A vs N2-B distinction — applies here. A pure threshold value-shift $\Theta_K \to \Theta_K \cdot h(R)$ at fixed R is quantitative and admissible. A *delay* mechanism that prevents instantaneous individuation upon threshold-crossing is qualitative and REFUTED in Case-R.

**Refuted sub-cases.** Curvature-induced individuation *delay* in Case-R rule-types. Pure quantitative threshold value-shifts in Case-R (no delay, instantaneous individuation upon crossing) and any threshold modulation in Case-P remain admissible.

**Verdict:** **GR-3B in Case-R-delay sub-cases REFUTED by C2.** Quantitative threshold-shift sub-cases admissible.

**Constraint violated:** C2 (Pauli exclusion / spin-statistics).

**Primitive dependencies of refutation:** Primitive 10 vanishing-on-coincidence; Stage R.2.5 spin-statistics; Stage N.3 §2.2 (N2-B Case-R refutation framework, applied in curved spacetime).

## 3. Sub-Case Refutations of FORCED Items

Theorem GR1 (GR-2A) is unconditionally FORCED per Stage GR.2. The FORCED class admits a range of specific functional forms (curved-space analogues of V2 exponential, V3 with $\alpha < 2$, V4 multi-scale). We identify three limit sub-cases that are REFUTED to bound the FORCED admissibility class.

### 3.1 GR-2A-δ — Zero-width limit REFUTED by C3

**Sub-case statement.** The GR-2A-δ sub-case is the zero-width limit of Theorem GR1: $K_\mathrm{vac}^\mathrm{curved}(x, x') \to c_0 \cdot \delta_g^{(4)}(x, x')$ where $\delta_g^{(4)}$ is the covariant Dirac distribution on the curved manifold.

**Refutation analysis.** This is the curved-spacetime analogue of Stage N.3 §3.1's V1-δ refutation. The structural argument extends directly:

In a local inertial frame at any event $x$, the covariant δ-distribution reduces to the flat-space δ-function in the local-frame coordinates. Fourier transforming with respect to local-inertial-frame coordinates yields constant frequency-weighting, exactly as in flat space. UV-amplification pathology applies pointwise at every event.

Primitive 01 event-discreteness imposes a primitive-level resolution scale $\ell_\mathrm{ED}$ at every event regardless of curvature. The δ-curved limit is incompatible with this primitive resolution scale.

**Verdict:** **GR-2A-δ REFUTED by C3.** Bounds Theorem GR1 from below: curved-spacetime kernels must have non-zero width at every event.

**Constraint violated:** C3 (UV-FIN, applied pointwise via local-inertial-frame analysis).

**Primitive dependencies of refutation:** Primitive 01 event-discreteness (metric-independent); Theorem Q3 UV-FIN; Theorem N1 V1-δ refutation framework.

### 3.2 GR-2A-∞ — Infinite-width limit REFUTED by C1 + locality

**Sub-case statement.** The GR-2A-∞ sub-case is the infinite-width limit of Theorem GR1: $K_\mathrm{vac}^\mathrm{curved}(x, x') \to c_\infty$ for all $x, x'$ — the kernel is constant across all separations.

**Refutation analysis.** This is the curved-spacetime analogue of Stage N.3 §3.2's V1-∞ refutation. A constant kernel implies infinite correlation length on the curved manifold: every event is correlated with every other event with equal weight, including events at arbitrary geodesic distance and events outside causal-cone structure.

In curved spacetime, this is even more pathological than in flat space: the constant kernel does not respect the causal structure of the underlying manifold (light cones, horizon structures from ED-Phys-10 acoustic-metric content). Generally-covariant locality (Primitive 11 commitment-event locality, extended to curved spacetime) is violated structurally.

**Verdict:** **GR-2A-∞ REFUTED by C1 + locality.** Bounds Theorem GR1 from above: curved-spacetime kernels must have finite (decaying) width at every event.

**Constraint violated:** C1 (general covariance, in its locality-respecting interpretation) + locality (Primitive 11).

**Primitive dependencies of refutation:** Primitive 06 (general covariance); Primitive 11 (commitment-event locality); Theorem N1 V1-∞ refutation framework; ED-Phys-10 causal-cone structure.

### 3.3 GR-3A non-geodesic — Non-geodesic free-chain sub-case REFUTED by eikonal limit

**Sub-case statement.** GR-3A is FORCED-conditional (Stage GR.2 §3) on the acoustic-metric identification: free chains follow geodesics of $g^\mathrm{ac}_{\mu\nu}$. The non-geodesic sub-case posits that free chains follow *non-geodesic* worldlines — e.g., subject to some fictitious force or curvature-induced deflection beyond the metric's geodesic structure.

**Refutation analysis.** Stage R.1 / R.3 derive Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations on curved spacetime (with minimal coupling generalised to $D_\mu \to \nabla_\mu + (iq/\hbar) A_\mu$). In the eikonal / WKB approximation, the high-frequency limit yields equations of motion for chain worldlines that are **necessarily geodesics** of the metric appearing in the wave equation. This is a derived consequence of the wave equation's structure — it is not an assumption.

A non-geodesic free-chain sub-case would contradict this derived consequence. For free chains (no commitment, no individuation pairing, no gauge coupling), the only equation of motion structurally consistent with KG/Dirac in curved spacetime is geodesic motion on $g_{\mu\nu}$.

**Verdict:** **GR-3A non-geodesic sub-case REFUTED by eikonal-limit derivation.** Free chains following non-geodesic worldlines contradicts the structural content of KG/Dirac equations on curved spacetime.

**Constraint violated:** Stage R.1 / R.3 eikonal-limit derivation (a Phase-2 theorem-level structural consequence); Primitive 02 worldline structure; Primitive 13 affine-parameter generalisation.

**Primitive dependencies of refutation:** Stage R.1 (Klein-Gordon on curved spacetime); Stage R.3 (Dirac on curved spacetime); WKB / eikonal approximation framework.

## 5. GR-4A (Einstein-Equation Emergence) — Detailed Analysis

GR-4A is the most aggressive Phase-3 catalogue claim and deserves separate treatment. Stage GR.2 §7.1 verdict: **NOT FORCED, SPECULATIVE — ED-Phys-10 baseline preserved.** Stage GR.3 evaluates whether GR-4A is also REFUTED.

### 5.1 The structural status of GR-4A

GR-4A claims that the bandwidth-field equation, in the long-wavelength limit and in terms of the emergent acoustic metric $g^\mathrm{ac}_{\mu\nu}$, takes an Einstein-like form with kernel-weighted source:
$$
R_{\mu\nu} - \tfrac{1}{2} R g_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda_\mathrm{eff} g_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G_\mathrm{eff} \cdot T^\mathrm{kernel}_{\mu\nu}.
$$

### 5.2 Why GR-4A is NOT REFUTED

GR-4A as a *structural derivation from primitives* is NOT FORCED — ED-Phys-10's exhaustive analysis did not produce Einstein-equation emergence, and Phase-3's Theorem GR1 supplies kernel structure but not metric dynamics. However, GR-4A is also NOT REFUTED by any of C1/C2/C3/locality:

- **C1:** an Einstein-like equation is generally covariant by construction.
- **C2:** spin-statistics is preserved; GR-4A does not modify exchange dichotomy.
- **C3:** UV-FIN compatibility depends on $T^\mathrm{kernel}_{\mu\nu}$'s structure; with V1-kernel-weighted source per Theorem GR1, UV-FIN is preserved structurally.
- **Locality:** Einstein equations are local in $g_{\mu\nu}$ and its derivatives; GR-4A respects locality.

GR-4A is therefore **structurally admissible** as an effective-theory addition, but not derivable from primitives within current ED structural content.

### 5.3 Status of GR-4A

**Verdict:** **GR-4A is NOT REFUTED but remains SPECULATIVE.** Three sub-classes:

(a) **GR-4A as forced-from-primitives derivation:** NOT FORCED (Stage GR.2 §7.1); SPECULATIVE.
(b) **GR-4A as effective-theory addition:** ADMISSIBLE (passes all four constraints).
(c) **GR-4A as primitive-level structural commitment:** REQUIRES NEW PRIMITIVE — the current Primitive 01–13 stack does not derive Einstein dynamics; if Phase-3 (or a future phase) introduces a new primitive forcing curvature dynamics, GR-4A could be reclassified.

Phase-3 closes with GR-4A in status (a)+(b): SPECULATIVE as derivation, ADMISSIBLE as effective-theory addition, **NOT REFUTED structurally**. ED-Phys-10's "no Einstein equations from primitives" verdict is preserved, but ED admits Einstein equations as an external addition consistent with all four constraints.

### 5.4 GR-Phys-10 baseline vs Phase-3 GR-4A status

The honest framing:

- **GR-4C (acoustic-metric-only baseline):** what ED *has* structurally.
- **GR-4A (Einstein equations as effective addition):** what ED *admits* structurally.
- **GR-4A (Einstein equations as forced-from-primitives):** what ED *does not derive*.

This positions Phase-3 in the **(GR-add) ADMISSIBLE / (GR-emerge) NOT FORCED** quadrant — paralleling the Phase-3 opening §7.1 prior placement.
