## 3. The structural problem

### 3.1 What "generation" means in rule-type ontology

In SM, a generation is a set of fermion species sharing identical gauge quantum numbers but differing in mass (and therefore in some non-quantum-number property). The three generations have identical SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) representations; they differ only in mass values and Yukawa couplings.

In ED's rule-type ontology, this translates as:

**Generation = Multiple distinct rule-types {τ_1, τ_2, τ_3, …} sharing all of:**
- Same spin s (e.g., all 1/2).
- Same statistics class (Case R).
- Same Lorentz representation ((1/2, 0) ⊕ (0, 1/2) for Dirac fermions).
- Same gauge-group representation (e.g., SU(2)_L doublet, U(1)_Y hypercharge for left-handed leptons).

**…but differing in:**
- Bandwidth-partition pattern w_{τ_i}^X (Lever L1).
- Fierz-class assignment Γ_{τ_i} (Lever L3).
- Spectral-rate content ⟨(∂ ln b)²⟩_{τ_i} (chain-initial-condition data).
- Resulting σ_{τ_i} value (mass).
- Yukawa coupling Y_{ij} to Higgs sector.

The structural slot for generations is **rule-type multiplicity at fixed gauge / spin / statistics**.

### 3.2 Does ED constrain the multiplicity?

**No primitive-level constraint on rule-type count.** Primitive 07 §7.4 admits any finite count of rule-types; Levers L1, L3 are continuous (band-weight pattern) and discrete (Fierz-class) data with no primitive-imposed cardinality.

Therefore the generation count is **rule-type-empirical data** — a fact about how many distinct same-quantum-number rule-types are realised, not a primitive-level theorem.

(SQ-1) Can ED admit multiple same-(s, statistics, gauge-rep) rule-types? — §3.1 answers YES.
(SQ-2) Does ED force any specific count (1, 3, ∞, …)? — §3.2 answers NO.

## 4. Six candidate mechanisms

### 4.1 G1 — Rule-type duplication

**Statement.** Three or more independent rule-types {τ_R^{(1)}, τ_R^{(2)}, τ_R^{(3)}} exist at primitive level, each sharing (s, statistics, gauge rep) but differing in L1 / L3 data. Each is a separate primitive-level participation rule-type.

**Evaluation.**

- **Primitive 07:** admits arbitrary rule-type multiplicity at fixed (s, gauge rep). **Compatible.**
- **Stage R.2:** spin-statistics applies per rule-type identically. **Compatible.**
- **Stage M.2:** σ_τ values inherited per rule-type; different generations have different σ. **Compatible.**
- **Q.3:** Yukawa vertices Ψ̄_τ_i Γ Ψ_τ_j φ admissible across distinct rule-types. **Compatible.**

**Status:** **G1 is ADMISSIBLE-CLEAN at primitive level.** Most direct realisation of generations. Cleanly compatible with all primitives + closed stages.

**What G1 forces:** existence of multiplicity if multiplicity is occupied. Does NOT force any specific count.

### 4.2 G2 — Bandweight-pattern differentiation

**Statement.** A single rule-type τ_R admits multiple "modes" or "branches" distinguished by different w_τ^X bandweight patterns. Each branch behaves like a separate generation.

**Evaluation.**

- **Primitive 04 / Lever L1:** w_τ^X is rule-type data; if multiple distinct (w^int, w^adj, w^env, w^com) patterns are admitted at the same rule-type level, they could behave like sub-types.
- **Issue:** within a single rule-type, w_τ^X is usually treated as a fixed property of the rule-type (Lever L1 specifies *the* partition for *that* rule-type). Allowing multiple patterns within one rule-type would blur the rule-type boundary.

**Sub-status.** G2 collapses into G1 if the multiple bandweight patterns are reified as separate rule-types (which is the natural primitive-level reading). G2 as a single-rule-type-with-multiple-modes is **structurally inconsistent** with the rule-type-as-discrete-classification framework of Primitive 07 §7.4.

**Status:** **G2 is REFUTED as a distinct mechanism.** Collapses into G1. Not a separate route to generation structure.

### 4.3 G3 — Fierz-class differentiation

**Statement.** Different Fierz-class assignments Γ_τ at L3 distinguish generations of the same rule-type.

**Evaluation.**

- M.1.3 §3.4 established that Fierz class fixes mass-term TYPE (Dirac / Majorana / pseudoscalar), not generations within a single type.
- For three Dirac generations, all three carry Γ = 𝟙 (Dirac mass term); Fierz class does not differentiate them.

**Status:** **G3 is REFUTED as a generation mechanism within a fixed mass-term type.** Fierz class differentiates types (Dirac vs Majorana), not generations within a type.

G3 would only be admissible if the three SM generations correspond to three different Fierz classes, which they empirically do not (all charged leptons are Dirac-mass-class).

### 4.4 G4 — Vacuum-anchored differentiation

**Statement.** Different vacuum sectors (à la Q.8 vacuum structure) carry different generation labels. Generations are labels of distinct vacuum-pattern selections.

**Evaluation.**

- Requires Q.7 / Q.8 vacuum-structure closure to evaluate concretely.
- Conceptually similar to "θ-vacuum" labelling in QCD or Hilbert-space-superselection-sector arguments.
- Plausible but speculative.

**Status:** **DEFERRED to Q.7 / Q.8.** If Q.8 admits non-trivial vacuum sectors at primitive level, G4 becomes evaluable. Currently SPECULATIVE.

### 4.5 G5 — Radiative splitting

**Statement.** Single rule-type at tree level acquires generation-like splitting through radiative corrections (Q.5). Higher-order multi-chain participation contributions induce mass differentiation.

**Evaluation.**

- Q.5 found loop corrections admissible structurally; numerical content INHERITED.
- For radiative splitting to produce three distinct mass eigenstates from a single tree-level rule-type, the loop dynamics would need to admit multi-valued solutions or symmetry-breaking patterns within the loop integrals.

- Empirically, generation differences are tree-level (much larger than radiative corrections), making G5 phenomenologically inconsistent with SM data.

**Status:** **G5 is REFUTED as a SM-relevant generation mechanism.** Phenomenologically inconsistent (loop corrections are too small to explain m_t/m_e ≈ 350,000).

## 5. Mixing and CP violation

### 5.1 Mixing matrix structural admissibility

For multiple same-quantum-number rule-types {τ_1, τ_2, τ_3}, the mass-eigenstate basis (eigenstates of σ_τ operator after SSB) need not coincide with the gauge-eigenstate basis (eigenstates of gauge-charge operator). The unitary transformation between them is the **mixing matrix** (CKM for quarks, PMNS for neutrinos).

**Primitive evaluation.**

- **Q.3 vertices:** flavour-changing vertices Ψ̄_{τ_i} Γ Ψ_{τ_j} A_μ admissible if τ_i, τ_j carry compatible gauge representations. **Compatible.**
- **Q.4 Higgs / Yukawa:** Yukawa coupling matrix Y_{ij} at vertex Ψ̄_{τ_i} Γ Ψ_{τ_j} φ has off-diagonal elements producing mass-gauge basis misalignment. **Compatible.**

**Status:** **Mixing matrices are STRUCTURALLY ADMISSIBLE.** Their existence requires ≥ 2 generations (otherwise no mixing possible) and a non-diagonal Yukawa coupling. Both conditions are admissible per primitives + Q.3 / Q.4.

**What ED forces:** if multiple generations exist AND Yukawa matrix is non-diagonal, mixing matrix is FORCED to exist (linear-algebraic theorem).

**What ED does not force:** specific values of mixing-matrix elements (CKM angles, PMNS angles).

### 5.2 CP violation structural admissibility

CP violation in SM arises from the single complex phase in the CKM matrix (single non-trivial phase for three generations; for n generations, (n−1)(n−2)/2 phases).

**Primitive evaluation.**

- **Stage R.2.4:** Cl(3,1) bilinears Ψ̄γ^5Ψ (pseudoscalar) + Ψ̄γ^μγ^5Ψ (axial) couple to parity-violating combinations. **Compatible.**
- **Yukawa with complex matrix entries:** structurally admissible at L3; complex couplings produce CP-violating phases.
- **Number of independent CP phases:** linear-algebra count gives 1 for n = 3 (CKM Jarlskog-style), more for larger n. This is mathematics, not primitive content.

**Status:** **CP violation is STRUCTURALLY ADMISSIBLE.** Specific phases INHERITED.

**What ED forces:** if Yukawa matrix is complex AND has more than two generations, CP phase necessarily exists (linear algebra).

**What ED does not force:** specific phase value, sign of CP-violating effects, strong-CP θ-angle (different mechanism — Peccei-Quinn axion etc., out of Q.6 scope).

### 5.3 Yukawa hierarchies

**Question.** Can ED structurally constrain the spread m_t/m_e ≈ 350,000?

**Evaluation.** From Arc M (M.1.3 closure):

- No primitive-level mechanism constrains cross-rule-type w-pattern relationships.

**Status:** **Yukawa hierarchies are NOT structurally derivable.** Confirmed M.1.3 verdict carries forward to Q.6.
