# One-Page Pitch: Testing a New Diffusion Law with FRAP

## What is the experiment?

A standard FRAP experiment on concentrated BSA protein solutions. Photobleach a small spot in a dense fluorescent protein solution, then watch it recover. Record the movie. That is the entire experiment.

## Why is it interesting?

When proteins are packed tightly, diffusion slows down — everyone knows this. But *how* it slows down matters. Standard theory says the bleached spot recovers as the square root of time. We have a new theory that predicts something different: at high protein concentration, the recovery is three times slower than the square-root law.

This prediction comes from a mathematical framework (Event Density) that has been validated against 10 different materials for its static predictions (how diffusion coefficient depends on concentration). The FRAP experiment would be the first test of its *dynamic* prediction (how fast a concentration front actually moves). It is the most important open test of the theory.

The predicted recovery exponent is 0.16. The standard prediction is 0.50. These are easy to distinguish — even a rough measurement would be decisive.
