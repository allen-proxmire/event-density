1. PURPOSE OF THE ARCHITECTURAL CANON
========================================================================

The first eleven chapters of the ED-Arch series decomposed the unified
ED cosmology PDE into nine structural layers (motifs, laws, geometry,
field theory, energetics, causality, information, symmetry, invariants)
and then reassembled them into a single coherent system (synthesis).
This twelfth chapter extracts the canon: the minimal set of structural
principles that generate the entire architecture.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.1 WHAT A CANON IS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

An architectural canon, in the ED context, is:

  (i)   THE MINIMAL SET.  The smallest collection of independent
        structural principles from which every motif, law, geometric
        object, field component, energy functional, causal channel,
        information transformation, symmetry, and invariant of the
        ED architecture can be derived. If any principle is removed,
        something in the architecture becomes underivable. If any
        principle is added, it is redundant with the existing set.

  (ii)  THE IRREDUCIBLE CORE.  None of the canonical principles can be
        derived from the others. Each stands on its own as a structural
        axiom. Together they form a basis -- in the algebraic sense --
        for the architecture's entire content. The canon has no
        internal redundancy.

  (iii) THE IDENTITY CONDITIONS.  The canon defines what it means to
        be an ED architecture. Any PDE that satisfies all canonical
        principles is architecturally ED, regardless of its specific
        functional forms. Any PDE that violates even one canonical
        principle is not ED, regardless of how similar it may appear.
        The canon is the architecture's fingerprint.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.2 WHAT A CANON IS NOT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The canon must be distinguished from three other structural concepts
that appear in the ED-Arch series:

  LAWS (ED-Arch-03) are structural constraints that hold universally
  within the architecture. There are nine laws organized as three
  axioms and six theorems. Laws describe what the architecture must do.
  The canon explains WHY the laws hold: each law is a consequence of
  one or more canonical principles. Laws are derived; the canon is
  primitive.

  INVARIANTS (ED-Arch-10) are quantities and structures that remain
  unchanged under architectural transformations. There are nine
  irreducible invariants, seven invariant relations, and six
  equivalence classes. Invariants describe what does not change. The
  canon explains WHY certain things are invariant: the canonical
  principles impose the invariances. Invariants are consequences;
  the canon is the source.

  MOTIFS (ED-Arch-02) are recurring structural patterns. There are
  eight motifs. Motifs describe what the architecture does. The canon
  explains WHY these specific motifs exist: each motif is generated by
  the interaction of specific canonical principles. Motifs are
  manifestations; the canon is the generator.

The relationship is hierarchical:

  CANON --> LAWS --> MOTIFS --> BEHAVIORS --> FEATURES
                 --> INVARIANTS
                 --> GEOMETRY
                 --> (all other layers)

The canon sits at the apex. Everything else descends from it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.3 WHY THE CANON IS NEEDED
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The nine-layer architecture described in ED-Arch-02 through ED-Arch-10
is rich but redundant. Many features at higher layers are derivable
from features at lower layers. The laws imply the geometry. The
energetics imply the causal structure. The symmetries imply the
invariants. Working within the full nine layers, it is not immediately
clear which structural choices are fundamental and which are
consequences.

The canon resolves this by identifying the irreducible foundation.
It answers: what are the fewest choices you must make to specify the
ED architecture completely? The answer is seven. Seven independent
structural principles, no more and no fewer, generate the entire
ontology.

This matters for three reasons:

  IDENTITY: The canon tells you what ED is. Not a list of 8 motifs
  or 9 laws or 9 invariants, but 7 principles. If you know the
  canon, you know the architecture.

  UNIVERSALITY: The canon tells you why diverse PDEs can be
  architecturally identical. Any PDE satisfying the same 7 principles
  will exhibit the same motifs, obey the same laws, and possess the
  same invariants, even if its functional forms are different.

  FRAGILITY: The canon tells you exactly where the architecture is
  vulnerable. Violating any one of the 7 principles breaks the
  architecture in specific, predictable ways. The canon is the
  minimal attack surface.

2. THE SEVEN CANONICAL PRINCIPLES
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(a) CANONICAL PRINCIPLE 1 -- OPERATOR STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       Operator Structure
Statement:  The evolution of the density field is governed by a
            nonlinear operator of the form
            F[rho] = M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) + M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2
                     - P(rho),
            comprising curvature-dependent diffusion, gradient-squared
            nonlinearity, and density-dependent restoring force.

Why it is irreducible:
  The three-term structure of F is not derivable from any other
  principle. Channel complementarity (P2) describes how F enters the
  density equation, not what F is. The penalty equilibrium (P3)
  constrains the zero of P, not the existence of P as a term. The
  mobility capacity bound (P4) constrains M's behavior at rho_max,
  not M's role in the operator. The operator structure is the
  fundamental choice: the decision to build density evolution from
  curvature, gradient energy, and restoring force. No combination of
  the other six principles implies this choice.

What architectural layers it generates:
  - Motifs: Saddle (from nabla^2 sign-reversal at extrema), Nonlinear
    Triad (from M'|nabla(rho)|^2 coupling), Dissipator (from the
    diffusive character of M*nabla^2)
  - Laws: Modal Hierarchy (eigenvalue spacing from nabla^2), Harmonic
    Protection (triad ratio from M' coupling structure), Transient
    Structure (from dissipative operator character)
  - Geometry: Triad Locus (where M' coupling is active), Modal Decay
    Funnel (from k^2 spectral decay)
  - Field Theory: operator field F[rho] as the "rule" field
  - Energetics: V_M (mobility curvature energy), V_N (nonlinear
    interaction energy), D-channel dissipation
  - Causality: spatial propagation (diffusion), spectral propagation
    (triad mixing)
  - Information: routing (Saddle), mixing (Triad), compression (Modal
    Funnel)
  - Symmetry: spatial translation symmetry (nabla^2 is translation-
    invariant), sign-inversion symmetry at extrema
  - Invariants: gradient sign, modal ordering (k^2 decay), k3/k1
    ratio (from M' structure)

What breaks if removed:
  Without the operator structure, there is no spatial dynamics. The
  density field has no mechanism for redistribution, no diffusion,
  no gradient-driven flow, no harmonic generation. The Saddle,
  Nonlinear Triad, and Dissipator motifs cease to exist. The Modal
  Hierarchy law has no basis. The entire spatial causal channel
  vanishes. The architecture collapses to a set of uncoupled ODEs
  at each spatial point.

(b) CANONICAL PRINCIPLE 2 -- CHANNEL COMPLEMENTARITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       Channel Complementarity
Statement:  The density evolves through two complementary channels:
            drho/dt = D*F[rho] + H*v, with D + H = 1, D in [0,1].
            The direct channel D*F[rho] applies the operator
            immediately; the mediated channel H*v applies it through
            the participation memory.

Why it is irreducible:
  The existence of two channels with a conservation constraint is not
  derivable from any other principle. The operator structure (P1)
  defines F; it does not prescribe how F enters the density equation.
  The memory loop (P5) defines the v-equation; it does not prescribe
  the D+H=1 split. Channel complementarity is the fundamental
  coupling choice: the decision that density evolution is a convex
  combination of direct and mediated influence.

What architectural layers it generates:
  - Motifs: Boundary (from D+2*zeta=1 threshold, which requires
    both channels), Memory Loop (from H*v coupling), Dissipator
    (from D*F direct channel)
  - Laws: Channel Complementarity (D+H=1 as a structural axiom),
    Critical Damping Boundary (D+2*zeta threshold emerges from
    the channel split)
  - Geometry: Boundary Surface (D+2*zeta=1 in parameter space),
    the parameter-space simplex
  - Field Theory: the dual-channel coupling structure
  - Energetics: channel-specific dissipation rates (D-channel at
    D*k^2, zeta-channel at zeta/tau)
  - Causality: the competition between spatial (D) and temporal (H)
    influence
  - Information: regime switching between oscillatory and monotonic
    encoding
  - Symmetry: multiplicative channel symmetry (rescaling M preserves
    the D/H ratio structure)
  - Invariants: D+H=1 sum, linear boundary coefficient

What breaks if removed:
  Without channel complementarity, there is no principled way to
  combine direct and mediated influence. The three cosmological
  regimes (D=0 oscillatory, D=0.5 hybrid, D=1 parabolic) lose their
  structural basis. The Boundary motif has no threshold to define.
  The parameter space collapses from a two-parameter family to an
  unstructured set. The architecture loses its single most
  organizing constraint.

(c) CANONICAL PRINCIPLE 3 -- PENALTY EQUILIBRIUM
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       Penalty Equilibrium
Statement:  The penalty function P(rho) has exactly one zero at
            rho = rho*, with P monotonically increasing in rho.
            For rho > rho*, P is positive (restoring downward); for
            rho < rho*, P is negative (restoring upward). The
            equilibrium is unique and globally attracting.

Why it is irreducible:
  The uniqueness and monotonicity of the penalty zero is not derivable
  from any other principle. The operator structure (P1) includes a
  penalty term but does not constrain how many zeros it has. The
  capacity bound (P4) constrains M, not P. The penalty equilibrium
  is a separate structural choice: the decision that there is one
  and only one density value toward which all dynamics are directed.

What architectural layers it generates:
  - Motifs: Manifold (unique attractor creates a single convergence
    target, so all trajectories funnel onto one curve)
  - Laws: Unique Attraction (exactly one fixed point), One-Dimensional
    Relaxation (convergence to a point implies late-time 1D dynamics),
    Proportional Stability (perturbation response proportional to
    displacement from rho*)
  - Geometry: Attractor Point at (rho*, 0), Stable Manifold Curve
    (the 1D attracting curve leading to the point)
  - Field Theory: the restoring-force component of the operator field
  - Energetics: V_P (penalty potential, the dominant energy
    functional), the P-channel dissipation
  - Causality: the "direction of becoming" (all causal influence
    flows toward rho*)
  - Information: erasure (all initial condition information is
    eventually destroyed as rho approaches rho*)
  - Symmetry: broken density translation symmetry (the equilibrium
    at rho* breaks rho -> rho + c invariance)
  - Invariants: unique attractor location, monotone energy decrease
    (dE/dt <= 0)

What breaks if removed:
  Without the penalty equilibrium, there is no target. The system
  could have multiple equilibria (bistability), limit cycles
  (sustained oscillation), or no equilibrium at all (unbounded
  growth). The Law of Unique Attraction fails. The Manifold motif
  loses its organizing center. The direction of becoming is undefined.
  The information erasure pipeline has no terminal state. The
  architecture loses its teleological structure -- its sense of
  where dynamics go.

(d) CANONICAL PRINCIPLE 4 -- MOBILITY CAPACITY BOUND
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       Mobility Capacity Bound
Statement:  The mobility function M(rho) satisfies M(rho_max) = 0
            with M(rho) > 0 for all rho in [0, rho_max). The density
            field is bounded above by rho_max, and the system's
            ability to transport density through diffusion collapses
            as density approaches this ceiling.

Why it is irreducible:
  The capacity bound on M is not derivable from any other principle.
  The operator structure (P1) places M in the operator but does not
  require it to vanish anywhere. The penalty equilibrium (P3)
  provides a target below rho_max but does not impose an upper
  bound on rho itself. The capacity bound is a separate structural
  choice: the decision that density has a maximum, that the system
  has finite capacity, and that this limit is enforced kinetically
  through mobility collapse rather than through a hard boundary
  condition.

What architectural layers it generates:
  - Motifs: Horizon (mobility collapse near rho_max creates a
    transient kinetic barrier)
  - Laws: Finite Capacity (rho cannot exceed rho_max)
  - Geometry: Horizon Surface (the region in state space where
    M(rho) < threshold), bounded state space
  - Field Theory: the mobility field's vanishing at the boundary
  - Energetics: D-channel blockade (when M approaches 0, the
    D-channel dissipation rate collapses)
  - Causality: causal barrier (information cannot propagate
    diffusively through a horizon)
  - Information: truncation (the horizon truncates the information
    pipeline by blocking spatial transport)
  - Symmetry: broken amplitude scaling at capacity (rho -> c*rho
    is not a symmetry when c*rho exceeds rho_max), saturation
    symmetry (the architecture's behavior near rho_max is invariant
    to the details of how M vanishes, provided it vanishes)
  - Invariants: bounded capacity (rho <= rho_max), horizon threshold
    location

What breaks if removed:
  Without the capacity bound, density is unbounded. The Horizon motif
  does not exist. The Finite Capacity law fails. The state space
  extends to infinity. Initial conditions with arbitrarily large
  amplitude are permitted, and there is no kinetic saturation
  mechanism. The architecture loses its upper boundary and the
  reflective dynamics that the horizon creates.

(e) CANONICAL PRINCIPLE 5 -- PARTICIPATION FEEDBACK LOOP
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       Participation Feedback Loop
Statement:  A participation variable v evolves according to
            dv/dt = (1/tau)*(F[rho] - zeta*v), integrating the
            operator F[rho] with exponential decay at rate zeta/tau.
            The variable v feeds back into the density equation
            through the H*v coupling (Principle 2), creating a
            closed feedback loop F[rho] -> v -> rho -> F[rho].

Why it is irreducible:
  The participation feedback is not derivable from any other
  principle. The operator structure (P1) defines F but says nothing
  about a secondary variable. Channel complementarity (P2) references
  v but does not define its dynamics. The damping discriminant (P7)
  describes a consequence of the loop but does not generate the loop
  itself. The participation feedback is a separate structural choice:
  the decision that the system has memory, that the operator's output
  is accumulated over time, and that this accumulated memory
  influences future density evolution.

What architectural layers it generates:
  - Motifs: Spiral (oscillatory convergence from under-damped
    feedback), Memory Loop (the closed F -> v -> rho -> F cycle)
  - Laws: Critical Damping Boundary (the damping condition
    D+2*zeta=1 requires the loop's damping structure)
  - Geometry: Spiral Sheet (the set of spiral trajectories in phase
    space)
  - Field Theory: the participation field as the "memory" field,
    one of the three coupled fields
  - Energetics: V_v (participation memory energy), zeta-channel
    dissipation (zeta*v^2 damping)
  - Causality: temporal propagation (the v-channel carries causal
    influence forward in time), the memory causal rule
  - Information: storage (v stores operator output), feedback
    (v modifies future F[rho]), delay (the loop introduces temporal
    lag controlled by tau)
  - Symmetry: temporal reparameterization symmetry (rescaling tau
    stretches the loop uniformly), broken time-reversal symmetry
    (the loop is irreversible because of zeta damping)
  - Invariants: clockwise winding direction (spirals always wind
    clockwise in the rho-v plane), loop topology (the F->v->rho->F
    cycle)

What breaks if removed:
  Without the participation feedback loop, there is no memory and no
  oscillation. The system reduces to drho/dt = F[rho], a pure
  nonlinear diffusion with penalty. The Spiral and Memory Loop motifs
  vanish. The Critical Damping Boundary law becomes trivial (the
  system is always monotonic). Temporal causality disappears. The
  information storage and delay mechanisms are lost. The architecture
  reduces from a two-variable coupled system to a single-variable
  diffusion. This is the most dramatic removal: the architecture
  loses its participatory character -- the feature that distinguishes
  ED from conventional nonlinear diffusion.

(f) CANONICAL PRINCIPLE 6 -- DAMPING DISCRIMINANT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       Damping Discriminant
Statement:  The qualitative flow type (spiral vs. monotonic) is
            determined by the effective damping parameter D + 2*zeta.
            When D + 2*zeta < 1, the system is under-damped and
            approaches equilibrium through oscillatory spirals. When
            D + 2*zeta > 1, the system is over-damped and approaches
            equilibrium monotonically. The transition at D + 2*zeta = 1
            is the critical damping boundary.

Why it is irreducible:
  The damping discriminant is not derivable from the other principles.
  Channel complementarity (P2) provides the D+H=1 constraint. The
  participation loop (P5) provides the zeta damping. But the specific
  algebraic combination D + 2*zeta as the discriminant, and the
  threshold value of 1, are emergent from the linearized dynamics of
  the coupled system. No single principle predicts this combination.
  The discriminant is the emergent structural boundary that arises
  when P2 and P5 interact, but it cannot be derived from either
  alone.

  One might argue that P6 is derivable from P2 and P5 taken together.
  However, the discriminant is irreducible because: (a) P2 alone
  gives no threshold -- it describes a channel split, not a damping
  criterion; (b) P5 alone gives no threshold -- it describes a loop,
  not a flow-type transition; (c) the specific value D + 2*zeta = 1
  depends on the linearization of the coupled system around
  equilibrium, which is a structural fact about the PDE's local
  behavior that neither P2 nor P5 individually encodes.

What architectural layers it generates:
  - Motifs: Boundary (the topological transition surface at
    D+2*zeta=1)
  - Laws: Critical Damping Boundary (the law that formalizes the
    discriminant as a universal threshold)
  - Geometry: Boundary Surface (the plane D+2*zeta=1 in parameter
    space), Spiral Sheet vs. Monotonic Cone (the two geometric
    regimes separated by the boundary)
  - Field Theory: the regime-dependent field topology (complex
    eigenvalue pair below boundary, real eigenvalues above)
  - Energetics: energy dissipation rate crossover (oscillatory
    vs. monotonic energy decay profiles)
  - Causality: causal regime switch (below boundary: temporal
    causality dominates; above boundary: spatial causality dominates)
  - Information: encoding regime switch (below boundary: phase and
    amplitude encoding; above boundary: amplitude-only encoding)
  - Symmetry: the linear boundary symmetry (the transition is
    exactly linear in D and zeta)
  - Invariants: linear boundary coefficient (D+2*zeta), the sharp
    topological transition

What breaks if removed:
  Without the damping discriminant, there is no principled distinction
  between oscillatory and monotonic regimes. The Boundary motif does
  not exist. The parameter space is not partitioned into qualitatively
  distinct regions. The Critical Damping Boundary law has no
  discriminant to enforce. The architecture loses its most important
  organizational feature: the clean separation of the (D, zeta)
  plane into two dynamical regimes by a linear boundary.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(g) CANONICAL PRINCIPLE 7 -- NONLINEAR TRIAD COUPLING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       Nonlinear Triad Coupling
Statement:  The term M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2 in the operator F[rho] is
            a bilinear coupling between the density-dependent mobility
            derivative and the squared spatial gradient. This coupling
            generates higher-harmonic content (especially the k=3 mode
            from a k=1 input) through nonlinear spectral transfer.
            The ratio k3/k1 is architecturally invariant under all
            multiplicative perturbations.

Why it is irreducible:
  The triad coupling is not derivable from the operator structure
  (P1) alone. P1 states that F contains three terms; it does not
  specify the algebraic structure of the nonlinear term or predict
  its spectral consequences. The triad coupling is the specific fact
  that M'|nabla(rho)|^2 generates k=3 content from k=1 input, and
  that this generation is invariant to functional-form perturbations.
  This is a non-trivial consequence of the bilinear structure of the
  term, not a generic property of all three-term operators.

  One might argue that P7 is a consequence of P1. However, P1 asserts
  the existence of the M'|nabla(rho)|^2 term; P7 asserts its specific
  algebraic and spectral properties. Many nonlinear operators contain
  gradient-squared terms that do not generate locked harmonic ratios.
  The invariance of k3/k1 under multiplicative perturbation is a
  structural fact about THIS specific coupling that P1 does not
  predict.

What architectural layers it generates:
  - Motifs: Nonlinear Triad (the three-component interaction that
    generates harmonics)
  - Laws: Harmonic Protection (k3/k1 invariant to < 0.01% under
    multiplicative perturbation)
  - Geometry: Triad Locus (the region in spectral space where the
    k1 -> k3 transfer occurs)
  - Field Theory: the nonlinear coupling component of the operator
    field
  - Energetics: V_N (nonlinear interaction energy), the spectral
    energy relay from k=1 to k=3
  - Causality: spectral propagation (the triad transfers causal
    influence across Fourier modes)
  - Information: mixing (the triad transforms spectral information
    by generating new harmonic content from existing modes)
  - Symmetry: broken spectral permutation symmetry (k=1 and k=3 are
    not interchangeable because the coupling is directional),
    spectral phase symmetry (phases are irrelevant to the triad
    amplitude)
  - Invariants: locked harmonic ratio k3/k1, k3_max amplitude

What breaks if removed:
  Without the triad coupling, the operator reduces to
  F[rho] = M*nabla^2(rho) - P(rho), a linear-in-gradient diffusion
  with penalty. The k=3 harmonic is not generated. The spectral
  fingerprint vanishes. The Harmonic Protection law has nothing to
  protect. The architecture loses its spectral richness and its most
  robust quantitative invariant (the locked triad ratio). The
  information mixing mechanism is gone. The nonlinear Triad motif
  ceases to exist.

The seven canonical principles generate the entire ED architecture.

3.1 PRINCIPLES TO MOTIFS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Each motif is generated by one or more canonical principles:

  SADDLE:          P1 (operator structure -- nabla^2 sign-reversal)
  SPIRAL:          P5 (participation loop -- under-damped feedback)
                   + P6 (discriminant -- below D+2*zeta=1)
  HORIZON:         P4 (capacity bound -- M(rho_max)=0)
  MANIFOLD:        P3 (penalty equilibrium -- unique attractor)
                   + P1 (operator structure -- k^2 spectral gap)
  BOUNDARY:        P6 (discriminant -- D+2*zeta=1 threshold)
  MEMORY LOOP:     P5 (participation loop -- F->v->rho->F cycle)
                   + P2 (channels -- H*v coupling)
  DISSIPATOR:      P1 (operator structure -- diffusive character)
                   + P2 (channels -- D*F direct coupling)
  NONLINEAR TRIAD: P7 (triad coupling -- M'|nabla(rho)|^2)

Coverage: all 8 motifs are generated. No motif is left unexplained.

Each law is a consequence of one or more canonical principles:

  CHANNEL COMPLEMENTARITY: P2 (channels -- D+H=1)
  TRANSIENT STRUCTURE:     P1 (dissipative operator) + P3 (unique sink)
                           + P5 (damped loop)

Coverage: all 9 laws are generated. No law is left ungrounded.

Coverage: all 9 stages are generated by canonical principles.

3.8 PRINCIPLES TO SYMMETRIES AND SYMMETRY BREAKINGS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
