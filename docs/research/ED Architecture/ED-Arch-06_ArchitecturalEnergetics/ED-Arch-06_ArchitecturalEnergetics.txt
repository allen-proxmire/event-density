1. PURPOSE OF ARCHITECTURAL ENERGETICS
========================================================================

The Architectural Field Theory (ED-Arch-05) described the ED
architecture as a coupled field evolving under local rules with global
constraints. This sixth chapter asks the next question: what drives
that evolution? What quantities decrease, what channels carry the
decrease, and what variational principle organizes the whole process?

Architectural energetics is the study of energy-like quantities in
the ED framework. The term "energy-like" requires immediate
clarification:

  (i)   NOT PHYSICAL ENERGY.  The ED cosmology PDE is not a
        Hamiltonian system. It does not conserve energy. It has no
        kinetic energy in the mechanical sense, no potential energy
        in the gravitational sense, and no Hamiltonian that generates
        time evolution. The quantities studied here are not physical
        energies.

  (ii)  ARCHITECTURAL ENERGY.  The quantities are functionals of the
        field state (rho, v) that measure deviation from equilibrium,
        that decrease monotonically under the dynamics (at least on
        average), and that constrain the architecture's evolution.
        They are Lyapunov-like functionals: non-negative, zero only
        at equilibrium, and decreasing along trajectories.

  (iii) DIRECTION OF BECOMING.  In the ED ontology, becoming has a
        direction. The architecture does not wander randomly through
        state space. It moves toward equilibrium. Architectural
        energetics identifies the quantities whose decrease defines
        this direction. The architecture's "direction of becoming"
        is the direction of decreasing architectural energy.

Architectural energetics differs from PDE mechanics in the same way
that thermodynamics differs from molecular dynamics. PDE mechanics
computes trajectories. Energetics identifies the quantities that
determine which trajectories are possible and which direction they
flow. PDE mechanics is a calculation. Energetics is a principle.

The previous chapters established:
  - What patterns recur (motifs, ED-Arch-02).
  - What rules govern (laws, ED-Arch-03).
  - What shapes result (geometry, ED-Arch-04).
  - How the field works (field theory, ED-Arch-05).

This chapter establishes: what drives the field. Energetics is the
architecture's engine room. It explains not just what happens, but
why it must happen in one direction and not the other.

2. THE ENERGY-LIKE FUNCTIONALS
========================================================================

The ED architecture possesses four principal energy-like functionals.
Each measures a different aspect of the field's deviation from
equilibrium, and each decreases through a different mechanism.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(a) PENALTY POTENTIAL: V_P[rho]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
  V_P[rho] = integral [ Phi(rho(x)) ] dx

  where Phi(rho) is the antiderivative of the penalty function:
  Phi(rho) = integral_rho*^rho P_SY2(s) ds

  For the canonical penalty P_SY2(rho) = alpha*gamma*(rho - rho*)/
  sqrt((rho - rho*)^2 + rho_0^2):

  Phi(rho) = alpha*gamma * [ sqrt((rho - rho*)^2 + rho_0^2) - rho_0 ]

What it measures:
  V_P measures the total cost of density deviation from equilibrium.
  Every spatial point where rho differs from rho* contributes a
  positive amount to V_P. The contribution is approximately quadratic
  for small deviations (|rho - rho*| << rho_0) and approximately
  linear for large deviations (|rho - rho*| >> rho_0). The penalty
  potential is the architecture's measure of how far the density
  field is from its target.

Why it decreases:
  The penalty term -P_SY2(rho) in the operator field F[rho] acts
  as a gradient descent on V_P. The time derivative of V_P along
  the dynamics contains the term:

    -integral [ P_SY2(rho)^2 / (D_eff) ] dx

  which is strictly negative wherever rho differs from rho*. The
  penalty potential decreases because the penalty force is always
  directed downhill on V_P.

  The diffusion terms also contribute to decreasing V_P, because
  they smooth density toward the spatial mean, which the penalty
  simultaneously pulls toward rho*. The participation channel can
  temporarily increase V_P during oscillatory overshoots, but the
  net effect over each cycle is negative because the damping term
  zeta*v removes participation energy that would otherwise drive
  further overshoot.

Governing laws:
  - Law of Unique Attraction: V_P has a unique minimum at rho = rho*
    (because Phi(rho) >= 0 with equality only at rho*).
  - Law of Transient Structure: V_P -> 0 as t -> infinity.

Quantitative evidence:
  - ED-Phys-29: final_std ~ 10^-14 across all 66 runs, implying
    V_P has decreased to machine precision.
  - ED-Phys-25, 26: relaxation times of 640-688 time units measure
    the time for V_P to decrease by a factor of 1/e in the dominant
    mode.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(b) MOBILITY CURVATURE ENERGY: V_M[rho]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
  V_M[rho] = (1/2) * integral [ M(rho(x)) * |nabla(rho(x))|^2 ] dx

  This is the weighted gradient energy, where the weight is the
  mobility function M(rho).

What it measures:
  V_M measures the total spatial structure in the density field,
  weighted by the local ability to transport density. High V_M means
  the field has steep gradients in regions where mobility is high
  (and therefore where diffusion is active). V_M is zero only when
  the density field is spatially homogeneous (nabla(rho) = 0
  everywhere).

  V_M differs from the simple gradient energy integral(|nabla(rho)|^2 dx)
  by the mobility weighting. This weighting means that gradients near
  rho_max (where M is small) contribute less to V_M than gradients
  at low density (where M is large). V_M is the architecturally
  relevant measure of spatial structure, because it weights gradients
  by their dynamical significance.

Why it decreases:
  The diffusion term M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) in the operator field is
  the variational derivative of V_M (up to boundary terms and
  nonlinear corrections from M'). This means the D-channel of the
  PDE performs approximate gradient descent on V_M. The time
  derivative of V_M contains:

    -D * integral [ M(rho) * (nabla^2(rho))^2 ] dx  +  lower-order terms

  The leading term is strictly negative wherever nabla^2(rho) is
  nonzero. Spatial curvature is always being smoothed.

  The nonlinear term M'*|nabla(rho)|^2 provides a correction that
  can redistribute energy between modes but does not create net
  gradient energy. The participation channel can temporarily increase
  V_M (by driving density oscillations that create new gradients),
  but the net effect is dissipative.

Governing laws:
  - Law of Modal Hierarchy: V_M decreases at rate proportional to k^2
    for the k-th mode, establishing the spectral ordering of
    dissipation.
  - Law of One-Dimensional Relaxation: V_M's decrease is dominated
    by the higher modes, which decay fastest, leaving only the k=1
    contribution at late times.

Quantitative evidence:
  - ED-Phys-29: convergence rates lambda = 0.002 to 0.023 at D=0.5
    measure V_M's late-time decay rate projected onto the dominant
    mode.
  - ED-Phys-33: fastest convergence lambda = 0.139 (D=0, zeta=0.25)
    and slowest lambda = 0.006 (D=1, zeta=1) bound V_M's decay rate
    across the parameter space.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(c) PARTICIPATION MEMORY ENERGY: V_v[v]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
  V_v[v] = (1/2) * tau * integral [ v(x,t)^2 ] dx

  The factor of tau normalizes the energy to the participation
  time scale.

What it measures:
  V_v measures the total stored memory in the participation field.
  Wherever v differs from zero, the field is carrying information
  about past operator forcing. High V_v means the Memory Loop is
  strongly activated: the participation field has accumulated
  significant forcing history that has not yet been dissipated.

  V_v is zero only at equilibrium (v = 0 everywhere). During
  oscillatory transients, V_v oscillates: it peaks when v is at
  maximum amplitude and dips when v passes through zero. But the
  envelope of these oscillations decreases monotonically, because
  the damping term removes more energy from v than the operator
  injects over each complete cycle.

Why it decreases:
  The v-equation is:
    dv/dt = (1/tau) * (F[rho] - zeta*v)

  The time derivative of V_v is:
    dV_v/dt = tau * integral [ v * dv/dt ] dx
            = integral [ v * F[rho] - zeta * v^2 ] dx

  The -zeta*v^2 term is strictly negative wherever v is nonzero.
  This is the direct dissipation channel for participation energy.
  The v*F[rho] term can be positive (F driving v in the same
  direction) or negative (F opposing v). During oscillation, these
  terms alternate in sign, but the zeta*v^2 drain is always active.

  The net dissipation rate per cycle is proportional to zeta: higher
  damping removes participation energy faster. At zeta = 1.0, the
  dissipation is so strong that V_v never accumulates significantly:
  the system is overdamped. At zeta = 0.25, the dissipation is
  weaker, allowing V_v to build up and sustain many oscillation
  cycles before draining.

Governing laws:
  - Law of Critical Damping Boundary: the boundary D + 2*zeta = 1
    is the locus where V_v's oscillatory excursions transition to
    monotonic decay. Below the boundary, V_v oscillates with
    decreasing envelope. Above it, V_v decreases monotonically.
  - Law of Transient Structure: V_v -> 0 as t -> infinity.

Quantitative evidence:
  - ED-Phys-27: peak v increases from 0.148 (canonical) to 0.634
    (nonsmooth additive), indicating V_v amplification of roughly
    (0.634/0.148)^2 = 18x under additive perturbation.
  - ED-Phys-31: at zeta = 0.25, mean cycles = 91.5, indicating
    V_v oscillates many times before draining. At zeta = 1.0,
    mean cycles = 0, indicating V_v drains immediately.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(d) NONLINEAR INTERACTION ENERGY: V_N[rho]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
  V_N[rho] = integral [ |M'(rho(x))| * |nabla(rho(x))|^2 ] dx

  This is the unsigned magnitude of the nonlinear coupling term,
  integrated over space.

What it measures:
  V_N measures the total activity of the Nonlinear Triad: the
  strength of the mode-mode coupling at any instant. High V_N means
  the field has steep gradients in regions where the mobility is
  changing rapidly with density, creating strong harmonic generation.
  V_N is zero only at equilibrium (where nabla(rho) = 0 everywhere).

  V_N differs from V_M in that V_M weights gradients by mobility
  (measuring diffusive potential) while V_N weights gradients by
  the mobility derivative (measuring nonlinear coupling strength).
  The two energies are related but not proportional: V_M emphasizes
  gradients at moderate density, while V_N emphasizes gradients
  where density is changing the mobility most rapidly.

Why it decreases:
  V_N decreases because both of its factors decrease: |nabla(rho)|^2
  decreases through diffusion, and the density field approaches
  rho* where |M'| is moderate. V_N cannot increase without an
  increase in gradient energy, which the diffusion channel
  continuously removes.

  The Nonlinear Triad generates k=3 content from k=1, but this
  spectral transfer does not increase V_N. The k=3 mode decays 9x
  faster than k=1, so the generated harmonic content is immediately
  dissipated. The Triad is a spectral transformer, not an energy
  source.

Governing laws:
  - Law of Harmonic Protection: V_N's spectral output (k=3 from k=1)
    is architecturally determined. The ratio k3/k1 is fixed by the
    coupling structure, not by V_N's magnitude.
  - Law of Modal Hierarchy: V_N's spectral output decays faster
    (k=3 at rate 9*lambda_1) than its input (k=1 at rate lambda_1).

Quantitative evidence:
  - ED-Phys-25, 26: k3_max = 6.36-6.44, invariant across all
    perturbation variants. This invariance reflects V_N's structural
    relationship to the gradient field, not to specific parameter
    values.
  - ED-Phys-29: k3_max scales with amplitude (0.54 at A=10, 3.52
    at A=80), confirming V_N's dependence on gradient energy.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(e) TOTAL ARCHITECTURAL ENERGY: E[rho, v]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
  E[rho, v] = V_P[rho] + V_M[rho] + V_v[v]

  The total architectural energy is the sum of the penalty potential,
  the mobility curvature energy, and the participation memory energy.
  V_N is not included as an independent contribution because it is
  determined by V_M (both depend on |nabla(rho)|^2, differing only
  in the weighting function).

What it measures:
  E measures the total deviation of the architectural field from
  equilibrium, combining all three aspects: density deviation from
  rho* (V_P), spatial structure (V_M), and stored memory (V_v).
  E = 0 if and only if rho = rho* everywhere and v = 0 everywhere.

Why it decreases:
  E decreases because each of its three components decreases on
  average. The three dissipation channels (Section 3) independently
  drain each component. Even during oscillatory transients, where
  V_P and V_M may temporarily increase as the participation channel
  drives overshoots, the total E decreases because the participation
  damping (zeta*v^2) removes more energy than the overshoot injects.

  This can be shown formally: the time derivative of E along the
  dynamics is:

    dE/dt = -(diffusive dissipation) - (penalty dissipation)
            - (participatory dissipation) + (cross terms)

  The cross terms arise from the coupling between channels. In the
  oscillatory regime, the cross terms oscillate but have zero time-
  average. The dissipation terms are always non-positive. Therefore
  dE/dt averaged over a cycle is strictly negative.

  In the monotonic regime (D + 2*zeta > 1), the cross terms vanish
  and dE/dt is strictly negative at every instant.

Governing laws:
  E is the master Lyapunov-like functional. Its monotone decrease
  is the field-theoretic expression of the Law of Transient
  Structure. Its unique zero is the expression of the Law of
  Unique Attraction. Its spectral decomposition expresses the
  Law of Modal Hierarchy.


========================================================================
3. THE THREE DISSIPATION CHANNELS
========================================================================

Architectural energy leaves the system through three independent
channels. Each channel has a distinct physical mechanism, corresponds
to a distinct law, produces a distinct motif, is located on a
distinct geometric object, and acts on a distinct component of the
architectural field.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(a) PARABOLIC DISSIPATION (D-CHANNEL)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Mechanism:
  The D-channel operates through the term D*F[rho] in the density
  equation. It acts by direct nonlinear diffusion: the curvature
  term M*nabla^2(rho) smooths the density profile, and the penalty
  term -P_SY2 pulls density toward rho*. The D-channel converts
  spatial structure (V_M) and density deviation (V_P) into
  equilibrium.

Dissipation rate:
  The D-channel's dissipation rate is proportional to D. At D = 1
  (parabolic regime), it is the sole dissipation mechanism. At D = 0
  (oscillatory regime), it is absent.

  The rate scales with the spatial mode number: the k-th Fourier
  mode is dissipated at rate D*k^2*M(rho*), where M(rho*) is the
  mobility at equilibrium. This k^2 scaling creates the Modal Funnel
  geometry: high-k modes are dissipated rapidly, low-k modes persist.

Corresponding law:     Modal Hierarchy, One-Dimensional Relaxation
Corresponding motif:   Dissipator
Corresponding geometry: Monotonic Cone, Modal Funnel
Corresponding field:   Operator field F[rho] acting directly on rho

Quantitative evidence:
  - ED-Phys-31: at D = 1.0, mean cycles = 0 and lambda = 0.020
    across all tau and zeta combinations. The D-channel alone
    produces monotonic convergence.
  - ED-Phys-30: parabolic regime v-spread = 0.047, indicating
    minimal participation energy when the D-channel dominates.
  - ED-Phys-32: curvature 10.6-72.0 in the monotonic regime,
    confirming near-straight trajectories driven by the D-channel.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(b) PARTICIPATORY DAMPING (ZETA-CHANNEL)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Mechanism:
  The zeta-channel operates through the term -zeta*v/tau in the
  v-equation. It acts by exponential damping of the participation
  field: every nonzero v is pulled toward zero at rate zeta/tau.
  The zeta-channel converts participation memory energy (V_v) into
  equilibrium.

Dissipation rate:
  The zeta-channel's dissipation rate is proportional to zeta/tau.
  It acts uniformly across all spatial modes (zeta multiplies v,
  not nabla(v)), so it does not preferentially dissipate high-k
  participation content.

  The total participation energy dissipated per unit time is:

    dV_v/dt |_damping = -zeta * integral [ v^2 ] dx = -2*zeta/tau * V_v

  This is an exponential drain: V_v decays as exp(-2*zeta*t/tau)
  in the absence of forcing. The forcing (F[rho]) counteracts this
  drain during oscillatory transients, but the drain always wins
  eventually because F[rho] itself decreases as rho approaches
  equilibrium.

Corresponding law:     Critical Damping Boundary
Corresponding motif:   Memory Loop (the zeta-channel damps the loop)
Corresponding geometry: Spiral Sheet (the zeta-channel controls the
                        sheet's winding tightness)
Corresponding field:   Participation field v

Quantitative evidence:
  - ED-Phys-31: at zeta = 0.25, mean cycles = 91.5 (weak damping,
    slow V_v drain). At zeta = 0.50, mean cycles = 1.78 (moderate
    damping). At zeta = 1.0, mean cycles = 0 (strong damping,
    immediate V_v drain).
  - ED-Phys-27: canonical peak v = 0.148 (zeta-channel controls
    V_v). Nonsmooth additive peak v = 0.634 (additive forcing
    overwhelms the zeta-channel temporarily).
  - ED-Phys-32: curvature drops from 505,631 (zeta=0.25) to 10.6
    (zeta=1.0), measuring the zeta-channel's effect on spiral
    geometry.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(c) PENALTY RELAXATION (P-CHANNEL)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Mechanism:
  The P-channel operates through the penalty term -P_SY2(rho) in
  the operator field F[rho]. It acts by pulling the local density
  toward rho* at every spatial point. Unlike the D-channel (which
  smooths spatial gradients) and the zeta-channel (which damps
  participation), the P-channel acts directly on the density value,
  reducing V_P.

Dissipation rate:
  The P-channel's dissipation rate depends on the deviation from
  rho*. For small deviations (|rho - rho*| << rho_0):

    P_SY2(rho) approximately equal to alpha*gamma*(rho - rho*)/rho_0

  giving linear relaxation at rate alpha*gamma/rho_0. For large
  deviations (|rho - rho*| >> rho_0):

    P_SY2(rho) approximately equal to alpha*gamma*sign(rho - rho*)

  giving constant-rate relaxation independent of deviation. The
  penalty saturates at large deviations, preventing the relaxation
  rate from growing without bound.

  The P-channel acts on all spatial modes simultaneously (the
  penalty depends on rho(x), not on nabla(rho)). It does not have
  the k^2 spectral selectivity of the D-channel. However, because
  the penalty's effect on each mode is proportional to that mode's
  amplitude, it reinforces the D-channel's spectral hierarchy rather
  than competing with it.

Corresponding law:     Unique Attraction (the penalty's unique zero
                        at rho* guarantees the unique attractor)
Corresponding motif:   Saddle (the penalty creates the restoring
                        force at density extrema)
Corresponding geometry: Attractor Point (the P-channel defines the
                        attractor's location at rho*)
Corresponding field:   Operator field F[rho], specifically the
                        -P_SY2(rho) component

Quantitative evidence:
  - ED-Phys-25: relaxation times 448-688 time units across penalty
    variants. The P-channel's relaxation rate sets the time scale
    for V_P decrease.
  - ED-Phys-29: final_std ~ 10^-14 across all 66 runs, confirming
    that the P-channel drives V_P to machine-precision zero.
  - ED-Phys-34: all 20 runs converge to rho = rho* = 50.0,
    confirming the P-channel's unique target.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
SUMMARY: THE THREE CHANNELS AS A DISSIPATIVE TRIAD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The three dissipation channels form a complete dissipative system:

  Channel     Acts on        Drains        Rate scales with
  ---------   -----------    ----------    -------------------
  D-channel   rho directly   V_M, V_P     D * k^2 (spectrally selective)
  zeta-ch.    v directly     V_v           zeta/tau (spectrally uniform)
  P-channel   rho directly   V_P           alpha*gamma/rho_0 (deviation-dep.)

No energy-like functional is left without a drain:
  - V_P is drained by the P-channel and the D-channel.
  - V_M is drained by the D-channel.
  - V_v is drained by the zeta-channel.
  - V_N is drained indirectly through V_M (since V_N depends on
    gradients that the D-channel dissipates).

The three channels are independent in the sense that each can operate
alone (D=1, zeta=0 gives pure D-channel; D=0, zeta>0 gives pure
zeta-channel; penalty always operates). But they are coupled through
the field: the D-channel's smoothing of rho changes F[rho], which
changes the zeta-channel's input; the P-channel's regulation of rho
changes the gradients that the D-channel acts on.

The combined dissipation is what makes the architecture unconditionally
convergent. Any single channel might leave some energy-like quantity
undrained (the D-channel alone does not drain V_v; the zeta-channel
alone does not drain V_M; the P-channel alone does not drain spatial
gradients). But together, they drain everything.


========================================================================
4. VARIATIONAL INTERPRETATION
========================================================================

The unified ED cosmology PDE admits a variational interpretation:
it can be understood as a system that minimizes a functional subject
to dissipative constraints. This interpretation connects the PDE's
mechanics to the architectural energetics and reveals the variational
structure underlying the dynamics.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 GRADIENT FLOW IN THE PENALTY POTENTIAL
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Consider the density equation with D = 1 (pure parabolic, no
participation):

  drho/dt = F[rho] = M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) + M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2
                     - P_SY2(rho)

The first two terms constitute the weighted Laplacian of rho with
respect to the mobility function M. In variational language:

  M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) + M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2 = div(M(rho)*nabla(rho))

This is the divergence of the mobility-weighted gradient, which is
the variational derivative of the mobility curvature energy V_M:

  div(M(rho)*nabla(rho)) = -delta(V_M)/delta(rho)

  (up to sign conventions and boundary terms)

The penalty term -P_SY2(rho) is the variational derivative of the
penalty potential V_P:

  -P_SY2(rho) = -delta(V_P)/delta(rho)

Therefore the pure parabolic PDE is:

  drho/dt = -delta(V_M + V_P)/delta(rho) = -delta(E_rho)/delta(rho)

where E_rho = V_M + V_P is the total density-sector energy. This is
a GRADIENT FLOW: the density field evolves by steepest descent on
the energy functional E_rho. The gradient flow structure guarantees
monotone decrease of E_rho:

  dE_rho/dt = integral [ (delta E_rho/delta rho) * (drho/dt) ] dx
            = -integral [ (delta E_rho/delta rho)^2 ] dx
            <= 0

with equality only at equilibrium.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.2 DAMPED WAVE IN THE PARTICIPATION FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

When H > 0 (participation active), the coupled system can be
rewritten by eliminating v from the equations. Differentiating the
density equation and substituting the v-equation:

  tau * d^2(rho)/dt^2 + zeta * drho/dt
    = (D*tau + H) * dF/dt + D*zeta*F + H*F

This is a DAMPED WAVE EQUATION in the density field, with:
  - Inertia: tau (the participation time scale provides effective mass)
  - Damping: zeta (the participation damping provides friction)
  - Restoring force: F[rho] (the operator field provides the spring)

The wave interpretation explains why the system oscillates when
damping is insufficient (zeta too small) and why it relaxes
monotonically when damping is sufficient (zeta large enough).
The Critical Damping Boundary D + 2*zeta = 1 is the condition
under which the effective damping equals the critical damping of
this wave equation.

The damped wave has an energy:

  E_wave = (1/2)*tau*(drho/dt)^2 + E_rho[rho]

The first term is kinetic-like (proportional to the square of the
rate of change) and the second is potential-like (the gradient flow
energy). The damping term zeta*drho/dt dissipates the kinetic-like
energy, while the gradient flow structure dissipates the potential-
like energy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.3 NONLINEAR DIFFUSION IN THE MOBILITY FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The spatial evolution at any instant can be interpreted as a nonlinear
diffusion process with density-dependent diffusivity M(rho). The
diffusion flux is:

  J(x,t) = -M(rho) * nabla(rho)

and the diffusion equation (neglecting the penalty and participation
for the moment) is:

  drho/dt = -div(J) = div(M(rho)*nabla(rho))
          = M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) + M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2

This is a porous medium equation with mobility M(rho). The nonlinear
diffusion interpretation reveals:
  - At low density (rho << rho_max), M is large and diffusion is fast.
  - At high density (rho near rho_max), M is small and diffusion
    stalls.
  - The diffusion is degenerate at rho = rho_max (M = 0), creating
    the Horizon Surface.

The porous medium equation has a well-known variational structure: it
is a gradient flow in the Wasserstein metric (the optimal transport
metric) on the space of probability measures. In this interpretation,
the density field rho(x,t) is a distribution being optimally
transported to minimize the free energy V_P + V_M. The mobility M(rho)
determines the local transport cost.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.4 THE ARCHITECTURAL ACTION-DISSIPATION STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Combining the three variational interpretations, the full PDE can be
expressed in terms of two functionals:

  THE ARCHITECTURAL ACTION:
    A[rho, v] = V_P[rho] + V_M[rho] + V_v[v]
              = E[rho, v]

    This is the total architectural energy. It measures the total
    deviation from equilibrium across all fields.

  THE ARCHITECTURAL DISSIPATION:
    R[drho/dt, dv/dt] = (1/2) * integral [ (1/M(rho)) * (drho/dt)^2
                        + tau/zeta * (dv/dt)^2 ] dx

    This is the dissipation functional, measuring the rate of energy
    expenditure during evolution. The 1/M(rho) weighting penalizes
    rapid density changes in low-mobility regions. The tau/zeta
    weighting penalizes rapid participation changes relative to the
    damping rate.

  The PDE arises from the variational principle:

    Minimize R[drho/dt, dv/dt] subject to dA/dt = -2*R

  This is the Rayleigh dissipation principle: the system evolves
  along the path that dissipates energy at the rate determined by
  the dissipation functional. The factor of 2 is the standard
  relationship between the Rayleigh functional and the energy
  dissipation rate in a linear dissipative system.

  The Rayleigh structure reveals that the PDE is not merely a set
  of evolution equations but a dissipative variational system. The
  trajectory is the one that minimizes a cost functional (the
  dissipation) while satisfying a constraint (the energy balance).
  This variational structure is the deepest reason for the
  architecture's convergence: among all possible evolutions, the
  PDE selects the one that most efficiently dissipates architectural
  energy.


========================================================================
5. ENERGY FLOW GEOMETRY
========================================================================

Architectural energy does not simply decrease uniformly. It flows
through the geometric objects of ED-Arch-04 in specific patterns,
following channels determined by the field structure.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 ENERGY FLOW THROUGH THE SPIRAL SHEET
------------------------------------------------------------------------

On the Spiral Sheet (D + 2*zeta < 1), architectural energy flows
inward along the spiral trajectories. The flow has two components:

  RADIAL INFLOW: energy decreases as the trajectory approaches the
    Attractor Point. The radial component of the energy flow is
    monotonic: each successive spiral revolution has less total
    energy than the previous one. The rate of radial inflow is
    determined by the combined action of all three dissipation
    channels.

  TANGENTIAL CIRCULATION: energy cycles between V_P and V_v as
    the trajectory revolves. During the overshoot phase, V_v
    increases (the Memory Loop stores energy) while V_P decreases
    (density is moving toward rho*). During the undershoot phase,
    V_v decreases (the Memory Loop releases energy) while V_P
    increases (density has overshot past rho*). The tangential
    circulation is the energetic signature of oscillation.

The Spiral Sheet's winding geometry reflects the competition between
radial inflow and tangential circulation. Tight winding (many
revolutions, high curvature) means tangential circulation dominates
radial inflow: energy cycles many times between V_P and V_v before
being fully dissipated. Loose winding (few revolutions) means
radial inflow dominates: energy is dissipated faster than it can
circulate.

Energy inventory per cycle (canonical regime, D=0.5, zeta=0.5):
  Undershoot amplitude decreases from cycle to cycle. In ED-Phys-25,
  canonical undershoot = 2.26 with relaxation time 656 time units.
  In ED-Phys-27 (nonsmooth), undershoot decays geometrically:
  12.24 -> 4.14 -> 1.22 -> 0.27, with a decay ratio of approximately
  3.3 per cycle. Each cycle dissipates roughly 70% of the remaining
  architectural energy through the combined action of the three
  channels.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.2 ENERGY FLOW THROUGH THE MONOTONIC CONE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

On the Monotonic Cone (D + 2*zeta > 1), architectural energy flows
directly inward without circulation. There is no tangential component:
V_P, V_M, and V_v all decrease monotonically at every instant. The
energy flow is purely radial.

The Monotonic Cone's geometry reflects this direct inflow. The cone's
narrow aperture (curvature 10.6-72.0) means all trajectories take
nearly the same path to the Attractor Point. The energy flow is
channeled through a narrow geometric bottleneck: the Manifold Curve
at the cone's center.

Energy flow rate in the Monotonic Cone:
  At D=1.0, the D-channel is the sole dissipation mechanism (H=0,
  so the Memory Loop is inactive and V_v = 0 throughout). The
  energy flow rate is:

    dE/dt = -D * integral [ (F[rho])^2 / M(rho) ] dx

  This is the D-channel's dissipation rate, weighted inversely by
  mobility. Energy is dissipated fastest where F[rho] is large (far
  from equilibrium) and M is large (low-density regions). Near the
  equilibrium, the dissipation rate approaches the exponential decay
  rate lambda_1 = M(rho*) * (pi/L)^2, which equals the convergence
  rates measured in ED-Phys-31 (lambda = 0.020 at D=1.0).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.3 ENERGY FLOW THROUGH THE HORIZON SURFACE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

At the Horizon Surface, the D-channel shuts down (M -> 0). Energy
flow through the D-channel ceases at the horizon. But the P-channel
and the zeta-channel remain active:

  - The penalty P_SY2 continues to act, pulling rho back from
    rho_max toward rho*. This is a direct drain on V_P.
  - The participation damping zeta*v continues to act, draining V_v.

The Horizon Surface is therefore a partial energy flow blockage: one
of three dissipation channels is blocked, but the other two continue
to operate. This is why the horizon is transient: even with the
D-channel blocked, the remaining channels eventually pull the density
back from the horizon, restoring the D-channel's operation.

The energy dynamics at the horizon create a characteristic signature:
V_M temporarily stalls (because gradients cannot be smoothed when
M = 0), V_P continues to decrease (the penalty is unaffected by
mobility), and V_v continues its oscillatory cycling (the Memory
Loop operates independently of M). The stalling of V_M creates
a brief plateau in the total energy E, visible as a temporary
flattening of the convergence curve.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.4 ENERGY FLOW THROUGH THE MODAL FUNNEL
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Modal Funnel is the geometry of spectral energy dissipation. Each
Fourier mode k carries spectral energy S_k = |a_k|^2. The D-channel
dissipates S_k at rate proportional to D*k^2. The spectral energy
flow is therefore:

  High-k modes drain first (rate proportional to k^2).
  Low-k modes drain last (rate proportional to 1).
  The flow is from high-k to equilibrium, not from high-k to low-k.

This is a critical distinction: the Modal Funnel does not transfer
energy from high modes to low modes (as in a turbulent cascade). It
drains each mode independently, at a rate set by k^2. The apparent
"transfer" is an illusion created by the Nonlinear Triad, which
generates k=3 content from k=1. But this generation is not an energy
transfer in the architectural sense: it is a coupling that enslaves
the k=3 amplitude to the k=1 amplitude, not a flow of energy from
one to the other.

The Modal Funnel's energy flow geometry is a set of parallel drains,
one per mode, with the high-k drains being wider (faster). The total
energy drains as the sum of these parallel processes, dominated at
late times by the narrowest drain (k=1).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.5 ENERGY FLOW THROUGH THE TRIAD LOCUS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Triad Locus is not a significant site of energy dissipation. It
is a site of energy transformation: the Nonlinear Triad converts
k=1 gradient energy into k=3 gradient energy through the
M'|nabla(rho)|^2 coupling. The transformed energy (k=3) is then
rapidly dissipated by the D-channel at 9x the k=1 rate.

The energy flow through the Triad Locus is:

  k=1 gradient energy -> (Triad coupling) -> k=3 gradient energy
                                              -> (D-channel) -> 0

The Triad is an energy relay, not an energy sink. It diverts a
fraction of the k=1 energy into a fast-decay channel (k=3). The
effect on the total energy budget is small: the k=3 amplitude
(k3_max = 6.44) is modest compared to the k=1 amplitude, and
the k=3 contribution to V_M is small because it decays so rapidly.

The Triad Locus shrinks as the density field approaches equilibrium
(because |nabla(rho)|^2 decreases). The energy relay shuts down as
the gradients that feed it disappear. This is consistent with the
Law of Transient Structure: the Triad's activity is part of the
transient, not the equilibrium.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.6 PROPERTIES OF ARCHITECTURAL ENERGY FLOW
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The energy flow across all geometric objects shares four properties:

INWARD: energy flows toward the Attractor Point from all directions,
  through all geometric objects, via all dissipation channels. No
  outward flow is possible because no energy source exists.

MONOTONE: the total architectural energy E decreases along every
  trajectory (on average over oscillation cycles). Individual
  components (V_P, V_M, V_v) may fluctuate during oscillation, but
  their sum decreases.

SPECTRALLY ORDERED: high-k energy is dissipated before low-k energy.
  The spectral ordering of dissipation is set by the k^2 hierarchy
  and is independent of initial conditions, parameters, or
  perturbation class.

GEOMETRICALLY CONSTRAINED: the energy flow is channeled through
  specific geometric objects (Spiral Sheet, Monotonic Cone, Modal
  Funnel). The geometry determines the path of dissipation, not
  just the rate. Two trajectories with the same initial energy but
  different initial positions in state space may dissipate energy
  through entirely different geometric channels (one through the
  Spiral Sheet, the other through the Monotonic Cone).

6. CONSERVATION VS. DISSIPATION
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 WHAT IS CONSERVED
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Very little is conserved in the ED architecture. The system is almost
purely dissipative.

SPATIAL MEAN DENSITY (APPROXIMATELY CONSERVED):
  The divergence terms in the operator field (M*nabla^2(rho) and
  div(M*nabla(rho))) are conservative: they redistribute density
  without changing the spatial mean. The penalty breaks this
  conservation by adding or removing density to drive the mean
  toward rho*. In the early transient, when the mean is already
  near rho* (as in most ED-Phys experiments), the penalty's effect
  on the mean is small, and the mean is approximately conserved.
  In the late transient, the mean is exactly rho* and is trivially
  conserved.

  This is not a true conservation law. It is an approximate
  symmetry broken by the penalty.

THE D + H = 1 CONSTRAINT (EXACTLY CONSERVED):
  Channel complementarity is exact at all times. This is not a
  dynamical conservation law (it does not arise from a symmetry
  of the equations). It is a structural constraint imposed by the
  architecture's definition.

NOTHING ELSE:
  There is no conserved energy. There is no conserved momentum
  (the system is not translationally symmetric in a dynamical sense;
  the penalty breaks Galilean invariance). There is no conserved
  vorticity (the system has no curl structure). There is no conserved
  charge. There is no conserved topological invariant.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.2 WHAT IS DISSIPATED
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Almost everything is dissipated:

  - V_P: the penalty potential dissipates to zero.
  - V_M: the mobility curvature energy dissipates to zero.
  - V_v: the participation memory energy dissipates to zero.
  - V_N: the nonlinear interaction energy dissipates to zero.
  - E: the total architectural energy dissipates to zero.
  - Every Fourier mode amplitude dissipates to zero.
  - Every spatial gradient dissipates to zero.
  - Every velocity component dissipates to zero.

The equilibrium state (rho = rho*, v = 0) has zero value for every
energy-like functional, every mode amplitude, every gradient, and
every participation variable. The architecture dissipates everything
that can be dissipated.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.3 ED AS A DISSIPATIVE ARCHITECTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The ED cosmology PDE is a purely dissipative architecture. This is
its most fundamental energetic property. The implications are:

NO CONSERVED MOMENTUM:
  There is no analog of Newton's first law. A density perturbation
  does not persist by inertia. It persists only as long as the
  dissipation channels have not yet erased it. The participation
  field v provides a form of inertia (it creates overshoots and
  oscillations), but this inertia is itself damped by the zeta-
  channel. There is no perpetual motion.

NO CONSERVED ENERGY:
  There is no Hamiltonian. The architecture cannot store energy
  indefinitely. Every form of architectural energy is subject to
  at least one dissipation channel. The system runs downhill.

NO CONSERVED VORTICITY:
  The phase-space flow has no conserved rotational invariant. The
  spiral trajectories look rotational, but the rotation decays.
  The spiral is a dissipating vortex, not a conserved one.

ONLY THE DIRECTION OF BECOMING IS CONSERVED:
  The one invariant of the ED architecture is the direction of
  evolution: always toward equilibrium. This is not a conserved
  quantity in the traditional sense (it is not a number that stays
  constant). It is a conserved principle: the architectural energy
  always decreases, without exception, for all parameters, all
  initial conditions, and all admissible perturbations.

  The direction of becoming is the architectural analog of the
  second law of thermodynamics. In thermodynamics, entropy always
  increases. In ED, architectural energy always decreases. Both
  statements define an arrow: thermodynamics has an arrow of time;
  ED has an arrow of becoming.

  The arrow of becoming is what makes ED a theory of process rather
  than a theory of state. The architecture does not describe
  equilibria (there is only one, and it is trivial). It describes
  the process of approaching equilibrium: the transient richness,
  the motif vocabulary, the geometric structure, the spectral
  hierarchy, the oscillatory cycles. All of this is the arrow of
  becoming in action.

WHAT ARCHITECTURAL ENERGETICS REVEALS

The energetic analysis answers the five fundamental "why" questions
of the ED architecture:

WHY DOES THE ARCHITECTURE ALWAYS CONVERGE?
  Because the total architectural energy E[rho, v] is a Lyapunov-like
  functional with a unique zero at (rho*, 0), and the three
  dissipation channels (D-channel, zeta-channel, P-channel)
  collectively drain every component of E. No energy-like quantity
  escapes dissipation. Convergence is not a special property of
  particular parameter regimes or initial conditions. It is a
  structural consequence of the dissipation triad's completeness.

WHY DO OSCILLATIONS DECAY?
  Because the participation memory energy V_v is drained by the
  zeta-channel at rate proportional to zeta/tau. The Memory Loop
  circulates energy between V_P and V_v during each oscillation
  cycle, but the zeta-channel removes a fraction of V_v on every
  pass. After enough cycles, V_v is depleted and the oscillation
  ceases. The oscillation is a transient energy circulation pattern
  superimposed on a monotone energy drain.

WHY DO HORIZONS DISSOLVE?
  Because the horizon blocks only the D-channel (by setting M to
  zero). The P-channel and zeta-channel remain active at the horizon,
  continuing to drain V_P and V_v. The penalty pulls density away
  from rho_max, restoring M > 0 and re-opening the D-channel. The
  horizon dissolves because two of three dissipation channels remain
  operational even when the third is blocked.

WHY DOES THE MANIFOLD EXIST?
  Because the D-channel's dissipation rate scales as k^2, creating
  a spectral hierarchy in which high-k energy drains faster than
  low-k energy. After a few decay times of the k=2 mode, only the
  k=1 mode retains significant energy. The manifold is the set of
  states with zero energy in all modes except k=1. It exists because
  the spectral dissipation hierarchy leaves only one degree of
  freedom alive at late times.

WHY IS THE FIELD THEORY STABLE?
  Because the variational structure (gradient flow plus Rayleigh
  dissipation) guarantees that the PDE selects the trajectory of
  maximum energy dissipation rate. Any perturbation that pushes
  the trajectory off this optimal path results in even faster
  dissipation back to the optimal path. The variational principle
  provides self-correcting stability: the architecture not only
  converges, it converges along the most efficient dissipation
  pathway.
