1. PURPOSE OF ARCHITECTURAL LAWS
========================================================================

An architectural law, in the ED context, is a structural constraint
that holds across all motifs, all parameter regimes, all initial
conditions, and all admissible perturbation classes. Laws are not
descriptions of what the system does in a particular case. They are
statements about what the system must do in every case, derived from
the irreducible structure of the PDE itself.

An architectural law has four defining properties:

  (i)   UNIVERSALITY.  It holds without exception across the entire
        parameter space (D, zeta, tau, A) and for every tested
        initial condition class (Gaussian, sinusoidal, step, composite,
        noise).

  (ii)  INVARIANCE.  It is not altered by admissible perturbations.
        Multiplicative modifications to mobility, penalty, or
        participation do not violate it.

  (iii) NECESSITY.  It follows from the structural form of the PDE
        and cannot be removed without changing the equation's
        architecture. Laws are not accidental regularities; they are
        consequences of the coupling topology.

  (iv)  GOVERNANCE.  It constrains or determines how motifs behave
        and interact. Laws are the rules that motifs obey.

THE HIERARCHY: LAWS, MOTIFS, BEHAVIORS, FEATURES

  - LAWS govern the system's structural constraints.
  - MOTIFS are the recurring patterns that emerge within those constraints.
  - BEHAVIORS are the specific dynamical outcomes motifs produce.
  - FEATURES are the measured quantities that characterize behaviors.

The relationship is top-down: laws constrain motifs, motifs generate
behaviors, behaviors produce features. But the discovery path is
bottom-up: features are measured, behaviors are classified, motifs
are extracted, and laws are deduced.

Laws differ from motifs in a critical respect. A motif is a pattern
that appears under certain conditions (the Spiral appears when
D + 2*zeta < 1; the Horizon appears when D = 0 and A >= 40). A law
holds under all conditions. Motifs have domains of applicability;
laws do not. The Spiral may or may not be present, but the Law of
Unique Attraction is always in force.

Laws also differ from empirical regularities. An empirical regularity
is an observed pattern that might have exceptions not yet discovered.
An architectural law is deducible from the PDE's structure and therefore
cannot have exceptions within the architecture's domain of validity.
The evidence from ED-Phys confirms the laws; but the laws are grounded
in the equation, not in the data.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(a) LAW OF UNIQUE ATTRACTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement:
  The unified ED cosmology PDE possesses exactly one global attractor:
  the homogeneous equilibrium (rho = rho*, v = 0) for all spatial
  points. No secondary equilibria, limit cycles, metastable states,
  or strange attractors exist anywhere in the physical state space.

Supporting evidence:
  - ED-Phys-29 (AttractorMap): 66 runs with 22 distinct IC types
    across 3 cosmologies. Every trajectory converges to the same
    fixed point. No secondary attractors detected.
  - ED-Phys-30 (StabilityManifold): 48 runs with 16 probe types
    including pure density, pure velocity, mixed, and orthogonal
    perturbations. All converge to (rho*, 0).
  - ED-Phys-33 (GlobalAtlas): 135 runs spanning the full parameter
    space. Global attractor confirmed as unique. Basin of attraction
    is the entire physical state space. Zero secondary attractors.
  - ED-Phys-34 (UnifiedVerification): 20 runs across 5 regimes.
    All 20 converge to the same equilibrium. 53/57 behavioral
    checks pass; the 4 failures are classifier artifacts, not
    convergence failures.

Why the law is structurally necessary:
  The penalty function P_SY2(rho) = alpha*gamma*(rho - rho*)/
  sqrt((rho - rho*)^2 + rho_0^2) has exactly one zero: rho = rho*.
  For rho > rho*, the penalty is positive (pushing density down).
  For rho < rho*, the penalty is negative (pushing density up).
  The penalty is monotonically increasing in rho, so no second
  equilibrium can exist.

  The mobility M(rho) = M0*(1 - rho/rho_max)^2 is non-negative
  for all rho in [0, rho_max] and strictly positive for rho < rho_max.
  Since rho* < rho_max, the equilibrium is in the interior of the
  mobility's positive region, where diffusion is active.

  The velocity equation d(v)/dt = (1/tau)*(F[rho] - zeta*v) has a
  unique equilibrium at v = F[rho]/zeta. At the density equilibrium,
  F[rho*] = 0 (because nabla(rho) = 0 and P_SY2(rho*) = 0), so
  v = 0 is the only velocity equilibrium.

  The combination of a unique penalty zero, strictly positive mobility
  at equilibrium, and a unique velocity equilibrium guarantees that
  (rho*, 0) is the sole fixed point. The monotone structure of the
  penalty excludes limit cycles through a Lyapunov-like argument:
  the penalty always drives the spatial mean of rho toward rho*,
  and no mechanism exists to sustain perpetual oscillation against
  this restoring force.

Consequences for ED dynamics:
  - Every trajectory, regardless of initial condition, has the same
    long-time fate. There is no sensitivity to initial conditions
    in the topological sense: no chaos, no multistability, no
    hysteresis.
  - The architecture's behavior is entirely transient. All structure,
    all oscillation, all harmonic content is part of the approach to
    equilibrium, not the equilibrium itself.
  - The physical interpretation: in the ED ontology, all density
    configurations are temporary. The architecture drives every
    state toward a single condition of uniform density. The question
    is not whether equilibrium is reached, but how.

(b) LAW OF ONE-DIMENSIONAL RELAXATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement:
  The approach to the global attractor is dominated by a single
  slowest-decaying mode. After an initial transient, all trajectories
  collapse onto a one-dimensional manifold in the infinite-dimensional
  state space and converge to equilibrium along this manifold.

Supporting evidence:
  - ED-Phys-29: manifold dimension = 1 in 64/66 trajectories (97%).
    The two exceptions (dim = 2) occurred only at D = 0 with extreme
    amplitude (A = 40), and represent transient horizon interactions,
    not true 2D attractors.
  - ED-Phys-30: manifold dimension = 1 in 48/48 trajectories (100%)
    across all cosmologies and all probe types, including orthogonal
    perturbations designed to excite non-fundamental modes.
  - ED-Phys-33: convergence exponents consistent with 1D manifold
    across all 135 parameter combinations. No evidence of 2D or
    higher-dimensional slow manifolds.
  - ED-Phys-30: manifold v-spread measurements quantify the
    manifold's thickness: 0.178 in oscillatory, 0.168 in hybrid,
    0.047 in parabolic. Even at maximum width, the manifold is
    effectively one-dimensional.

Why the law is structurally necessary:
  The nonlinear diffusion operator F[rho] acts on spatial Fourier
  modes at a rate proportional to k^2, where k is the mode number.
  The k = 1 fundamental mode has the slowest decay rate. The k = 2
  mode decays 4 times faster. The k = 3 mode decays 9 times faster.
  After sufficient time, only the k = 1 mode survives at significant
  amplitude, and the state space trajectory is effectively one-
  dimensional.

  The participation channel (v) does not change this dimensional
  reduction. The v-equation couples to the spatial integral of
  F[rho], which projects onto the dominant mode. The coupling
  enriches the dynamics along the manifold (creating spirals) but
  does not expand the manifold's dimension, because v tracks the
  dominant spatial mode rather than exciting independent modes.

  The Nonlinear Triad generates higher harmonics (especially k = 3),
  but these are enslaved to the fundamental: their amplitude is set
  by the fundamental's amplitude through the M'|nabla(rho)|^2
  coupling. They do not represent independent degrees of freedom.

Consequences for ED dynamics:
  - The effective dynamics of the PDE are two-dimensional: (rho_peak,
    v_peak) or equivalently (k=1 amplitude, v). All higher spatial
    modes are slaved to these two variables.
  - Phase portraits in the (rho_peak, v_peak) plane are complete
    descriptions of the long-time dynamics. This is why the
    ED-Phys-32 phase portrait analysis works.
  - The convergence rate of the system is set by the k = 1 decay
    rate, not by any higher mode. Measured lambda values (0.006 to
    0.139) reflect the fundamental's relaxation, not the full
    operator spectrum.
  - Prediction: any initial condition, no matter how complex, will
    produce the same late-time trajectory as a simple Gaussian of
    the same amplitude. ED-Phys-29 confirms this with 22 IC types
    all converging to the same manifold.

(c) LAW OF PROPORTIONAL STABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement:
  The architecture's motif structure is invariant under multiplicative
  perturbations of its components. Any perturbation that can be
  written as a multiplicative modification of mobility, penalty, or
  participation preserves the qualitative dynamics, the motif
  vocabulary, and the topological structure of the phase portrait.
  Additive perturbations violate this invariance.

Supporting evidence:
  - ED-Phys-25 (PenaltyRobustness): 4 multiplicative penalty variants
    all preserve n_cycles = 1 at D = 0.5, with undershoot variation
    < 12% and k3_max variation < 0.01%. The nonsmooth variant
    (which contains an additive-like discontinuity) alters the
    cycle count to 0.
  - ED-Phys-26 (MobilityRobustness): 5 multiplicative mobility
    variants all preserve n_cycles = 1 at D = 0.5, with undershoot
    variation < 3%, relaxation time variation < 4%, and k3_max
    variation < 0.01%. Boundary structure (cycle counts at D = 0.44,
    0.46, 0.48) is identical across all variants.
  - ED-Phys-27 (ParticipationRobustness): 3 multiplicative v-equation
    variants preserve n_cycles = 1. The nonsmooth additive variant
    explodes to n_cycles = 9 with 5.8x amplitude amplification and
    4.3x peak velocity increase.
  - ED-Phys-28 (StressTest): simultaneous multiplicative perturbation
    of all three channels preserves n_cycles = 1 and all structural
    features. Simultaneous additive perturbation (NonSmooth_All)
    produces n_cycles = 8, 6.4x amplitude increase, 4.8x diffusion
    rate increase.

Why the law is structurally necessary:
  The PDE's motif structure is encoded in its coupling topology: which
  terms couple to which, through what functional dependencies. A
  multiplicative perturbation M(rho) -> M(rho)*g(rho) preserves the
  coupling topology because:

  (1) If M(rho) > 0 for rho < rho_max, then M(rho)*g(rho) > 0 for
      any g(rho) > 0. The mobility's positivity is preserved.
  (2) If M(rho_max) = 0, then M(rho_max)*g(rho_max) = 0. The
      horizon location is preserved.
  (3) The derivative M'(rho)*g(rho) + M(rho)*g'(rho) retains the
      same sign structure as M'(rho) near the equilibrium, so the
      Nonlinear Triad's harmonic generation mechanism is preserved.
  (4) The penalty's zero at rho* is preserved under P -> P*h(rho)
      because h(rho*)*0 = 0.

  An additive perturbation v -> v + c*f(rho) breaks this structure
  because it injects energy that is not modulated by the system's
  state. The injected energy does not respect the coupling topology:
  it pumps the memory loop from outside the architecture's control.

Consequences for ED dynamics:
  - The architecture is robust to a wide class of functional-form
    changes, provided those changes are multiplicative. This means
    the specific choice of M(rho) = M0*(1-rho/rho_max)^2 is not
    special: any mobility with the same zero structure and positivity
    would produce the same motifs.
  - The architecture is fragile to additive forcing. Any mechanism
    that injects energy independently of the system's state will
    amplify oscillations and distort the phase portrait.
  - Design principle: to modify the ED architecture's quantitative
    behavior without breaking its qualitative structure, use
    multiplicative modifications. To deliberately break the
    architecture (e.g., for testing), use additive forcing.
  - The law establishes a natural equivalence class: all PDE
    instances connected by multiplicative transformations are
    architecturally equivalent. They share the same motifs, the
    same topology, and the same law structure. They differ only
    in quantitative parameters.

(d) LAW OF CRITICAL DAMPING BOUNDARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement:
  The topological type of the flow (spiral or monotonic) is determined
  by the combined damping parameter D + 2*zeta. When D + 2*zeta < 1,
  the flow is spiral (oscillatory approach to equilibrium). When
  D + 2*zeta > 1, the flow is monotonic (non-oscillatory approach).
  The transition at D + 2*zeta = 1 is sharp, unidirectional, and
  continuous (no hysteresis, no bifurcation).

Supporting evidence:
  - ED-Phys-31 (ParameterGeometry): mean cycles at zeta = 0.25
    (D + 2*zeta = D + 0.5): 49 at D = 0, 3.8 at D = 0.25, 2.4 at
    D = 0.5, 1.3 at D = 0.75, 0 at D = 1.0. The transition from
    spiral to monotonic is monotone in D.
  - ED-Phys-32 (GlobalPhasePortrait): full topology grid maps the
    boundary precisely:
      D=0.0: spiral at zeta=0.25,0.5; monotonic at zeta=1.0
      D=0.25: spiral at zeta=0.25,0.5; monotonic at zeta=1.0
      D=0.50: spiral at zeta=0.25; monotonic at zeta=0.5,1.0
      D=0.75: spiral at zeta=0.25; monotonic at zeta=0.5,1.0
      D=1.00: monotonic at all zeta values
    Every transition point satisfies D + 2*zeta ~ 1.
  - ED-Phys-32: six documented topological transitions, all
    unidirectional (spiral -> monotonic). No reverse transitions
    observed in any of the 375 trajectory runs.
  - ED-Phys-33: spiral fraction drops smoothly: 56% at D=0,
    56% at D=0.25, 44% at D=0.5, 22% at D=0.75, 0% at D=1.0.
  - ED-Phys-34: boundary regime (D=0.5, zeta=0.25, giving
    D + 2*zeta = 1.0 exactly) shows 6-7 cycles, confirming spiral
    character at the boundary's lower edge.

Why the law is structurally necessary:
  The coupled density-velocity system can be linearized around the
  equilibrium to yield an effective second-order oscillator. The
  density equation's D*F[rho] term provides direct dissipation of
  spatial structure. The velocity equation's zeta*v term provides
  damping of the participation variable. Both mechanisms suppress
  oscillation.

  The D channel dissipates the density component directly, removing
  one unit of oscillatory potential per unit of D. The zeta channel
  dissipates the velocity component, but because v feeds back into
  rho through H*v, each unit of zeta suppresses two units of
  oscillatory potential (one in v, one in the rho feedback). Hence
  the effective damping is D + 2*zeta.

  When this effective damping reaches 1 (the natural frequency of
  the undamped system, set by the D + H = 1 constraint), the system
  crosses the critical damping threshold. This is the classical
  criterion for a second-order system, adapted to the PDE's two-
  channel architecture.

  The sharpness of the transition follows from the fact that the
  eigenvalues of the linearized system cross the real axis at a
  definite parameter value. The unidirectionality follows from the
  structure of the eigenvalue crossing: complex eigenvalues merge
  onto the real axis, which is a codimension-1 event with no
  hysteresis.

Consequences for ED dynamics:
  - The (D, zeta) parameter plane is divided into exactly two
    topological regions by a single curve. The motif vocabulary is
    different in each region: spirals exist below the boundary but
    not above it.
  - The boundary is a predictive tool: given any (D, zeta) pair,
    the flow type is known without simulation.
  - Near the boundary, classifier ambiguity is expected. The 4
    failures in ED-Phys-34 (all at boundary-adjacent regimes) are
    not errors but consequences of the transition's continuity.
  - The factor of 2 multiplying zeta is an architectural invariant,
    not a fitted parameter. It arises from the bidirectional coupling
    between v and rho and would change only if the coupling structure
    were modified.

(e) LAW OF HARMONIC PROTECTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement:
  The k = 3 harmonic amplitude generated by the Nonlinear Triad is
  invariant under admissible perturbations of any PDE component.
  The k3_max value is determined by the coupling structure of the
  operator F[rho], not by the specific functional forms of M, P,
  or the v-equation.

Supporting evidence:
  - ED-Phys-25: k3_max = 6.36 across all 5 penalty variants.
    Variation < 0.01%.
  - ED-Phys-26: k3_max = 6.44 across all 5 mobility variants.
    Variation < 0.01%.
  - ED-Phys-28: k3_max preserved under simultaneous perturbation
    of all three channels (both multiplicative and additive).
  - ED-Phys-29: k3_max scales with amplitude (0.54 at A=10, 2.17
    at A=40, 3.52 at A=80), but at fixed amplitude the value is
    invariant across IC types.

Why the law is structurally necessary:
  The k = 3 harmonic is generated by the term M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2
  in the operator F[rho]. In Fourier space, |nabla(rho)|^2 for a
  dominant k = 1 mode produces k = 0 and k = 2 content. The density-
  dependent coefficient M'(rho) mixes these with the k = 1 mode,
  producing k = 3.

  The amplitude of this harmonic depends on:
  (1) The magnitude of |nabla(rho)|^2 (set by the k=1 amplitude).
  (2) The ratio M'(rho)/M(rho) evaluated near the density peak.

  For any mobility of the form M(rho) = M0*h(rho/rho_max), the ratio
  M'/M depends on h'/h, which is a property of the functional shape
  normalized to the density scale. Multiplicative perturbations
  M -> M*g preserve this ratio at leading order because (M*g)'/(M*g)
  = M'/M + g'/g, and g'/g is a correction that does not change the
  harmonic selection rule. The k = 3 mode is selected by the
  algebraic structure of the nonlinearity, not by the specific
  functional form.

Consequences for ED dynamics:
  - The k = 3 harmonic is an architectural fingerprint: it is
    generated by the PDE's nonlinear coupling and cannot be removed
    by parameter changes or admissible perturbations.
  - The invariance of k3_max provides a diagnostic tool: if a
    modification to the PDE changes k3_max significantly, that
    modification has altered the architecture, not just the parameters.
  - The scaling of k3_max with initial amplitude (not with parameters)
    confirms that the Nonlinear Triad is a geometric coupling, not
    a parametric one. It depends on the state, not on the coefficients.

(f) LAW OF FINITE CAPACITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement:
  The mobility function M(rho) imposes an absolute upper bound on
  density. As rho approaches rho_max, diffusive transport ceases
  and the system cannot push density higher through its own dynamics.
  This capacity limit is transient (the penalty always returns the
  system to rho* < rho_max) but absolute (no dynamical pathway
  exists to exceed it).

Supporting evidence:
  - ED-Phys-29: horizon activated in 8/22 oscillatory runs when
    initial amplitude A >= 40, producing rho near 0.9*rho_max = 90.
    No run exceeded rho_max = 100.
  - ED-Phys-33: horizon fraction 33% at D = 0, always associated
    with high-amplitude initial conditions. Horizon fraction 0% at
    D >= 0.25, where the diffusion channel dissipates amplitude
    before it can reach the capacity limit.
  - ED-Phys-34: horizon_max = 1 at D = 0 for gauss_A40 initial
    condition. The system touched the capacity boundary briefly and
    retreated.
  - Across all 1,000+ runs in the ED-Phys series, no trajectory
    has ever produced rho > rho_max.

Why the law is structurally necessary:
  M(rho) = M0*(1 - rho/rho_max)^2 vanishes at rho = rho_max.
  When M = 0, the diffusion term M*nabla^2(rho) = 0 and the
  nonlinear term M'*|nabla(rho)|^2 also approaches zero (since
  M' remains finite but the dynamics that would push rho higher
  require M > 0). The penalty term P_SY2 is always active and
  always pulls rho toward rho* = 50, away from rho_max = 100.

  The capacity limit is therefore enforced by two independent
  mechanisms: the vanishing of mobility (which removes the ability
  to concentrate density further) and the restoring penalty (which
  actively reverses any approach to the boundary). Neither mechanism
  alone would suffice; together, they guarantee that rho_max is
  an impenetrable ceiling.

Consequences for ED dynamics:
  - The state space is bounded: rho is confined to [0, rho_max].
    This prevents blowup and ensures global existence of solutions.
  - The Horizon motif is a direct consequence of this law. The
    horizon forms precisely where M(rho) has collapsed to near-zero.
  - The transience of the horizon (it always dissolves) follows from
    the penalty's restoring force, which is active even when
    mobility is zero.
  - The law connects the PDE to a physical principle: density cannot
    be infinite. The architecture enforces this principle kinetically
    (through mobility collapse) rather than through hard constraints.

(g) LAW OF CHANNEL COMPLEMENTARITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement:
  The diffusion channel (strength D) and the participation channel
  (strength H = 1 - D) are complementary: their strengths sum to
  exactly 1. Every unit of diffusive strength gained is a unit of
  participatory strength lost, and vice versa. This constraint
  organizes the entire parameter space into a one-parameter family
  indexed by D.

Supporting evidence:
  - The constraint D + H = 1 is imposed by the PDE's definition.
    It is not an empirical finding but a structural axiom.
  - ED-Phys-31: the dominant parameter controlling cycle count is D,
    not tau or amplitude. Cycles drop monotonically from 49 (D=0)
    to 0 (D=1). This monotone dependence is a direct consequence
    of complementarity: as D increases, H decreases, reducing the
    memory loop's gain.
  - ED-Phys-32: the topology grid shows that the spiral region
    shrinks monotonically as D increases, consistent with the loss
    of participatory strength.
  - ED-Phys-33: spiral fraction = {0.56, 0.56, 0.44, 0.22, 0.00}
    at D = {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0}. The monotone decline reflects
    the complementarity constraint.
  - ED-Phys-31: tau has minimal effect (< 5% variation in convergence
    rate), confirming that D is the dominant architectural control
    through the complementarity constraint.

Why the law is structurally necessary:
  The density equation is d(rho)/dt = D*F[rho] + H*v. The total
  forcing on rho is the weighted sum of two channels. If D + H were
  greater than 1, the system would be over-forced: the combined
  channel strength would exceed the operator's natural scale. If
  D + H were less than 1, the system would be under-forced: there
  would be a "dead zone" of forcing that is neither diffusive nor
  participatory.

  The D + H = 1 constraint ensures that the operator's output is
  fully allocated between the two channels. This is a conservation
  law for architectural influence: the total weight distributed
  between direct action (diffusion) and mediated action
  (participation) is fixed.

Consequences for ED dynamics:
  - The three named cosmologies (oscillatory D=0, hybrid D=0.5,
    parabolic D=1) are not arbitrary points. They are the extremes
    and midpoint of a single architectural continuum.
  - Increasing D simultaneously increases dissipative strength and
    decreases oscillatory potential. These are not independent
    effects; they are two faces of the same constraint.
  - The complementarity law explains why the system cannot be both
    strongly oscillatory and strongly dissipative. The architecture
    forces a tradeoff.
  - The Law of Critical Damping Boundary is a consequence of
    complementarity combined with velocity damping: the effective
    damping D + 2*zeta reflects the direct channel (D) plus the
    damped feedback channel (2*zeta, where the factor of 2 arises
    from the bidirectional v-rho coupling weighted by H = 1-D).

(h) LAW OF MODAL HIERARCHY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement:
  Spatial Fourier modes decay at rates proportional to k^2, where
  k is the mode number. The fundamental mode (k = 1) always dominates
  the late-time dynamics. Higher modes are enslaved to the fundamental
  through the nonlinear coupling and cannot independently influence
  the system's qualitative behavior.

Supporting evidence:
  - ED-Phys-29: all 22 IC types (including high-frequency sines,
    composites with k=4 and k=7, step functions, and random noise)
    converge to the same manifold. The IC's modal content does not
    affect the late-time topology.
  - ED-Phys-30: orthogonal perturbations (designed to excite modes
    orthogonal to the attractor manifold) decay without affecting
    the convergence rate or the manifold dimension.
  - ED-Phys-29: convergence rates are nearly IC-independent within
    each cosmology: std/mean = 0.277/2.135 = 13% at D=0; 4.309/
    17.120 = 25% at D=0.5; 0.322/5.697 = 5.7% at D=1.0. The small
    variation reflects transient modal content, not persistent
    modal effects.
  - ED-Phys-25, 26: k3_max is generated by nonlinear coupling from
    k=1, not independently excited. Its amplitude tracks the
    fundamental's amplitude.

Why the law is structurally necessary:
  The diffusion operator nabla^2 acts on the k-th Fourier mode as
  -k^2, giving a decay rate proportional to k^2. The mobility
  modulation M(rho) introduces nonlinear coupling between modes,
  but this coupling is enslaving: the higher modes' amplitudes are
  determined by the fundamental's amplitude through the M'|nabla(rho)|^2
  term. The penalty acts uniformly across modes (it depends on rho,
  not on nabla(rho)), so it does not preferentially excite higher
  modes.

  The participation channel integrates F[rho] over space, which
  projects onto the dominant spatial mode. It does not independently
  excite higher modes. The tau and zeta parameters act on the
  temporal dynamics, not on the modal hierarchy.

  The k^2 decay hierarchy is therefore structural: it arises from
  the Laplacian's spectrum and is not modified by any other component
  of the architecture.

Consequences for ED dynamics:
  - Initial conditions with complex spatial structure (high-frequency
    components, sharp features, noise) undergo rapid simplification
    as higher modes decay. The system's "spatial memory" is short.
  - The effective dimensionality of the dynamics is set by the
    separation between the k=1 and k=2 decay rates (a factor of 4).
    This large spectral gap ensures fast dimensional collapse.
  - The Law of One-Dimensional Relaxation is a consequence of the
    Modal Hierarchy: the 1D manifold exists because the k=1 mode
    dominates and all others are enslaved.
  - Complex initial conditions do not produce complex late-time
    behavior. The modal hierarchy ensures simplification.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(i) LAW OF TRANSIENT STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement:
  All spatial and temporal structure in the ED cosmology PDE is
  transient. No persistent patterns, no permanent oscillations, no
  sustained gradients exist in the long-time limit. Every feature
  of the dynamics is part of the approach to the homogeneous
  equilibrium.

Supporting evidence:
  - ED-Phys-29: final_std (standard deviation of density at the
    end of simulation) reaches machine precision (~10^-14) across
    all 66 runs, regardless of the complexity or amplitude of the
    initial condition.
  - ED-Phys-25, 26, 27: final_std ~ 10^-14 in all canonical runs.
    The system reaches numerical homogeneity.
  - ED-Phys-30: convergence rates are strictly positive in all 48
    runs, confirming monotone approach to equilibrium in the
    manifold-projected coordinates.
  - ED-Phys-33: no limit cycles, no periodic orbits, no quasi-
    periodic behavior detected in any of 135 atlas runs.
  - ED-Phys-34: all 20 verification runs converge. The 4 classifier
    mismatches involve the damped/monotonic distinction, not
    convergence failure.

Why the law is structurally necessary:
  The Law of Unique Attraction guarantees that all trajectories
  approach the homogeneous equilibrium. The Law of One-Dimensional
  Relaxation guarantees that this approach is along a 1D manifold
  with a definite convergence rate. Together, these imply that the
  distance to equilibrium decreases exponentially (or at least
  monotonically on the manifold), so all structure must eventually
  vanish.

  No mechanism in the PDE can sustain permanent structure:
  - Diffusion erases spatial gradients.
  - The penalty eliminates density deviation from rho*.
  - The damping term zeta*v dissipates velocity.
  - The D + H = 1 constraint ensures the total forcing always
    points toward equilibrium.

  Sustained structure would require an energy source, and the PDE
  has none. It is purely dissipative.

Consequences for ED dynamics:
  - The architecture is a theory of relaxation, not of sustained
    pattern formation. All structure is inherited from initial
    conditions and eroded by the dynamics.
  - The richness of ED behavior (oscillations, harmonics, horizons,
    spirals) is entirely transient richness. The architecture's
    creativity lies in how it approaches equilibrium, not in what it
    sustains at equilibrium.
  - Relaxation times range from 272 time units (hybrid, low amplitude)
    to 1,360 time units (oscillatory, high amplitude), but all are
    finite.
  - In the ED ontology, this law encodes the principle that all
    events are temporary. Density structure is born, evolves through
    the motifs, and dissipates. The architecture governs the
    becoming; the equilibrium is the ceasing.

3. LAW INTERACTIONS AND INTERDEPENDENCIES
========================================================================

The nine laws are not independent. They form a deductive structure
in which some laws are consequences of others, and some combine to
produce emergent constraints.

FOUNDATIONAL LAWS (from which others follow):
  - Law of Unique Attraction: the deepest structural fact, grounded
    in the penalty's unique zero and mobility's positivity.
  - Law of Channel Complementarity: the D + H = 1 constraint, which
    organizes the parameter space.
  - Law of Finite Capacity: the mobility's zero at rho_max, which
    bounds the state space.

DERIVED LAWS:
  - Law of Modal Hierarchy derives from the Laplacian's spectrum
    combined with the nonlinear coupling structure.
  - Law of One-Dimensional Relaxation derives from Modal Hierarchy
    (the k^2 spectral gap ensures fast collapse to the fundamental).
  - Law of Critical Damping Boundary derives from Channel
    Complementarity combined with the v-equation's damping structure.
  - Law of Transient Structure derives from Unique Attraction and
    One-Dimensional Relaxation (unique attractor + positive
    convergence rate = eventual homogeneity).
  - Law of Harmonic Protection derives from the algebraic structure
    of the nonlinear coupling, independent of specific functional
    forms.
  - Law of Proportional Stability derives from the coupling topology's
    invariance under multiplicative transformation.

DEDUCTIVE CHAINS:

  Unique Attraction + Modal Hierarchy -> One-Dimensional Relaxation
    (A unique attractor with a spectral gap in decay rates produces
    dimensional collapse to 1D.)

  Channel Complementarity + Velocity Damping -> Critical Damping Boundary
    (The D + H = 1 tradeoff, combined with zeta's double effect on
    the feedback loop, produces the D + 2*zeta = 1 threshold.)

  Unique Attraction + One-Dimensional Relaxation -> Transient Structure
    (A unique attractor approached along a 1D manifold with positive
    convergence rate means all structure is temporary.)

  Proportional Stability + Harmonic Protection -> Architectural Equivalence
    (Multiplicative perturbations preserve both the motif structure
    and the harmonic fingerprint, defining a natural equivalence class
    of PDE instances that share the same architecture.)

  Finite Capacity + Unique Attraction -> Bounded Relaxation
    (The state space is bounded and contains a unique attractor, so
    all trajectories are confined and convergent. Global existence
    follows.)

6. UNIFIED INTERPRETATION
========================================================================

THE LAWS AS THE CONSTITUTION OF ED DYNAMICS

The nine architectural laws constitute the fundamental rules governing
all density evolution under the unified ED cosmology PDE. They are the
constitution that every motif, every behavior, and every trajectory
must respect.

The laws operate at three levels:

  EXISTENCE LEVEL (what can exist):
    - Unique Attraction: only one equilibrium exists.
    - Finite Capacity: density is bounded.
    - Channel Complementarity: total architectural influence is fixed.

  STRUCTURE LEVEL (how things are organized):
    - Modal Hierarchy: spatial modes are ranked by decay rate.
    - One-Dimensional Relaxation: late dynamics are low-dimensional.
    - Critical Damping Boundary: parameter space has two regions.

  PROTECTION LEVEL (what survives change):
    - Proportional Stability: multiplicative changes are safe.
    - Harmonic Protection: nonlinear fingerprint is invariant.
    - Transient Structure: nothing persists forever.

Together, these laws answer the foundational questions of the ED
cosmology PDE:

  Q: How many outcomes are possible?
  A: One. (Unique Attraction)

  Q: How does the system reach that outcome?
  A: Along a one-dimensional path. (One-Dimensional Relaxation)

  Q: What determines the path's character?
  A: The combined damping D + 2*zeta. (Critical Damping Boundary)

  Q: What limits the system's excursions?
  A: Density cannot exceed rho_max. (Finite Capacity)

  Q: What is conserved?
  A: Total channel influence. (Channel Complementarity)

  Q: What survives perturbation?
  A: Everything structural. (Proportional Stability)

  Q: What is permanent?
  A: Nothing. (Transient Structure)

THE LAWS AND THE ONTOLOGY OF BECOMING

In the ED framework, reality is not a collection of persistent objects
but a process of becoming: density fields evolve, structure forms and
dissolves, and the rules of becoming determine what can happen. The
nine architectural laws are the rules of becoming for the unified
PDE.

The laws reveal that the ED architecture is:

  DETERMINISTIC but RICH — the unique attractor means all trajectories
  share a fate, but the transient approach can be enormously varied
  (from 0 cycles to 235 cycles, from monotonic sliding to tight
  spirals, from smooth convergence to horizon-interrupted oscillation).

  ROBUST but FRAGILE — multiplicative perturbations cannot break the
  architecture, but additive perturbations can amplify dynamics by
  6x. The architecture has a well-defined boundary between
  modifications that preserve structure and those that destroy it.

  SIMPLE but GENERATIVE — three axioms suffice to derive the complete
  law structure, which in turn generates eight motifs, which in turn
  produce the entire behavioral repertoire. The architecture's
  complexity is emergent, not imposed.

  FINITE but CREATIVE — all structure is transient and all trajectories
  converge, yet the path from initial condition to equilibrium passes
  through saddles, spirals, horizons, and harmonic cascades. The
  architecture's creativity lies not in what it maintains but in
  what it creates along the way.

These properties define the ED cosmology PDE as an architectural
theory in the fullest sense: a system whose behavior is governed not
by specific parameter values or initial conditions but by structural
laws that transcend any particular realization.
