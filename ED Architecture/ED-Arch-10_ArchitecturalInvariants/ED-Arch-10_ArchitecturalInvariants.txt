1. PURPOSE OF ARCHITECTURAL INVARIANTS
========================================================================

The previous chapter (ED-Arch-09) identified the symmetries of the
ED architecture: the transformations under which the architecture
looks the same. This tenth chapter identifies the invariants: the
specific quantities, structures, and relationships that are left
unchanged by those transformations. Symmetries describe what you can
do to the system without changing it. Invariants describe what
specifically does not change.

An architectural invariant is:

  (i)   A QUANTITY OR STRUCTURE that remains unchanged under one or
        more architectural transformations. The transformation may be
        a parameter change, an initial condition change, a
        multiplicative perturbation, a temporal reparameterization,
        or a spatial translation. The invariant is whatever survives.

  (ii)  A FIXED POINT of the symmetry group. In the language of
        group theory, the invariants are the elements of the
        architecture's state space that are mapped to themselves by
        every element of the symmetry group. They are the architecture's
        immovable structural elements.

  (iii) A STRUCTURAL CONSTANT. Not a numerical constant (like the
        speed of light or Planck's constant), but a structural one:
        a property that defines the architecture's identity regardless
        of how it is instantiated. The manifold dimension is 1, the
        attractor is unique, the boundary is linear — these are
        structural constants that no admissible transformation can
        alter.

Invariants differ from symmetries and from conserved quantities:

  SYMMETRIES are transformations. They are actions you can perform
  on the architecture. Invariants are properties that survive those
  actions.

  CONSERVED QUANTITIES are numbers that remain constant along
  trajectories (in time). The ED architecture has almost no conserved
  quantities (ED-Arch-06). Invariants are properties that remain
  constant under structural transformations (across the equivalence
  class), not necessarily along trajectories.

  A conserved quantity is invariant under time evolution. An
  architectural invariant is invariant under structural
  transformation. These are different kinds of invariance. The ED
  architecture has few conserved quantities but many structural
  invariants.

The invariant perspective provides the sharpest possible
characterization of the ED architecture. The architecture is, in
the most precise sense, the set of all its invariants. Everything
that can change under admissible transformations is parametric
detail. Everything that cannot change is architecture.

2. INVARIANTS OF THE ARCHITECTURAL FIELD
========================================================================

The three components of the architectural field — density, participation,
and operator — each carry invariant properties that survive the
architecture's symmetry transformations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 DENSITY FIELD INVARIANTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

INVARIANT D1: EQUILIBRIUM LOCATION
  The density field has a unique homogeneous equilibrium at rho = rho*.
  This equilibrium is invariant under:
    - All multiplicative perturbations of M, P, and v-coupling
      (because multiplicative perturbation preserves P's zero at rho*)
    - All initial condition changes (the equilibrium exists regardless
      of what IC is chosen)
    - All parameter changes (D, zeta, tau do not affect rho*)
    - Temporal reparameterization (the equilibrium is static)
    - Spatial translation (the equilibrium is homogeneous)

INVARIANT D2: CAPACITY BOUND
  The density field satisfies rho(x,t) <= rho_max for all x, t.
  This bound is invariant under:
    - All multiplicative perturbations (M -> M*g preserves M's zero
      at rho_max if g(rho_max) is finite)
    - All initial conditions (provided the IC satisfies the bound)
    - All parameter changes (rho_max is structural, not parametric)

INVARIANT D3: GRADIENT SIGN PATTERN AT SADDLES
  At every density extremum, nabla(rho) passes through zero with a
  definite sign change: positive on one side, negative on the other.
  This sign pattern is invariant under:
    - All multiplicative perturbations (they cannot change the sign
      of nabla(rho))
    - All amplitude scalings (they rescale |nabla(rho)| but not its
      sign)
    - Spatial translation (the sign pattern moves with the extremum)

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2 PARTICIPATION FIELD INVARIANTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

INVARIANT V1: EQUILIBRIUM VALUE
  The participation field has a unique equilibrium at v = 0 for all
  spatial points, coincident with the density equilibrium at rho = rho*.
  This is invariant under all admissible transformations.

INVARIANT V2: MEMORY KERNEL STRUCTURE
  The participation field is always a leaky integrator of F[rho]:
    v(x,t) = (1/tau) * integral exp(-zeta(t-s)/tau) * F[rho](x,s) ds
  The exponential kernel structure is invariant under multiplicative
  perturbations of the v-equation (which modify the integrand F[rho]
  but not the kernel exp(-zeta(t-s)/tau)).

INVARIANT V3: DAMPING SIGN
  The damping term -zeta*v always opposes v. This sign relationship
  (negative feedback) is invariant under all admissible transformations.
  It is the structural guarantee that the participation field always
  decays toward zero.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3 OPERATOR FIELD INVARIANTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

INVARIANT F1: THREE-TERM STRUCTURE
  The operator field always has three terms:
    F[rho] = (curvature diffusion) + (nonlinear gradient coupling)
             - (penalty restoring force)
  This three-term structure is invariant under multiplicative
  perturbations (which modify each term's magnitude but not the
  structure) and under all parameter and IC changes.

INVARIANT F2: TRIAD HARMONIC RATIO
  The ratio k3_max / k1_max is determined by the algebraic structure
  of the M'|nabla(rho)|^2 coupling and is invariant under:
    - All multiplicative perturbations of M (< 0.01% variation
      across 5 mobility variants in ED-Phys-26)
    - All multiplicative perturbations of P (< 0.01% variation
      across 5 penalty variants in ED-Phys-25)
    - All combined multiplicative perturbations (ED-Phys-28)
    - All parameter changes (D, zeta, tau)
    - All initial condition types at fixed amplitude

INVARIANT F3: EQUILIBRIUM ZERO
  F[rho*] = 0 for the homogeneous equilibrium. The operator field
  vanishes at equilibrium because nabla(rho) = 0, nabla^2(rho) = 0,
  and P_SY2(rho*) = 0. This triple zero is invariant under all
  admissible transformations.

INVARIANT F4: SIGN STRUCTURE AT DEVIATIONS
  For rho > rho* (uniformly): F[rho] < 0 (net operator pushes
  density down, toward rho*). For rho < rho* (uniformly): F[rho] > 0
  (net operator pushes density up, toward rho*). This sign structure
  is invariant because the penalty P_SY2 is monotonically increasing
  in rho and the diffusion terms vanish for homogeneous states.

3. INVARIANT-MOTIF CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each motif possesses characteristic invariants that define its
identity across all parameter regimes, initial conditions, and
admissible perturbations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SADDLE: INVARIANT SIGN PATTERN
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Invariant: the gradient sign reversal at every density extremum.
At a peak, nabla(rho) changes from positive (left flank) to negative
(right flank). At a trough, the pattern reverses. This sign pattern
is topological: it cannot be removed by any smooth transformation
of the density field that preserves the extremum.

The Saddle's invariant is the simplest topological invariant in the
architecture: the sign of nabla(rho) changes at every extremum. This
is true for Gaussians, sines, composites, steps, noise, and every
other tested IC type. It is true at D=0, D=0.5, and D=1. It is true
under all multiplicative perturbations. It is the architectural
definition of "there is a peak here."

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SPIRAL: INVARIANT WINDING DIRECTION AND EIGENVALUE RATIO
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Invariant 1: the winding direction. In the (rho_peak, v_peak) phase
plane, the spiral always winds CLOCKWISE (for the canonical sign
conventions). This is because the Memory Loop's feedback has a
definite phase relationship: positive F drives v positive, positive
v drives rho positive (via H*v), and the resulting increase in rho
eventually makes F negative (through the penalty). The sequence
F+ -> v+ -> rho+ -> F- defines a clockwise rotation.

The winding direction is invariant under all admissible
transformations. It is set by the sign structure of the coupling
(H > 0, tau > 0, zeta > 0) and cannot be reversed without changing
the architecture's sign conventions.

Invariant 2: the eigenvalue ratio sigma/omega (contraction rate
divided by rotation rate). This ratio determines the spiral's
tightness and is invariant under amplitude scaling (within the
linear regime). It depends on D and zeta through the damping tensor
but is independent of IC type, IC amplitude (small amplitude), and
multiplicative perturbations of M, P, or v-coupling.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE HORIZON: INVARIANT ACTIVATION THRESHOLD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Invariant: the horizon activates at rho approximately 0.9*rho_max.
This threshold is set by the functional form of M(rho) = M0*(1 -
rho/rho_max)^2: at rho = 0.9*rho_max, M has fallen to 1% of its
peak value.

The threshold is invariant under:
  - Multiplicative perturbations of M (M -> M*g preserves the
    quadratic vanishing at rho_max; the effective threshold shifts
    slightly with g but the existence and approximate location of
    the threshold are preserved)
  - Parameter changes (D, zeta, tau do not affect M's functional form)
  - IC changes (the threshold depends on M, not on the IC)

The activation threshold is conditional: the Horizon exists only
when trajectories reach it (D=0, A >= 40 in practice). But the
threshold itself — the density at which diffusion effectively
ceases — is a permanent structural feature of the mobility field.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MANIFOLD: INVARIANT ONE-DIMENSIONAL TOPOLOGY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Invariant: the manifold dimension is 1. This is the architecture's
most robust topological invariant. It holds across:
  - All 66 trajectories in ED-Phys-29 (97% strictly 1D; the 2 cases
    of dim=2 are transient)
  - All 48 trajectories in ED-Phys-30 (100% strictly 1D)
  - All 135 parameter combinations in ED-Phys-31, 33
  - All multiplicative perturbation variants in ED-Phys-25, 26, 28
  - All IC types (Gaussians, sines, composites, steps, noise)

The manifold dimension of 1 is invariant because it depends only on
the spectral gap between the k=1 and k=2 decay rates (a factor of 4),
which is set by the Laplacian's spectrum and cannot be altered by
any admissible transformation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE BOUNDARY: INVARIANT LINEAR DISCRIMINANT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Invariant: the spiral-monotonic boundary is the linear surface
D + 2*zeta = 1 in parameter space. The linearity of this surface
is invariant: it cannot be bent into a curve by any multiplicative
perturbation, IC change, or parameter variation (because the
damping tensor depends linearly on D and zeta).

The coefficient 2 multiplying zeta is an architectural invariant.
It arises from the bidirectional coupling between v and rho: each
unit of zeta suppresses two units of oscillatory potential (one
in v directly, one through the H*v feedback). This factor of 2 is
determined by the coupling topology, not by any parameter value.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MEMORY LOOP: INVARIANT LOOP TOPOLOGY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Invariant: the causal loop F -> v -> rho -> F exists whenever H > 0
(equivalently, D < 1). The loop's existence is a topological
invariant: it is either present (H > 0) or absent (H = 0), with no
intermediate states.

The loop topology is invariant under multiplicative perturbations
(which modify the loop's gain but not its existence) and under IC
changes (which affect what flows through the loop but not whether
the loop exists). The loop is broken only by setting D = 1 (H = 0),
which severs the v -> rho coupling.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE DISSIPATOR: INVARIANT MONOTONICITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Invariant: in the pure dissipative regime (D = 1, or D + 2*zeta > 1
for the reduced dynamics), convergence is monotonic. The density
approaches rho* without oscillation. The participation field decays
without oscillation. The total energy decreases without oscillation.

Monotonicity is invariant under multiplicative perturbations (which
may change the convergence rate but cannot introduce oscillation in
the overdamped regime), under IC changes (which affect the starting
point but not the monotonic character), and under amplitude scaling
(within the linear regime).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE NONLINEAR TRIAD: INVARIANT HARMONIC RATIO
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Invariant: the amplitude ratio k3_max/k1_max at fixed initial
amplitude is constant to within 0.01% across all tested perturbation
variants and parameter changes.

Numerical values:
  - k3_max = 6.36 across 5 penalty variants (ED-Phys-25)
  - k3_max = 6.44 across 5 mobility variants (ED-Phys-26)
  - k3_max preserved under combined stress (ED-Phys-28)

The slight difference between 6.36 and 6.44 reflects different
parameter settings between experiments (D=0.5 vs different D), not
perturbation effects. Within each experiment, the variation is
< 0.01%.

The harmonic ratio is the architecture's spectral fingerprint: the
one number that uniquely identifies the nonlinear coupling structure.

4. INVARIANT-LAW CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each of the nine architectural laws (ED-Arch-03) defines or implies
a specific invariant. The laws are the generators of invariants: they
are the structural constraints from which the invariant quantities
follow.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF UNIQUE ATTRACTION -> INVARIANT ATTRACTOR LOCATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The law states that the PDE has exactly one global attractor. The
invariant it generates is the attractor's location: (rho*, 0) in
the reduced phase space, and rho(x) = rho* for all x, v(x) = 0
for all x in the full state space.

The attractor location is invariant under:
  - All parameter changes (D, zeta, tau do not appear in the
    equilibrium conditions F[rho*] = 0, v = F/zeta = 0)
  - All multiplicative perturbations (rho* is defined by P's zero,
    which is preserved under P -> P*g because g(rho*)*0 = 0)
  - All IC changes (the attractor exists regardless of starting point)

This is the architecture's most fundamental invariant: the destination
that every trajectory shares.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF ONE-DIMENSIONAL RELAXATION -> INVARIANT MANIFOLD DIMENSION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The law states that trajectories collapse onto a 1D manifold. The
invariant it generates is the number 1: the manifold dimension.

The dimension is a topological invariant: it is an integer that
cannot change continuously. It would require a qualitative change in
the PDE's spectrum (elimination of the spectral gap between k=1 and
k=2) to alter the manifold dimension. No admissible transformation
accomplishes this.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF PROPORTIONAL STABILITY -> INVARIANT MULTIPLICATIVE CLASS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The law states that the architecture is invariant under multiplicative
perturbations. The invariant it generates is the multiplicative
equivalence class itself: the set of all PDE instances connected by
multiplicative transformations of M, P, and the v-coupling.

The equivalence class is the largest invariant structure in the
architecture. It contains infinitely many PDE instances, all sharing
the same motifs, laws, geometry, causality, information processing,
and symmetry properties. The class is the architecture's orbit under
its own symmetry group.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF CRITICAL DAMPING BOUNDARY -> INVARIANT DISCRIMINANT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The law states that the spiral-monotonic transition occurs at
D + 2*zeta = 1. The invariant it generates is the linear discriminant
D + 2*zeta.

The discriminant is invariant in the sense that its functional form
(linear in D and zeta, with coefficient 2 on zeta) is structurally
fixed. The critical value (= 1) is set by the D + H = 1 constraint.
Neither the form nor the critical value can be altered by admissible
transformations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF FINITE CAPACITY -> INVARIANT UPPER BOUND
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The law states that rho cannot exceed rho_max. The invariant it
generates is the bound rho_max: the maximum density value, at which
M vanishes.

The bound is invariant under all parameter changes (rho_max is
defined by M's zero, not by D, zeta, or tau), all IC changes
(the bound exists regardless of starting point), and all
multiplicative perturbations of P and v (which do not affect M's
zero location).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF HARMONIC PROTECTION -> INVARIANT TRIAD AMPLITUDE RATIO
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The law states that the k3/k1 ratio is architecturally fixed. The
invariant it generates is this ratio: a specific real number
determined by the algebraic structure of the M'|nabla(rho)|^2
coupling evaluated at the equilibrium density and the canonical
gradient structure.

The ratio is invariant to within 0.01% across all tested variations.
It is the architecture's most precisely measured invariant.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF MODAL HIERARCHY -> INVARIANT ORDERING OF DECAY RATES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The law states that modes decay at rates proportional to k^2. The
invariant it generates is the ordering: lambda_1 < lambda_2 <
lambda_3 < ... < lambda_N.

This ordering is invariant because it depends on the Laplacian's
eigenvalue structure (k^2 scaling), which is a property of the
spatial domain and the differential operator, not of the PDE's
specific coefficients. No multiplicative perturbation, no parameter
change, and no IC change can reverse the ordering of any two modes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF CHANNEL COMPLEMENTARITY -> INVARIANT SUM CONSTRAINT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The law states that D + H = 1. The invariant it generates is this
sum: the total channel weight is always exactly 1.

This is an exact algebraic invariant, not an approximate one. It
holds by definition, at every point, at every time, for every
parameter setting. It is the architecture's conservation law for
channel influence: the total weight distributed between diffusion
and participation is fixed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF TRANSIENT STRUCTURE -> INVARIANT DIRECTION OF BECOMING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The law states that all structure is transient. The invariant it
generates is the direction of becoming: the monotone decrease of
total architectural energy E[rho, v].

The direction is invariant in the strongest sense: it holds along
every trajectory, for every parameter setting, for every IC, and
under every admissible perturbation. The total energy never
increases (on average over oscillation cycles). The direction of
becoming is the architecture's arrow, and the arrow never reverses.

5. GEOMETRIC INVARIANTS
========================================================================

The geometric objects of ED-Arch-04 possess invariant properties
that survive the architecture's symmetry transformations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL SHEET: INVARIANT WINDING ORIENTATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Spiral Sheet always winds in the same direction: clockwise in
the (rho_peak, v_peak) plane (for canonical sign conventions). This
winding orientation is a topological invariant: it is determined by
the sign of the coupling constants (H > 0, tau > 0, zeta > 0) and
cannot be reversed without changing the coupling signs.

The orientation is invariant across:
  - All parameter values below the Critical Damping Boundary
  - All IC types and amplitudes
  - All multiplicative perturbations

No spiral trajectory in any of the 1,000+ runs across ED-Phys-01
through ED-Phys-34 has ever wound counterclockwise.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MONOTONIC CONE: INVARIANT RADIAL ORDERING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Within the Monotonic Cone, trajectories closer to the Attractor
Point always have lower energy than trajectories farther from it.
The radial ordering (distance from attractor correlates monotonically
with energy) is invariant. No trajectory can overtake another along
the radial direction, because the energy decrease is monotone and
the radial ordering is preserved by the flow.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
HORIZON SURFACE: INVARIANT LOCATION IN DENSITY SPACE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Horizon Surface is located at rho approximately rho_max in the
density field. Its location (in terms of rho) is invariant: it is
defined by M(rho) = 0, which occurs at rho = rho_max regardless of
parameters, ICs, or multiplicative perturbations.

The Horizon's location in phase space (in terms of rho_peak) varies
with ICs and cosmology (it is reached only at D=0, A >= 40). But
its location in density space (the density value at which it
activates) is structurally fixed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
BOUNDARY SURFACE: INVARIANT PLANE IN (D, ZETA) SPACE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Boundary Surface is the plane D + 2*zeta = 1. Its location,
orientation, and equation are all invariant. It is a flat, unbent,
rigid object in parameter space. The plane's equation is:
  - Linear in D (coefficient 1)
  - Linear in zeta (coefficient 2)
  - Independent of tau
  - Independent of amplitude
  - Independent of IC type
  - Independent of multiplicative perturbation class

The Boundary Surface is the most geometrically rigid invariant in
the architecture.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MODAL FUNNEL: INVARIANT ELLIPSOIDAL ORDERING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Modal Funnel's cross-sections are nested ellipsoids with
semi-axes proportional to exp(-k^2*lambda*t). The ordering of the
semi-axes (k=1 is always the longest, k=2 is always the second
longest, etc.) is invariant. The nesting (smaller t gives larger
ellipsoids) is invariant. The axis-alignment (each axis corresponds
to a specific Fourier mode) is approximately invariant (the Triad
introduces slight correlations but does not rotate the principal axes
significantly).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRIAD LOCUS: INVARIANT FLANK LOCATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Triad Locus is always located on the flanks of density extrema,
where |nabla(rho)|^2 is maximal and M'(rho) is significant. Its
location relative to the Saddle (on the flanks, not at the peak or
in the flat regions) is invariant. The Triad never acts at the
Saddle point itself (where nabla(rho) = 0) and never acts far from
density features (where |nabla(rho)| is negligible).

6. EQUIVALENCE CLASSES AND INVARIANT STRUCTURE
========================================================================

The invariants define equivalence classes that partition the
architecture's state space and parameter space into groups of
structurally identical configurations. The following is a systematic
catalog of these partitions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 SYMMETRY-EQUIVALENT STATES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: two states are symmetry-equivalent if they are related
by an architectural symmetry transformation (spatial translation,
amplitude scaling, multiplicative perturbation, spectral phase shift,
or temporal reparameterization).

Shared invariants: all architectural invariants (attractor location,
manifold dimension, boundary equation, triad ratio, capacity bound,
decay ordering, channel sum, winding direction, direction of becoming).

This is the broadest equivalence class. It contains every state and
every PDE instance that shares the ED architecture. It is the class
of everything that "is ED."

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.2 MANIFOLD-EQUIVALENT STATES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: two states at time t are manifold-equivalent if they
occupy the same point on the 1D manifold in the (rho_peak, v_peak)
phase plane.

Shared invariants: all late-time dynamical properties (convergence
rate, cycle count, flow type, future trajectory).

Partition structure: the manifold is a 1D curve, so manifold-
equivalent classes are parameterized by a single real number (the
manifold coordinate). At each coordinate value, the equivalence
class contains all states that have collapsed to that point from
different initial conditions. The class is large at late times
(many ICs map to the same manifold point) and small at early times
(before collapse, states retain their high-k distinctions).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.3 SPECTRALLY-EQUIVALENT STATES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: two states are spectrally equivalent if they have the
same k=1 Fourier amplitude and are both on the manifold.

Shared invariants: the complete future trajectory (since the manifold
is 1D and k=1 is a complete coordinate on it).

Partition structure: identical to manifold-equivalent states after
manifold collapse. Before collapse, spectrally-equivalent states
may differ in high-k content but share the same k=1 projection.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.4 ENERGETICALLY-EQUIVALENT STATES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: two states are energetically equivalent if they have
the same total architectural energy E = V_P + V_M + V_v.

Shared invariants: the energy level. NOT the trajectory, the
spatial structure, or the flow type (two states with the same E
may be in different cosmological regimes).

Partition structure: the iso-energy surfaces nest inside each other,
forming a foliation of state space. The innermost surface (E = 0) is
the Attractor Point. Each outer surface is a high-dimensional
manifold. The nesting is invariant: E_inner < E_outer for all
nested pairs.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.5 CAUSALLY-EQUIVALENT STATES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: two states are causally equivalent if they follow the
same causal pathway type (spiral, monotonic, or horizon-spiral)
through the architecture's processing pipeline.

Shared invariants: the causal graph topology (cyclic vs acyclic),
the motif sequence, the dissipation channel priorities.

Partition structure: three classes total:
  Class S (spiral): D + 2*zeta < 1, A < horizon threshold.
    Motif sequence: Saddle, Spiral, Memory Loop, Triad, Manifold,
    Dissipator.
  Class H (horizon-spiral): D + 2*zeta < 1, D = 0, A >= 40.
    Motif sequence: Saddle, Spiral, Horizon, Memory Loop, Triad,
    Manifold, Dissipator.
  Class M (monotonic): D + 2*zeta > 1.
    Motif sequence: Saddle, Triad, Manifold, Dissipator.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.6 INFORMATIONALLY-EQUIVALENT STATES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: two initial conditions are informationally equivalent if
they produce the same compressed representation on the 1D manifold.

Shared invariants: all late-time properties (trajectory, convergence
rate, cycle count, flow type, total energy at manifold entry).

Partition structure: each informational equivalence class is a large
set (approximately 63-dimensional for N_x = 128) of ICs that project
onto the same k=1 amplitude. This is the architecture's operational
definition of "same input": two ICs that are informationally
equivalent produce indistinguishable late-time dynamics.

Evidence: ED-Phys-29 demonstrates that 22 IC types with widely
varying spatial complexity converge to the same manifold. The
architecture cannot distinguish between a Gaussian and a step
function that happen to have the same k=1 projection.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.7 HIERARCHY OF EQUIVALENCE CLASSES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The equivalence classes form a refinement hierarchy:

  COARSEST: Symmetry-equivalent (everything that is ED)
    |
    +-- Causally-equivalent (3 classes: S, H, M)
    |     |
    |     +-- Energetically-equivalent (continuous foliation by E)
    |           |
    |           +-- Informationally / IC-equivalent (k=1 projection)
    |                 |
    |                 +-- Manifold-equivalent (1D manifold point)
    |                       |
    |                       +-- Spectrally-equivalent (same as manifold
    |                             after collapse, one point per class)
    |
    FINEST: Individual state (a specific rho(x), v(x) at a specific t)

Each level of the hierarchy is defined by a set of shared invariants.
Moving from coarsest to finest, the number of shared invariants
increases and the size of each class decreases.

The hierarchy reveals the architecture's information structure: at
the coarsest level, the only shared invariant is the architecture
itself (the set of all structural constants). At the finest level,
every detail of the field state is specified. The intermediate levels
represent different degrees of structural resolution.


========================================================================
7. INVARIANT RELATIONS
========================================================================

Beyond individual invariant quantities, the architecture possesses
invariant relationships: structural equations and inequalities that
hold among the invariants themselves. These relations define the
architecture's internal consistency.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 CHANNEL COMPLEMENTARITY: D + H = 1
------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is an exact algebraic identity. It holds by definition of the
PDE's structure. The sum of the diffusive and participatory channel
weights equals 1 at all times, for all parameters, for all states.

The relation is structurally fixed because the density equation is
defined as drho/dt = D*F + H*v with D + H = 1. Changing this sum
would change the PDE's definition, not just its parameters.

Consequences:
  - The parameter space is effectively one-dimensional in the channel
    balance (parameterized by D alone, since H = 1 - D).
  - Increasing diffusive strength necessarily decreases participatory
    strength and vice versa.
  - The three named cosmologies (D=0, D=0.5, D=1) are the endpoints
    and midpoint of this one-dimensional space.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.2 CRITICAL DAMPING: D + 2*zeta = 1 (AT THE BOUNDARY)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is a structural relation between the channel balance and the
damping coefficient. The factor of 2 on zeta is an architectural
invariant arising from the bidirectional v-rho coupling.

The relation is structurally fixed because the linearized damping
tensor's eigenvalues depend linearly on D and zeta, with the
coefficient 2 arising from the feedback structure (zeta damps v,
which feeds back to rho through H*v, creating a double effect).

Derivation of the factor 2:
  The v-equation contributes damping at rate zeta/tau to the v-field.
  The H*v feedback converts v-oscillations into rho-oscillations.
  The combined effect on the density dynamics is an effective damping
  that is proportional to H*zeta/tau + D*lambda_1 in the density
  channel. Since H = 1 - D, the critical condition becomes
  D + (effective zeta contribution) = 1, where the effective zeta
  contribution is 2*zeta (from the round-trip v -> rho -> F -> v
  amplification factor of the damping).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.3 TRIAD SCALING: k3 PROPORTIONAL TO k1 SQUARED
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The k=3 harmonic amplitude scales as the square of the k=1 amplitude.
This quadratic scaling is an invariant relationship:

  k3_max proportional to (k1_max)^2

7.5 MODAL DECAY ORDERING: lambda_1 < lambda_2 < lambda_3 < ...
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The decay rates are strictly ordered by mode number. The k=1 mode
always decays slowest. Each subsequent mode decays faster. The
ordering is:

  lambda_k proportional to k^2

so lambda_1 : lambda_2 : lambda_3 = 1 : 4 : 9 : 16 : ...

This ordering is invariant because the Laplacian's eigenvalues scale
as k^2 for Fourier modes on a periodic domain. The mobility
modulation M(rho) scales all eigenvalues by the same factor (M(rho*)
at equilibrium), preserving the ordering. The penalty adds a
mode-independent shift, also preserving the ordering.

modes. The k=1 mode is always the slowest, and this fact is the
