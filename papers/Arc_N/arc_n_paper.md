## 5. Theorem N1: Finite-Width Vacuum Memory Kernel FORCED

### 5.1 Statement

> **Theorem N1 (Finite-Width Vacuum Memory Kernel FORCED).** *The vacuum-response kernel $K_\mathrm{vac}(x - x')$ acting on chain participation perturbations is structurally FORCED at primitive level to satisfy:*
>
> *(i) Finite width: $K_\mathrm{vac}(x - x')$ is non-singular at $x = x'$ (rules out $\delta^4$-function form).*
>
> *(ii) Decaying support: $K_\mathrm{vac}(x - x') \to 0$ as $|x - x'| \to \infty$ (rules out constant kernels).*
>
> *(iii) Lorentz-scalar form: $K_\mathrm{vac}$ depends only on Lorentz invariants of $x - x'$.*
>
> *(iv) Bounded short-time behaviour: kernel growth is sub-power-law-2 in the proper-time-invariant separation in 3+1D (rules out $\alpha \geq 2$ power-law forms).*
>
> *Specific functional forms — exponential decay (V2), restricted power-law $\alpha < 2$ (V3), multi-scale composition (V4) — are admissible specific realisations within the FORCED class. Specific values (kernel widths, decay constants, multi-scale weights) are INHERITED.*

### 5.2 Proof sketch

The proof proceeds through three structural-argument legs.

#### 5.2.1 Leg 1 — UV-FIN forces non-zero finite width

Suppose the kernel were a $\delta$-function: $K_\mathrm{vac}(x - x') = c_0 \delta^4(x - x')$. In Fourier space, $\widetilde{K_\mathrm{vac}}(\omega, \mathbf{k}) = c_0$ — constant across all frequencies and wavenumbers. In any continuum-approximation loop integral involving the vacuum response, this constant frequency-weighting amplifies high-frequency contributions, threatening UV-FIN (Arc Q Theorem Q3 [4]).

Primitive 01 event-discreteness imposes a primitive-level event-resolution scale $\ell_\mathrm{ED}$ below which the manifold structure is not continuum-like. Primitive 13 supplies finite proper-time intervals between commitment events. Jointly, vacuum response cannot resolve perturbations at sub-primitive resolution; the kernel must be smoothed at the $\ell_\mathrm{ED}$ scale.

The $\delta$-limit is therefore REFUTED by C3. The finite-width condition (i) follows.

#### 5.2.2 Leg 2 — Lorentz covariance + locality forces decaying support

Suppose the kernel were a constant: $K_\mathrm{vac}(x - x') = c_\infty$ for all $x, x'$. While trivially Lorentz-scalar, this implies infinite correlation length: every event is correlated with every other event with equal weight, including arbitrary spacelike separations.

Such a kernel is structurally pathological. It does not respect causal-structure constraints embedded in Lorentz covariance, and is incompatible with Primitive 11 commitment-event locality (commitment events are point-events, not infinitely-extended objects). Any structurally consistent finite-width kernel must have *some* decay with separation.

The infinite-width limit is therefore REFUTED by C1 (in its locality-respecting interpretation). The decaying-support condition (ii) follows.

#### 5.2.3 Leg 3 — UV-FIN refinement forces sub-power-law-2 decay

Power-law kernels $K_\mathrm{vac} \propto |x - x'|^{-2\alpha}$ in 3+1D produce UV-divergent integrals when $2\alpha \geq 4$, i.e., $\alpha \geq 2$. The integral
$$
\int d^4 z \, |z|^{-2\alpha} \cdot \phi(z),
$$
with $\phi$ a bounded test function, converges at small $|z|$ iff $\alpha < 2$ in 3+1D. Power-law kernels with $\alpha \geq 2$ are REFUTED by C3.

The sub-power-law-2 condition (iv) follows.

#### 5.2.4 Synthesis

The three legs jointly force $K_\mathrm{vac}$ to satisfy conditions (i)–(iv) of Theorem N1. The Lorentz-scalar form (iii) follows from C1 directly: any kernel must be expressible as a function of Lorentz invariants of $x - x'$.

Specific functional forms within the bounded class are admissible specific realisations; specific values INHERITED. $\square$
