1. PURPOSE OF ARCHITECTURAL CAUSALITY
========================================================================

The previous chapters described what the architecture is (motifs, laws,
geometry), how it works (field theory), and why it evolves as it does
(energetics). This seventh chapter asks a different question: how does
influence move through the architecture? When the density changes at
one point, what happens elsewhere, and through what channels does that
effect propagate?

Architectural causality is the study of influence propagation in the
ED field. It is:

  (i)   NOT PHYSICAL CAUSALITY.  The ED cosmology PDE does not
        describe relativistic spacetime. There is no light cone, no
        causal horizon in the relativistic sense, and no requirement
        that influence propagate at finite speed. The PDE is parabolic
        (in the D-channel) and hyperbolic-like (in the v-channel),
        giving it a mixed causal character that differs from both
        Newtonian and relativistic causality.

  (ii)  ARCHITECTURAL CAUSALITY.  The study of how changes in the
        field at one point, one time, or one spectral mode influence
        the field at other points, times, and modes. This is a
        structural question: it asks about the topology and geometry
        of influence propagation, not about signal speed or retarded
        potentials.

  (iii) DIRECTIONAL CONSTRAINT.  Architectural causality is not
        symmetric. The architecture has a preferred direction of
        influence flow: from deviation toward equilibrium, from high
        energy toward low energy, from high modes toward the
        fundamental. The energetic arrow of becoming (ED-Arch-06)
        imposes a directionality on all causal flow.

Architectural causality differs from PDE time evolution in the same
way that a circuit diagram differs from a voltage trace. Time evolution
computes the field's state at each moment. Causality maps the pathways
through which one part of the field influences another. Time evolution
is a trajectory. Causality is a wiring diagram.

The previous chapters answered:
  ED-Arch-01: What happens.
  ED-Arch-02: What recurs.
  ED-Arch-03: What governs.
  ED-Arch-04: What shapes emerge.
  ED-Arch-05: How the field is structured.
  ED-Arch-06: Why the field evolves as it does.

This chapter answers: through what pathways does the field's influence
propagate, and what constrains those pathways?

2. CAUSAL STRUCTURE OF THE ARCHITECTURAL FIELD
========================================================================

The architectural field (rho, v, F[rho]) has three components, and
each plays a distinct causal role. Influence propagates through three
channels — spatial, temporal, and spectral — each carried by a
different aspect of the field.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 THE DENSITY FIELD AS CAUSAL SOURCE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The density field rho(x,t) is the primary causal source. It is the
field from which all other causal influences originate. The operator
field F[rho] is computed from rho. The participation field v is driven
by F[rho]. The density evolution itself depends on F[rho] and v.
Every causal chain in the architecture begins with rho.

The density field's causal influence is LOCAL: it propagates through
spatial derivatives. The operator F[rho] at point x depends on:
  - rho(x): the local density (through M and P_SY2)
  - nabla(rho)(x): the local gradient (through M'|nabla(rho)|^2)
  - nabla^2(rho)(x): the local curvature (through M*nabla^2(rho))

These are differential quantities: they depend on rho in an
infinitesimal neighborhood of x. The density field's causal influence
is therefore nearest-neighbor: each point directly influences only
its immediate spatial neighbors, through the gradient and Laplacian.

Long-range influence arises through ITERATION. A change at point x
affects nabla(rho) at x and its neighbors. At the next time step,
the changed gradient at the neighbors affects their neighbors, and
so on. The influence spreads outward at a speed set by the diffusion
coefficient: approximately sqrt(M(rho) * dt/dx^2) grid points per
time step. For the CFL-limited time stepping used in ED-Phys
(dt = eta * dx^2 / M0, eta = 0.2), this is approximately
sqrt(0.2) ~ 0.45 grid points per step, meaning influence requires
roughly 2 time steps to propagate one grid spacing.

The density field is both causal source and causal target. Changes
in rho generate influence (through F[rho]), and the generated
influence modifies rho (through D*F + H*v). The density field is
the architecture's causal substrate: the medium through which all
influence flows.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2 THE PARTICIPATION FIELD AS CAUSAL MEMORY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The participation field v(x,t) is the architecture's causal memory.
It records the temporal history of operator forcing and feeds it back
into the density evolution. Without v, the architecture would have no
temporal depth: each moment's evolution would depend only on the
current density configuration, with no influence from the past.

The participation field's causal role is TEMPORAL: it transmits
influence across time. The v-equation:

  dv/dt = (1/tau) * (F[rho] - zeta*v)

is a first-order temporal filter. At any moment, v(x,t) is the
exponentially weighted average of past F[rho] values at point x:

  v(x,t) = (1/tau) * integral_0^t exp(-zeta*(t-s)/tau) * F[rho](x,s) ds

This integral is the causal kernel of the participation field. It
shows that v at time t depends on F[rho] at all past times, with
exponentially decaying weight. The decay rate zeta/tau sets the
memory horizon: influences older than approximately tau/zeta time
units have negligible effect on the current v.

The participation field's causal memory has three properties:

  LOCALITY: v(x,t) depends only on the history of F[rho] at the
    same spatial point x. There is no spatial propagation through
    the participation channel alone. (Spatial propagation occurs
    indirectly: v influences rho, rho has spatial gradients, and
    those gradients propagate spatially through the D-channel.)

  EXPONENTIAL DECAY: the influence of past events decays
    exponentially with time constant tau/zeta. Old events are
    forgotten. The memory is leaky.

  SIGN SENSITIVITY: v records whether past forcing was positive or
    negative. Positive v means recent forcing pushed density upward.
    Negative v means recent forcing pushed density downward. The sign
    of v determines the direction of the participatory feedback.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3 THE OPERATOR FIELD AS CAUSAL MEDIATOR
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The operator field F[rho] is the architecture's causal mediator. It
is not an independent state variable; it is computed from rho at each
instant. But it plays a central causal role as the link between the
density field and the participation field, and as the mechanism
through which spatial structure is translated into temporal dynamics.

F[rho] mediates causality in three ways:

  SPATIAL TO TEMPORAL: F[rho] converts spatial information (gradients,
    curvatures) into temporal forcing (driving v, modifying drho/dt).
    A spatial gradient at point x produces a nonzero F[rho](x), which
    drives v(x) and modifies rho(x) at the next time step.

  NONLINEAR TO LINEAR: F[rho] converts the nonlinear density field
    (through M(rho), M'(rho), P_SY2(rho)) into a forcing term that
    enters the linear v-equation. The nonlinearity is contained in
    F; the participation channel's response to F is linear (a leaky
    integrator).

  LOCAL TO GLOBAL: F[rho] is computed locally (depending on rho and
    its derivatives at each point), but its effect is global (through
    the participation feedback loop, a local change in F propagates
    through v back to rho, then through spatial derivatives to
    neighboring points, then through F again).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.4 THE THREE CAUSAL CHANNELS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Influence propagates through the architectural field via three
distinct channels:

SPATIAL PROPAGATION (via mobility):
  Channel: D * F[rho] term in the density equation.
  Mechanism: the Laplacian nabla^2(rho) couples each spatial point
    to its neighbors. Mobility M(rho) modulates the coupling strength.
  Speed: diffusive (distance proportional to sqrt(t)).
  Range: entire spatial domain (through iterative nearest-neighbor
    propagation).
  Character: smoothing. Spatial propagation always reduces gradients.
    It carries influence from regions of high curvature to regions
    of low curvature, evening out the density field.

TEMPORAL PROPAGATION (via participation):
  Channel: H * v term in the density equation, driven by the
    v-equation.
  Mechanism: F[rho] drives v, which feeds back into drho/dt.
  Speed: instantaneous (v acts on rho at the same spatial point
    without delay), but the effect of past events decays
    exponentially with time constant tau/zeta.
  Range: temporal memory horizon approximately tau/zeta time units.
  Character: oscillatory (below the Critical Damping Boundary) or
    monotonic (above it). Temporal propagation can reverse the
    current direction of density change by feeding back past forcing
    with a sign reversal.

SPECTRAL PROPAGATION (via nonlinear triad):
  Channel: M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2 term in the operator field.
  Mechanism: the squared gradient of a k=1 mode generates k=0 and
    k=2 content. Multiplication by the density-dependent M'(rho)
    couples these to k=1, generating k=3.
  Speed: instantaneous (the coupling is algebraic, not differential).
  Range: specific mode pairs determined by the triad's selection
    rules (primarily k=1 to k=3).
  Character: enslaving. The generated mode (k=3) is locked to the
    generating mode (k=1) in a fixed amplitude ratio. Spectral
    propagation does not create independent modes; it creates
    dependent harmonics.

The three channels operate simultaneously at every point and every
time step. Their combined effect is the full architectural evolution.
No single channel alone can reproduce the architecture's behavior;
all three are needed.

3. CAUSAL-MOTIF CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each motif from ED-Arch-02 plays a specific role in the architecture's
causal structure. The motifs are not just patterns; they are causal
agents with defined functions in the propagation of influence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SADDLE: CAUSAL ROUTING POINT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Saddle motif (gradient sign reversal at density extrema) acts as
a causal routing point. At a density peak, the gradient nabla(rho)
points inward from both sides. The operator field F[rho] receives
contributions from nabla^2(rho) (negative at the peak, driving
density outward) and P_SY2 (positive or negative depending on
whether rho > rho* or rho < rho*).

The Saddle routes causal influence by determining the DIRECTION of
spatial flow. On the left flank of a peak, the gradient drives
density leftward. On the right flank, it drives density rightward.
The Saddle is the point where these two opposing flows originate.
It is the spatial origin of all redistribution in the density field.

Causal function: the Saddle determines WHERE density flows, by
establishing the gradient geometry that the D-channel acts upon.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SPIRAL: CAUSAL DELAY LOOP
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Spiral motif (oscillatory approach to equilibrium) acts as a
causal delay loop. During each spiral revolution, the density
overshoots equilibrium, the operator reverses sign, the participation
field integrates the reversed forcing, and the density undershoots.
The cycle repeats with decreasing amplitude.

The Spiral delays convergence by circulating causal influence between
the density and participation fields. Without the spiral (in the
monotonic regime), influence flows directly from deviation to
equilibrium. With the spiral, influence circulates through the Memory
Loop multiple times before being dissipated. Each circulation adds
a delay of approximately one oscillation period.

Causal function: the Spiral determines HOW LONG convergence takes,
by circulating influence through the Memory Loop before dissipation.

Quantitative: at (D=0, zeta=0.25), the spiral imposes 49 mean
cycles of delay (ED-Phys-31). At (D=0.5, zeta=0.5), it imposes
approximately 1 cycle. The delay is controlled by the distance
below the Critical Damping Boundary.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE HORIZON: CAUSAL BARRIER
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Horizon motif (mobility collapse at high density) acts as a
causal barrier. When rho approaches rho_max and M collapses to
near-zero, the spatial propagation channel shuts down. Influence
can no longer propagate through the D-channel at the horizon point.

The Horizon blocks one of three causal channels. Spatial propagation
(via the Laplacian weighted by M) ceases. But temporal propagation
(via v) and regulatory propagation (via P_SY2) continue. The
Horizon is therefore a PARTIAL causal barrier: it blocks diffusive
influence while permitting participatory and regulatory influence.

The barrier is transient: the penalty's continued operation at the
horizon drives density back below the mobility threshold, restoring
the spatial channel. The Horizon's causal effect is to temporarily
redirect influence from the spatial channel to the temporal and
regulatory channels.

Causal function: the Horizon determines WHERE influence CANNOT
propagate spatially, creating a temporary causal shadow behind the
barrier.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MANIFOLD: CAUSAL ATTRACTOR PATH
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Manifold motif (1D attractor curve) acts as the architecture's
causal attractor path. After the Modal Funnel has collapsed the
higher modes, all remaining causal influence is channeled along the
manifold. The manifold is not just a geometric object; it is the
causal highway along which the system's final approach to equilibrium
occurs.

On the manifold, causal influence flows in one direction only:
toward the Attractor Point. There is no backward flow, no branching,
no detours. The manifold is a one-lane road with one destination.

Causal function: the Manifold determines the FINAL PATH of
influence flow, channeling all late-time causality into a single
trajectory.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE BOUNDARY: CAUSAL PHASE TRANSITION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Boundary motif (D + 2*zeta = 1 surface) acts as a causal phase
transition. Crossing the boundary does not change the destination
(the Attractor Point is the same on both sides) but fundamentally
changes the CAUSAL PATHWAY to that destination.

Below the boundary: influence circulates through the Memory Loop
multiple times before reaching equilibrium. The causal pathway is
helical (spiral approach).

Above the boundary: influence flows directly to equilibrium without
circulation. The causal pathway is radial (monotonic approach).

The transition is sharp: at the boundary, the causal pathway
switches from circulating to direct. There is no gradual blending.
The 4 classifier ambiguities in ED-Phys-34 reflect the difficulty
of measuring causal pathway type precisely at the transition point,
not a gradual change in the pathway's character.

Causal function: the Boundary determines WHICH TYPE of causal
pathway the system follows.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MEMORY LOOP: CAUSAL FEEDBACK CIRCUIT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Memory Loop motif (F -> v -> rho -> F cycle) is the architecture's
causal feedback circuit. It is the mechanism through which temporal
causality operates: past events influence present evolution through
the stored participation field.

The Memory Loop amplifies or attenuates causal influence depending
on its phase relative to the current forcing:

  AMPLIFICATION: when v and F[rho] have the same sign, the Memory
    Loop reinforces the current forcing. The density moves further
    in the direction it is already moving. This is the mechanism of
    overshoot.

  ATTENUATION: when v and F[rho] have opposite signs, the Memory
    Loop opposes the current forcing. The density's motion is slowed
    or reversed. This is the mechanism of undershoot.

The alternation between amplification and attenuation creates the
oscillatory character of the spiral. The Memory Loop is the causal
engine of oscillation.

Causal function: the Memory Loop determines HOW MUCH influence past
events exert on present dynamics, creating temporal feedback.

Quantitative: the loop gain is H/tau. At D=0, H=1, the loop operates
at full gain. At D=1, H=0, the loop is disconnected. The gain
directly controls the amplitude of overshoots: peak v = 0.148 at
canonical parameters, rising to 0.634 under additive perturbation
that artificially boosts the loop (ED-Phys-27).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE DISSIPATOR: CAUSAL SINK
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Dissipator motif (direct diffusive convergence) acts as a causal
sink. It absorbs influence and converts it into equilibrium. Every
causal chain in the architecture terminates at the Dissipator: spatial
gradients are smoothed, density deviations are regulated, and the
field approaches homogeneity.

The Dissipator is the terminal motif in every causal pathway. Whether
the system spirals or descends monotonically, the final approach to
equilibrium is dissipative: gradients vanish, F[rho] approaches zero,
v decays to zero, and all causal activity ceases.

Causal function: the Dissipator is WHERE causal influence terminates.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE NONLINEAR TRIAD: CAUSAL MIXER
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Nonlinear Triad motif (M'|nabla(rho)|^2 harmonic generation)
acts as a causal mixer. It takes causal influence in one spectral
mode (k=1) and generates influence in another mode (k=3). The Triad
does not create new causal influence from nothing; it redistributes
existing influence across the spectral domain.

The mixing is asymmetric: influence flows from k=1 to k=3, not the
reverse. The k=3 mode is enslaved to k=1 (its amplitude is locked
to k=1's amplitude through the coupling). This asymmetry is a
consequence of the Modal Hierarchy: k=3 decays 9x faster than k=1,
so any independent k=3 excitation is rapidly dissipated. Only the
k=1-driven component persists.

Causal function: the Triad determines HOW influence is distributed
across spectral modes, creating harmonic structure from the
fundamental.

4. CAUSAL-LAW CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each architectural law from ED-Arch-03 imposes a constraint on the
causal structure. The laws are the rules that causal flow must obey.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF UNIQUE ATTRACTION -> GLOBAL CAUSAL SINK
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Causal constraint: all causal pathways terminate at (rho*, 0). There
is no causal cycle that avoids the attractor. There is no causal
pathway that leads to an alternative destination. Every chain of
influence, no matter how long or convoluted, ends at the same point.

This is the strongest causal constraint in the architecture. It means
that causality in ED is CONVERGENT: influence always flows toward
equilibrium, never away from it. There is no mechanism for creating
persistent causal influence. All influence is eventually absorbed by
the global sink.

The global causal sink is the architectural analog of a black hole in
causal terms: all influence enters, none escapes. But unlike a black
hole, the ED attractor is reachable in finite time and has no event
horizon. The comparison is topological, not physical.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF ONE-DIMENSIONAL RELAXATION -> CAUSAL CHANNEL NARROWING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Causal constraint: the effective number of independent causal channels
decreases from N (the number of spatial modes) to 1 (the fundamental
mode) during evolution. The causal structure undergoes dimensional
reduction: initially, N modes can carry independent influence. After
the Modal Funnel has acted, only one mode carries significant
influence.

Causal channel narrowing is the architectural analog of a bottleneck.
Information about the initial condition is progressively lost as
higher modes decay. The architecture's causal memory of spatial
detail is short-lived: within a few k=2 decay times, only the k=1
mode's information remains. Everything else has been dissipated.

This narrowing explains why diverse initial conditions produce
similar late-time behavior: the causal channel through which
influence reaches the late-time dynamics is too narrow (1D) to
transmit the diversity of the initial conditions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF PROPORTIONAL STABILITY -> CAUSAL INVARIANCE CLASS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Causal constraint: the causal structure (the topology of influence
pathways) is invariant under multiplicative perturbations of the
field components. Replacing M(rho) with M(rho)*g(rho) does not
change which points influence which other points, or through which
channels. It changes only the quantitative strength of the influence.

This constraint defines a causal equivalence class: all PDE instances
connected by multiplicative perturbations have the same causal
wiring diagram. They differ in the amplitude of signals flowing
through the wires, not in the wires themselves.

Additive perturbations break causal invariance by introducing new
influence sources that do not pass through the existing wiring. An
additive term in the v-equation creates a causal input that bypasses
the F[rho] -> v pathway, injecting influence directly into the
Memory Loop from outside the architecture's control.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF CRITICAL DAMPING BOUNDARY -> CAUSAL REGIME SWITCH
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Causal constraint: the causal pathway type switches discretely at
D + 2*zeta = 1. Below the boundary, influence circulates through
the Memory Loop (helical causal pathway). Above the boundary,
influence flows directly to the sink (radial causal pathway). There
is no intermediate pathway type.

The regime switch is a causal phase transition: the topology of
the influence pathway changes. In the spiral regime, the causal
graph has a cycle (the Memory Loop). In the monotonic regime, the
causal graph is acyclic (all influence flows one-way to the sink).
The boundary is the locus where the cycle breaks.

The transition is unidirectional in parameter space: moving from
spiral to monotonic is a loss of causal structure (the Memory Loop
cycle is removed). Moving from monotonic to spiral is a gain of
causal structure (the cycle is added). The 6 documented transitions
in ED-Phys-32 are all consistent with this picture.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF FINITE CAPACITY -> CAUSAL CUTOFF
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Causal constraint: spatial causal propagation cannot occur where
M(rho) = 0. The mobility collapse at rho_max creates a causal
cutoff: a spatial point at which diffusive influence cannot propagate.

The cutoff is partial (only the D-channel is blocked) and transient
(the penalty restores M > 0). But while it is active, it creates a
causal shadow: spatial points behind the horizon cannot receive
diffusive influence from points in front of it. The shadow is
short-lived (lasting only as long as rho remains near rho_max), but
it represents a genuine interruption of one causal channel.

The causal cutoff is the architectural mechanism of saturation: the
architecture cannot propagate spatial influence through regions of
maximum density. This limits the speed at which density perturbations
can grow: even if the Memory Loop drives density upward, the spatial
channel shuts down before rho_max is reached, preventing further
accumulation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF HARMONIC PROTECTION -> CAUSAL SPECTRAL LOCKING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Causal constraint: the spectral propagation channel (the Triad)
produces a fixed harmonic ratio k3/k1 regardless of the system's
state or parameters. The causal relationship between k=1 and k=3
is locked: k=3 is always a specific fraction of k=1, determined
by the coupling tensor's algebraic structure.

This locking means that k=3's causal influence is not independent.
It is a SHADOW of k=1's influence, transformed by the Triad but
not independently generated. The spectral causal structure is
therefore simpler than it appears: what looks like two independent
modes (k=1 and k=3) is actually one independent mode (k=1) and
one enslaved harmonic (k=3).

The invariance of the locking ratio (< 0.01% variation across all
perturbation variants in ED-Phys-25, 26, 28) means the spectral
causal structure is architecturally fixed. No admissible perturbation
can change the causal relationship between k=1 and k=3.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF MODAL HIERARCHY -> CAUSAL ORDERING OF MODES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Causal constraint: higher modes lose causal influence faster than
lower modes. The k-th mode's causal influence decays at rate
proportional to k^2. After time t, the k-th mode's causal weight
relative to k=1 is proportional to exp(-(k^2 - 1)*lambda*t).

This creates a strict causal ordering: at any time t, mode k=1
has the most causal influence, k=2 has less, k=3 has less still,
and so on. The ordering is fixed by the Laplacian's spectrum and
cannot be reversed by any dynamical process within the architecture.

The causal ordering ensures that the long-time dynamics are
controlled by the lowest mode. High-mode initial conditions (sharp
features, noise) have high causal influence initially but lose it
rapidly. Low-mode initial conditions (smooth Gaussians) have lower
initial influence but retain it longer. The Modal Hierarchy is a
causal filter: it progressively removes high-frequency causal
content and preserves low-frequency content.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAW OF TRANSIENT STRUCTURE -> CAUSAL DECAY OF ALL INFLUENCE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Causal constraint: all causal influence eventually decays to zero.
No persistent influence exists in the architecture. Every causal
chain has a finite lifetime, set by the convergence rate of the
mode that carries it.

The transience of causal influence is the architectural analog of
the thermodynamic arrow: influence flows in one direction (toward
equilibrium) and is consumed (dissipated) in the process. The
architecture is a causal consumer: it takes influence (deviation
from equilibrium) and converts it into equilibrium (absence of
influence). When all influence has been consumed, the system has
reached its final state.

The longest-lived causal influence is carried by the k=1 mode,
which decays at rate lambda_1. In the oscillatory regime with low
damping, lambda_1 can be as slow as 0.002 (ED-Phys-29, D=0), giving
a causal lifetime of approximately 1/0.002 = 500 time units. In the
strongly damped regime, lambda_1 can be as fast as 0.139 (ED-Phys-33,
D=0, zeta=0.25, tau=0.5, A=10), giving a causal lifetime of
approximately 7 time units. The architecture's causal memory spans
this range.


========================================================================
5. GEOMETRY OF CAUSAL FLOW
========================================================================

The geometric objects from ED-Arch-04 are not just shapes; they are
the pathways through which causal influence flows. Each geometric
object defines a specific causal flow pattern.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL SHEET: HELICAL CAUSAL PROPAGATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

On the Spiral Sheet, causal influence propagates helically. The
influence moves inward toward the Attractor Point (radial component)
while simultaneously circulating between the density and participation
fields (tangential component).

The helical propagation has a definite pitch: the ratio of radial
advance per revolution to the revolution's circumference. At low
damping (D=0, zeta=0.25), the pitch is small (many revolutions per
radial unit, tight helix). At moderate damping (D=0.5, zeta=0.25),
the pitch is larger (fewer revolutions, looser helix). At the
Boundary, the pitch becomes infinite (the helix opens into a
straight line).

The causal content of each revolution: during one spiral revolution,
the system samples the full cycle of the Memory Loop. Density
overshoots, the operator reverses, v integrates the reversal, density
undershoots, the operator reverses again, and v integrates the second
reversal. Each revolution tests the full causal circuit of the
architecture. The information gained per revolution (about the
architecture's response to overshoots and undershoots) is redundant
after the first few cycles: the architecture's linear response
dominates, and each subsequent cycle repeats the same causal pattern
at lower amplitude.

The Spiral Sheet's helical causality explains why cycle count does
not strongly affect the final state: whether the system makes 1
cycle or 235 cycles, the destination is the same. The additional
cycles carry redundant causal information.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MONOTONIC CONE: RADIAL CAUSAL FLOW
------------------------------------------------------------------------

On the Monotonic Cone, causal influence propagates radially: straight
from the initial state to the Attractor Point, without circulation.
The causal pathway is the shortest possible path in phase space.

The radial flow has no tangential component: there is no circulation
between V_P and V_v, because the Memory Loop is overdamped and v
decays too quickly to create significant feedback. The causal pathway
is purely dissipative: influence is absorbed by the three dissipation
channels as it flows inward.

The Monotonic Cone's radial causality is the architecture's most
efficient causal mode. No influence is wasted on oscillatory
circulation. Every unit of causal influence is converted directly
into convergence. This is why the monotonic regime, despite having
slower convergence rates (lambda = 0.006-0.093), approaches
equilibrium without the extended transients that the spiral regime
exhibits.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
HORIZON SURFACE: CAUSAL REFLECTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Horizon Surface acts as a causal reflector. When a trajectory
reaches the horizon, the spatial causal channel (D-channel) is
blocked. Influence that was propagating spatially through the
D-channel is reflected back through the temporal channel (v) and
the regulatory channel (P_SY2).

The reflection is not specular (angle of incidence does not equal
angle of reflection). It is a causal mode conversion: spatial
influence is converted into temporal and regulatory influence. The
density stops spreading spatially (because M = 0) but continues to
evolve temporally (because v is still active) and regulatorily
(because P_SY2 is still active). The trajectory in phase space
shows a characteristic kink at the horizon encounter, where the
spatial component of the causal flow reverses while the temporal
and regulatory components continue.

In ED-Phys-29, horizon encounters produce horizon_max values of
1-7 density units above the effective threshold, indicating brief
penetration followed by rapid reflection. The reflection time
(residence time at the horizon) is short compared to the oscillation
period.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
BOUNDARY SURFACE: CAUSAL BIFURCATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Boundary Surface is the locus of causal bifurcation: the point
in parameter space where the causal pathway type splits. Below the
boundary, the causal pathway contains a cycle (the Memory Loop).
Above the boundary, it does not.

This is a true bifurcation in the causal graph's topology. The graph
transitions from cyclic to acyclic. The transition is sharp (it
occurs at a definite parameter value, D + 2*zeta = 1) and universal
(it affects all trajectories, all initial conditions, all spatial
modes simultaneously).

The Boundary Surface divides the architecture's causal repertoire
into two qualitatively different types. This is the deepest causal
fact about the parameter space: it has exactly two causal topologies,
separated by a single surface.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MODAL FUNNEL: SPECTRAL CAUSAL NARROWING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Modal Funnel is the geometry of spectral causal narrowing. As
higher modes decay, the number of independent causal channels
decreases. The funnel narrows from N channels (at t=0) to 1 channel
(at late times).

The narrowing is not gradual: it proceeds in discrete jumps as each
mode's causal influence drops below significance. The k=2 mode loses
significance after approximately (1/3*lambda_1) time units. The k=3
mode loses significance after approximately (1/8*lambda_1) time
units. The k=4 mode after (1/15*lambda_1) time units. The funnel
narrows rapidly, with most of the narrowing occurring in the first
few percent of the total evolution time.

The causal content of the narrowing: the architecture progressively
forgets spatial detail. Sharp edges, fine structure, and high-
frequency components of the initial condition lose their causal
influence early. Only the broad, low-frequency character of the
initial condition survives to influence the late-time dynamics. The
Modal Funnel is the architecture's information bottleneck.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRIAD LOCUS: CAUSAL MIXING ZONE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Triad Locus is the spatial region where causal influence is
mixed between spectral modes. At the flanks of density peaks, the
M'|nabla(rho)|^2 coupling takes k=1 influence and generates k=3
influence. This is a causal transformation: influence in one
frequency band is converted into influence in another frequency band.

The mixing is localized in both space and time. Spatially, it occurs
only where gradients are steep and M' is significant (the flanks of
density peaks). Temporally, it occurs only during the early-to-middle
transient when gradients are still large. The Triad Locus shrinks
as the density field approaches equilibrium, and the causal mixing
ceases.

The mixing is also asymmetric: influence flows from k=1 to k=3 but
not the reverse. The generated k=3 influence is immediately subject
to the Modal Hierarchy's fast decay (9x k=1 rate). The Triad Locus
is a one-way causal transformer: it creates spectral influence that
is immediately consumed by the dissipation channels.


========================================================================
6. CAUSAL PROPAGATION RULES
========================================================================

The causal structure of the ED architecture can be summarized in five
propagation rules. These rules govern how influence moves through the
field and are the causal expression of the architectural laws.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 1: THE LOCALITY RULE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement: Influence propagates spatially through the gradient
nabla(rho) and the Laplacian nabla^2(rho), affecting only
nearest-neighbor points at each time step.

Mechanism: The operator field F[rho] depends on rho and its first
two spatial derivatives at each point. These are local differential
quantities. The influence of point x on point x+dx is mediated by
the gradient (first derivative) and the curvature (second derivative).

Consequence: Long-range spatial influence requires multiple time
steps. A change at one end of the domain reaches the other end only
after the information has diffused across the entire domain. The
diffusion time scales as L^2/M, where L is the domain length and M
is the mobility.

Governing law: Modal Hierarchy (the Laplacian's structure determines
the spatial coupling).

Governing motif: Saddle (the gradient geometry at extrema routes
local influence).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 2: THE MEMORY RULE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement: Influence persists temporally through the participation
field v, which integrates past operator forcing with exponential
decay at rate zeta/tau.

Mechanism: The v-equation dv/dt = (1/tau)*(F - zeta*v) is a leaky
integrator. It stores a weighted average of past F values. The stored
influence feeds back into the density equation through the H*v term.

Consequence: The architecture has a temporal memory horizon of
approximately tau/zeta time units. Events within this horizon
influence the present dynamics. Events beyond it have negligible
effect. The memory is local in space (v at point x depends only on
the history at point x) and exponentially decaying in time.

Governing law: Critical Damping Boundary (the damping ratio
D + 2*zeta determines whether the memory creates oscillation or
merely delays convergence).

Governing motif: Memory Loop (the F -> v -> rho -> F cycle is the
mechanism of temporal memory).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 3: THE DISSIPATION RULE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement: Influence decays through three independent dissipation
channels: spatial smoothing (D-channel, rate D*k^2), participation
damping (zeta-channel, rate zeta/tau), and penalty regulation
(P-channel, rate alpha*gamma/rho_0). All three channels are always
active (except the D-channel at the Horizon Surface).

Mechanism: Each channel converts architectural energy into
equilibrium. The D-channel smooths spatial gradients. The
zeta-channel damps stored memory. The P-channel pulls density
toward rho*. Together, they drain all forms of influence.

Consequence: No influence persists indefinitely. Every causal chain
has a finite lifetime, bounded by the slowest dissipation rate
(typically the k=1 mode in the D-channel, with rate lambda_1).

Governing law: Transient Structure (all influence eventually
decays).

Governing motif: Dissipator (the terminal motif where influence
is absorbed).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 4: THE SPECTRAL RULE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement: Influence cascades from the fundamental mode (k=1) to
the third harmonic (k=3) through the nonlinear coupling
M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2. The cascade ratio is fixed by the coupling
tensor's algebraic structure and is invariant under admissible
perturbations.

Mechanism: The squared gradient of a k=1 mode generates k=0 and
k=2 content. Multiplication by the density-dependent M'(rho) mixes
these with k=1, generating k=3. The generated k=3 amplitude is
proportional to the square of the k=1 amplitude (since
|nabla(rho)|^2 is quadratic in the gradient, which is proportional
to the k=1 amplitude).

Consequence: The k=3 mode's causal influence is entirely determined
by the k=1 mode. It is not an independent source of influence. The
spectral cascade is a one-way, locked coupling, not a free transfer.

Governing law: Harmonic Protection (the cascade ratio is invariant).

Governing motif: Nonlinear Triad (the mixer that performs the
cascade).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 5: THE CAPACITY RULE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Statement: Influence cannot propagate spatially through points
where rho = rho_max. The mobility collapse M(rho_max) = 0 creates
an absolute causal cutoff for the D-channel at maximum density.

Mechanism: M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) = 0 when M = 0. The diffusion term
vanishes, and the spatial coupling between neighboring points is
severed (through the D-channel). The P-channel and zeta-channel
remain operative, providing alternative influence pathways.

Consequence: The Horizon Surface is a partial causal barrier. It
prevents diffusive influence from crossing the high-density region
but allows regulatory and participatory influence to continue. The
barrier is transient because the penalty drives density away from
rho_max, restoring M > 0.

Governing law: Finite Capacity (M(rho_max) = 0).

Governing motif: Horizon (the transient causal barrier).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
UNIFICATION OF THE FIVE RULES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The five causal propagation rules form a coherent system:

  Rule 1 (Locality) determines the spatial pathway of influence.
  Rule 2 (Memory) determines the temporal persistence of influence.
  Rule 3 (Dissipation) determines the lifetime of influence.
  Rule 4 (Spectral) determines the frequency content of influence.
  Rule 5 (Capacity) determines the spatial limits of influence.

Together, the rules answer every question about how influence moves
through the architectural field:

  Where does influence go? Through spatial neighbors (Rule 1), along
  the Manifold Curve (Rule 3 + Modal Hierarchy), to the Attractor
  Point (Rule 3 + Unique Attraction).

  How long does influence last? Spatially: until the D-channel
  dissipates it (Rule 3, rate D*k^2). Temporally: until the
  zeta-channel dissipates it (Rule 3, rate zeta/tau). Overall:
  until the total architectural energy reaches zero (Rule 3).

  What spectral content does influence carry? Primarily k=1, with
  enslaved k=3 content generated by the Triad (Rule 4). Higher modes
  decay rapidly and carry negligible late-time influence (Rule 3 +
  Modal Hierarchy).

  Where can influence NOT go? Through points of maximum density
  (Rule 5), where the spatial channel is blocked.

  How does influence change character? Below D + 2*zeta = 1, it
  circulates (Rule 2 + Spiral). Above, it flows directly (Rule 1 +
  Dissipator). At the boundary, it transitions (Rule 2 + Boundary).

The five rules, together with the nine architectural laws and the
eight motifs, provide a complete description of the ED architecture's
causal structure.

HOW THE ARCHITECTURE CHANNELS INFLUENCE

The ED architecture is a causal machine: a structure that takes
influence (deviation from equilibrium) as input and produces
equilibrium as output. The machine has three input channels (spatial
gradients, temporal memory, spectral content), three processing
elements (the D-channel, the Memory Loop, and the Triad), and one
output (the Attractor Point).

The architecture channels influence through a specific sequence:

  INITIAL CONDITION -> high-dimensional influence in many modes
  -> Modal Funnel narrows to k=1 (Rules 1, 3)
  -> Triad generates k=3 harmonic (Rule 4)
  -> Memory Loop circulates or transmits influence (Rule 2)
  -> Dissipation drains influence toward equilibrium (Rule 3)
  -> Horizon reflects if capacity is reached (Rule 5)
  -> Attractor Point absorbs all remaining influence

This sequence is the architecture's causal program: the fixed
algorithm through which every initial condition is processed. The
program's output is always the same (equilibrium). The program's
execution path may differ (spiral or monotonic, with or without
horizon encounters), but the destination is invariant.

WHY ED DYNAMICS ARE DIRECTIONAL

The directionality of ED dynamics — the fact that all influence flows
toward equilibrium and never away from it — is not a single property
but the convergence of five independent causal constraints:

  (1) The Lyapunov functional E[rho, v] always decreases (ED-Arch-06).
  (2) All three dissipation channels drain influence (Rule 3).
  (3) The Modal Hierarchy removes high-frequency influence first,
      preventing its regeneration (Rule 4's cascade is enslaved,
      not independent).
  (4) The Capacity Rule prevents density from exceeding rho_max,
      bounding the state space (Rule 5).
  (5) The unique attractor has a global basin with no competing
      sinks (Law of Unique Attraction).

Any one of these would create a directional preference. Together,
they create an absolute directional constraint: there is no dynamical
pathway, no parameter choice, no initial condition, and no admissible
perturbation that can reverse the direction of causal flow in the
ED architecture.

WHY MOTIFS HAVE THEIR CAUSAL ROLES

The causal roles of the motifs are not assigned arbitrarily. Each
role follows from the motif's structural position in the
architecture:

  The Saddle is a routing point BECAUSE it is located where gradients
  reverse — the natural point at which spatial influence changes
  direction.

  The Spiral is a delay loop BECAUSE the Memory Loop circulates
  influence with insufficient damping — the natural behavior of an
  under-damped feedback circuit.

The motifs are not design choices. They are causal inevitabilities
