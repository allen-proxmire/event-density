# Division of Labor: What We Provide vs. What They Do

## What We Provide

| Item | Status |
|------|--------|
| Complete experimental protocol (concentrations, bleach settings, imaging parameters) | Ready |
| Sample preparation instructions | Ready |
| BSA-FITC reagent (if needed) | Can ship |
| Data analysis pipeline (front extraction, fitting, statistics) | Validated |
| Theoretical prediction to compare against | Published |
| Manuscript preparation (first draft, figures, interpretation) | Our responsibility |
| All troubleshooting for data quality issues | Our responsibility |

## What They Do

| Task | Time | Difficulty |
|------|------|------------|
| Prepare BSA-FITC solutions at 4 concentrations | 2-3 hours | Routine |
| Load samples into chambers | 30 min | Easy |
| Run confocal FRAP (5 replicates per concentration) | 1-2 hours | Standard |
| Save raw TIFF stacks | 5 min | Trivial |
| Send data via shared folder | 10 min | Trivial |
| **Total** | **4-6 hours** | **Standard lab skills** |

## What They Do NOT Need to Do

- No theory. They do not need to understand Event Density, porous medium equations, or the derivation of alpha_R.
- No analysis. They do not need to fit curves, extract fronts, or compute statistics.
- No writing. We write the manuscript. They review and approve the experimental sections.
- No special equipment. Standard confocal with FRAP module. Nothing custom.
- No long-term commitment. This is a one-time experiment, not an ongoing collaboration (unless both parties want to continue).

## Authorship

If the experiment produces a publishable result (positive or negative):
- The experimentalist is a co-author.
- They review the manuscript before submission.
- Specific authorship order is discussed after results are available.

## Summary

The collaborator's total investment is **one afternoon of standard confocal imaging**. Everything else — design, analysis, interpretation, writing — is handled on our side.
