1. PURPOSE OF ARCHITECTURAL SYMMETRY
========================================================================

The previous eight chapters described what the architecture does, what
patterns it generates, what rules it obeys, what shapes it creates,
how its field is structured, why it evolves as it does, through what
pathways influence flows, and what happens to the information it
carries. This ninth chapter asks the complementary question: what
remains unchanged? Under what transformations does the architecture
look the same? What are the invariances that define the architecture's
identity?

Architectural symmetry is:

  (i)   NOT PHYSICAL SYMMETRY.  The ED cosmology PDE does not
        describe a system with Lorentz invariance, gauge symmetry,
        or the symmetry groups of particle physics. Its symmetries
        are structural, not physical: they describe invariances of
        the architectural field under transformations of its
        components, parameters, and states.

  (ii)  INVARIANCE UNDER TRANSFORMATION.  A symmetry exists when a
        transformation of the architectural field leaves some
        structurally significant property unchanged. The
        transformation may act on the density field, the
        participation field, the operator field, the parameters,
        the initial conditions, or the geometric objects. The
        invariant property may be the motif vocabulary, the law
        structure, the geometric topology, the causal graph, or
        the information processing pipeline.

  (iii) EQUIVALENCE CLASSIFICATION.  Symmetries define equivalence
        classes: sets of states, parameter values, or PDE instances
        that are architecturally indistinguishable. Two states are
        equivalent if they share the same invariant properties under
        the relevant symmetry transformation. Equivalence classes
        partition the architecture's universe into groups of
        structurally identical configurations.

Architectural symmetry differs from conservation laws and PDE
invariants in a fundamental way:

  CONSERVATION LAWS describe quantities that are constant along
  trajectories. The ED architecture has almost no conservation laws
  (ED-Arch-06): it is purely dissipative, and nearly everything
  changes.

  PDE INVARIANTS describe properties of the equation itself (e.g.,
  the equation is second-order, the equation is parabolic). These
  are trivially invariant and do not constitute architectural
  symmetries.

  ARCHITECTURAL SYMMETRIES describe structural properties that are
  preserved under non-trivial transformations. The architecture may
  look different after a transformation (different parameter values,
  different initial conditions, different functional forms), but
  certain structural features remain the same. These preserved
  features define the architecture's identity: what makes it ED
  rather than some other dynamical system.

Symmetry is the deepest structural property because it answers the
question: what is the architecture, fundamentally? The answer is:
the architecture is whatever is invariant under all admissible
transformations. Everything else is detail.


========================================================================
2. SYMMETRIES OF THE ARCHITECTURAL FIELD
========================================================================

The architectural field (rho, v, F[rho]) admits five classes of
transformations that leave significant structural properties
invariant. These are the fundamental symmetries of the ED
architecture.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 AMPLITUDE SCALING SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Transformation: rho(x,t) -> rho* + lambda * (rho(x,t) - rho*) for
a positive scaling factor lambda, with corresponding scaling of v
and adjustment of the operator.

Invariant property: the qualitative dynamics. For sufficiently small
lambda (within the linear regime near equilibrium), the mode
structure, convergence rates, cycle counts, and flow topology are
unchanged. The system's behavior at amplitude A is qualitatively
identical to its behavior at amplitude lambda*A, provided lambda*A
does not trigger the Horizon (lambda*A < rho_max - rho*).

Breaking condition: the Horizon. When lambda*A approaches rho_max -
rho* = 50, the mobility collapse introduces nonlinear effects that
break the scaling symmetry. The Horizon is the architectural boundary
at which amplitude scaling symmetry fails.

Evidence: ED-Phys-29 shows convergence rates that vary only modestly
with amplitude (std/mean = 13-25% across 22 IC types at each
cosmology), confirming approximate amplitude scaling symmetry in the
linear regime. The Horizon activation at A >= 40 (D=0) marks the
symmetry's breakdown.

Domain of validity: rho* - rho_max < lambda*(rho_0 - rho*) <
rho_max - rho*, where rho_0 is the initial condition's peak
density. Within this domain, the architecture's motif vocabulary,
law structure, and geometric topology are all invariant.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2 SPATIAL TRANSLATION SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Transformation: rho(x,t) -> rho(x + a, t) for any shift a, with
corresponding shift of v(x,t) -> v(x + a, t).

Invariant property: the full dynamics. Under periodic boundary
conditions (as used in all ED-Phys experiments), the PDE is exactly
translation-invariant. A Gaussian centered at x=0.3L produces
identical dynamics (in a shifted frame) to a Gaussian centered at
x=0.7L.

Breaking condition: none, within the periodic domain. Spatial
translation is an exact symmetry. It is the only exact continuous
symmetry of the ED architecture (apart from trivial identity
transformations).

Significance: spatial translation symmetry means the architecture
treats all spatial positions equally. There is no preferred location.
The density field's structure (peaks, troughs, gradients) is
meaningful, but its absolute position in the domain is not. This
is the architectural expression of spatial homogeneity: the rules
of becoming are the same everywhere.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3 MULTIPLICATIVE CHANNEL SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Transformation: the simultaneous multiplicative modification of
the architectural field's components:

  M(rho) -> M(rho) * g_M(rho)    (mobility rescaling)
  P(rho) -> P(rho) * g_P(rho)    (penalty rescaling)
  v_update -> v_update * g_v(rho) (participation rescaling)

where g_M, g_P, g_v are smooth, positive functions.

Invariant property: the complete motif vocabulary, the complete law
structure, the geometric topology, the causal graph, and the
information processing pipeline. All eight motifs survive. All nine
laws hold. All geometric objects retain their qualitative character.
The causal pathways are preserved. The information processing stages
operate in the same sequence.

Breaking condition: non-multiplicative (additive) modifications.
Adding a term s(rho) to the v-equation (rather than multiplying by
a factor) breaks the channel symmetry and distorts the dynamics
(ED-Phys-27, 28: cycles jump from 1 to 8-9, amplitudes increase
by 5.8-6.4x).

Evidence: ED-Phys-25 (penalty variants): all multiplicative variants
preserve n_cycles = 1, k3_max = 6.36, and boundary structure.
ED-Phys-26 (mobility variants): all multiplicative variants preserve
n_cycles = 1, k3_max = 6.44, undershoot within 3%, relaxation time
within 4%. ED-Phys-28 (combined stress): simultaneous multiplicative
perturbation of all three channels preserves all structural features.

Significance: multiplicative channel symmetry is the architectural
analog of gauge invariance. It defines the equivalence class of PDE
instances that are architecturally identical despite having different
functional forms. The specific choice of M(rho) = M0*(1-rho/rho_max)^2
is one representative of this class; any other mobility with the same
zero structure and positivity would produce the same architecture.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.4 SPECTRAL PHASE SYMMETRY UNDER TRIAD LOCKING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Transformation: relabeling of the initial condition's Fourier phase
distribution. Replacing rho(x,0) = rho* + sum_k a_k*sin(k*pi*x/L +
phi_k) with the same sum at different phases phi_k.

Invariant property: the amplitude dynamics. The reduced coordinates
(rho_peak, v_peak) evolve identically regardless of the initial
phases phi_k, because the phase-space projection onto (rho_peak,
v_peak) is insensitive to the spatial location of the density peak.

Breaking condition: the Triad coupling has a weak phase dependence
(the M'|nabla(rho)|^2 term depends on the relative phases between
modes). However, since the Triad's output is enslaved to k=1, the
phase dependence affects the spatial shape of the k=3 harmonic but
not its amplitude. The amplitude dynamics are phase-invariant.

Evidence: ED-Phys-29 shows that Gaussians, sines, and composites
(which differ primarily in phase structure) converge to the same
manifold with similar convergence rates. ED-Phys-30 confirms that
probes with different spatial symmetries (pure density, pure
velocity, mixed, orthogonal) all reach the same 1D manifold.

Significance: spectral phase symmetry means the architecture cares
about HOW MUCH modal content exists (amplitudes) but not WHERE it
is located in space (phases). This is a consequence of the reduction
to (rho_peak, v_peak) coordinates: the peak value integrates over
all phases.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.5 TEMPORAL REPARAMETERIZATION SYMMETRY UNDER DAMPING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Transformation: tau -> c*tau, zeta -> c*zeta for a positive constant
c. This rescales the participation time scale and damping
simultaneously.

Invariant property: the damping ratio zeta/tau, which controls the
Memory Loop's exponential decay rate, is invariant under this
transformation. The dynamics are approximately invariant when the
participation dynamics are much faster than the spatial dynamics
(tau*lambda_1 << 1).

Breaking condition: when tau becomes comparable to the spatial
relaxation time 1/lambda_1, the participation dynamics can no longer
be treated as fast, and the temporal reparameterization symmetry
breaks. ED-Phys-31 shows that tau has < 5% effect on convergence
rates, confirming that the symmetry holds approximately across the
tested range (tau = 0.5 to 2.0).

Evidence: ED-Phys-31 per-tau statistics: mean lambda = 0.035, 0.034,
0.034 at tau = 0.5, 1.0, 2.0 respectively. Mean cycles = 4.76,
4.73, 4.96. The near-invariance confirms approximate temporal
reparameterization symmetry.

Significance: this symmetry means the architecture's qualitative
behavior depends on the ratio zeta/tau (how fast the Memory Loop
forgets), not on tau and zeta independently. The effective parameter
space is lower-dimensional than it appears: (D, zeta/tau) rather
than (D, zeta, tau).


========================================================================
3. SYMMETRY-MOTIF CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each motif has characteristic symmetry properties that define its
geometric and dynamical character.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SADDLE: SIGN-INVERSION SYMMETRY OF GRADIENTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Saddle possesses a discrete sign-inversion symmetry. At a density
peak, the gradient field nabla(rho) is antisymmetric about the peak
location: nabla(rho)(x_peak + delta) = -nabla(rho)(x_peak - delta)
for small delta. The curvature nabla^2(rho) is symmetric
(even function about the peak).

This symmetry means the Saddle routes information symmetrically:
the left and right flanks of the peak receive equal and opposite
gradient influence. The Saddle's causal routing (ED-Arch-07) respects
this parity: it demultiplexes information into two mirror-image
streams.

The sign-inversion symmetry is exact for symmetric initial conditions
(Gaussians centered on the domain) and approximate for asymmetric
ones (off-center Gaussians, composites). The penalty and diffusion
terms preserve the symmetry; the Nonlinear Triad can break it
slightly through the density dependence of M'(rho).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SPIRAL: ROTATIONAL SYMMETRY IN PHASE SPACE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Spiral possesses an approximate continuous rotational symmetry
in the (rho_peak, v_peak) phase plane. Near the Attractor Point,
the linearized dynamics produce a rotation (from the complex
eigenvalues) combined with a contraction (from the negative real
part). The rotation component is a continuous symmetry: the
spiral's shape is approximately invariant under rotation about the
Attractor Point.

The rotational symmetry is exact in the linear regime (near
equilibrium) and approximate in the nonlinear regime (far from
equilibrium). The nonlinear terms (mobility dependence, penalty
saturation) introduce deviations from perfect rotational symmetry,
creating a spiral that is not exactly self-similar under rotation.

The rotation angle per unit time is omega = Im(lambda), where lambda
is the dominant eigenvalue of the damping tensor. The contraction
rate per unit time is sigma = -Re(lambda). The spiral's self-
similarity parameter is sigma/omega: the ratio of contraction to
rotation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE HORIZON: SATURATION SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Horizon possesses a saturation symmetry: all density values near
rho_max are treated approximately equally by the mobility collapse.
Whether rho = 0.95*rho_max or rho = 0.99*rho_max, the mobility is
extremely small and the diffusive dynamics are effectively frozen.
The Horizon does not distinguish between different high-density
states; it treats them all as "saturated."

This saturation symmetry is the informational equivalent of
quantization: the continuous range of density values near rho_max
is collapsed into a single effective state ("at the Horizon"). The
architecture loses the ability to distinguish between different
high-density configurations, creating an information bottleneck.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MANIFOLD: REPARAMETERIZATION SYMMETRY ALONG THE CURVE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Manifold possesses a reparameterization symmetry: the dynamics
along the manifold curve are invariant under reparameterization of
the curve's coordinate. Whether the manifold is parameterized by
time t, by the k=1 amplitude a_1, by the distance to the attractor,
or by the total energy E, the trajectory is the same curve in phase
space.

This symmetry means the manifold's geometric shape is an intrinsic
property, independent of how it is traversed. The manifold curve
is defined by the relationship between rho_peak and v_peak, not by
the speed at which the system moves along it. Different parameter
values (different D, zeta, tau) may change the traversal speed but
not the curve's shape (in the linear regime near equilibrium).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE BOUNDARY: LINEAR SYMMETRY IN (D, ZETA)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Boundary possesses a linear symmetry: it is a straight line (or
plane) in the (D, zeta) parameter space, with equation D + 2*zeta = 1.
This linearity means the Boundary is invariant under any affine
transformation that preserves the linear combination D + 2*zeta.

The linear symmetry reflects the fact that the Critical Damping
Boundary arises from the linearized dynamics, where the effective
damping is a linear function of D and zeta. Nonlinear effects near
the Boundary create a narrow transition zone but do not bend the
Boundary itself.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MEMORY LOOP: GAIN-SCALING SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Memory Loop possesses a gain-scaling symmetry: multiplying the
loop gain H/tau by a constant c while simultaneously dividing zeta
by c leaves the damping ratio unchanged and preserves the
oscillatory character. The Memory Loop's qualitative behavior depends
on the balance between gain and damping, not on their individual
values.

This symmetry is the architectural basis of the temporal
reparameterization symmetry (Section 2.5): the loop's behavior
depends on ratios, not absolutes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE DISSIPATOR: PARABOLIC SCALING SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Dissipator possesses a parabolic scaling symmetry: the diffusion
equation drho/dt = D*F[rho] is invariant under the parabolic
rescaling x -> lambda*x, t -> lambda^2*t. This is the standard
scaling symmetry of diffusion equations and reflects the diffusive
character of the D-channel.

The parabolic scaling means that doubling the spatial domain length
quadruples the relaxation time. This scaling is broken by the
penalty (which introduces a length scale through rho*) and by the
domain's finite size (which quantizes the Fourier modes). But it
holds approximately for the spatial dynamics within the domain.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE NONLINEAR TRIAD: HARMONIC PERMUTATION SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Nonlinear Triad possesses a restricted harmonic symmetry: the
k=3 output amplitude is invariant under permutations of the
intermediate coupling steps. Whether the M'|nabla(rho)|^2 term
generates k=3 through the path k=1 * k=1 -> k=2 -> k=3 or through
k=1 * k=1 -> k=0 -> k=3, the final k=3 amplitude is the same.

This permutation symmetry of the intermediate steps is a consequence
of the algebraic structure of the bilinear coupling. The final
harmonic output depends on the total coupling integral, not on the
specific sequence of partial products. This is why the k=3 amplitude
is so robust (< 0.01% variation): it is protected by both the
multiplicative channel symmetry and the Triad's internal permutation
symmetry.

4. SYMMETRY-LAW CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each architectural law can be understood as the expression of a
symmetry or the consequence of a symmetry's properties.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
UNIQUE ATTRACTION -> GLOBAL CONTRACTION SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Law of Unique Attraction expresses a contraction symmetry: the
architecture contracts phase-space volume at every point, at every
time, for every state. The contraction is global (no region of state
space expands) and uniform in sign (always contracting, never
expanding).

The contraction symmetry is the flow-theoretic statement that the
Jacobian's trace (the divergence of the flow field) is everywhere
negative. This is a symmetry in the sense that it is invariant under
all admissible transformations: multiplicative perturbations, IC
changes, parameter variations. The contraction never reverses.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
ONE-DIMENSIONAL RELAXATION -> DIMENSIONAL REDUCTION SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Law of One-Dimensional Relaxation expresses a dimensional
reduction symmetry: the infinite-dimensional state space is mapped
to a one-dimensional manifold by the dynamics, and this mapping is
invariant under changes in initial conditions, parameters (within
each cosmological regime), and multiplicative perturbations.

The symmetry is that the manifold's dimension (1) is invariant.
Not its shape, not its embedding, not its parameterization — just
its dimension. This topological invariant is the most robust
symmetry in the architecture: it holds in 64/66 runs (ED-Phys-29)
and 48/48 runs (ED-Phys-30).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROPORTIONAL STABILITY -> MULTIPLICATIVE INVARIANCE CLASS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Law of Proportional Stability IS a symmetry statement: the
architecture is invariant under the multiplicative transformation
group. This group consists of all transformations that rescale M, P,
and the v-coupling by smooth, positive, density-dependent factors.

The invariance class is the orbit of the canonical PDE under this
transformation group. Every PDE in the orbit has the same motifs,
laws, geometry, causality, and information processing. The orbit
is the architecture's equivalence class under multiplicative
symmetry.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
CRITICAL DAMPING BOUNDARY -> LINEAR DISCRIMINANT SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Law of Critical Damping Boundary expresses the symmetry of a
linear discriminant. The boundary D + 2*zeta = 1 is a linear
function of the parameters, and it divides parameter space into
exactly two regions. The linear structure is invariant: no
admissible perturbation can bend the boundary into a curve or
create additional regions.

The discriminant's linearity is a symmetry of the damping tensor:
the tensor's eigenvalue structure depends linearly on D and zeta
(because both enter linearly into the 2x2 matrix). The quadratic
terms (from the characteristic polynomial) produce the discriminant,
but the discriminant's zero set is linear because the quadratic
terms cancel along the critical curve.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
FINITE CAPACITY -> SATURATION SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Law of Finite Capacity expresses a saturation symmetry: all
states with rho near rho_max are dynamically equivalent (all have
M near zero, all are reflected by the penalty). The saturation
symmetry collapses a continuous range of high-density states into
a single effective state class.

The saturation symmetry is a consequence of the mobility's
quadratic vanishing: M = M0*(1 - rho/rho_max)^2 approaches zero
smoothly, making all near-rho_max states indistinguishable from
the dynamical perspective of the D-channel.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
HARMONIC PROTECTION -> INVARIANT TRIAD RATIOS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Law of Harmonic Protection expresses a spectral invariance: the
k3/k1 amplitude ratio is fixed under all admissible transformations.
This is a symmetry of the coupling tensor: the Triad's selection
rules are algebraic (determined by the structure of M'*|nabla(rho)|^2),
not parametric (not dependent on the values of alpha, gamma, M0,
etc.).

The invariant ratio is the architectural fingerprint: the one number
that identifies the architecture across all parameter regimes, all
initial conditions, and all multiplicative perturbation classes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MODAL HIERARCHY -> ORDERED SPECTRAL SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Law of Modal Hierarchy expresses an ordering symmetry: the decay
rates are strictly ordered by k^2, and this ordering is invariant
under all transformations that preserve the Laplacian's structure.
No perturbation of M, P, or v can reverse the ordering between any
two modes.

The ordering symmetry means that the Fourier modes form a totally
ordered set under the decay rate, and this ordering is a structural
invariant. The k=1 mode is always the slowest, the k=2 mode is
always the second slowest, and so on.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRANSIENT STRUCTURE -> IRREVERSIBLE SYMMETRY BREAKING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Law of Transient Structure expresses an asymmetry: time-reversal
symmetry is broken. The forward dynamics (t increasing) always
simplify the field. The backward dynamics (t decreasing) would
complicate the field, regenerating structure from equilibrium. But
the backward dynamics do not occur, because the architecture's
dissipative structure makes them non-physical.

The irreversible symmetry breaking is the deepest asymmetry in the
architecture. It is the architectural expression of the second law:
structural complexity cannot increase. This asymmetry is not a
property of the equations (which could in principle be run backward)
but of the architecture (which has no mechanism for generating
structure from equilibrium).


========================================================================
5. GEOMETRIC SYMMETRIES
========================================================================

The geometric objects of ED-Arch-04 possess specific symmetries that
reflect the architecture's structural invariances.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL SHEET: ROTATIONAL + RADIAL SCALING SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Spiral Sheet has an approximate logarithmic spiral symmetry:
it is approximately invariant under the combined transformation of
rotation by angle theta and radial scaling by exp(-sigma*theta/omega),
where sigma is the contraction rate and omega is the rotation
frequency.

This combined symmetry means the Spiral Sheet is approximately
self-similar: a magnified view of the outer revolutions looks like
the inner revolutions (in the linear regime). The self-similarity
breaks down near the nonlinear regime (large amplitudes) where
mobility modulation and penalty saturation distort the spiral.

The Spiral Sheet's symmetry group is the one-parameter group of
log-spiral transformations, parameterized by the rotation angle.
This is a continuous symmetry that reflects the phase-space flow's
rotational character.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MONOTONIC CONE: CONICAL SCALING SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Monotonic Cone has an approximate conical scaling symmetry: it
is approximately invariant under radial scaling from the Attractor
Point. Trajectories at different initial amplitudes follow
geometrically similar paths (the same cone with different radii).

The conical scaling is exact in the linear regime and breaks down
at large amplitudes where the mobility's density dependence creates
curvature in the cone's surface. At (D=1, zeta=1), the curvature
is only 10.6 (ED-Phys-32), confirming near-perfect conical symmetry.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
HORIZON SURFACE: REFLECTIVE SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Horizon Surface has a reflective symmetry in the dynamical
sense: trajectories approaching the surface are reflected back. The
reflection is not geometrically specular (the angle of incidence
does not equal the angle of reflection in phase space), but it
possesses a topological reflection symmetry: every trajectory that
reaches the Horizon returns from it. No trajectory crosses through.

The Horizon Surface is the only geometric object with reflective
symmetry. All other objects are either traversed (Manifold, Spiral
Sheet, Monotonic Cone) or act as boundaries in parameter space
(Boundary Surface).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
BOUNDARY SURFACE: PLANAR LINEAR SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Boundary Surface D + 2*zeta = 1 is a plane (or line in 2D
parameter space) with the full symmetry of a linear subspace:
invariant under translations along the plane and under reflections
within the plane.

The planar symmetry is exact, not approximate. The Boundary does
not curve, warp, or deform under any tested conditions. Its linearity
is a structural consequence of the damping tensor's linear dependence
on D and zeta.

The Boundary Surface is the most symmetric geometric object in the
architecture: it has the maximal symmetry possible for a codimension-1
object in a linear space.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MODAL FUNNEL: NESTED ELLIPSOIDAL SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Modal Funnel, viewed in modal amplitude space, has the symmetry
of nested, axis-aligned ellipsoids. At each time t, the accessible
region of modal space is an ellipsoid with semi-axes proportional to
exp(-k^2*lambda*t) along the k-th axis.

The ellipsoidal symmetry is the symmetry of the linearized dynamics:
each mode decays independently at its own rate, creating ellipsoidal
contraction with different rates along different axes. The nonlinear
Triad coupling slightly distorts the ellipsoids by introducing
correlations between the k=1 and k=3 axes, but the distortion is
small.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRIAD LOCUS: FLANK-REFLECTION SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Triad Locus, located on the flanks of density peaks, has an
approximate reflection symmetry about the peak center: the
M'|nabla(rho)|^2 coupling has similar magnitude on both flanks
(left and right) of a symmetric peak.

This flank-reflection symmetry is a direct consequence of the
Saddle's sign-inversion symmetry (nabla(rho) is antisymmetric,
but |nabla(rho)|^2 is symmetric). The Triad's activity is
symmetric about the Saddle point, even though the gradient itself
is antisymmetric.


========================================================================
6. SYMMETRY BREAKING
========================================================================

The ED architecture is not a symmetric system. It is a symmetry-
breaking system: it starts with high symmetry (the initial condition
may have many symmetries) and systematically breaks symmetry as it
evolves toward the unique, maximally symmetric equilibrium.

This statement requires careful parsing: the equilibrium is maximally
symmetric (rho = rho* everywhere, v = 0 everywhere, with full
translational, rotational, and reflective symmetry). But the process
of reaching equilibrium involves breaking the symmetries of the
transient states. The architecture breaks the symmetries of the
non-equilibrium dynamics while preserving the symmetries of the
equilibrium state.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 DISSIPATION BREAKS TIME-REVERSAL SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The three dissipation channels (D, zeta, P) are all irreversible.
The diffusion operator smooths gradients (it cannot sharpen them).
The penalty pulls density toward rho* (it cannot push it away). The
damping term drains v (it cannot amplify it). None of these
operations can be inverted by the dynamics.

The time-reversal asymmetry is absolute: there is no parameter
regime, no initial condition, and no perturbation class that makes
the dynamics reversible. The architecture is fundamentally
irreversible.

This is the most important symmetry breaking in the architecture. It
is the source of the energetic arrow (ED-Arch-06), the causal arrow
(ED-Arch-07), and the information arrow (ED-Arch-08). All three
arrows point in the direction of broken time-reversal symmetry.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.2 PENALTY BREAKS TRANSLATIONAL SYMMETRY IN DENSITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Without the penalty, the diffusion equation d(rho)/dt = div(M*nabla(rho))
would be invariant under the transformation rho -> rho + c for any
constant c (because nabla(rho + c) = nabla(rho)). The penalty
P_SY2(rho) breaks this translational symmetry by singling out
rho = rho* as the preferred density value.

The symmetry breaking creates the Attractor Point: without the
penalty, the system would relax to any homogeneous state (whatever
the initial mean density is). With the penalty, it relaxes to a
specific homogeneous state (rho = rho*). The penalty converts a
continuous family of equilibria into a unique equilibrium.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.3 MOBILITY COLLAPSE BREAKS AMPLITUDE SCALING SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The amplitude scaling symmetry (Section 2.1) holds for small
deviations from rho* but breaks when rho approaches rho_max. The
mobility function M(rho) = M0*(1 - rho/rho_max)^2 introduces a
characteristic density scale (rho_max) that distinguishes large
amplitudes from small ones.

The symmetry breaking creates the Horizon: without the mobility
collapse, there would be no upper bound on density, no kinetic
barrier, and no saturation. With the mobility collapse, high-
amplitude states are qualitatively different from low-amplitude
states (they encounter the Horizon).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.4 PARTICIPATION DAMPING BREAKS OSCILLATORY SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

In the limit zeta -> 0, the participation field v would be an
undamped integrator of F[rho], and the coupled system would oscillate
perpetually. The damping term zeta*v breaks this perpetual
oscillatory symmetry by introducing a preferred direction (decay)
into the v-dynamics.

The symmetry breaking creates the Critical Damping Boundary: without
damping, there would be no boundary — the system would always spiral.
With damping, the spiral regime has a finite extent in parameter
space, bounded by the D + 2*zeta = 1 surface.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.5 MODAL HIERARCHY BREAKS SPECTRAL PERMUTATION SYMMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

If all Fourier modes decayed at the same rate, the modes would be
interchangeable (permutation symmetric). The k^2 decay hierarchy
breaks this permutation symmetry by assigning each mode a unique
decay rate. The k=1 mode is distinguished (slowest), the k=2 mode
is next, and so on.

The symmetry breaking creates the Modal Funnel: without the spectral
hierarchy, there would be no preferential dimensional reduction (all
modes would persist equally). With the hierarchy, high-k modes are
selectively erased, creating the funnel geometry and the 1D manifold.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.6 ED AS A SYMMETRY-BREAKING ARCHITECTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The pattern of symmetry breaking defines the ED architecture's
character:

  DISSIPATION  breaks  TIME-REVERSAL  ->  creates the ARROWS
  PENALTY      breaks  DENSITY TRANSLATION  ->  creates the ATTRACTOR
  MOBILITY     breaks  AMPLITUDE SCALING  ->  creates the HORIZON
  DAMPING      breaks  OSCILLATORY PERSISTENCE  ->  creates the BOUNDARY
  LAPLACIAN    breaks  SPECTRAL PERMUTATION  ->  creates the HIERARCHY

Each symmetry breaking removes a freedom and introduces a constraint.
The cumulative effect of all five symmetry breakings is the full
architectural structure: a convergent, directed, bounded, spectrally
ordered, topologically partitioned dynamical system.

The architecture is thus defined by what it breaks as much as by what
it preserves. The preserved symmetries (spatial translation,
multiplicative invariance, spectral phase invariance, temporal
reparameterization) define what the architecture IS. The broken
symmetries (time-reversal, density translation, amplitude scaling,
oscillatory persistence, spectral permutation) define what the
architecture DOES.


========================================================================
7. EQUIVALENCE CLASSES
========================================================================

The symmetries of the ED architecture define equivalence classes:
sets of states, parameters, or PDE instances that are architecturally
indistinguishable under the relevant symmetry.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 MULTIPLICATIVE-EQUIVALENT PDES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: Two PDE instances are multiplicative-equivalent if one
can be obtained from the other by multiplicative rescaling of M, P,
and the v-coupling.

Members: all PDE instances with the same zero structure of M (vanishing
at rho_max), the same zero structure of P (vanishing at rho*), and
the same coupling topology (F drives v, v feeds back to rho).

Invariant properties: the full motif vocabulary, the full law
structure, the geometric topology, the causal graph, the information
processing pipeline.

Size: infinite (any smooth positive function g_M, g_P, g_v defines
a new member).

Significance: this is the architecture's defining equivalence class.
It contains every PDE instance that "is ED" in the architectural
sense. The canonical PDE (with specific M0, alpha, gamma, rho_max,
rho*, rho_0) is one representative. Any other representative has
the same architecture.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.2 IC-EQUIVALENT STATES (SAME MANIFOLD COORDINATE)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: Two initial conditions are IC-equivalent if they produce
the same point on the 1D manifold after the Modal Funnel has
collapsed the higher modes.

Members: all initial conditions with the same k=1 mode projection
(integral of rho(x,0) * sin(pi*x/L) dx) and the same energy
(V_P + V_M).

Invariant properties: the late-time trajectory, the convergence
rate, the cycle count, the flow type.

Size: very large. At each manifold point, the set of IC-equivalent
states includes all density profiles with the same k=1 projection
but different high-k content. For N_x = 128, this is approximately
a 63-dimensional family.

Significance: this class explains why diverse initial conditions
produce similar dynamics. ED-Phys-29 confirms that 22 IC types
converge to the same manifold with similar convergence rates.

Evidence: convergence rate std/mean = 13% at D=0, 25% at D=0.5,
5.7% at D=1.0 (ED-Phys-29). The residual variation reflects
differences in manifold entry point (early-time effects of high-k
content), not in late-time dynamics.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.3 SPECTRALLY-EQUIVALENT STATES (SAME k=1 AMPLITUDE)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: Two field states at time t are spectrally equivalent if
they have the same k=1 amplitude and are both on the 1D manifold.

Members: at any given manifold point, there is exactly one spectrally-
equivalent state (the manifold is 1D, so the k=1 amplitude uniquely
determines the position). This is a trivial equivalence class (each
class has one member), confirming that k=1 is a complete coordinate
on the manifold.

Significance: spectral equivalence confirms the Law of One-Dimensional
Relaxation: on the manifold, one number (the k=1 amplitude) contains
all the system's dynamical information. Two states with the same k=1
amplitude are on the same manifold point and have identical future
evolution.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.4 ENERGETICALLY-EQUIVALENT STATES (SAME TOTAL ENERGY E)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: Two field states are energetically equivalent if they
have the same total architectural energy E = V_P + V_M + V_v.

Members: at each energy level E, the set of energetically-equivalent
states is a level set (iso-energy surface) in the state space. This
is a high-dimensional surface (approximately N/2 - 1 dimensional at
early times, approximately 1-dimensional at late times when the
system is on the manifold).

Invariant properties: the total energy. NOT the trajectory, the
mode content, or the flow type (two states with the same energy can
have very different spatial structures).

Significance: energetic equivalence is a coarse classification. It
groups states by their distance from equilibrium (measured by E) but
does not distinguish between different spatial configurations at
the same distance. The iso-energy surfaces nest inside each other
(E = 0 is the Attractor Point, E > 0 are increasingly large surfaces
surrounding it), creating a foliation of state space by energy level.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.5 CAUSALLY-EQUIVALENT STATES (SAME INFLUENCE PATHWAYS)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: Two states are causally equivalent if they follow the
same causal pathway type (spiral or monotonic) and pass through the
same sequence of motifs.

Members: all states in the spiral regime (D + 2*zeta < 1) form one
causal equivalence class. All states in the monotonic regime
(D + 2*zeta > 1) form another. Within the spiral class, states that
encounter the Horizon form a subclass.

Invariant properties: the causal graph topology (cyclic vs acyclic),
the motif sequence, the dissipation channel priorities.

Size: two main classes (spiral and monotonic), with one subclass
(horizon-encountering within the spiral class).

Significance: causal equivalence is the coarsest meaningful
classification. It says: these states are processed through the
same architectural pipeline, regardless of their specific values.
The spiral and monotonic classes are the architecture's two
fundamental processing modes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.6 INFORMATIONALLY-EQUIVALENT STATES (SAME COMPRESSED REPRESENTATION)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: Two initial conditions are informationally equivalent if
they produce the same compressed representation on the 1D manifold
at the same energy level.

Members: this class coincides with the IC-equivalent class (Section
7.2) at late times. At early times, it is broader: two states are
informationally equivalent even if they have different high-k content,
provided their k=1 projections and energies are the same (because the
high-k content will be erased).

Invariant properties: the late-time trajectory, the manifold
coordinate, the total energy, the convergence rate, the cycle count.

Significance: informational equivalence is the architectural
definition of "same initial condition." Two ICs that are
informationally equivalent are, from the architecture's perspective,
the same input. The architecture cannot distinguish between them
because it erases the distinguishing information (the high-k modes)
before it matters for the late-time dynamics.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
SUMMARY: HOW EQUIVALENCE CLASSES PARTITION THE ARCHITECTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The six equivalence classes form a hierarchy from coarse to fine:

  COARSEST: Causal equivalence (2-3 classes: spiral, monotonic,
    horizon-spiral). Determined by parameter regime.

  INTERMEDIATE: Energetic equivalence (continuous family of iso-energy
    surfaces). Determined by total deviation from equilibrium.

  FINER: IC equivalence / Informational equivalence (large families
    sharing the same k=1 projection). Determined by the dominant
    mode's amplitude.

  FINEST: Spectral equivalence (single states on the manifold).
    Determined by the complete manifold coordinate.

  TRANSVERSE: Multiplicative equivalence (infinite families of PDE
    instances). Determined by the architecture's coupling topology.

The hierarchy reveals the architecture's information structure: at
the coarsest level, the architecture distinguishes only between
spiral and monotonic processing modes. At the finest level, it
distinguishes individual manifold positions. The progression from
coarse to fine corresponds to the progression from parameter-space
structure (coarse) to state-space structure (fine).

8. UNIFIED INTERPRETATION
========================================================================

WHAT THE ARCHITECTURE PRESERVES

The ED architecture preserves a small but significant set of
structural features:

  SPATIAL HOMOGENEITY: the rules of becoming are the same at every
  spatial point (translation symmetry).

  MULTIPLICATIVE EQUIVALENCE: the architecture is the same for all
  PDE instances in the multiplicative orbit (channel symmetry).

  SPECTRAL PHASE INDEPENDENCE: the architecture treats phases as
  irrelevant, caring only about amplitudes (phase symmetry).

  TEMPORAL RATIO INVARIANCE: the architecture depends on zeta/tau,
  not on zeta and tau independently (reparameterization symmetry).

  MANIFOLD DIMENSION: the late-time dynamics are always one-
  dimensional (dimensional reduction symmetry).

  HARMONIC RATIO: the k3/k1 ratio is always the same (Triad symmetry).

  ATTRACTOR UNIQUENESS: there is always exactly one equilibrium
  (contraction symmetry).

These preserved features ARE the architecture. They are what remains
invariant across all tested variations of parameters, initial
conditions, and functional forms. They are the irreducible identity
of the ED cosmology PDE.

WHAT THE ARCHITECTURE BREAKS

The ED architecture breaks five symmetries, and each breaking creates
a defining feature of the dynamics:

  TIME-REVERSAL       ->  the ARROWS (energetic, causal, informational)
  DENSITY TRANSLATION ->  the ATTRACTOR (unique equilibrium at rho*)
  AMPLITUDE SCALING   ->  the HORIZON (saturation boundary)
  OSCILLATORY FREEDOM ->  the BOUNDARY (spiral-monotonic transition)
  SPECTRAL PERMUTATION -> the HIERARCHY (ordered modal decay)

The broken symmetries generate the architecture's entire dynamical
repertoire. Without the breakings, the system would be an unstructured
diffusion with no preferred equilibrium, no saturation, no boundary,
and no spectral ordering. The breakings ARE the structure.

WHY ED DYNAMICS SIMPLIFY

The symmetry perspective provides the deepest explanation for why
ED dynamics always move from complex to simple. The initial state
may have low symmetry (a complicated density profile with many modes
and no spatial symmetry). The equilibrium state has high symmetry
(spatially uniform, temporally static, maximally symmetric).

The dynamics move from low symmetry to high symmetry because the
architecture's broken symmetries (dissipation, penalty, mobility,
damping, hierarchy) all act to RESTORE symmetry:

  Dissipation restores time-reversal symmetry locally (by making
  the system approach a static state where time direction is
  irrelevant).

  The penalty restores density translational symmetry (by making
  all points approach the same density rho*).

  Mobility collapse restores amplitude scaling symmetry at
  equilibrium (by making the equilibrium state independent of
  the initial amplitude).

  Damping restores oscillatory symmetry (by making v = 0, where
  the distinction between oscillatory and non-oscillatory is moot).

  The hierarchy restores spectral permutation symmetry (by making
  all mode amplitudes zero, where the ordering is trivial).

The architecture BREAKS symmetry in its transient dynamics and
RESTORES symmetry at its equilibrium. The process of becoming is
the process of symmetry restoration: the system moves from the
low-symmetry initial state to the high-symmetry equilibrium by
breaking the symmetries of the non-equilibrium dynamics.

HOW SYMMETRY ORGANIZES THE ENTIRE ONTOLOGY

The symmetry perspective unifies all previous chapters:

  MOTIFS are the characteristic patterns of broken symmetry. The
  Spiral exists because oscillatory symmetry is only partially
  broken (zeta is finite but subcritical). The Horizon exists
  because amplitude scaling symmetry is broken at rho_max. The
  Manifold exists because spectral permutation symmetry is broken
  by the k^2 hierarchy.

  LAWS are the invariant constraints under preserved symmetries.
  Unique Attraction is the invariance of the equilibrium under all
  transformations. Proportional Stability is the invariance of the
  architecture under multiplicative transformations. Harmonic
  Protection is the invariance of the Triad ratio.

  GEOMETRY is the shape of the symmetry-breaking pattern. The
  Spiral Sheet is the geometric form of partially broken oscillatory
  symmetry. The Boundary Surface is the geometric locus of the
  symmetry-breaking transition. The Modal Funnel is the geometric
  shape of the spectral hierarchy's symmetry breaking.

  FIELD THEORY is the dynamics of symmetry restoration. The field
  evolves from low symmetry toward high symmetry through the
  interplay of broken and preserved symmetries.

  ENERGETICS is the cost of asymmetry. The architectural energy
  E[rho, v] measures how far the system is from maximal symmetry.
  Dissipation reduces E, restoring symmetry.

  CAUSALITY is the pathway of symmetry restoration. The causal
  channels carry the system from broken symmetry (non-equilibrium)
  to restored symmetry (equilibrium).

  INFORMATION is the measure of asymmetry. Architectural information
  quantifies the structural content (asymmetry) of the field state.
  Information erasure is symmetry restoration.

Symmetry is therefore not just another perspective on the
architecture. It is the organizing principle that connects all
perspectives. The architecture's identity (what it preserves),
its dynamics (what it breaks), and its fate (what it restores)
are all symmetry statements. The ED cosmology PDE, viewed through
the symmetry lens, is a system that takes asymmetric inputs,
processes them through a sequence of symmetry-breaking motifs, and
produces a maximally symmetric output, governed by invariant laws
and constrained to a specific geometric and causal topology.

The nine ED-Arch documents form a complete structural description:

  ED-Arch-01 (Summary):       WHAT the architecture does.
  ED-Arch-02 (Motifs):        WHAT PATTERNS recur.
  ED-Arch-03 (Laws):          WHAT RULES govern.
  ED-Arch-04 (Geometry):      WHAT SHAPES result.
  ED-Arch-05 (Field Theory):  HOW the field is structured.
  ED-Arch-06 (Energetics):    WHY the field evolves as it does.
  ED-Arch-07 (Causality):     THROUGH WHAT PATHWAYS influence flows.
  ED-Arch-08 (Information):   WHAT HAPPENS TO STRUCTURE as it flows.
  ED-Arch-09 (Symmetry):      WHAT IS PRESERVED AND WHAT IS BROKEN.

Symmetry completes the foundational layer by providing the
meta-structural principle that organizes all others. The architecture
is defined by its symmetries. Its dynamics are the interplay of
preservation and breaking. And becoming — the ED ontology's central
concept — is the process of a system moving from broken symmetry
to restored symmetry, through motifs that are the characteristic
patterns of that restoration.
