1. PURPOSE OF ARCHITECTURAL FIELD THEORY
========================================================================

The first four chapters of the ED-Arch series decomposed the unified
ED cosmology PDE into its structural components: motifs (the recurring
patterns), laws (the invariant constraints), and geometry (the shapes
in state space). This fifth chapter reassembles them into a single
field-theoretic description: a continuous, law-governed structure that
assigns dynamical content to every point in state space simultaneously.

An architectural field theory, in the ED context, is:

  (i)   A CONTINUOUS RULE assigning structure to every point.
        At every spatial point x and every instant t, the field theory
        specifies the local density, the local velocity, the local
        operator output, the local mobility, the local curvature, the
        local restoring force, and the local memory state. Nothing is
        left unspecified. The field theory is a complete local
        description.

  (ii)  A UNIFICATION of laws, motifs, and geometry.
        The laws are the field's global constraints. The motifs are the
        field's characteristic local patterns. The geometry is the
        field's shape in state space. The field theory contains all
        three, not as separate layers but as aspects of a single
        object.

  (iii) A GENERATIVE DESCRIPTION of how ED evolves.
        The field theory does not merely classify the architecture's
        behavior. It generates that behavior. Given the field state at
        time t, the field theory determines the field state at time
        t + dt, everywhere, simultaneously. It is the architecture's
        engine.

FIELD THEORY VS. PDE MECHANICS

The unified ED cosmology PDE is a set of coupled partial differential
equations. The Architectural Field Theory is a reinterpretation of
those equations as a field structure. The distinction is one of
perspective:

  PDE MECHANICS asks: given these equations and these initial
  conditions, what is the solution?

  FIELD THEORY asks: what is the continuous structure that the
  equations define at every point, and how does that structure
  determine the system's behavior?

The PDE is a computation. The field theory is an understanding. Both
describe the same system, but the field theory reveals the
architecture that the PDE implements.

2. THE ARCHITECTURAL FIELD
========================================================================

The ED architecture is built on three coupled fields. Together, they
form the architectural field: a composite object that encodes the
complete dynamical state and the complete dynamical rule at every
point in space and time.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 THE DENSITY FIELD rho(x,t)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The density field is the primary state variable. It assigns a non-
negative real number to every spatial point x at every time t. The
density field is the architecture's substance: it is what evolves,
what has structure, and what the other fields act upon.

The density field carries several local quantities:

  LOCAL VALUE:     rho(x,t) — the density at point x.
  LOCAL GRADIENT:  nabla(rho) — the rate of spatial change.
  LOCAL CURVATURE: nabla^2(rho) — the concavity of the profile.

These three quantities (value, gradient, curvature) are the density
field's local geometry. They determine how every other component of
the architecture acts at that point.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2 THE PARTICIPATION FIELD v(x,t)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The participation field is the secondary state variable. It records
the integrated history of the operator's output at each spatial point,
filtered through the damping coefficient zeta and the time scale tau.
The participation field is the architecture's memory: it retains
information about past forcing and feeds it back into the density
evolution.

The participation field evolves according to:

  dv/dt = (1/tau) * (F[rho] - zeta * v)

This equation says: v integrates F[rho] with exponential decay at
rate zeta/tau. The participation field is a leaky integrator of the
operator field. Its local value at any point represents a damped,
time-averaged summary of what the operator has been doing at that
point.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3 THE OPERATOR FIELD F[rho]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The operator field is not an independent state variable. It is a
derived field: a functional of the density field that encodes the
architecture's local update rule. At every point x, the operator
field combines three contributions:

  F[rho](x) = M(rho) * nabla^2(rho)     [curvature-driven diffusion]
             + M'(rho) * |nabla(rho)|^2  [nonlinear gradient coupling]
             - P_SY2(rho)                [penalty restoring force]

The operator field is the architecture's engine: it computes, at every
point, the net forcing that acts on both the density (through the D
channel) and the participation (through the v-equation). It is the
single object through which all architectural laws, motifs, and
geometry are expressed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.4 THE COUPLED ARCHITECTURAL FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The three fields form a coupled system:

  drho/dt = D * F[rho] + H * v
  dv/dt   = (1/tau) * (F[rho] - zeta * v)

with D + H = 1.

The coupling structure is:

  rho  -->  F[rho]  -->  v  -->  rho
  (state)  (operator)  (memory)  (state)

This is the Memory Loop motif expressed as a field coupling. The
density field determines the operator field (through M, nabla, and P).
The operator field drives the participation field (through the
v-equation). The participation field modifies the density field
(through the H*v term). The loop closes.

The architectural field is the composite object (rho, v, F[rho])
considered as a single entity. It is defined at every spatial point
and evolves according to the coupled equations. The field is not merely
three separate fields that happen to interact. It is a single
dynamical structure with three aspects: substance (rho), memory (v),
and rule (F).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.5 THE FIELD'S LOCAL ENCODING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

At every point x, the architectural field encodes four local quantities
that determine the architecture's behavior at that point:

  LOCAL CURVATURE: nabla^2(rho).
    The concavity of the density profile. Positive curvature (troughs)
    attracts density. Negative curvature (peaks) repels density. This
    is the diffusion channel's local driving force.

  LOCAL MOBILITY: M(rho) = M0 * (1 - rho/rho_max)^2.
    The density-dependent diffusion coefficient. High mobility (low
    density) permits rapid redistribution. Low mobility (high density)
    restricts transport. This is the field's local kinetic capacity.

  LOCAL RESTORING FORCE: P_SY2(rho).
    The penalty function that pulls density toward rho*. Positive for
    rho > rho* (pushes density down), negative for rho < rho* (pushes
    density up). Zero at equilibrium. This is the field's local
    regulatory mechanism.

  LOCAL MEMORY: v(x,t).
    The integrated operator history, damped by zeta. Positive v
    indicates recent positive forcing. Negative v indicates recent
    negative forcing. This is the field's local temporal context.

These four quantities — curvature, mobility, restoring force, and
memory — are the architectural field's complete local content. Every
motif, every law, and every geometric object can be expressed in
terms of these four local encodings and their interactions.

3. FIELD-LAW CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each architectural law from ED-Arch-03 has a precise expression in
the language of the architectural field. The laws are not external
constraints imposed on the field; they are properties of the field
itself.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(a) LAW OF UNIQUE ATTRACTION -> GLOBAL POTENTIAL FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Field-theoretic expression:
  The operator field F[rho] has the structure of a gradient flow with
  respect to a global potential. Define the spatial-mean density
  rho_bar = (1/L) * integral(rho dx). The penalty P_SY2 acts as a
  restoring force on rho_bar, pulling it toward rho*. The diffusion
  terms redistribute density without changing rho_bar (they are
  conservative in the spatial mean). The combined effect is a
  potential landscape in the space of density profiles with a single
  minimum at the homogeneous state rho = rho* everywhere.

  The potential is not a simple function of a single variable. It is
  a functional of the entire density profile:

    V[rho] = integral [ Phi(rho(x)) + (1/2) * M(rho) * |nabla(rho)|^2 ] dx

  where Phi(rho) is the antiderivative of P_SY2(rho). This functional
  has a unique minimizer: the homogeneous profile rho(x) = rho* for
  all x, at which V = 0. All other profiles have V > 0.

  The participation field v does not create new minima. It introduces
  oscillatory approach to the minimum (through the Memory Loop) but
  cannot create secondary minima, because v itself decays to zero at
  the density equilibrium.

  The field-theoretic statement of Unique Attraction is: the
  architectural field possesses a global Lyapunov-like functional
  with a unique zero, and the field dynamics decrease this functional
  at every step.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(b) LAW OF ONE-DIMENSIONAL RELAXATION -> DOMINANT EIGENFIELD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Field-theoretic expression:
  Linearize the operator field F[rho] around the equilibrium. The
  linearized operator has a spectrum of eigenfields (spatial modes)
  with eigenvalues (decay rates). The eigenfields are approximately
  the Fourier modes sin(k*pi*x/L), and the eigenvalues are
  approximately -k^2 * M(rho*) * (pi/L)^2 - alpha*gamma/rho_0
  (combining diffusion and penalty contributions).

  The dominant eigenfield is the k = 1 mode: the slowest-decaying
  spatial pattern. Its eigenvalue lambda_1 sets the system's
  fundamental relaxation rate. All other eigenfields have eigenvalues
  lambda_k with |lambda_k| >= 4 * |lambda_1| (since k^2 >= 4 for
  k >= 2).

  The field-theoretic statement of One-Dimensional Relaxation is:
  the linearized operator field has a spectral gap of at least
  factor 4 between its dominant eigenvalue and the next. This gap
  ensures that the dominant eigenfield controls the late-time
  evolution, and all other eigenfields are enslaved to it.

  The dominant eigenfield, combined with its associated participation
  component, defines a two-variable reduced system. The Manifold
  Curve in phase space is the trajectory of this reduced system. The
  Law of One-Dimensional Relaxation is the statement that the
  dominant eigenfield pair captures the full dynamics after transient
  decay.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(c) LAW OF PROPORTIONAL STABILITY -> MULTIPLICATIVE INVARIANCE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Field-theoretic expression:
  Consider the architectural field as a tensor-like object with
  components: the mobility tensor M(rho), the penalty field P(rho),
  and the participation coupling. A multiplicative perturbation acts
  as a conformal rescaling of these components:

    M(rho) -> M(rho) * g(rho)
    P(rho) -> P(rho) * h(rho)
    v_coupling -> v_coupling * f(rho)

  where g, h, f are smooth positive functions.

  Under such a rescaling:
  - The zero structure of M is preserved (M vanishes where it did
    before, because g > 0 does not create new zeros).
  - The zero structure of P is preserved (P vanishes at rho* because
    h(rho*) * 0 = 0).
  - The coupling topology is preserved (v still feeds back to rho,
    F still drives v).

  The field-theoretic statement of Proportional Stability is: the
  architectural field's qualitative structure is invariant under
  positive conformal rescaling of its component tensors. This defines
  a gauge-like equivalence class of field theories that share the
  same architecture.

  Additive perturbations (adding a source term s(rho) to the
  v-equation) break this invariance because they introduce a field
  component that is not modulated by the existing coupling structure.
  The source term s(rho) acts regardless of the system's state,
  injecting energy into the Memory Loop from outside the
  architectural field.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(d) LAW OF CRITICAL DAMPING BOUNDARY -> DAMPING TENSOR
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Field-theoretic expression:
  Define the damping tensor of the architectural field as the
  2x2 matrix governing the linearized (rho_peak, v_peak) dynamics:

    d/dt [delta_rho]   [ -D*lambda_1    H         ] [delta_rho]
         [delta_v  ] = [ lambda_1/tau   -zeta/tau  ] [delta_v  ]

  where lambda_1 is the dominant eigenvalue of the spatial operator
  and delta_rho, delta_v are deviations from equilibrium projected
  onto the dominant eigenfield.

  The eigenvalues of this damping tensor determine the flow type:
  - Complex eigenvalues: spiral (oscillatory decay).
  - Real eigenvalues: node (monotonic decay).

  The discriminant of the characteristic polynomial is:

    Delta = (D*lambda_1 - zeta/tau)^2 - 4*(D*lambda_1*zeta/tau
            - H*lambda_1/tau)

  Setting Delta = 0 and using H = 1 - D gives the critical damping
  condition. For the canonical parameter scaling, this simplifies to
  the observed boundary D + 2*zeta approximately equal to 1.

  The field-theoretic statement of the Critical Damping Boundary is:
  the damping tensor's discriminant vanishes on a codimension-1
  surface in parameter space, separating complex-eigenvalue (spiral)
  from real-eigenvalue (monotonic) dynamics. The boundary is a
  property of the field's linearized damping structure, not of any
  particular trajectory.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(e) LAW OF FINITE CAPACITY -> MOBILITY FIELD COLLAPSE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Field-theoretic expression:
  The mobility field M(rho(x,t)) is a non-negative scalar field
  defined at every spatial point. It has the property:

    M(rho) -> 0 as rho -> rho_max

  The mobility field's zero set {x : M(rho(x,t)) approximately 0}
  defines the Horizon Surface in physical space. At any point in this
  zero set, the diffusive component of the operator field vanishes:

    M * nabla^2(rho) = 0  (because M = 0)
    M' * |nabla(rho)|^2 -> 0  (because M' remains finite but the
    dynamics driving rho higher require M > 0)

  The field-theoretic statement of Finite Capacity is: the mobility
  field possesses a zero set at the boundary of the physical state
  space, and the operator field degenerates on this zero set. The
  penalty field P_SY2 does not degenerate (it depends on rho, not
  on M), so the restoring force remains active at the mobility
  boundary, guaranteeing reflection.

  The mobility field collapse is the field-theoretic mechanism of
  saturation. Where M vanishes, the field cannot redistribute
  density through diffusion. Transport ceases locally. The penalty
  provides the only remaining dynamical channel, and it always
  points inward.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(f) LAW OF HARMONIC PROTECTION -> NONLINEAR COUPLING TENSOR
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Field-theoretic expression:
  The operator field F[rho] contains a nonlinear coupling term
  M'(rho) * |nabla(rho)|^2. This term can be expressed as a
  bilinear form on the gradient field:

    N(rho, nabla(rho)) = M'(rho) * <nabla(rho), nabla(rho)>

  where <.,.> is the spatial inner product. This is a rank-2
  tensor contracted against the gradient field twice.

  In Fourier space, the contraction <nabla(rho), nabla(rho)>
  produces mode-mode coupling:

    |nabla(rho)|^2 = sum_{k,j} k*j * a_k * a_j * cos((k-j)*x)

  The dominant coupling at late times is between the k=1 mode and
  itself, producing k=0 and k=2 content. When this is multiplied
  by M'(rho), which itself has Fourier content from the density
  field, the result includes k=3 generation.

  The field-theoretic statement of Harmonic Protection is: the
  nonlinear coupling tensor's selection rules are determined by
  the algebraic structure of the bilinear form, not by the specific
  functional form of M or P. Any admissible mobility function
  (smooth, positive on the interior, vanishing at rho_max) produces
  the same harmonic selection rule. The k=3 amplitude is a
  topological invariant of the coupling tensor.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(g) LAW OF MODAL HIERARCHY -> SPECTRAL FIELD STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Field-theoretic expression:
  The linearized operator field can be decomposed into a sum of
  eigenfield contributions:

    F_lin[rho] = sum_k lambda_k * a_k * phi_k(x)

  where phi_k are the spatial eigenfields (approximately Fourier
  modes), a_k are the mode amplitudes, and lambda_k are the
  eigenvalues satisfying |lambda_k| proportional to k^2.

  The spectral field structure is the distribution of eigenvalues
  in the complex plane. For the ED architecture:
  - All eigenvalues have negative real part (all modes decay).
  - The decay rates scale as k^2 (quadratic spectral scaling).
  - The spectral gap between k=1 and k=2 is a factor of 4.

  The field-theoretic statement of Modal Hierarchy is: the
  operator field's spectral decomposition has a quadratic
  eigenvalue scaling with a large spectral gap, ensuring rapid
  dimensional reduction and dominance of the fundamental
  eigenfield.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(h) LAW OF TRANSIENT STRUCTURE -> FIELD DISSIPATION OPERATOR
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Field-theoretic expression:
  Define the total field energy as:

    E(t) = integral [ (rho - rho*)^2 + tau * v^2 ] dx

  This quantity measures the total deviation from equilibrium in
  both the density and participation fields.

  The time derivative of E is:

    dE/dt = 2 * integral [ (rho - rho*) * drho/dt
                          + tau * v * dv/dt ] dx

  Substituting the evolution equations and using the structure of
  F[rho]:

    dE/dt = 2 * integral [ (rho - rho*) * (D*F + H*v)
                          + v * (F - zeta*v) ] dx

  The diffusion terms contribute non-positively (they reduce
  gradients). The penalty terms contribute non-positively (they
  reduce density deviations). The damping term -zeta*v^2 contributes
  strictly negatively wherever v is nonzero.

  The field-theoretic statement of Transient Structure is: the
  architectural field possesses a dissipation operator that
  strictly decreases the total field energy at every time step.
  The field energy is a Lyapunov-like functional: E >= 0 everywhere,
  E = 0 only at equilibrium, and dE/dt <= 0 always. This guarantees
  that all field structure is transient and all trajectories converge.

4. FIELD-MOTIF CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each motif from ED-Arch-02 is a characteristic pattern of the
architectural field. The field theory provides a precise specification
of each motif as a local or global field configuration.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SADDLE: SIGN STRUCTURE OF THE DIFFERENTIAL FIELDS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Saddle motif occurs wherever the gradient field nabla(rho) changes
sign and the curvature field nabla^2(rho) is nonzero. At a density
peak:

  nabla(rho) passes through zero (gradient reversal)
  nabla^2(rho) < 0 (negative curvature, concave down)

The operator field F[rho] at a saddle point receives competing
contributions: the curvature term M*nabla^2(rho) < 0 (diffusion
pushes density away from the peak) while the penalty P_SY2 may be
positive or negative depending on whether rho > rho* or rho < rho*.
The saddle is the field-theoretic locus where the diffusive and
regulatory components of the operator field compete.

The nonlinear term M'*|nabla(rho)|^2 vanishes exactly at the
saddle point (where nabla(rho) = 0) but is maximal on either side.
The saddle is therefore a zero of the nonlinear field flanked by
maxima — the characteristic geometry of a saddle in the operator
field.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SPIRAL: UNDERDAMPED EIGENFIELD PAIR
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Spiral motif arises when the damping tensor (Section 3d) has
complex eigenvalues. In field-theoretic terms, the dominant eigenfield
pair consists of two modes with complex conjugate decay rates:

  lambda = -sigma +/- i*omega

where sigma > 0 is the decay rate and omega > 0 is the oscillation
frequency. The ratio omega/sigma determines the spiral's tightness.

The eigenfield pair generates a rotating flow in the (rho_peak,
v_peak) plane. The density component and the velocity component
oscillate 90 degrees out of phase, creating the characteristic
spiral winding. The field-theoretic content of the spiral is: the
dominant eigenfield pair of the linearized damping tensor is complex,
and the field evolves by rotation combined with contraction in the
plane spanned by these eigenfields.

The spiral's curvature (up to 505,631 in ED-Phys-32) measures the
eigenfield pair's rotation rate relative to its contraction rate.
High curvature means many rotations per e-folding of amplitude decay.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE HORIZON: MOBILITY FIELD ZERO SET
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Horizon motif is the physical manifestation of the mobility
field's zero set. In field-theoretic terms, define:

  Z(t) = { x : M(rho(x,t)) < epsilon }

for some small threshold epsilon. The set Z(t) is the Horizon at
time t: the spatial region where the mobility field has collapsed
and diffusive transport has ceased.

Inside Z(t):
  - The operator field's diffusive component vanishes: M*nabla^2 = 0.
  - The penalty field remains active: P_SY2(rho) > 0 (since rho
    is near rho_max, far above rho*).
  - The participation field continues to evolve (v is not coupled
    to M).

The Horizon is the region of the architectural field where the
diffusive channel has been silenced but the regulatory and
participatory channels remain active. It is a partial degeneration
of the field: one component shuts down while the others continue.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MANIFOLD: DOMINANT EIGENFIELD TRAJECTORY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Manifold motif is the trajectory of the dominant eigenfield
pair in the (rho_peak, v_peak) phase plane. In field terms, define
the dominant eigenfield projection:

  rho_dom(t) = <rho(x,t) - rho*, phi_1(x)>
  v_dom(t)   = <v(x,t), phi_1(x)>

where phi_1 is the k=1 eigenfield and <.,.> is the spatial inner
product. The Manifold Curve is the trajectory (rho_dom(t), v_dom(t))
in the two-dimensional reduced field space.

After the Modal Funnel has collapsed the higher modes, the full
field dynamics are captured by this projection. The Manifold is the
field's effective state after dimensional reduction: a one-parameter
curve parameterized by the dominant eigenfield amplitude, along
which the field slides toward equilibrium.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE BOUNDARY: DAMPING TENSOR CRITICAL SURFACE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Boundary motif is the surface in parameter space where the
damping tensor's eigenvalues transition from complex to real. In
field terms, it is the locus where:

  discriminant(damping tensor) = 0

This is a property of the field's linearized structure, not of any
particular field configuration. The Boundary exists in parameter
space, not in state space. It tells us where the field's dominant
eigenfield pair changes character from oscillatory to monotonic.

The field-theoretic content is: the architectural field has two
qualitatively different linearized behaviors (focus and node), and
the Boundary is the codimension-1 surface separating them.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MEMORY LOOP: CLOSED FIELD COUPLING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Memory Loop motif is the closed coupling cycle of the
architectural field:

  rho(x,t) --> F[rho](x,t) --> v(x,t) --> rho(x,t)

In field-theoretic terms, this is a functional feedback loop. The
density field determines the operator field at every point. The
operator field drives the participation field at every point. The
participation field modifies the density field at every point. The
loop operates simultaneously at all spatial locations.

The loop's field-theoretic content is:
  - Loop gain: proportional to H/tau at each point. When H = 0
    (D = 1), the loop is broken and the field is purely dissipative.
  - Loop damping: zeta/tau at each point. This is the rate at which
    the participation field forgets its history.
  - Loop delay: tau sets the time scale over which v integrates
    F[rho]. Larger tau means slower integration and weaker coupling.

The Memory Loop is the architectural field's self-referential
structure. It is what makes the field participatory rather than
merely diffusive.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE DISSIPATOR: PARABOLIC FIELD FLOW
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Dissipator motif corresponds to the D-channel of the
architectural field operating alone (H = 0, or more generally,
D dominating H). In field-theoretic terms, when D = 1:

  drho/dt = F[rho]

This is a pure nonlinear parabolic flow. The field evolves by
local diffusion and penalty regulation, without memory. The
operator field acts directly on the density field without passing
through the participation field.

The field-theoretic content of the Dissipator is: the architectural
field reduces to a gradient flow on the potential functional V[rho].
The flow is strictly downhill in V, monotonic, and non-oscillatory.
Every spatial mode decays independently (no coupling through v),
and the decay is exponential at rate k^2*lambda_eff.

The Dissipator is the architectural field's memoryless limit. It
retains all laws except the Memory Loop, which requires H > 0.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE NONLINEAR TRIAD: CUBIC FIELD INTERACTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Nonlinear Triad motif corresponds to the M'(rho)*|nabla(rho)|^2
term in the operator field. In field-theoretic terms, this is a
cubic interaction: it involves the product of one power of M'(rho)
(which depends on rho) and two powers of nabla(rho) (the squared
gradient). The interaction is:

  N[rho] = M'(rho) * nabla(rho) . nabla(rho)

This is a self-interaction of the density field: the field couples
to its own gradient through a density-dependent coefficient. The
cubic character (one rho-dependent factor times two gradient factors)
is what generates the k=3 harmonic from a k=1 input.

The field-theoretic content is: the operator field contains a cubic
self-interaction tensor whose contraction with the gradient field
produces specific harmonic output determined by the interaction's
algebraic structure. The output (k=3 from k=1) is a selection rule
of the tensor, analogous to selection rules in quantum field theory.


========================================================================
5. FIELD GEOMETRY
========================================================================

The architectural field can be decomposed into five component fields,
each with its own geometry. Their intersection produces the geometric
objects catalogued in ED-Arch-04.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 THE POTENTIAL LANDSCAPE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The potential functional V[rho] defines a landscape in the space of
density profiles. Every density profile rho(x) corresponds to a point
in this landscape, and V[rho] assigns a height to that point.

Shape:
  - Global minimum at rho(x) = rho* for all x: V = 0.
  - The landscape rises in all directions from this minimum.
  - Along the k=1 eigenfield direction, V rises quadratically
    (harmonic potential near the minimum).
  - Along higher eigenfield directions, V rises more steeply
    (steeper walls for higher modes).
  - The landscape is bounded below (V >= 0) and unbounded above.

The potential landscape's geometry determines the Attractor Point
(the minimum) and the Modal Funnel (the differential steepness in
different eigenfield directions). Trajectories roll downhill on
this landscape, with the Memory Loop adding inertia (through v)
that allows overshooting of the minimum.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.2 THE FLOW FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The flow field is the vector field in (rho_peak, v_peak) space
defined by the right-hand side of the reduced equations:

  drho_peak/dt = D * F_peak + H * v_peak
  dv_peak/dt   = (1/tau) * (F_peak - zeta * v_peak)

where F_peak is the operator field evaluated at the density peak.

Shape:
  Below the Critical Damping Boundary: the flow field spirals inward.
    Flow vectors have both radial (inward) and tangential (rotational)
    components. The tangential component creates the spiral. The
    radial component creates the contraction.
  Above the Critical Damping Boundary: the flow field points
    directly inward. No tangential component. Trajectories follow
    straight or nearly straight paths to the attractor.

The flow field's geometry produces the Spiral Sheet (the integral
curves below the boundary) and the Monotonic Cone (the integral
curves above the boundary). The Boundary Surface is the locus where
the tangential component of the flow field vanishes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.3 THE DAMPING FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The damping field is the scalar field defined at every point in
(D, zeta, tau) parameter space that quantifies the effective damping:

  Gamma(D, zeta) = D + 2*zeta

This is a linear field over the parameter space. Its level sets are
straight lines (or planes if tau is included).

Shape:
  - Gamma = 0 at the origin (D=0, zeta=0): zero damping, maximum
    oscillation.
  - Gamma = 1 along the Boundary Surface: critical damping.
  - Gamma > 1 above the Boundary Surface: overdamping.

The damping field's geometry is trivially simple (it is a linear
function), but its consequences are profound. The Boundary Surface
is a level set of the damping field. The spiral-monotonic transition
is controlled by whether the local value of the damping field
exceeds the critical threshold.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.4 THE MOBILITY FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The mobility field M(rho(x,t)) is a non-negative scalar field
defined at every spatial point. Unlike the damping field (which
lives in parameter space), the mobility field lives in physical
space and changes with the density configuration.

Shape:
  - Maximum at rho = 0: M = M0.
  - Decreases quadratically as rho increases.
  - Zero at rho = rho_max: M = 0.
  - At equilibrium (rho = rho*): M = M0 * (1 - rho*/rho_max)^2
    = M0 * (1 - 0.5)^2 = 0.25 * M0.

The mobility field's geometry creates the Horizon Surface (where
M collapses to near-zero) and modulates the strength of the
diffusion channel everywhere. The mobility field is the architecture's
local kinetic capacity: it determines how rapidly density can be
redistributed at each point.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.5 THE NONLINEAR INTERACTION FIELD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The nonlinear interaction field is:

  N(x,t) = M'(rho(x,t)) * |nabla(rho(x,t))|^2

This field is defined at every spatial point and quantifies the
local strength of the nonlinear mode coupling.

Shape:
  - Zero at density peaks and troughs (where nabla(rho) = 0).
  - Zero far from density features (where |nabla(rho)| is small).
  - Maximum on the flanks of density peaks, where both M' and
    |nabla(rho)|^2 are significant.
  - The field is always non-positive (since M' < 0 for rho < rho_max
    and |nabla(rho)|^2 >= 0).

The nonlinear interaction field's geometry defines the Triad Locus
(the spatial region where N is large in magnitude). It is a
transient field that shrinks as the density profile approaches
homogeneity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.6 FIELD INTERSECTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The five component fields intersect to produce the geometric objects:

SPIRAL SHEET = Flow Field (spiral region) restricted to the domain
  where Damping Field < 1. The sheet's winding is determined by the
  flow field's tangential component, which is itself determined by
  the Memory Loop coupling.

MONOTONIC CONE = Flow Field (nodal region) restricted to the domain
  where Damping Field > 1. The cone's opening angle is determined by
  the eigenvalue ratio of the damping tensor.

HORIZON SURFACE = Mobility Field zero set, intersected with the
  trajectory space. The horizon forms when trajectories enter the
  region where M < epsilon, and dissolves when the penalty pushes
  them back out.

BOUNDARY SURFACE = Damping Field level set at Gamma = 1. This is
  a purely parameter-space object, independent of the density
  configuration.

MODAL FUNNEL = Potential Landscape restricted to trajectories
  approaching the minimum. The funnel's shape is set by the
  differential steepness of V along different eigenfield directions.

TRIAD LOCUS = Nonlinear Interaction Field's region of significant
  magnitude, intersected with the Saddle geometry (where nabla(rho)
  changes sign nearby).

6. FIELD DYNAMICS
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 LOCAL UPDATE RULES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The architectural field evolves according to local rules. At every
spatial point x and every time t, the field update is determined
entirely by local quantities:

  drho(x,t)/dt = D * [M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) + M'(rho)*|nabla(rho)|^2
                       - P_SY2(rho)]  +  H * v(x,t)

  dv(x,t)/dt = (1/tau) * [M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) + M'(rho)*|nabla(rho)|^2
                            - P_SY2(rho)]  -  (zeta/tau) * v(x,t)

The locality of these rules is significant. The operator field F[rho]
depends only on rho, nabla(rho), and nabla^2(rho) at the point x.
These are local differential quantities. The penalty depends only on
rho(x). The memory depends only on v(x) and F[rho](x).

The architectural field is a local field theory: the rule at each
point depends only on the field's value and its spatial derivatives
at that point. There is no action at a distance. The boundary
conditions (periodic, via np.roll) provide the only spatial coupling,
and this coupling is local in the sense that each point communicates
only with its immediate neighbors through the gradient and Laplacian.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.2 GLOBAL CONSTRAINTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Despite the local update rules, the architectural field is subject
to global constraints that shape its behavior:

CONSERVATION OF MASS:
  The spatial integral of rho is not exactly conserved (the penalty
  breaks conservation by adding or removing density to drive toward
  rho*). However, the diffusion terms M*nabla^2(rho) and
  M'*|nabla(rho)|^2 are conservative: they redistribute density
  without changing the total. The penalty's effect on the mean is
  to pull it toward rho*, so the mean density approaches rho* from
  any initial value.

CHANNEL COMPLEMENTARITY:
  D + H = 1 at every point, at every time. This is a global algebraic
  constraint, not a dynamical one. It structures the field's update
  rule by fixing the total weight of the diffusive and participatory
  channels.

BOUNDED STATE SPACE:
  rho(x,t) is bounded in [0, rho_max] at every point. This is
  enforced dynamically by the mobility collapse at rho_max and by
  the penalty structure. The global constraint is that the field
  trajectory remains in a bounded subset of the state space at all
  times.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.3 ENERGY-LIKE QUANTITIES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The architectural field possesses several energy-like functionals
that characterize its state:

TOTAL FIELD ENERGY:
  E(t) = integral [ (rho - rho*)^2 + tau * v^2 ] dx

  This is the total deviation from equilibrium in both fields. It
  is non-negative, zero only at equilibrium, and monotonically
  decreasing under the field dynamics. It serves as a Lyapunov
  functional for the global convergence.

GRADIENT ENERGY:
  G(t) = integral [ |nabla(rho)|^2 ] dx

  This measures the total spatial structure in the density field.
  The diffusion terms decrease G (they smooth gradients). The
  participation channel can temporarily increase G (by driving
  density oscillations that create new gradients), but the net
  effect over a full oscillation cycle is to decrease G.

SPECTRAL ENERGY:
  S_k(t) = |a_k(t)|^2 for each mode k.

  The k-th spectral energy measures the amplitude of the k-th
  Fourier component. S_k decays at rate proportional to k^2 for
  the diffusion channel, modified by the participation coupling.
  The hierarchy S_1 > S_2 > S_3 > ... establishes quickly and
  persists.

PARTICIPATION ENERGY:
  P(t) = integral [ v^2 ] dx

  This measures the total stored memory in the participation field.
  It is generated by F[rho] (the operator drives v) and dissipated
  by the zeta*v damping. At equilibrium, P = 0. The participation
  energy is the field-theoretic measure of the Memory Loop's
  activation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.4 DISSIPATION STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The architectural field is strictly dissipative. Every energy-like
quantity decreases over time (at least on average over oscillation
cycles). The dissipation has three independent channels:

DIFFUSIVE DISSIPATION (rate proportional to D):
  The D*F[rho] term in the density equation directly dissipates
  spatial structure. Its effect is to smooth the density field
  toward homogeneity. The dissipation rate scales as D * k^2 for
  the k-th mode.

PENALTY DISSIPATION (rate proportional to alpha*gamma):
  The penalty P_SY2 dissipates density deviations from rho*. Its
  effect is to pull the mean density toward the equilibrium value.
  This acts on the k=0 (mean) mode and indirectly on all other
  modes through the nonlinear coupling.

PARTICIPATORY DISSIPATION (rate proportional to zeta/tau):
  The zeta*v term in the v-equation dissipates participation energy.
  Its effect is to drain the Memory Loop of stored history, reducing
  the feedback's influence over time.

The total dissipation is the sum of all three channels. The field
cannot sustain structure against this combined drain. This is the
field-theoretic proof of the Law of Transient Structure: three
independent dissipation channels, all active, all draining energy,
with no energy source.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.5 WHY ED DYNAMICS HAVE THEIR CHARACTER
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The field-theoretic description explains four fundamental properties
of ED dynamics:

GLOBALLY CONVERGENT: because the total field energy E(t) is a
  Lyapunov functional that decreases at every step. The potential
  landscape has a unique minimum, and the dissipation structure
  ensures downhill flow. There is no mechanism to create secondary
  minima, no energy source to sustain motion, and no topological
  obstruction to convergence.

LOCALLY NONLINEAR: because the operator field F[rho] contains the
  cubic interaction N[rho] = M'*|nabla(rho)|^2, which couples modes
  and generates harmonic content. The nonlinearity is local (it
  depends on the field at each point) and cubic (it involves three
  factors of the field or its derivatives). This is the minimum
  nonlinearity needed to produce the observed harmonic structure.

SPECTRALLY HIERARCHICAL: because the linearized operator field has
  eigenvalues scaling as k^2. This is a consequence of the diffusion
  operator's Laplacian structure. The spectral hierarchy is not
  imposed by hand; it is inherited from the mathematical structure
  of second-order spatial differentiation.

GEOMETRICALLY CONSTRAINED: because the mobility field creates a
  bounded state space, the damping field creates a topological
  partition, and the potential landscape creates a unique basin.
  The field's geometry is not free; it is determined by the
  component fields' shapes and their intersections.

HOW BECOMING IS ENCODED IN THE FIELD

The ED ontology asserts that reality is a process of becoming: density
fields evolve, structure forms and dissolves, and the rules of
becoming determine what can happen. The Architectural Field Theory
provides the precise encoding of this ontology.

Becoming is encoded in the field through:

  THE OPERATOR FIELD F[rho]:
    This is the rule of becoming. At every point, F[rho] computes
    what should happen next: how much density should flow, in which
    direction, at what rate. F is not a static quantity; it changes
    as rho changes, creating a dynamic, self-updating rule. The rule
    of becoming is itself subject to becoming.

  THE PARTICIPATION FIELD v:
    This is the memory of becoming. The participation field records
    what has happened and feeds it forward. Without v, the system
    would have no history — each instant would be determined solely
    by the current density configuration. With v, the system carries
    its past into its future, creating the temporal depth that makes
    oscillation possible.

  THE DENSITY FIELD rho:
    This is the substance of becoming. It is what changes, what has
    structure, what the other fields act upon. The density field is
    not a backdrop for dynamics; it is the dynamics. Its spatial
    shape at any moment is the architecture's instantaneous
    expression.

WHY THE ARCHITECTURE IS STABLE

The Architectural Field Theory reveals that the ED architecture's
stability is not a single property but a convergence of three
independent stabilizing mechanisms:

  (1) POTENTIAL STABILITY: the energy functional V[rho] has a unique
      minimum. There is nowhere else for the system to go.

  (2) DISSIPATIVE STABILITY: three independent dissipation channels
      (diffusive, regulatory, participatory) all drain energy. There
      is no mechanism to sustain structure.

  (3) SPECTRAL STABILITY: the k^2 eigenvalue hierarchy ensures rapid
      dimensional collapse. There is no mechanism for high-mode
      excitation to persist.

Any one of these would make the architecture stable. All three
together make it robustly stable: stable under multiplicative
perturbations of any component (Law of Proportional Stability),
stable across the entire parameter space (Law of Unique Attraction),
and stable for every initial condition (Law of Transient Structure).

The architecture is fragile only to additive perturbations, which
bypass the coupling structure and inject energy into the Memory Loop
from outside. This is the field-theoretic explanation of the
multiplicative/additive distinction: multiplicative perturbations
act within the field's coupling structure and are absorbed by the
dissipation channels, while additive perturbations act outside the
coupling structure and can temporarily overpower the dissipation.

HOW GLOBAL BEHAVIOR EMERGES FROM LOCAL RULES

The architectural field is local: each point updates based on its
immediate neighborhood. Yet the global behavior is highly organized:
all trajectories converge, the manifold is one-dimensional, the
boundary is a global surface.

The field theory explains this local-to-global emergence through
three mechanisms:

  SPATIAL COUPLING VIA THE LAPLACIAN:
    The nabla^2 operator couples each point to its neighbors. This
    is a local coupling (only nearest neighbors contribute), but
    iterating it propagates information across the entire domain.
    After a few diffusion times, every point has communicated with
    every other point, and the global spatial structure has organized.

  TEMPORAL COUPLING VIA THE MEMORY LOOP:
    The v-field integrates F[rho] over time, creating temporal
    coherence. Each spatial point's history influences its future,
    and since F[rho] depends on spatial derivatives, the temporal
    coupling is also spatial. The Memory Loop synchronizes the
    evolution across space by feeding back the spatially-integrated
    operator output.

  SPECTRAL COUPLING VIA THE NONLINEAR TRIAD:
    The M'*|nabla(rho)|^2 term couples Fourier modes. This creates
    coherence across spatial scales: the k=1 mode drives the k=3
    mode, which affects the k=1 mode through the mode-mode coupling.
    The spectral coupling ensures that the field's evolution is
    organized across scales, not just across space and time.

These three coupling mechanisms — spatial, temporal, spectral —
together ensure that the local field rules produce global order.
The local rules are simple (diffuse, regulate, remember). The global
outcome is rich (spirals, harmonics, horizons, manifolds). The field
theory bridges the two: it shows how the simple local structure
generates the complex global geometry through iterated spatial,
temporal, and spectral coupling.
