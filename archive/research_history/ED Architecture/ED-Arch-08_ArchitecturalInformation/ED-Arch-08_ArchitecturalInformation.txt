1. PURPOSE OF ARCHITECTURAL INFORMATION
========================================================================

The previous seven chapters described the ED architecture from
structural, dynamical, energetic, and causal perspectives. This
eighth chapter asks: what happens to the information carried by the
field as it evolves? How is the initial condition's structure encoded,
transmitted through the architecture's channels, compressed by its
mechanisms, transformed by its couplings, and ultimately erased by
its dissipation?

Architectural information is:

  (i)   NOT SHANNON INFORMATION.  Shannon information theory measures
        the entropy of a probability distribution over messages.
        The ED architecture does not transmit messages between a
        sender and a receiver. Its "information" is not measured in
        bits, and its "entropy" is not a probabilistic quantity.

  (ii)  ARCHITECTURAL INFORMATION.  The structural content carried
        by the field state (rho, v). This includes spatial structure
        (gradients, curvatures, mode amplitudes), temporal structure
        (participation memory), and spectral structure (harmonic
        content). Architectural information is the answer to the
        question: how much can be learned about the initial condition
        by observing the field at time t?

  (iii) A MEASURE OF STRUCTURAL COMPLEXITY.  At t=0, the field may
        have arbitrary complexity: sharp features, multiple modes,
        noise. As time progresses, the architecture systematically
        reduces this complexity through dissipation, modal decay,
        and manifold collapse. Architectural information measures
        what structural complexity remains at each moment.

Architectural information differs from physical entropy and PDE data
in the following ways:

  PHYSICAL ENTROPY measures disorder in a thermodynamic system. The
  ED architecture is not a thermodynamic system. It has no
  temperature, no partition function, and no equilibrium ensemble.
  However, the architecture has an analog of the second law: its
  structural complexity always decreases, just as entropy always
  increases in thermodynamics.

  PDE DATA is the numerical content of a simulation: floating-point
  arrays, grid values, time-step records. Architectural information
  is not about numerical precision or computational representation.
  It is about the structural content of the field: what shapes are
  present, what modes are active, what memory is stored.

The architectural information perspective reveals the ED cosmology
PDE as an information-processing system. The initial condition is
the input. The equilibrium is the output. The architecture's motifs,
laws, and geometry are the processing stages. And the question
"what happens to the information?" is the question that unifies all
previous chapters into a single narrative.

2. INFORMATION IN THE ARCHITECTURAL FIELD
========================================================================

The architectural field (rho, v, F[rho]) carries information in three
distinct forms. Each form has its own encoding scheme, its own
capacity, its own decay dynamics, and its own relationship to the
architecture's motifs and laws.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 SPATIAL INFORMATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: The structural content encoded in the density field's
spatial shape — its gradients, curvatures, and Fourier mode amplitudes.

Encoding: Spatial information is encoded in the mode amplitude
vector (a_1, a_2, ..., a_N), where rho(x,t) = rho* + sum_k a_k(t) *
phi_k(x) and phi_k are the spatial eigenfields. Each mode amplitude
a_k carries one independent piece of spatial information: the
strength of the k-th spatial pattern in the density field.

Capacity: The total spatial information capacity is N, the number of
resolved spatial modes. For a grid of N_x points, there are
approximately N_x/2 independent modes. In the ED-Phys experiments
with N_x = 128, the spatial information capacity is approximately 64
independent mode amplitudes.

Decay dynamics: Each mode amplitude decays at rate proportional to
k^2 (Law of Modal Hierarchy). The k=1 mode carries information
longest; the k=64 mode carries information for approximately
1/64^2 = 1/4096 of the k=1 lifetime. The spatial information
content therefore decreases rapidly with time, as high-k modes lose
their information to dissipation.

Current state at equilibrium: zero. All mode amplitudes are zero.
No spatial information survives.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2 TEMPORAL INFORMATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: The structural content encoded in the participation
field v(x,t) — the stored memory of past operator forcing.

Encoding: Temporal information is encoded in v(x,t) as an
exponentially weighted integral of past F[rho] values:

  v(x,t) = (1/tau) * integral_0^t exp(-zeta*(t-s)/tau) * F[rho](x,s) ds

At each spatial point, v carries a single real number that summarizes
the recent history of operator forcing at that point. The encoding
is lossy: the exponential weighting means that recent events are
represented with high fidelity while old events are progressively
forgotten.

Capacity: At each spatial point, v carries one real number's worth
of temporal information. The total temporal information capacity is
N_x real numbers. However, the effective capacity is much lower
because v is driven by F[rho], which is itself a function of rho.
The participation field does not carry independent information; it
carries a temporally filtered version of the density field's
information. The true independent temporal information is the
difference between what v encodes and what can be reconstructed
from rho alone.

Decay dynamics: Temporal information decays at rate zeta/tau
(the participation damping rate). At zeta = 0.25 and tau = 1.0,
the decay rate is 0.25 per time unit, giving a memory half-life of
approximately 2.8 time units. At zeta = 1.0, the half-life drops
to approximately 0.7 time units. The temporal information horizon
(the time beyond which stored information has negligible influence)
is approximately 3*tau/zeta.

Current state at equilibrium: zero. v = 0 everywhere. No temporal
information survives.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3 SPECTRAL INFORMATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: The structural content encoded in the relationships
between mode amplitudes — the harmonic structure generated by the
Nonlinear Triad.

Encoding: Spectral information is encoded in the amplitude ratios
between coupled modes. The primary spectral relationship is the
k=3 to k=1 ratio, locked by the Triad's coupling tensor. Secondary
relationships exist between k=1, k=2, k=0 (the Triad's intermediate
products). These ratios are not independent information; they are
determined by the coupling structure and the k=1 amplitude.

Capacity: Spectral information capacity is paradoxically very low
despite the apparent complexity of the Fourier spectrum. The Triad's
output is enslaved to k=1 (Law of Harmonic Protection). The k=3
amplitude is a deterministic function of the k=1 amplitude and the
coupling coefficient. The spectral information is therefore
redundant: it adds no independent content beyond what k=1 already
carries. The Triad transforms information rather than creating it.

Decay dynamics: Spectral information decays as the k=1 mode decays,
because the harmonic structure is enslaved to k=1. When k=1 reaches
zero, all spectral relationships vanish. The spectral information
lifetime equals the k=1 lifetime.

Current state at equilibrium: undefined. With all amplitudes zero,
the amplitude ratios are 0/0. Spectral information ceases to exist,
not because it decays to zero, but because the substrate on which
it is encoded (the mode amplitudes) ceases to exist.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.4 THE ED INFORMATION STATE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The total information state of the architectural field at time t is
the combination of spatial, temporal, and spectral information:

  I(t) = { spatial: (a_1(t), a_2(t), ..., a_N(t)),
            temporal: v(x,t),
            spectral: (k3/k1 ratio, coupling structure) }

The three components are not independent. The spectral component is
determined by the spatial component (through the Triad coupling).
The temporal component is driven by the spatial component (through
F[rho]). The spatial component is modified by the temporal component
(through H*v feedback).

The information state is therefore a coupled, self-referential
structure: each component influences the others, and all three
evolve simultaneously. The total independent information content
is bounded by the spatial component alone (approximately N/2 real
numbers at t=0), because the temporal and spectral components are
derived from it.

As time progresses, the independent information content decreases:
  t = 0:     N/2 independent real numbers (full modal content)
  t ~ 1/lambda_2:  approximately N/4 (k >= 2 modes significantly
                    decayed)
  t ~ 1/lambda_1:  approximately 1 (only k=1 mode survives, plus
                    its enslaved harmonics and associated v)
  t -> infinity:   0 (equilibrium, no structure)

The ED architecture is an information compressor: it takes a high-
dimensional input (the initial condition) and progressively reduces
its information content to zero (the equilibrium). The compression
is lossy and irreversible.

3. INFORMATION-MOTIF CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each motif plays a specific role in the architecture's information
processing. The motifs are the architecture's information-processing
primitives.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SADDLE: INFORMATION ROUTER AND PARTITIONER
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Saddle motif (gradient sign reversal at density extrema)
partitions spatial information into two streams. At a density peak,
the gradient field splits: leftward gradients carry information about
the left flank of the peak, rightward gradients carry information
about the right flank. The Saddle is the point where this split
occurs.

Information role: the Saddle routes spatial information by determining
which neighboring points receive which components of the gradient
field's content. Points to the left of the Saddle receive information
about the left flank; points to the right receive information about
the right flank. The Saddle is a spatial information demultiplexer.

The Saddle also partitions information between the diffusion and
penalty channels. At the Saddle itself, nabla(rho) = 0, so the
Nonlinear Triad is inactive (M'|nabla(rho)|^2 = 0). The curvature
nabla^2(rho) is maximal (in magnitude). Information at the Saddle
is processed by curvature-driven diffusion and penalty regulation,
not by nonlinear mixing.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE SPIRAL: INFORMATION DELAY AND PHASE ENCODING
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Spiral motif (oscillatory convergence) delays information
processing by circulating it through the Memory Loop multiple times
before erasure. Each spiral revolution carries the same structural
information (the system's deviation from equilibrium) through one
complete cycle of the F -> v -> rho -> F loop.

Information role: the Spiral is an information delay line. It does
not create new information (each revolution is a lower-amplitude
repetition of the previous one). It does not preserve information
(each revolution has less content than the last, due to dissipation).
It delays the erasure of information by circulating it through the
participatory feedback circuit.

The Spiral also encodes information in the PHASE of the oscillation.
At any moment during a spiral revolution, the relative signs of rho
deviation and v indicate where in the cycle the system is: positive
rho deviation with positive v means the system is still overshooting;
positive rho deviation with negative v means the system has begun
returning from overshoot. This phase information is a temporal
encoding that does not exist in the monotonic regime.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE HORIZON: INFORMATION TRUNCATION AND SATURATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Horizon motif (mobility collapse at rho_max) truncates spatial
information by shutting down the diffusion channel. At the Horizon,
the D-channel can no longer transmit spatial information (gradients
cannot propagate when M = 0). The information carried by spatial
gradients near the Horizon is temporarily frozen: it cannot be
processed by diffusion until the penalty restores M > 0.

Information role: the Horizon is an information buffer with overflow
protection. When the density field carries too much amplitude
(approaching rho_max), the Horizon prevents further accumulation
by blocking the diffusive pathway. The excess information (the
amount by which rho exceeds the Horizon threshold) is clamped by
the mobility collapse. Information above the threshold is not
destroyed; it is temporarily inaccessible to the spatial channel
and must be processed through the penalty and participation channels
instead.

The Horizon imposes an upper bound on the spatial information that
can be represented at any single point: rho(x) cannot exceed rho_max,
so the density field cannot encode structure beyond this ceiling.
This is the architectural information capacity limit.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MANIFOLD: INFORMATION COMPRESSOR TO 1D
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Manifold motif (1D attractor curve) is the architecture's
primary information compressor. Before manifold collapse, the field
state has N/2 independent real numbers of information (the modal
amplitudes). After manifold collapse, it has approximately 2
(the k=1 amplitude and the associated v-projection).

Information role: the Manifold compresses the field's information
from N/2 dimensions to 1 dimension. The compression ratio is
approximately N/4 : 1 (for N_x = 128, this is approximately 32:1).
The compression is lossy: all information about modes k >= 2 is
irreversibly erased. Only the k=1 mode's information survives.

The Manifold's compression explains why 22 different initial
conditions in ED-Phys-29 (Gaussians, sines, composites, steps,
noise) all converge to the same manifold. The initial conditions
differ in their high-k content, but the Manifold erases all high-k
information, leaving only the k=1 projection — which is similar
across many IC types at the same amplitude.

Compression rate: the Manifold compresses at a rate determined by
the spectral gap between k=1 and k=2 decay rates. The compression
is essentially complete after approximately 1/(3*lambda_1) time
units (three e-folding times of the k=2 mode relative to k=1).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE BOUNDARY: INFORMATION REGIME SWITCH
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Boundary motif (D + 2*zeta = 1 surface) switches the
architecture's information processing mode between two regimes:

Below the Boundary (spiral regime): information is processed through
the Memory Loop with oscillatory phase encoding. The system retains
phase information (where in the oscillation cycle it is) in addition
to amplitude information (how far from equilibrium it is). The
information state includes both magnitude and phase.

Above the Boundary (monotonic regime): information is processed
through direct dissipation without phase encoding. The system retains
only amplitude information. Phase information does not exist because
there is no oscillation.

The Boundary is an information bifurcation: it determines whether
the architecture's information state includes phase degrees of
freedom or not. Below the Boundary, the effective information
dimensionality per mode is 2 (amplitude + phase). Above the
Boundary, it is 1 (amplitude only).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MEMORY LOOP: INFORMATION RETENTION AND FEEDBACK
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Memory Loop motif (F -> v -> rho -> F cycle) is the architecture's
information retention mechanism. Without the Memory Loop (at D = 1,
H = 0), the architecture has no memory: each time step depends only
on the current density field. With the Memory Loop (H > 0), the
architecture retains information about past dynamics in the
participation field v.

Information role: the Memory Loop stores a compressed summary of
past operator forcing at each spatial point. The compression is
exponential in time: events within the memory horizon (approximately
tau/zeta time units) are represented with significant weight, while
older events are exponentially attenuated.

The Memory Loop also FEEDS BACK stored information into the density
dynamics through the H*v term. This feedback creates temporal
correlations in the density evolution: the density at time t+dt
depends not only on rho(t) but also on the weighted history
summarized by v(t). The feedback is the mechanism by which past
information influences present dynamics.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE DISSIPATOR: INFORMATION ERASER
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Dissipator motif (monotonic convergence via D-channel) is the
architecture's information eraser. It converts spatial structure
(information) into homogeneity (absence of information) through
diffusive smoothing. Every gradient it smooths, every mode it decays,
every deviation it corrects represents information being erased.

Information role: the Dissipator is the terminal information
processor. All information that enters the architecture eventually
passes through the Dissipator and is erased. The Dissipator does not
transform information (like the Triad), delay it (like the Spiral),
or compress it (like the Manifold). It destroys it.

The Dissipator's erasure rate is proportional to D*k^2 for the k-th
mode. High-k information is erased first (fast erasure), low-k
information is erased last (slow erasure). The erasure is complete
at equilibrium: zero information remains.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE NONLINEAR TRIAD: INFORMATION MIXER AND HARMONIC GENERATOR
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Nonlinear Triad motif (M'|nabla(rho)|^2 coupling) transforms
information between spectral modes. It takes information encoded in
the k=1 mode and generates a representation of that information in
the k=3 mode. The generated k=3 content is not new information; it
is a deterministic transformation of the k=1 information.

Information role: the Triad is an information transformer. It does
not create information (the k=3 output is fully determined by the
k=1 input). It does not destroy information (the k=1 input is not
consumed by the transformation). It creates a redundant copy of the
k=1 information in a different spectral encoding.

The redundant copy serves no functional purpose in the architecture's
convergence. The k=3 mode decays 9x faster than k=1 and does not
feed back into k=1 significantly. The Triad's information
transformation is architecturally inevitable (it follows from the
nonlinearity) but informationally inert (it does not change the
system's independent information content).

4. INFORMATION-LAW CORRESPONDENCE
========================================================================

Each architectural law constrains the information structure.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
UNIQUE ATTRACTION -> GLOBAL INFORMATION SINK
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The unique attractor at (rho*, 0) is a state of zero information.
All spatial modes are zero. All participation memory is zero. All
spectral structure is absent. The Law of Unique Attraction guarantees
that all information eventually flows into this sink.

Information constraint: the architecture has exactly one
zero-information state, and all dynamics converge to it. There is
no non-trivial information-preserving attractor (no limit cycle, no
quasi-periodic orbit, no strange attractor). The only long-time
state is the state of complete information loss.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
ONE-DIMENSIONAL RELAXATION -> ASYMPTOTIC COMPRESSION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The 1D manifold collapse compresses the field's information content
from N/2 dimensions to 1 dimension. This is the architecture's most
dramatic information-processing event: a reduction of independent
information by a factor of approximately N/4.

Information constraint: the late-time information state is one-
dimensional. No matter how complex the initial condition, the
asymptotic information content is bounded by 1 real number (the
k=1 mode amplitude) plus its deterministic derivatives (the
enslaved k=3 harmonic and the associated v projection).

Quantitative evidence: ED-Phys-29 confirms manifold dimension = 1
in 64/66 trajectories (97%) and 48/48 in ED-Phys-30 (100%). The
compression is empirically universal.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROPORTIONAL STABILITY -> INVARIANCE OF INFORMATION PATHWAYS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Multiplicative perturbations preserve the architecture's information
processing pathways. The routing (Saddle), compression (Manifold),
mixing (Triad), and erasure (Dissipator) all operate the same way
under multiplicative perturbation. Only the quantitative rates change.

Information constraint: the information processing topology is
invariant under admissible perturbations. The architecture has a
unique information processing program that cannot be altered by
changing the functional forms of M, P, or v within the
multiplicative equivalence class.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
CRITICAL DAMPING BOUNDARY -> ENCODING REGIME SWITCH
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The boundary switches the information encoding between two regimes:

Below: amplitude + phase encoding (2 degrees of freedom per mode).
Above: amplitude-only encoding (1 degree of freedom per mode).

Information constraint: the architecture's effective information
dimensionality per mode is either 1 or 2, depending on the parameter
point. The boundary determines which encoding is used.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
FINITE CAPACITY -> UPPER BOUND ON INFORMATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The mobility collapse at rho_max bounds the density field to
[0, rho_max]. This creates an upper bound on the information that
can be encoded at any spatial point: the density value is bounded,
so the dynamic range is finite.

Information constraint: the architecture cannot represent arbitrarily
large density deviations. The maximum representable amplitude is
rho_max - rho* = 50 (canonical parameters). This limits the initial
information content and prevents unbounded information growth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
HARMONIC PROTECTION -> INVARIANT SPECTRAL SIGNATURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The k3/k1 ratio is fixed by the Triad's coupling structure,
invariant under perturbation. This means the spectral information
content is architecturally determined: no perturbation can change
the harmonic structure.

Information constraint: the spectral encoding is fixed. The
architecture produces the same spectral fingerprint (k=3 from k=1,
at a fixed ratio) regardless of parameters or initial conditions
(at fixed amplitude). The spectral information is not a free
variable but a structural constant.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MODAL HIERARCHY -> ORDERED INFORMATION DECAY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The k^2 decay hierarchy imposes a strict ordering on information
loss. High-k information is lost first, low-k information last.
The ordering is universal and cannot be reversed by any dynamical
process.

Information constraint: the architecture erases information in a
fixed order. Fine spatial detail (high k) is always erased before
coarse spatial structure (low k). This ordering is the architectural
analog of a low-pass filter with a time-dependent cutoff frequency
that decreases monotonically.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRANSIENT STRUCTURE -> EVENTUAL COMPLETE ERASURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

All architectural information is eventually erased. No permanent
information storage exists. The architecture is a transient
information processor: it takes an information-rich input, processes
it through its motifs and channels, and produces an information-free
output.

Information constraint: the architecture's information content is
bounded by a monotonically decreasing function of time. At t = 0,
the information content is maximal (set by the initial condition).
At t = infinity, the information content is zero (equilibrium). The
decrease is irreversible.

5. INFORMATION GEOMETRY
========================================================================

The geometric objects of ED-Arch-04 are not just dynamical shapes;
they are information-processing structures. Each geometric object
defines a specific information flow pattern.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
SPIRAL SHEET: HELICAL INFORMATION TRAJECTORIES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

On the Spiral Sheet, information traces a helical path in the
(amplitude, phase) space. Each revolution of the helix represents
one cycle of the Memory Loop's information circulation: amplitude
information is converted to phase information (during overshoot),
phase information is reconverted to amplitude information (during
undershoot), and a fraction of the total information is dissipated
(through the zeta-channel).

The Spiral Sheet's information geometry reveals that oscillatory
dynamics are informationally redundant. Each revolution carries
approximately the same structural information as the previous one,
at lower amplitude. The helical repetition encodes no new
information; it merely delays the erasure of existing information.

Information content per revolution: approximately 70% of the
previous revolution's content (based on the decay ratio of 3.3
per cycle measured in ED-Phys-27 for the nonsmooth variant:
each cycle carries (1/3.3) ~ 30% of the previous cycle's
amplitude, corresponding to roughly (0.30)^2 ~ 9% of the
energy, meaning ~91% of the information-carrying capacity is
erased per cycle in that extreme case). In the canonical regime,
the per-cycle information loss is smaller but still monotone.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MONOTONIC CONE: RADIAL INFORMATION COLLAPSE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

On the Monotonic Cone, information collapses radially: amplitude
information decreases monotonically without phase encoding. The
information trajectory is a straight line from the initial state
to zero information.

The Monotonic Cone's information geometry is the simplest possible:
one-dimensional, monotone, direct. There is no redundant cycling, no
phase encoding, no temporal delay. Information is erased at the
maximum rate permitted by the dissipation channels.

This makes the monotonic regime informationally efficient: it erases
information (converges to equilibrium) without wasting processing
cycles on oscillatory repetition. However, it also means the
monotonic regime retains less information about the approach history.
In the spiral regime, the phase of the current oscillation encodes
information about how many cycles have elapsed. In the monotonic
regime, no such temporal encoding exists.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
HORIZON SURFACE: INFORMATION CUTOFF BOUNDARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Horizon Surface is an information cutoff: beyond it (rho > rho_max),
no density information can be encoded. The mobility collapse prevents
the spatial channel from processing information at the boundary.

The Horizon's information geometry is that of a reflecting wall in
information space. Information approaching the Horizon is reflected
back: the density cannot increase beyond rho_max, so the information
about further density increase is lost (it was never representable).
The reflection is partial: spatial information is blocked, but
temporal and regulatory information continue through the v and
P_SY2 channels.

The Horizon creates an information shadow in the spatial channel:
points at the Horizon cannot transmit spatial information to their
neighbors through diffusion. The shadow is transient (the penalty
removes the Horizon) and partial (non-diffusive channels remain
open).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
BOUNDARY SURFACE: INFORMATION BIFURCATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Boundary Surface bifurcates the architecture's information
processing into two topologically distinct modes. On one side,
the information state has phase degrees of freedom (spiral regime).
On the other, it does not (monotonic regime).

The bifurcation is an information phase transition: the dimensionality
of the information state per mode changes discretely from 2 to 1
as the Boundary is crossed. This is the most significant information-
processing event in parameter space: it determines whether the
architecture encodes temporal phase information or only amplitude
information.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MODAL FUNNEL: HIERARCHICAL INFORMATION COMPRESSION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Modal Funnel is the architecture's primary information
compressor. It takes the N/2-dimensional initial information state
and compresses it to 1 dimension through the k^2 decay hierarchy.

The compression is hierarchical: the highest modes (carrying the
finest spatial detail) are compressed first. The compression
proceeds from the outside in, peeling away layers of spatial detail
until only the coarsest structure (k=1) remains.

The funnel's information geometry is that of a series of nested
filters, each with a lower cutoff frequency than the last. At
t=0, all frequencies pass. At t = 1/(3*lambda_1), only k=1
passes. The funnel narrows continuously, but the narrowing is
not uniform: it is weighted by k^2, so high-k content is removed
disproportionately fast.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRIAD LOCUS: INFORMATION MIXING ZONE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Triad Locus is a zone where information is mixed between
spectral modes. The mixing does not create new information (the
k=3 output is determined by the k=1 input). It creates a
redundant representation of existing information in a different
spectral band.

The Triad Locus's information geometry is that of a nonlinear lens:
it refracts information from one spectral channel (k=1) into another
(k=3), preserving the information content but changing its encoding.
The refraction is lossless in principle (no information is created
or destroyed by the Triad itself), but the refracted beam (k=3) is
immediately subject to the Modal Hierarchy's fast erasure (9x the
k=1 rate).

6. INFORMATION FLOW RULES
========================================================================

The five causal propagation rules from ED-Arch-07 have direct
informational counterparts. Each causal rule describes a pathway;
each informational rule describes what happens to the structural
content carried along that pathway.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 1: THE LOCALITY RULE (INFORMATION SPREADS THROUGH GRADIENTS)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Spatial information propagates through the gradient nabla(rho) and
the Laplacian nabla^2(rho). At each time step, each point's
information content is influenced by, and influences, its nearest
neighbors. Long-range information transfer requires multiple steps,
with the transfer speed set by the diffusion coefficient sqrt(M*dt).

The locality rule constrains information processing to be inherently
sequential in space: the architecture cannot instantaneously
distribute spatial information across the domain. Information about
a local perturbation takes O(L^2/M) time steps to reach the
opposite end of the domain. This delay is the spatial information
processing latency.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 2: THE MEMORY RULE (INFORMATION PERSISTS THROUGH v)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Temporal information is stored in the participation field v with
exponential decay at rate zeta/tau. The memory horizon is
approximately 3*tau/zeta time units. Information older than this
horizon has negligible influence on the current dynamics.

The memory rule constrains information persistence to be finite
and exponentially decaying. The architecture cannot store information
indefinitely. The memory is volatile: it degrades continuously,
with the degradation rate set by the damping parameter zeta.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 3: THE DISSIPATION RULE (INFORMATION DECAYS THROUGH D AND ZETA)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

All information decays through the three dissipation channels.
Spatial information decays at rate D*k^2 (D-channel). Temporal
information decays at rate zeta/tau (zeta-channel). Density
deviation information decays at rate alpha*gamma/rho_0 (P-channel).

The dissipation rule constrains all information to have finite
lifetime. No information is conserved. The longest-lived information
is the k=1 mode amplitude, with lifetime approximately
1/lambda_1 = 1/(D*pi^2/L^2 * M(rho*) + alpha*gamma/rho_0).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 4: THE SPECTRAL RULE (INFORMATION CASCADES THROUGH THE TRIAD)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Spectral information is mixed by the Nonlinear Triad, which
transforms k=1 information into k=3 information at a fixed ratio.
The mixing is deterministic, locked, and does not increase the
system's independent information content.

The spectral rule constrains spectral information to be redundant.
The k=3 mode's information content is a deterministic function of
k=1's information content. The architecture's independent spectral
information is bounded by 1 mode (k=1), regardless of how many
harmonics the Triad generates.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 5: THE CAPACITY RULE (INFORMATION BOUNDED BY rho_max)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The density field is bounded by [0, rho_max], limiting the dynamic
range of spatial information. The maximum information per spatial
point is bounded by log_2(rho_max/delta_rho) bits, where delta_rho
is the minimum resolvable density difference.

The capacity rule constrains the architecture's total representable
information. The system cannot encode arbitrarily complex structure:
the maximum density deviation is rho_max - rho* = 50, and the
maximum gradient is bounded by the grid resolution. The architecture
is a finite-capacity information channel.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
UNIFIED INFORMATION FLOW SYSTEM
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The five rules define the architecture as an information-processing
system with specific characteristics:

  INPUT: an initial density profile with up to N/2 independent
  mode amplitudes, each bounded by the capacity rule.

  PROCESSING: the architecture routes (Saddle), delays (Spiral),
  truncates (Horizon), compresses (Manifold), switches (Boundary),
  retains (Memory Loop), erases (Dissipator), and mixes (Triad)
  the input information.

  OUTPUT: the equilibrium state (rho*, 0), containing zero
  information.

  THROUGHPUT: the architecture's information processing rate is
  bounded by the fastest dissipation rate (D*k_max^2 for the
  highest resolved mode) and the slowest dissipation rate
  (lambda_1 for the fundamental mode).

  LATENCY: the time from input to output (complete information
  erasure) is approximately 1/lambda_1, ranging from 7 to 500
  time units across the parameter space.


========================================================================
7. INFORMATION TRANSFORMATION
========================================================================

The architectural field performs four principal information
transformations. These transformations convert information from one
form to another as it moves through the architecture's processing
stages.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 SPATIAL -> SPECTRAL (VIA THE TRIAD)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Nonlinear Triad converts spatial information (encoded in
gradients) into spectral information (encoded in mode amplitude
ratios). At the flanks of density peaks, the M'|nabla(rho)|^2
coupling takes the k=1 mode's spatial gradient energy and generates
k=3 spectral content.

This transformation is one-to-one (each k=1 amplitude maps to a
unique k=3 amplitude) and deterministic (the mapping is set by the
coupling structure). It does not create new information; it creates
a spectral copy of existing spatial information.

The transformation's significance is architectural: it demonstrates
that the architecture's nonlinear structure generates spectral
complexity from spatial simplicity. A single Fourier mode (k=1)
with a simple spatial shape (one peak, one trough) produces a
richer spectral state (k=1, k=3, and their coupling) through the
Triad's action.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.2 SPECTRAL -> TEMPORAL (VIA PARTICIPATION)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The participation field v integrates the operator field F[rho], which
contains spectral information (the mode amplitudes determine F's
value at each point). The integration converts spectral information
(which modes are active and at what amplitudes) into temporal
information (what the recent history of forcing has been).

This transformation is many-to-one: many different spectral states
can produce the same v value over time (because v integrates F with
exponential decay, losing track of the specific time profile of F).
The transformation is compressive: the detailed spectral history is
reduced to a single number per spatial point.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.3 TEMPORAL -> SPATIAL (VIA H*v FEEDBACK)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The H*v term in the density equation converts temporal information
(stored in v) back into spatial information (modifications to rho
at each point). Where v is positive, the density increases. Where
v is negative, the density decreases. The spatial pattern of v
determines where and how much the density field is modified.

This transformation is the Memory Loop's feedback stage: temporal
information becomes spatial structure. The feedback is what creates
overshoots and undershoots: stored temporal information drives the
density past equilibrium, creating new spatial gradients that will
be processed in the next iteration of the loop.

The transformation preserves spatial locality (v at point x modifies
rho at point x) but introduces temporal nonlocality (the modification
depends on past events through v's integration history).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.4 HIGH-DIMENSIONAL -> LOW-DIMENSIONAL (VIA MANIFOLD COLLAPSE)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The manifold collapse transforms the field's information state from
high-dimensional (N/2 independent modes) to low-dimensional (1 mode).
This is the architecture's most dramatic information transformation:
a compression of approximately 32:1 (for N_x = 128).

The compression proceeds through the Modal Funnel: each mode decays
at rate k^2, so high-k modes are compressed (erased) first. The
compression is irreversible: once a mode's amplitude has decayed
below the noise floor, its information content is permanently lost.

The compression's output (the 1D manifold) retains only the
information needed to specify the system's position along the
manifold: one amplitude (k=1) and one velocity (the associated
v projection). This is the minimal information state from which
the system's future evolution can be predicted: given the manifold
position, the full trajectory to equilibrium is determined.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHY ED IS AN INFORMATION-COMPRESSING ARCHITECTURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The four transformations share a common theme: compression. The Triad
copies information redundantly (compression of independent content:
no new independent information). The participation integration is
many-to-one (compression of temporal detail). The manifold collapse
is N:1 (compression of modal content). The feedback creates temporary
new structure, but that structure is itself subject to compression.

The net effect is a one-directional information flow from complexity
to simplicity:

  High-dimensional IC -> Modal Funnel -> 1D Manifold -> Attractor

At each stage, information is lost. None of the stages is reversible.
The architecture cannot reconstruct the initial condition from the
late-time state, because the manifold's 1D state does not encode
the initial condition's high-k content.

The compression ratio depends on the initial condition's complexity:
  - A simple Gaussian (essentially k=1 only): compression ratio ~ 1:1
    (no high-k content to lose).
  - A step function (many modes): compression ratio ~ N/4:1
    (most modes erased).
  - Random noise (all modes excited): compression ratio ~ N/4:1
    (nearly all content erased).

ED-Phys-29 demonstrates this: all 22 IC types converge to the same
manifold with convergence rates that vary by only 13-25% across IC
types. The architecture's output is nearly independent of the
input's complexity, because the compression erases the complexity.

8. INFORMATION ERASURE AND IRREVERSIBILITY
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
8.1 WHAT INFORMATION IS LOST
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The architecture erases information in a definite sequence, determined
by the Modal Hierarchy and the dissipation channels:

FIRST TO BE ERASED: HIGH-k SPATIAL MODES (k >= 3)
  These carry fine spatial detail: sharp edges, narrow features,
  high-frequency oscillations. They decay at rate proportional to k^2,
  so k=3 decays 9x faster than k=1, k=5 decays 25x faster, and k=10
  decays 100x faster. Within a few k=2 decay times, all information
  about spatial detail finer than the fundamental wavelength is lost.

  Quantitative: for the canonical parameters (D=0.5, lambda_1 ~ 0.015),
  the k=3 mode has lost 99% of its information after approximately
  4.6/(9*0.015) ~ 34 time units. The k=10 mode after approximately
  4.6/(100*0.015) ~ 3 time units.

SECOND TO BE ERASED: PHASE INFORMATION
  In the spiral regime, the oscillation phase encodes temporal
  information about the cycle count and the Memory Loop's state.
  This phase information decays as the oscillation amplitude decays.
  After the last oscillation cycle, all phase information is gone.
  In the monotonic regime, phase information never exists.

  Quantitative: phase information persists for approximately
  (n_cycles) * (period per cycle) time units. At (D=0, zeta=0.25),
  this is roughly 49 cycles, giving a phase information lifetime of
  hundreds of time units. At (D=0.5, zeta=0.5), it is roughly 1
  cycle, giving a phase lifetime of tens of time units.

THIRD TO BE ERASED: LOCAL GRADIENTS
  The k=1 mode amplitude decays at rate lambda_1, the slowest rate
  in the system. The last surviving spatial information is the
  difference between the density field's peak and trough values (the
  k=1 amplitude). This information is erased as the system
  approaches the flat equilibrium state.

  Quantitative: the k=1 information has lost 99% of its content
  after approximately 4.6/lambda_1 time units. For lambda_1 = 0.015
  (hybrid canonical), this is approximately 307 time units.

LAST TO BE ERASED: PARTICIPATION MEMORY
  The participation field v decays at rate zeta/tau, independently of
  the spatial channel. If zeta/tau < lambda_1, the participation
  memory outlasts the spatial information: v retains a fading record
  of past dynamics even after the density field has become nearly
  homogeneous. If zeta/tau > lambda_1, the participation memory is
  erased before the spatial information.

  Quantitative: at zeta = 0.5, tau = 1.0, the participation decay
  rate is 0.5 per time unit. At lambda_1 = 0.015, the spatial decay
  rate is 0.015 per time unit. Participation memory is erased ~33x
  faster than spatial information. In this regime, spatial information
  outlasts temporal information.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
8.2 WHY ED DYNAMICS ARE IRREVERSIBLE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The ED architecture's dynamics are irreversible in the strongest
possible sense: the late-time state contains strictly less information
than the early-time state, making backward reconstruction impossible.
Four independent mechanisms enforce this irreversibility:

DISSIPATIVE CHANNELS:
  The three dissipation channels (D, zeta, P) continuously drain
  information from the field. There is no energy source or information
  source that could reverse the drain. The dissipation is one-way:
  information flows from the field into "heat" (equilibrium), never
  the reverse. This is the architectural analog of the thermodynamic
  arrow of time.

MODAL HIERARCHY:
  The k^2 decay hierarchy creates an irreversible ordering of
  information loss. High-k modes cannot be regenerated from low-k
  modes through the architecture's dynamics. The Triad generates
  k=3 from k=1, but the generated k=3 is enslaved (locked to k=1's
  amplitude), not independent. True independent high-k content,
  once erased, cannot be recovered.

  The irreversibility can be quantified: the k=2 mode at time t
  carries information proportional to exp(-4*lambda_1*t). To
  reconstruct the k=2 mode's initial information from the state
  at time t would require amplifying it by exp(+4*lambda_1*t),
  which the architecture cannot do (it has no amplification
  mechanism for spatial modes).

PENALTY POTENTIAL:
  The penalty drives the spatial mean of rho toward rho*,
  irreversibly erasing information about the initial mean density.
  Once the mean has reached rho*, the information about its initial
  deviation is lost and cannot be recovered from the field state.

NO CONSERVED INFORMATION:
  The architecture conserves nothing except the direction of becoming
  (the energetic arrow from ED-Arch-06). There is no conserved
  quantity that could serve as a carrier of information across the
  dissipative process. In Hamiltonian systems, energy conservation
  makes dynamics reversible (Liouville's theorem preserves phase-
  space volume). In the ED architecture, no such conservation exists,
  and phase-space volume contracts monotonically.

  The phase-space contraction rate can be estimated from the
  divergence of the flow field. The trace of the Jacobian of the
  reduced dynamics is approximately -(D*lambda_1 + zeta/tau), which
  is strictly negative. This means the flow contracts phase-space
  volume at every point, confirming that information is being
  irreversibly erased at every instant.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
8.3 THE INFORMATION ARROW
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The ED architecture has an information arrow: the direction of
monotonically decreasing architectural information content. This
arrow parallels the energetic arrow (ED-Arch-06) and the causal
arrow (ED-Arch-07):

  Energetic arrow: architectural energy always decreases.
  Causal arrow: influence always flows toward equilibrium.
  Information arrow: structural information always decreases.

The three arrows are not independent. They are three aspects of the
same underlying principle: the architecture's dissipative structure.
Energy decreases because the dissipation channels drain it. Influence
converges because the causal pathways lead to the sink. Information
decreases because the processing stages compress and erase it.

The information arrow is the most fundamental of the three, because
it subsumes the others. Energy decrease is a consequence of
information decrease (less structure means less energy, since
E[rho,v] measures deviation from the structureless equilibrium).
Causal convergence is a consequence of information decrease (fewer
degrees of freedom means fewer possible causal pathways).

The information arrow is the architectural formulation of
irreversibility: the system always moves from information-rich
states to information-poor states, and the reverse process is
structurally impossible within the architecture's dynamics.

9. UNIFIED INTERPRETATION
========================================================================

HOW THE ARCHITECTURE ENCODES STRUCTURE

The ED architecture encodes structure in three interleaved forms:
spatial (mode amplitudes in the density field), temporal (participation
memory in the v field), and spectral (harmonic relationships via the
Triad). The encoding is redundant: the temporal and spectral forms
are derived from the spatial form and carry no independent information.
The true information content is bounded by the spatial mode amplitude
vector, which has N/2 independent components at t=0.

The encoding is also hierarchical: the k=1 mode carries the most
persistent information, the k=2 mode carries less persistent
information, and so on. The hierarchy means that the architecture's
information state simplifies predictably over time: spatial detail
is erased first, gross structure last.

HOW INFORMATION FLOWS THROUGH MOTIFS AND GEOMETRY

Information enters the architecture through the initial condition and
flows through a definite processing pipeline:

  1. ROUTING (Saddle): spatial information is partitioned at density
     extrema into left-flowing and right-flowing streams.
  2. MIXING (Triad): k=1 spatial information generates k=3 spectral
     copies at the flanks of density peaks.
  3. COMPRESSION (Modal Funnel): high-k information is erased by the
     k^2 decay hierarchy, funneling all content into k=1.
  4. STORAGE (Memory Loop): operator history is integrated into the
     participation field, creating temporal information.
  5. FEEDBACK (H*v): temporal information is converted back into
     spatial information, driving overshoots and undershoots.
  6. DELAY (Spiral): information circulates through the Memory Loop,
     delaying erasure but not preventing it.
  7. TRUNCATION (Horizon): spatial information is capped at the
     mobility collapse boundary, preventing overflow.
  8. COLLAPSE (Manifold): all information is compressed to 1D.
  9. ERASURE (Dissipator): the final 1D information is erased as
     the system approaches equilibrium.

The pipeline is unidirectional. Information flows from step 1 to
step 9. There is no backward flow. The only apparent exception is
step 5 (feedback), which creates new spatial structure from temporal
information. But this created structure is itself subject to the
full pipeline, and each pass through the pipeline reduces the
information content.

WHY ED DYNAMICS SIMPLIFY OVER TIME

The architecture simplifies its information state because:

  (1) The Modal Hierarchy erases high-k information faster than
      low-k information, progressively removing spatial detail.
  (2) The participation damping erases temporal information,
      progressively removing memory.
  (3) The penalty erases density deviation information, progressively
      removing the driving force for all other structure.
  (4) No mechanism regenerates erased information. The Triad creates
      copies, not originals. The Memory Loop stores summaries, not
      records. The feedback creates transients, not persistent
      structure.

The simplification is the architecture's fundamental informational
property. The ED cosmology PDE is, at its informational core, a
simplification engine: it takes complex inputs and produces simple
outputs through a sequence of compressions, erasures, and dissipations.

HOW INFORMATION THEORY COMPLETES THE ED-ARCH ONTOLOGY

The eight ED-Arch documents form a complete structural description of
the unified ED cosmology PDE:

  ED-Arch-01 (Summary):       WHAT the architecture does.
  ED-Arch-02 (Motifs):        WHAT PATTERNS recur.
  ED-Arch-03 (Laws):          WHAT RULES govern.
  ED-Arch-04 (Geometry):      WHAT SHAPES result.
  ED-Arch-05 (Field Theory):  HOW the field is structured.

  ED-Arch-07 (Causality):     THROUGH WHAT PATHWAYS influence flows.
  ED-Arch-08 (Information):   WHAT HAPPENS TO STRUCTURE as it flows.

Information theory provides the final interpretive layer. The
architecture's motifs are information-processing primitives (routers,
delays, compressors, mixers, erasers). Its laws are information
constraints (bounds on capacity, ordering of erasure, invariance of
pathways). Its geometry is information topology (helical trajectories,
radial collapse, funnel narrowing, bifurcation surfaces). Its field
theory is information dynamics (how structural content evolves). Its
energetics is information thermodynamics (why structural content
decreases). Its causality is information routing (through what
pathways structural content flows).

The informational perspective unifies all previous perspectives into
a single narrative: the ED cosmology PDE is an architecture that
takes structured inputs and simplifies them to a structureless
equilibrium through a sequence of information-processing operations
governed by invariant laws, organized by characteristic motifs, and
channeled through a definite geometric topology.

The process of becoming — the ED ontology's central concept — is,
in informational terms, the process of information flowing through
the architecture's processing pipeline from complex to simple, from
structured to uniform, from information-rich to information-free.
The architecture does not create. It does not preserve. It processes,
compresses, and erases. And the path of that erasure — through
Saddles and Spirals, along Manifolds and through Funnels, past
Horizons and across Boundaries — is the architecture's complete
informational story.
