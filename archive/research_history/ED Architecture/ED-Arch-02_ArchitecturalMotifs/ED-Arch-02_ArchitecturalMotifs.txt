1. DEFINITION OF AN ARCHITECTURAL MOTIF
========================================================================

An architectural motif, in the ED context, is a recurring structural
pattern that emerges from the PDE architecture itself rather than from
any particular choice of parameters, initial conditions, or boundary
conditions. Motifs are the irreducible geometric and dynamical shapes
that the equation generates across its entire operating range.

A motif has four defining properties:

  (i)   RECURRENCE.  It appears across multiple experiments, multiple
        parameter regimes, and multiple initial condition classes.

  (ii)  PARAMETER INDEPENDENCE.  Its qualitative character persists
        even as D, zeta, tau, and amplitude are varied. Quantitative
        details change; the structural pattern does not.

  (iii) IC INDEPENDENCE.  Whether the initial density is a Gaussian,
        a sine mode, a step function, a composite waveform, or pure
        noise, the motif still appears in some form.

  (iv)  ARCHITECTURAL EMERGENCE.  The motif is not imposed by hand. It
        arises from the interaction of the PDE's structural components:
        nonlinear diffusion, penalty regulation, and participatory
        feedback.

Motifs differ from features and behaviors in the following way:

  - A FEATURE is a specific measured quantity: a cycle count, a
    convergence rate, a harmonic amplitude. Features are data points.

  - A BEHAVIOR is a specific dynamical outcome: oscillation, monotonic
    decay, horizon formation. Behaviors are what the system does in a
    particular configuration.

  - A MOTIF is the structural pattern that generates entire families
    of behaviors and explains why certain features cluster together.
    Motifs are the architecture's vocabulary.

The relationship is hierarchical: motifs generate behaviors, and
behaviors produce features. To understand the ED cosmology PDE at the
architectural level is to understand its motifs.

2. LIST OF CORE MOTIFS
========================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
(a) THE SADDLE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       The Saddle
Definition: A sign-reversal geometry in the density gradient field,
            where spatial regions of compression (drho/dx > 0) meet
            regions of expansion (drho/dx < 0), creating a local
            extremum that acts as a dynamical pivot.

Where it appears:
  - ED-Phys-01 through ED-Phys-05: every Gaussian initial condition
    creates a saddle at the peak of the density profile.
  - ED-Phys-29 (AttractorMap): all 22 IC types generate saddle
    structures within the first few time steps.
  - ED-Phys-32 (GlobalPhasePortrait): saddle geometry visible in
    every one of the 375 trajectory runs.

Why it is structurally inevitable:
  The nonlinear diffusion operator F[rho] = M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) +
  M'(rho)*|nabla(rho)|^2 - P_SY2(rho) acts on the curvature of the
  density field. Any non-uniform density profile has at least one
  extremum. At that extremum, nabla^2(rho) changes sign. The diffusion
  term drives material away from peaks and toward troughs. The penalty
  term pulls toward rho*. These two forces create a saddle-like
  geometry in the (rho, nabla(rho)) plane that organizes all
  subsequent evolution.

How it arises from PDE architecture:
  The saddle is the geometric consequence of having both a curvature-
  sensitive diffusion operator and a density-dependent restoring force.
  The diffusion operator cares about spatial shape; the penalty cares
  about local density value. Their competition creates the saddle as
  the pivot around which all redistribution organizes.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(b) THE SPIRAL
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       The Spiral
Definition: An oscillatory, convergent trajectory in the reduced
            phase space (rho_peak, v_peak), in which the system
            approaches its equilibrium through repeated overshoots
            and undershoots with monotonically decreasing amplitude.

Where it appears:
  - ED-Phys-29: 22/22 runs oscillatory at D=0; 19/22 at D=0.5.
  - ED-Phys-30: 16/16 oscillatory approach at D=0; 13/16 at D=0.5.
  - ED-Phys-31: mean 49 cycles at D=0, zeta=0.25; mean 3.8 cycles
    at D=0.25.
  - ED-Phys-32: spiral topology dominant for D + 2*zeta < 1, with
    curvatures reaching 505,631 at (D=0, zeta=0.25).
  - ED-Phys-34: confirmed in pure oscillatory, hybrid fast, and
    boundary regimes.

Why it is structurally inevitable:
  The participation channel introduces a memory variable v that
  integrates the operator F[rho] and feeds back into the density
  equation through the H*v coupling. This creates a second-order
  dynamical system in the reduced coordinates. When the damping
  coefficient zeta is insufficient to critically damp the feedback
  loop, the system necessarily spirals. The spiral is the generic
  behavior of an under-damped second-order system.

How it arises from PDE architecture:
  The density equation contains the term H*v, and the velocity
  equation contains (1/tau)*(F[rho] - zeta*v). Together, these
  form a coupled oscillator: F[rho] drives v, v drives rho, rho
  determines F[rho]. The loop delay (controlled by tau) and the
  damping (controlled by zeta) set the spiral's tightness and
  convergence rate. The D+H=1 constraint ensures that as D
  increases, H decreases, reducing the spiral's energy source.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(c) THE HORIZON
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       The Horizon
Definition: A transient kinetic boundary formed when local density
            approaches rho_max, causing the mobility function
            M(rho) = M0*(1 - rho/rho_max)^2 to collapse toward zero
            and effectively freezing diffusive transport.

Where it appears:
  - ED-Phys-29: horizon activated in 8/22 runs at D=0 (36%),
    exclusively when amplitude A >= 40. Never at D >= 0.25.
  - ED-Phys-33 (GlobalAtlas): horizon fraction 33% at D=0, 0% at
    all other D values across 135 runs.
  - ED-Phys-34: horizon activated at D=0 for gauss_A40 initial
    condition (horizon_max = 1).

Why it is structurally inevitable:
  The mobility function M(rho) vanishes quadratically as rho
  approaches rho_max. This is not a numerical artifact; it is built
  into the definition of M. Any initial condition with amplitude
  large enough to push rho near rho_max will encounter this kinetic
  barrier. The barrier is transient because the penalty term P_SY2
  always pulls rho back toward rho* = 50, well below rho_max = 100.
  The horizon forms, persists while the density is elevated, and
  dissolves as the penalty draws the system back.

How it arises from PDE architecture:
  The horizon is the architectural consequence of finite capacity.
  M(rho) encodes the principle that density cannot grow without
  bound: as rho approaches the maximum, the system's ability to
  redistribute density through diffusion collapses. The horizon
  appears at rho approximately equal to 0.9*rho_max = 90, where
  M(rho) has fallen to roughly 1% of its maximum value. This is
  the PDE's built-in saturation mechanism.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(d) THE MANIFOLD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       The Manifold
Definition: A universal one-dimensional attracting curve in the
            infinite-dimensional state space, onto which all
            trajectories collapse regardless of initial condition,
            and along which they converge to the unique fixed point
            at (rho*, 0).

Where it appears:
  - ED-Phys-29: 1D manifold in 64/66 trajectories (97%). The two
    exceptions showed dim=2 only at D=0 with extreme amplitude.
  - ED-Phys-30: 1D manifold in 48/48 trajectories (100%) across
    all 16 probe types and all 3 cosmologies.
  - ED-Phys-31: convergence exponents consistent with 1D manifold
    across all 135 parameter combinations.
  - ED-Phys-33: global attractor confirmed as unique across all
    135 atlas runs; no secondary attractors found.

Why it is structurally inevitable:
  The PDE has a unique homogeneous equilibrium: rho = rho*
  everywhere, v = 0 everywhere. The penalty P_SY2 has exactly one
  zero at rho = rho*. The mobility M(rho) is strictly positive for
  rho < rho_max. Together, these ensure that every trajectory is
  drawn toward the same fixed point. The manifold emerges because
  the approach to this fixed point is dominated by a single slowest-
  decaying mode. Higher modes decay exponentially faster, leaving
  the dynamics effectively one-dimensional after an initial transient.

How it arises from PDE architecture:
  The manifold is the intersection of three architectural facts:
  (1) the penalty creates a unique equilibrium, (2) the diffusion
  operator erases spatial structure at a rate proportional to k^2
  (Fourier mode number squared), and (3) the participation channel
  couples all modes through the spatially integrated operator F[rho].
  The k=1 mode decays slowest, and the manifold is the trajectory
  along which k=1 relaxation occurs. The manifold's v-spread
  narrows from 0.178 in oscillatory to 0.047 in parabolic regimes,
  reflecting the loss of participation coupling as H decreases.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(e) THE BOUNDARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       The Boundary
Definition: A sharp topological transition surface in the (D, zeta)
            parameter plane, located at D + 2*zeta approximately
            equal to 1, across which the flow type switches from
            spiral to monotonic.

Where it appears:
  - ED-Phys-31: zeta=0.25 gives 91.5 mean cycles; zeta=0.50 gives
    1.78 cycles; zeta=1.0 gives 0 cycles. The transition is sharp.
  - ED-Phys-32: the full topology grid maps the boundary precisely.
    Spiral at (D=0.25, zeta=0.5), monotonic at (D=0.5, zeta=0.5).
    Spiral at (D=0.75, zeta=0.25), monotonic at (D=1.0, zeta=0.25).
    Six documented topological transitions, all unidirectional.
  - ED-Phys-33: spiral fraction drops from 56% at D=0 to 0% at
    D=1.0, with the boundary's position controlled by zeta.
  - ED-Phys-34: boundary regime at (D=0.5, zeta=0.25) confirmed
    as spiral with 6-7 cycles.

Why it is structurally inevitable:
  The participation channel's damping term is zeta*v. The diffusion
  channel's strength is D. The participation channel's strength is
  H = 1 - D. The effective damping ratio of the coupled system is
  proportional to (D + 2*zeta). When this exceeds approximately 1,
  the system is overdamped and cannot spiral. The boundary is
  therefore a consequence of the D + H = 1 constraint combined with
  the linear damping structure of the v-equation.

How it arises from PDE architecture:
  The boundary encodes the competition between two dissipation
  mechanisms: diffusive dissipation (controlled by D) and damped
  participation (controlled by zeta). Both act to suppress
  oscillations, but through different channels. The diffusion
  channel dissipates spatial structure directly. The damping term
  dissipates velocity. When their combined strength crosses the
  critical threshold, oscillation becomes impossible. The boundary
  is the locus of critical damping in the PDE's parameter space.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(f) THE MEMORY LOOP
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       The Memory Loop
Definition: The closed feedback cycle F[rho] -> v -> rho -> F[rho],
            in which the operator output drives the participation
            variable, the participation variable modifies the density,
            and the modified density changes the operator output.

Where it appears:
  - ED-Phys-27: the participation channel is the unique source of
    oscillatory dynamics. When the v-equation is perturbed with
    nonsmooth additive forcing, cycles jump from 1 to 9 and
    amplitudes increase by 5.8x, demonstrating that the memory
    loop amplifies perturbations.
  - ED-Phys-28: combined stress test confirms that the memory loop
    is the most perturbation-sensitive component. NonSmooth_All
    produces 8 cycles with 6.4x amplitude enhancement.
  - ED-Phys-30: approach direction analysis shows velocity-dominated
    approach in 7/16 oscillatory probes, confirming the loop's role
    in organizing convergence geometry.
  - ED-Phys-32: curvature values of 505,631 at (D=0, zeta=0.25)
    reflect tight spiral winding driven by the memory loop.

Why it is structurally inevitable:
  The PDE architecture contains two coupled evolution equations:
  one for rho and one for v. Whenever H > 0 (i.e., D < 1), the
  v-equation receives input from F[rho] and the rho-equation
  receives input from v. This bidirectional coupling is a closed
  loop. Closed loops with delay (tau) and insufficient damping
  (zeta) generate oscillations. This is not a special feature; it
  is the generic behavior of coupled dynamical systems.

How it arises from PDE architecture:
  The density equation: d(rho)/dt = D*F[rho] + H*v.
  The velocity equation: d(v)/dt = (1/tau)*(F[rho] - zeta*v).
  The loop is: rho determines F[rho] (through mobility, gradient,
  and penalty); F[rho] drives v (through the velocity equation);
  v drives rho (through the H*v coupling). The loop's gain is
  proportional to H/tau, and its damping is proportional to zeta.
  The memory loop is what makes ED a participatory theory rather
  than a pure diffusion equation.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(g) THE DISSIPATOR
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       The Dissipator
Definition: The diffusive channel D*F[rho], which acts directly on
            the density field without passing through the participation
            variable, producing monotonic, non-oscillatory convergence
            to equilibrium.

Where it appears:
  - ED-Phys-29: at D=1.0 (parabolic), only 2/22 runs show
    oscillatory behavior (9%), and those are transient.
  - ED-Phys-30: at D=1.0, 16/16 probes show monotonic approach,
    0/16 oscillatory. Manifold v-spread collapses to 0.047.
  - ED-Phys-31: at D=1.0, mean cycles = 0.0 across all 27 runs.
    Convergence rate lambda = 0.020, the slowest among all D values.
  - ED-Phys-32: monotonic flow type at all (D=1.0, zeta) pairs.
    Curvature drops to 10.6-72.0, reflecting near-straight
    trajectories.
  - ED-Phys-34: parabolic monotonic regime passes 12/16 checks
    (4 marginal failures are classifier artifacts at damped/monotonic
    boundary).

Why it is structurally inevitable:
  When D = 1 and H = 0, the density equation reduces to
  d(rho)/dt = F[rho], a pure nonlinear diffusion equation with
  penalty. There is no coupling to v, no feedback loop, no
  mechanism for oscillation. The system relaxes monotonically
  because F[rho] is a gradient-like operator: it always drives
  rho toward rho* through spatial smoothing and penalty regulation.

How it arises from PDE architecture:
  The dissipator is the D-channel of the unified PDE, operating
  independently of the participation variable. It inherits the
  stability properties of nonlinear diffusion: spatial gradients
  are always reduced, density always moves toward the penalty
  equilibrium, and the approach is monotonic because there is no
  second variable to create oscillation. The dissipator is the
  PDE's memoryless channel.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
(h) THE NONLINEAR TRIAD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Name:       The Nonlinear Triad
Definition: The three-component nonlinear interaction encoded in the
            operator F[rho] = M(rho)*nabla^2(rho) + M'(rho)*|nabla(rho)|^2
            - P_SY2(rho), which generates higher-harmonic content
            (especially the k=3 mode) through the coupling of density-
            dependent mobility with spatial gradient structure.

Where it appears:
  - ED-Phys-25: k3_max = 6.36, invariant across all 5 penalty
    perturbation variants to within 0.01%.
  - ED-Phys-26: k3_max = 6.44, invariant across all 5 mobility
    perturbation variants to within 0.01%.
  - ED-Phys-28: k3_max preserved even under combined stress testing.
  - ED-Phys-29: k3_max scales with initial amplitude: 0.54 at A=10,
    2.17 at A=40, 3.52 at A=80, confirming nonlinear origin.

Why it is structurally inevitable:
  The M'(rho)*|nabla(rho)|^2 term is a product of two nonlinear
  quantities: the mobility derivative and the squared gradient. In
  Fourier space, a squared gradient of a k=1 mode produces k=0 and
  k=2 content. Multiplication by a density-dependent coefficient
  (through M'(rho)) mixes these with the original k=1 mode,
  generating k=3 content. This harmonic generation is intrinsic to
  any nonlinear diffusion operator with density-dependent mobility.

How it arises from PDE architecture:
  The triad consists of three interacting components:
  (1) M(rho)*nabla^2(rho): linear diffusion modulated by density.
  (2) M'(rho)*|nabla(rho)|^2: the explicitly nonlinear term, which
      couples the rate of change of mobility to the spatial gradient
      energy. This is the harmonic generator.
  (3) P_SY2(rho): the penalty, which regulates the equilibrium
      around which the nonlinear coupling operates.
  The triad's k=3 amplitude is architecturally protected because
  it depends on the functional form of the nonlinearity (M' and
  nabla^2 coupling), not on the specific shape of M, P, or v.
  Multiplicative perturbations preserve the coupling structure;
  additive perturbations disrupt it.

3. MOTIF INTERACTIONS
========================================================================

The eight motifs do not operate in isolation. They combine to produce
the full dynamical repertoire of the ED cosmology PDE. The following
interactions are the primary generative combinations.

SPIRAL + HORIZON: OSCILLATORY SATURATION
  When the memory loop drives oscillations large enough to push rho
  near rho_max, the horizon activates and temporarily freezes
  diffusive transport. The spiral continues in the v-channel but the
  density channel stalls. This creates a characteristic flattening of
  the rho-peak near rho_max followed by release as the penalty pulls
  rho back down. Observed at D=0, A >= 40 in ED-Phys-29, where
  horizon_max reached 7 density units above the activation threshold.
  The horizon caps the spiral's amplitude without destroying the
  spiral itself.

MANIFOLD + DISSIPATOR: MONOTONIC RELAXATION
  When D = 1 (pure parabolic), the dissipator dominates and the
  manifold narrows to a v-spread of 0.047. Trajectories collapse
  onto this thin manifold and slide monotonically toward equilibrium
  with near-zero curvature (10.6 at D=1.0, zeta=1.0). The manifold
  geometry becomes almost a straight line in phase space. This is
  the simplest motif interaction: a one-dimensional attractor
  traversed without oscillation.

MEMORY LOOP + PENALTY: RINGING AROUND EQUILIBRIUM
  The penalty defines the target rho*. The memory loop creates
  oscillatory approach. Their interaction produces the characteristic
  ringing pattern: rho overshoots rho*, F[rho] reverses sign, v
  accumulates in the opposite direction, rho undershoots rho*, and
  the cycle repeats with decreasing amplitude. The ringing frequency
  and decay rate are set by the loop gain (H/tau) and damping (zeta).
  At D=0, zeta=0.25, this interaction produces 49 mean cycles
  (ED-Phys-31) with curvatures exceeding 500,000 (ED-Phys-32).

BOUNDARY + SPIRAL: TOPOLOGICAL TRANSITION
  The boundary motif governs the existence of the spiral motif. Below
  D + 2*zeta = 1, spirals exist; above it, they do not. At the
  boundary itself, the spiral's amplitude decays to near-zero in
  approximately 1-2 cycles, producing a critically damped transient
  that is neither clearly oscillatory nor clearly monotonic. The four
  classifier failures in ED-Phys-34 (93% pass rate) all occurred at
  or near this boundary, where the damped transient is too weak for
  the oscillation detector but too structured for the monotonic
  classifier. The boundary is where the spiral motif dies.

SADDLE + NONLINEAR TRIAD: HARMONIC GENERATION
  The saddle geometry at the density peak creates the spatial
  structure (gradients, curvatures) on which the nonlinear triad
  operates. The M'(rho)*|nabla(rho)|^2 term couples the saddle's
  gradient field to the density-dependent mobility derivative,
  generating the k=3 harmonic. The saddle sets the stage; the triad
  performs the spectral transfer. Without the saddle (i.e., in a
  perfectly uniform field), the triad has nothing to act on.

HORIZON + DISSIPATOR: TRANSIENT BARRIER
  In the pure parabolic regime (D=1), the horizon can still form if
  the initial amplitude is large enough, but the dissipator erases
  it rapidly because there is no memory loop to sustain oscillations
  that might drive rho back toward rho_max. The horizon appears and
  dissolves in a single pass. In the oscillatory regime (D=0), the
  memory loop can repeatedly drive rho back toward the horizon,
  creating recurring barrier encounters.

MEMORY LOOP + MANIFOLD: SPIRAL WINDING ON THE ATTRACTOR
  The manifold provides the one-dimensional curve; the memory loop
  provides the oscillatory motion along and around that curve. The
  resulting trajectory is a spiral wound tightly onto the manifold
  surface. The manifold's curvature (up to 69,277 in ED-Phys-30)
  reflects the memory loop's winding. As zeta increases, the winding
  loosens, the curvature drops, and the spiral degenerates into
  monotonic sliding.

4. MOTIF STABILITY
========================================================================

The robustness experiments ED-Phys-25 through ED-Phys-28 tested which
motifs survive perturbation and which break. The results reveal a
sharp architectural distinction between perturbation types.

MULTIPLICATIVE PERTURBATIONS (PRESERVE MOTIF STRUCTURE)
  Multiplicative perturbations modify the amplitude or shape of a
  component without changing its qualitative coupling to the rest of
  the architecture. Examples: steeper mobility, flatter penalty,
  stronger damping. Under multiplicative perturbation:

  - The Saddle survives: gradient sign-reversal geometry is preserved.
  - The Spiral survives: cycle count unchanged (1 cycle at D=0.5
    across all 4 multiplicative mobility variants in ED-Phys-26).
  - The Horizon survives: activation threshold shifts slightly but
    the mechanism persists.
  - The Manifold survives: 1D dimensionality maintained.
  - The Boundary survives: spiral-monotonic transition location
    shifts by at most one grid point.
  - The Memory Loop survives: loop gain changes quantitatively but
    loop structure is preserved.
  - The Dissipator survives: monotonic convergence still monotonic.
  - The Nonlinear Triad survives: k3_max varies by < 0.01% across
    all multiplicative variants.

  Quantitative evidence: in ED-Phys-26, undershoot varies by less
  than 3% across 5 mobility variants. Relaxation time varies by less
  than 4%. Boundary structure (cycle counts at D=0.44, 0.46, 0.48)
  is identical across all variants.

NONSMOOTH ADDITIVE PERTURBATIONS (BREAK MOTIF STRUCTURE)
  Additive perturbations inject new dynamical content that is not
  modulated by the existing architecture. They bypass the coupling
  structure rather than modifying it. Under additive perturbation:

  - The Spiral AMPLIFIES: cycles jump from 1 to 9 (ED-Phys-27)
    or from 1 to 8 (ED-Phys-28). Amplitude increases by 5.8-6.4x.
  - The Boundary SHIFTS: at D=0.44, canonical gives 3 cycles but
    nonsmooth gives 11 cycles (ED-Phys-27). The boundary location
    moves far from D + 2*zeta = 1.
  - The Memory Loop OVERDRIVES: peak v increases from 0.148 to 0.634
    (4.3x), indicating the loop gain has been artificially boosted.
  - The Manifold PERSISTS: even nonsmooth perturbations do not break
    the 1D manifold dimension. The system still converges to the
    same fixed point. The topology is preserved; the geometry is not.
  - The Nonlinear Triad PERSISTS: k3_max is preserved even under
    additive perturbation, because the triad's harmonic generation
    depends on the coupling structure of nabla^2 and M', not on the
    v-equation.
  - The Horizon is NOT TESTED: nonsmooth runs at D=0.5 do not
    approach rho_max.

WHY THE DISTINCTION EXISTS
  Multiplicative perturbations can be written as M(rho) -> M(rho)*g(rho)
  for some modifying function g. This preserves the zeros, the sign
  structure, and the coupling topology of the original operator. The
  perturbed system has the same motif vocabulary as the original; only
  the quantitative parameters of each motif change.

  Additive perturbations can be written as v_update -> v_update + h(rho)
  for some forcing function h. This injects energy into the system at
  every time step, regardless of the system's current state. The
  forcing does not respect the architecture's coupling structure. It
  pumps the memory loop from outside, creating oscillations that the
  natural damping cannot suppress.

  The architectural principle: the PDE's motif structure is encoded in
  the coupling topology (which components talk to which, through what
  functional forms). Multiplicative perturbations modify the conversation
  without changing who talks to whom. Additive perturbations introduce
  a new speaker who talks out of turn.

  ALWAYS PRESENT (all parameter values, all ICs):

    - Spiral:          requires D + 2*zeta < 1
    - Memory Loop:     requires H > 0 (i.e., D < 1)

    - Manifold NARROWS: v-spread drops from 0.178 to 0.047
    - Horizon DISAPPEARS: not accessible for D > 0

    - Spiral DIES: cycles drop (91.5 -> 1.78 -> 0)
    - Memory Loop DAMPENS: loop energy absorbed by zeta*v term
    - Curvature COLLAPSES: 505,631 -> 209 -> 10.6

  as the critical curve separating two qualitatively different
  motif configurations:
    - Below the boundary: Spiral + Memory Loop + Saddle + Triad + Manifold
    - Above the boundary: Dissipator + Saddle + Triad + Manifold

  The transition is sharp, unidirectional, and has been observed at
  all 6 documented topological transition points in ED-Phys-32.
  No reverse transitions (monotonic -> spiral) have ever been observed
  in any of the 1,000+ runs across the entire ED-Phys series.

6. UNIFIED INTERPRETATION

WHAT THE MOTIFS REVEAL ABOUT ED AS AN ARCHITECTURAL THEORY

The eight architectural motifs of the ED cosmology PDE are not ad hoc
observations. They form a complete structural vocabulary: a finite set
of irreducible patterns from which all observed behaviors can be
composed. This is what makes ED an architectural theory rather than
merely a dynamical system.

A dynamical system has solutions. An architectural theory has motifs.
The distinction matters because solutions are particular (this initial
condition at these parameter values produces this trajectory), while
motifs are universal (the spiral appears whenever under-damping exists,
regardless of specifics). The motif level of description is the correct
abstraction for understanding what the PDE does across its entire
operating range.

THE MOTIF ABSTRACTION LAYER

The motifs sit between two other levels of description:

  PDE MECHANICS (below):
    Specific terms in the equation: M(rho)*nabla^2(rho),
    M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2, P_SY2(rho), H*v, (1/tau)(F[rho]-zeta*v).
    These are the equation's components. They are not self-explanatory:
    knowing that M'(rho)|nabla(rho)|^2 appears in the operator does
    not immediately reveal that it generates k=3 harmonics.

  OBSERVED BEHAVIORS (above):
    Specific measured outcomes: 49 cycles at D=0, convergence rate
    0.091 at (D=0.25, zeta=0.25), k3_max = 6.44. These are data
    points. They are accurate but not explanatory: knowing that
    there are 49 cycles does not reveal why cycles exist.

  MOTIFS (between):
    Structural patterns that connect PDE mechanics to observed
    behaviors. The Memory Loop motif explains why cycles exist (the
    F -> v -> rho -> F feedback with insufficient damping). The
    Boundary motif explains where cycles stop (D + 2*zeta = 1). The
    Nonlinear Triad explains why k3 is generated (M' coupling to
    gradient squared). Motifs are the causal link between the
    equation's structure and the system's behavior.

HOW MOTIFS UNIFY THE EXPERIMENTAL RECORD

The 34 ED-Phys experiments and the ED-Arch-01 synthesis produced over
1,000 simulation runs spanning initial conditions from single Gaussians
to random noise, parameters from (D=0, zeta=0.25) to (D=1, zeta=1),
and perturbation classes from smooth multiplicative to nonsmooth
additive. The diversity of measured features across these runs is
enormous: cycle counts from 0 to 235, convergence rates from 0.006 to
0.139, curvatures from 10.6 to 505,631.

Yet this diversity is generated by exactly eight motifs and their
interactions. Every observed behavior can be decomposed into a
combination of motifs:

  - 235 cycles at D=0, A=80: Memory Loop at full gain, Horizon
    repeatedly activated, Spiral winding tightly on the Manifold.

  - 0 cycles at D=1.0: Dissipator dominant, Memory Loop absent,
    Manifold traversed monotonically.

  - 6 cycles at D=0.5, zeta=0.25: Memory Loop at half gain, Spiral
    near the Boundary, Saddle and Triad active.

  - k3_max = 6.44 invariant across perturbations: Nonlinear Triad
    protected by multiplicative coupling architecture.

  - 93% pass rate in verification: Manifold, Saddle, and Triad
    universally confirmed; 4 failures localized to the Boundary
    where Spiral and Dissipator are in competition.

The motifs are not merely descriptive. They are predictive. Given a
parameter point (D, zeta, tau), the motif map predicts which motifs
will be active, how they will interact, and what qualitative behavior
will result. The 93% verification rate in ED-Phys-34 confirms that
the motif-level description has genuine predictive power.

THE RULES OF BECOMING

In the ED ontology, the fundamental question is not what exists but
how things come to be: the rules of becoming. The eight motifs are
the rules of becoming for density fields governed by the unified
PDE. They encode:

  - How structure forms (Saddle: gradient geometry organizes flow)
  - How structure oscillates (Spiral + Memory Loop: under-damped
    feedback creates ringing)
  - How structure saturates (Horizon: finite capacity creates
    transient barriers)
  - How structure dissipates (Dissipator: diffusion erases gradients)
  - How structure generates complexity (Nonlinear Triad: harmonic
    transfer creates spectral richness)
  - How structure converges (Manifold: all trajectories collapse to
    one dimension)
  - How regimes change (Boundary: topology transitions at critical
    damping)

Together, these motifs constitute the architectural grammar of the ED
cosmology PDE. They are the minimal structural vocabulary needed to
describe everything the equation can do. Nothing in the experimental
record lies outside their scope.
