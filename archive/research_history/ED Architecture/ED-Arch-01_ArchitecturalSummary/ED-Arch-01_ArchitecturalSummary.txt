1. THE UNIFIED COSMOLOGICAL EQUATION
======================================

1.1 Canonical Form

The unified ED cosmology PDE in one spatial dimension is:

    d rho / dt  =  D * F[rho]  +  H * v
    d v   / dt  =  (1/tau) * ( F[rho]  -  zeta * v )

with the constraint  D + H = 1,  and the canonical operator:

    F[rho]  =  M(rho) * laplacian(rho)
             + M'(rho) * |grad(rho)|^2
             - P_SY2(rho)

where:

    M(rho)    =  M0 * max(1 - rho/rho_max, 0)^2      [mobility]
    P_SY2(rho) =  alpha * gamma * (rho - rho*)
                  / sqrt((rho - rho*)^2 + rho_0^2)    [restoring penalty]

Canonical parameters:  alpha=0.1, gamma=0.5, M0=1.0, rho_max=100,
rho*=50, rho_0=0.5, tau in {0.5, 1.0, 2.0}, zeta in {0.25, 0.50, 1.00}.


1.2 Three Architectural Channels

The PDE decomposes into three structural components:

(a) Restoring penalty P(rho).  The penalty P_SY2 provides a smooth,
    bounded restoring force toward the equilibrium density rho*. Its
    saturating form (approaching alpha*gamma at large deviations)
    prevents unbounded forcing while ensuring universal attraction.

(b) Mobility M(rho).  The parabolic mobility M(rho) controls the rate
    of density diffusion. It vanishes at the saturation boundary
    rho = rho_max, enforcing a hard ceiling on density. Its derivative
    M'(rho) couples gradient energy into the dynamics through the
    |grad rho|^2 term.

(c) Participation channel v.  The velocity field v acts as a memory
    of the forcing history. It is driven by F[rho] and damped by zeta,
    with the timescale tau controlling how quickly v responds. When
    H > 0, the participation channel feeds back into the density
    equation, enabling oscillatory dynamics.


1.3 Equilibrium and Invariants

The unique equilibrium is (rho, v) = (rho*, 0), where rho* = 50.
At equilibrium: M(rho*) = 0.25, P_SY2(rho*) = 0, F[rho*] = 0, v = 0.

This equilibrium is:
  - Globally attracting (confirmed across 66 ICs, 135 parameter combos)
  - Unique (no secondary fixed points anywhere in parameter space)
  - Structurally stable (preserved under all tested perturbations)



2. ARCHITECTURAL INVARIANTS
=============================

The following properties hold across ALL tested parameter combinations,
initial conditions, and perturbation classes (ED-Phys-01 through 34):


2.1 Unique Global Attractor

Every trajectory in every experiment converges to (rho*, 0). This was
tested across:
  - 22 distinct IC types (Gaussians, sines, composites, steps, noise)
  - 3 cosmologies (oscillatory, hybrid, parabolic)
  - 135 parameter combinations (D x zeta x tau x A)
  - 375 phase-portrait trajectories
  - 5 perturbation classes (penalty, mobility, participation variants)

Total trajectories tested: >700. Secondary attractors found: zero.


2.2 One-Dimensional Stable Manifold

All trajectories collapse onto a 1D curve in (rho_peak, v_peak) phase
space, regardless of IC shape or entry direction. This was confirmed in
ED-Phys-30 across 48 manifold probes:
  - Oscillatory: 21/22 runs show dim=1 (one case dim=2 at extreme A)
  - Hybrid: 22/22 runs show dim=1
  - Parabolic: 22/22 runs show dim=1
  - v-spread < 0.18 across all cosmologies


2.3 No Bifurcations or Chaos

The flow topology varies continuously across the (D, zeta, tau) space.
No period-doubling, Hopf bifurcations, or chaotic attractors were
detected. All topological transitions are smooth: spiral -> damped ->
monotonic, with no reverse transitions or hysteresis.


2.4 Horizon Activation Rules

The density saturation boundary (rho -> 0.9 * rho_max) activates only
under specific conditions:
  - Requires A >= 40 (large initial perturbations)
  - Requires D = 0 (pure oscillatory channel)
  - Never activates for D >= 0.25 at any amplitude tested
  - 33% of D=0 runs activate horizons; 0% for D > 0

Horizon activation is transient: horizons form and dissolve as the
density relaxes to rho*. They do not create persistent structures.


2.5 Nonlinear Coupling Invariance

The k=3 Fourier mode amplitude (a measure of nonlinear harmonic
generation) remains essentially constant across all perturbation
classes at k3_max ~ 6.44. This invariance holds for:
  - All 5 penalty variants (ED-Phys-25)
  - All 5 mobility variants (ED-Phys-26)
  - All 5 participation variants (ED-Phys-27)
  - All 5 combined stress variants (ED-Phys-28)

The nonlinear coupling structure is architecturally protected.


2.6 Parameter Monotonicities

Three strict monotonicities hold across the full parameter space:

  D:    Increasing D monotonically decreases oscillation count,
        undershoot depth, spiral fraction, and horizon activation.
        Convergence rate peaks near D=0.25 and decreases toward D=1.

  zeta: Increasing zeta monotonically suppresses oscillations.
        zeta=0.25: 92 cycles avg, 73% spiral
        zeta=0.50: 1.8 cycles avg, 40% spiral
        zeta=1.00: 0 cycles, 0% spiral
        Zeta is the dominant oscillation control parameter.

  tau:  Tau has weak effect on convergence rate (varies < 5% across
        tau = 0.5 to 2.0). It modulates cycle count but does not
        change the qualitative behavior or flow topology.



3. PHASE STRUCTURE
====================

The unified PDE exhibits three distinct dynamical phases, controlled
primarily by D and zeta:


3.1 Oscillatory Regime (D small, zeta small)

  Conditions: D < 0.25, zeta < 0.50
  Character:  Many oscillation cycles (14-800+), spiral manifold,
              slow convergence (lambda ~ 0.002-0.005)
  Horizon:    Activates at high amplitudes when D = 0
  Manifold:   Tightly wound spiral in (rho, v) space
  Physics:    Participation channel dominates; density oscillates
              around rho* with slow amplitude decay


3.2 Hybrid Regime (intermediate D and zeta)

  Conditions: D ~ 0.25-0.50, zeta ~ 0.25-0.50
  Character:  Few oscillation cycles (1-10), fastest convergence
              (lambda up to 0.091 at D=0.25, zeta=0.25)
  Horizon:    Never activates
  Manifold:   Lightly damped spiral or overdamped approach
  Physics:    Optimal balance of parabolic dissipation and oscillatory
              energy extraction; fastest convergence in the system


3.3 Parabolic Regime (D large, zeta large)

  Conditions: D > 0.75 or zeta > 0.50
  Character:  Zero oscillation cycles, monotonic convergence,
              moderate rate (lambda ~ 0.006-0.02)
  Horizon:    Never activates
  Manifold:   Straight-line approach along density axis
  Physics:    Pure diffusive relaxation; v-channel inactive or
              heavily damped


3.4 Spiral-Monotonic Boundary

The transition between spiral and monotonic flow follows the
approximate rule:

    D + 2*zeta ~ 1

Below this boundary: spiral dynamics dominate.
Above this boundary: monotonic dynamics dominate.

The transition is smooth (no bifurcation). At the boundary, trajectories
show a few damped oscillations before settling into monotonic approach.
18 topological transitions were mapped across the full (D, zeta, tau)
space; all are spiral-to-monotonic with no reverse transitions.



4. MANIFOLD GEOMETRY
======================

4.1 Universal 1D Manifold

Regardless of initial condition shape (Gaussian, sinusoidal, step,
noise, composite), all trajectories collapse onto a single 1D curve
in (rho_peak, v_peak) phase space. The manifold is the same whether
the trajectory enters from the density direction, the velocity
direction, or any mixed direction.

This universality was tested with 16 distinct manifold probes across
3 cosmologies (ED-Phys-30), confirming that the manifold is an
intrinsic geometric property of the PDE, not an artifact of the IC.


4.2 Spiral vs Monotonic Approach

The manifold geometry depends on the cosmology:

  Oscillatory (D=0):  Tightly wound spiral, 200-470 zero-crossings,
                       all 16 probes spiral. Mean curvature nonzero.

  Hybrid (D=0.5):     Lightly damped spiral (2-5 crossings for most
                       probes). 13/16 oscillatory, 3/16 monotonic.
                       Near-zero curvature.

  Parabolic (D=1):    Always monotonic (0 crossings from density ICs).
                       Tightest v-spread (0.047). Zero curvature.


4.3 Convergence Rate Hierarchy

                                   Mean lambda    Std
  Hybrid (D=0.5, zeta=0.5):        0.0148       0.0065
  Parabolic (D=1.0, zeta=1.0):     0.0059       0.0006
  Oscillatory (D=0.0, zeta=0.25):  0.0023       0.0004

The hybrid channel converges fastest because it combines diffusive
dissipation with oscillatory energy extraction. Pure parabolic is
intermediate. Pure oscillatory is slowest, trading speed for extensive
ringing.

Across the full parameter space (ED-Phys-33):
  Fastest: lambda = 0.139  (D=0.0, zeta=0.25, tau=0.5, A=10)
  Slowest: lambda = 0.006  (D=1.0, zeta=1.0, any tau, A=40)


4.4 Curvature Structure

Manifold curvature is nonzero only in the oscillatory regime (D=0)
at extreme amplitudes. In hybrid and parabolic regimes, the manifold
is effectively flat (zero curvature near equilibrium). This means the
approach to equilibrium is locally linear in the damped regimes and
nonlinear only in the spiral regime.


4.5 Parameter-Dependent Deformation

The manifold deforms continuously across parameter space:
  - Increasing D unwinds the spiral toward a radial line
  - Increasing zeta compresses the spiral amplitude
  - Tau shifts the spiral pitch but preserves its topology
  - A scales the entry point but does not change the manifold shape

No topological changes (tearing, folding, or splitting) occur at any
parameter values. The manifold is a single connected curve everywhere.



5. ROBUSTNESS ARCHITECTURE
=============================

ED-Phys-25 through ED-Phys-28 tested the architectural stability of
the PDE under perturbations to each structural component.


5.1 Penalty Robustness (ED-Phys-25)

Five penalty variants tested (steeper, flatter, asymmetric, nonsmooth,
and a re-parameterized form). Results:
  - All multiplicative variants: 1 cycle, undershoot 2.1-2.3,
    identical boundary structure (3c/2c/1c at D=0.44/0.46/0.48)
  - Nonsmooth additive variant: 0 cycles, different relaxation
  - k3 coupling: invariant across all variants


5.2 Mobility Robustness (ED-Phys-26)

Five mobility variants tested at epsilon = 0.05. Results:
  - All variants: exactly 1 cycle at D=0.50
  - Undershoot range: 2.10-2.18 (< 3% variation)
  - Relaxation time: 664-688 (< 4% variation)
  - Boundary structure: identical across all 5 variants
  - k3 coupling: 6.4413-6.4415 (< 0.01% variation)

The mobility channel is the most robust of the three components.


5.3 Participation Robustness (ED-Phys-27)

Five participation variants tested. Results:
  - Multiplicative variants (stronger, weaker, asymmetric):
    1 cycle, metrics within 3% of canonical
  - Nonsmooth additive variant (+epsilon*tanh(F)):
    9 cycles, 6x deeper undershoot, 4x participation amplitude,
    boundary completely destroyed (10-11 cycles at all D values)

The participation channel is the most sensitive to additive
perturbations because the tanh(F) term injects energy that does not
self-cancel at equilibrium.


5.4 Combined Stress Test (ED-Phys-28)

All three components perturbed simultaneously. Results:
  - Smooth-All (multiplicative): 1 cycle, within 3% of canonical
  - Mixed-Signs (M up, P/v down): 1 cycle, within 5%
  - Asymmetric-All: 1 cycle, undershoot +8%, boundary marginally shifts
  - NonSmooth-All: 8 cycles, 6x undershoot, boundary destroyed


5.5 Structural Principle

The robustness results reveal a clean architectural rule:

    MULTIPLICATIVE perturbations preserve the architecture.
    ADDITIVE perturbations break it.

This is because multiplicative perturbations scale the existing
forces proportionally. At equilibrium, where F = 0, the perturbation
also vanishes: (1 + epsilon) * 0 = 0. The equilibrium and its
stability are preserved.

Additive perturbations inject a constant forcing term that persists
at equilibrium: 0 + epsilon*tanh(0) = 0, but epsilon*tanh(F) != 0
during transients. The energy injection does not self-cancel, driving
persistent oscillations that overwhelm the natural damping.

This principle generalizes: the ED architecture is stable under any
perturbation class that vanishes at the fixed point and scales
proportionally to the canonical forcing away from it.

7. UNIFIED INTERPRETATION
============================

7.1 What the Architecture Means

The unified ED cosmology PDE describes a density field rho(x,t) that
is universally attracted to a homogeneous equilibrium rho*. The
dynamics are controlled by the interplay of three architectural
channels: diffusion (via mobility M), restoring force (via penalty P),
and memory (via participation v).

The PDE is not merely a diffusion equation with an oscillatory addon.
The three channels form a coupled system whose global behavior --
unique attractor, 1D manifold, spiral-to-monotonic transition, horizon
activation -- emerges from their interaction. No single channel
produces this behavior alone.


7.2 How the Components Interact

The operator F[rho] combines diffusion and penalty into a single
forcing term that drives both rho and v. The density equation mixes
F with the participation channel through the weights D and H = 1 - D.
The v-equation acts as a bandpass filter on F: it amplifies sustained
forcing (creating oscillations) while damping transient forcing
(enabling convergence).

The D/H balance controls how much of the forcing goes directly into
density (parabolic channel) versus through the participation memory
(oscillatory channel). At D = 1, forcing is instantaneous. At D = 0,
all forcing is mediated by v, creating maximal oscillatory behavior.


7.3 Why the PDE Is Structurally Stable

The structural stability of the architecture rests on three properties:

(a) The penalty P_SY2 is bounded and monotone. It can never produce
    unbounded forcing, and it always points toward rho*. This
    guarantees global attraction.

(b) The mobility M vanishes at the saturation boundary. This prevents
    density from exceeding rho_max, providing a hard geometric
    constraint on the state space.

(c) The v-equation is linearly damped. The term -zeta*v ensures that
    participation memory decays exponentially in the absence of
    forcing, preventing runaway oscillations.

Together, these three properties create a dissipative structure:
energy is always extracted from the system, never injected. The only
way to break this is through additive perturbations that inject
energy independently of the system state -- precisely the failure
mode identified in ED-Phys-27 and ED-Phys-28.


7.4 How ED Dynamics Emerge from Architectural Rules

The emergent dynamics of the PDE -- oscillatory ringing, horizon
formation, mode coupling, spiral manifolds -- are not imposed by
hand. They arise from the architectural rules:

  Oscillations emerge because the v-channel creates a delayed
  feedback loop: F drives v, which drives rho, which changes F.
  The delay (set by tau) and damping (set by zeta) determine whether
  the feedback is underdamped (spiral) or overdamped (monotonic).

  Horizons emerge because high-amplitude perturbations push rho
  toward rho_max, where M -> 0 and diffusion freezes. This creates
  transient density ceilings that dissolve as the penalty pulls rho
  back toward rho*.

  Mode coupling emerges because F[rho] is nonlinear: the M'|grad rho|^2
  term generates harmonic content from smooth initial conditions. The
  k=3 mode amplitude of ~6.44 is set by the balance between nonlinear
  generation and penalty-driven relaxation.

  The 1D manifold emerges because the v-channel is slaved to rho at
  long times: when F -> 0, v -> 0 exponentially, so the late-time
  dynamics are determined entirely by the density profile.

None of these behaviors are parameters of the model. They are
consequences of the architecture.
