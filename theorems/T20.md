**Status:** FORCED-unconditional (ratified 2026-04-27).
**Layer:** Theorem-level structural result.

## Summary

States that the gravitational transition acceleration is FORCED at the substrate level via a dipole-mode azimuthal-periodicity mechanism:

`a₀ = c · H₀ / (2π) ≈ 1.08 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s²`

Derivation: an accelerating chain breaks the three-dimensional isotropy of its participation adjacency, producing a privileged axis along the acceleration direction. The cosmic decoupling surface (radius `R_H = c/H₀`) projects onto the chain's accessible region not as an isotropic full-sphere integration but as a directional projection. The leading anisotropic mode coupling to the chain's adjacency structure is the dipole `(l = 1, m = 0)` aligned with the acceleration axis, whose only spatial periodicity is the `2π` azimuthal cycle. Setting the chain's experienced fluctuation rate `γ_chain = a/c` equal to the dipole-mediated effective cosmic rate `γ_cosmic^eff = H₀ / (2π)` yields the transition acceleration. The 2π factor is geometric (azimuthal period of the leading anisotropic projection mode), not phenomenological. Parameter-free; matches the empirical MOND constant `a₀^(emp) ≈ 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s²` to within ~10% and is robust to the Hubble tension at ~15%. Originated in the substrate-gravity arc (arc-SG, §§4–5 of paper).
