# Event Density

**A constitutive ontology for physics — from laboratory diffusion to galactic dynamics.**

Event Density (ED) begins with a single premise: physical structure arises from the local rate of becoming. From four primitives and seven axioms, a unique PDE is derived whose three channels correspond exactly to exponential decay, porous-medium diffusion, and telegraph oscillation. The same mobility law fits three condensed-matter systems with a mean exponent of 2.00 ± 0.29. Coupled to gravity, the PDE produces naturally cored galaxy halos matching observations to within 10%. The temporal-tension channel predicts indefinitely flat circular velocities to 1 Mpc — consistent with recent weak-lensing data — and derives the baryonic Tully-Fisher relation as a scaling law with the observed exponent 1/4.

Two predictions uniquely distinguish ED from both MOND and ΛCDM: activity-dependent weak-lensing velocities, and temporal-tension lags in merging clusters. Both are testable with current surveys.
