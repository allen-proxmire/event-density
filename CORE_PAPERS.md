# Core Papers — Reading Order

The ED programme comprises four canonical papers, read in this order.

---

## 1. The Foundational Paper

**File:** [`manuscript/foundational_paper/ED_Foundational_Paper.md`](documents/manuscript/foundational_paper/ED_Foundational_Paper.md)

Derives the canonical ED PDE from seven axioms. Constructs six structural analogues testing the three channels against known physical laws: RC decay, Barenblatt self-similarity, Stefan horizons, telegraph oscillation, oscillation-modulated PME, and temporal tension. Five produce quantitative matches; one produces an informative negative result; one discovers an emergent pair interaction. Appendix G extends three analogues to 3D, confirming dimension-independence.

*What it establishes:* The ED PDE is unique, its channels correspond to real physics, and the architecture is falsifiable.
*Read next:* The Dimensional Atlas.

## 2. The Dimensional Atlas

**File:** [`research/ED PAPERS/ED-Dimensional-Master_The_Unified_Atlas.md`](documents/research/ED%20PAPERS/ED-Dimensional-Master_The_Unified_Atlas.md)

Maps the canonical PDE to physical units across five regimes — quantum (ℏ/2m), Planck (√(ℏG/c)), condensed matter (thermal diffusivity), galactic (v_circ · L₀), and cosmological (c²/H₀) — spanning 61 orders of magnitude in length. Establishes cross-regime invariants, the anchoring hierarchy, and the nondimensional identity D_phys · T₀/L₀² = D_nd in all regimes.

*What it establishes:* The same PDE governs physics at every scale; the dimensional framework is consistent.
*Read next:* The Mathematical Foundations.

## 4. The Synthesis Paper

**File:** [`research/ED PAPERS/ED-Final_Synthesis_EventDensity_Physics.md`](documents/research/ED%20PAPERS/ED-Final_Synthesis_EventDensity_Physics.md)

Connects the ED ontology to empirical physics at two scales. In condensed matter: the mobility law fits three materials with β = 2.00 ± 0.29. At galactic scales: the ED–Poisson system produces cored halos, the temporal-tension channel predicts the BTFR with exponent 1/4, and weak-lensing flatness to 1 Mpc. Derives two ED-unique predictions (activity dependence, merger lag) that distinguish ED from both MOND and ΛCDM.

*What it establishes:* ED makes quantitative contact with real data and produces falsifiable predictions that can be tested with current surveys.
