## 2. Summary of Results Across BH-2 to BH-6

Universal across black-hole / Rindler / cosmological / acoustic horizons: all are continuum shadows of substrate decoupling surfaces. Not a primitive geometric boundary; an emergent statistical feature of the bandwidth-suppression mechanism.

**BH-3 — Singularity-as-Manifold-Artifact + Strong-Curvature Audit.**

Replacement: finite-thickness saturated participation zone where ED-gradients reach maximum viable value, cross-bandwidth collapses in all directions, only commitment-order direction remains structurally meaningful, substrate dynamics remain finite.

**BH-4 — Information vs Entanglement + Evaporation.**
Committed structure (P11-irreversible participation history) blocked at horizon; entanglement (uncommitted correlation potential) straddles. Information-blocking is participation-bandwidth prohibition, not causal prohibition. Evaporation = participation re-routing through pair-creation-class events at the decoupling surface: one leg commits inward, one outward, joint correlation preserved globally. **No information paradox** — the four paradox-generating assumptions (global unitarity, global Cauchy data, geometric boundary, monogamy at boundary) are not imposed at substrate level. No firewall, no complementarity contradiction.

**BH-5 — Area-Law Entropy + 1/4 Coefficient.**

Closed-form $\log g$ from substrate constants not currently in hand. No observational tension (BH entropy coefficient not directly measured).

**BH-6 — Wave-BH Scattering (substrate derivation).**

## 3. Structural Integration: The ED Black-Hole Architecture

The six memos integrate into a single architectural picture:

- **Formation.** Steep participation-density gradients drive $|\nabla\rho|\,\ell_P^2/\rho_\mathrm{local}$ across the threshold $\log(R_\mathrm{cg}/\ell_P)$; cross-bandwidth collapses; decoupling surface $\Sigma_H$ emerges (BH-2).
- **Interior.** Gradients continue to steepen but saturate at the substrate-imposed maximum; finite-thickness saturated zone replaces the singular endpoint of the manifold extrapolation (BH-3).
- **Information.** Committed structure cannot cross $\Sigma_H$ (bandwidth-blocked); entanglement amplitude can straddle the surface (does not require ongoing cross-bandwidth) (BH-4).
- **Thermal behavior.** Asymmetric participation flow at the saturated decoupling surface produces, at the coarse-grained continuum level, the statistical features standardly read as thermal (BH-4 sets the mechanism; spectrum derivation deferred to B4).
- **Entropy.** Area-law from participation capacity of $\Sigma_H$: $S = (A/\ell_P^2)\log g$ (BH-5).
- **Scattering.** Minimal ED-channel acquires global phase shift through saturated-gradient exterior region; helicity behavior and Kerr twist follow from anisotropy-basis transport (BH-6).
- **Evaporation.** Pair-creation-class events at the decoupling surface route participation outward and inward; correlation structure preserved globally; no committed structure destroyed (BH-4).

The architecture is **internally consistent** across all six memos: the decoupling-surface condition $|\nabla\rho|\,\ell_P^2/\rho_\mathrm{local} \gtrsim \log(R_\mathrm{cg}/\ell_P)$ governs horizon formation (BH-2), interior saturation (BH-3), information blocking (BH-4), participation-capacity saturation (BH-5), and the strong-curvature scattering region (BH-6). One substrate-level condition supports the entire architecture.

**No singularity. No information paradox. No global unitarity requirement. No firewall. No complementarity contradiction. No geometric-boundary primitive.** The standard puzzles are not solved within a paradox-generating framework; they are not generated in the first place, because the ED substrate does not impose the assumptions that generate them.

- Absence of singularities — three independent substrate constraints jointly forbid divergence (BH-3).

- BHPT phase-shift structure as global path-integral invariant — channel-action machinery + minimality of channel (BH-6).
- Helicity preservation/flip mechanism — anisotropy-basis transport through axisymmetric vs frame-dragging backgrounds (BH-6).
- Kerr twist as integrated vorticity — frame-dragging acts on the channel's polarization frame along its trajectory (BH-6).
