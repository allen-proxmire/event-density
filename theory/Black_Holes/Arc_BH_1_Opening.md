## 1. Purpose

This arc consolidates the Event Density program's substantial pre-existing structural content on black-hole architecture into a single substrate-grounded account. The arc:

- **Consolidates the existing ED-I black-hole architecture** dispersed across four papers (ED-06 *Horizons as Event Density Decoupling Surfaces*; ED-10 *Emergence of Spacetime, Geometry, Information*; ED-I-06 *Fields and Forces in Event Density*; ED-I-26 *Wave–Black-Hole Scattering*) into a single coherent arc trajectory.
- **Grounds the interpretive claims of ED-10 + ED-I-06 + ED-I-26 in DCGT substrate derivations.** The existing ED-I content is largely *interpretive* — articulating the structural reading without working out the substrate-derivation chain. This arc routes the content through Arc D (DCGT) + Arc ED-10 (acoustic-metric scalar-tensor) machinery to produce explicit form-FORCED / value-INHERITED demarcation parallel to the rest of the program.
- **Audits the strong-curvature regime** where the acoustic-metric approximation may fail. Arc ED-10 closure (condition C5) flagged that the acoustic-metric kinematic reading requires Lorentzian-non-degenerate substrate participation density — a condition that may break down in BH interiors approaching the standard-GR singularity. The arc tests whether the framework's machinery extends cleanly to strong-curvature, identifies a structural-conditional content if not.
- **Derives the area-law entropy proportionality constant** (1/4 factor or alternative). ED-10 §5.6 establishes the area-law form FORCED by the holographic participation-count bound, but the standard Bekenstein-Hawking entropy $S = A/(4\ell_P^2)$ has a specific 1/4 factor whose substrate origin is genuinely open derivation work parallel to T19's $G$ derivation.
- **Establishes the substrate-level structure of wave-black-hole scattering.** ED-I-26 articulates the BHPT phase shift as global invariant of the ED-channel at interpretive level. The arc produces the substrate-derivation chain showing how the phase shift emerges from the substrate's high-multiplicity-gradient structure rather than just being relabeled with ED vocabulary.

The arc is methodologically a **consolidation + DCGT-grounding arc** with two new-derivation memos sprinkled in (BH-5 area-law 1/4 + BH-6 wave-BH substrate-derivation; BH-3 also contains substantive new strong-curvature audit work). Closer in pattern to Arc SG (consolidation under DCGT) than Arc ED-10 (genuine new derivation). Estimated 7 memos, 6–10 effective sessions.

**Important framing.** Hawking-spectrum derivation (priority list item B4) is **not** part of this arc. ED-06 §7.3 explicitly flags Hawking-spectrum substrate-derivation as future work. The conceptual framing is articulated; the substrate-derivation chain via V5 cross-chain correlations is a separate technically-heavy arc that may follow Arc BH closure if the present arc lands cleanly.

## 4. Structural Questions to Resolve

Six load-bearing questions for the arc:

**(Q1) How does a horizon emerge as a participation-decoupling surface under DCGT?** ED-06 + ED-10 articulate the participation-bandwidth-collapse mechanism at conceptual level. The substrate-derivation chain through DCGT must show: (a) when ED gradients exceed a critical threshold, the V1-mediated chain-step propagation across the boundary fails coherence; (b) the resulting one-way participation produces the observed horizon phenomenology (redshift, freezing, fading); (c) the universalization across BH / cosmic / Rindler / acoustic horizons is FORCED by the same substrate mechanism.

**(Q2) How does ED prevent singularities and what replaces them in strong-curvature regimes?** ED-10 §9.4 + ED-06 §2.4 give the conceptual answer (singularities are manifold-artifacts; substrate participation-density saturation prevents geometric divergence). The substrate-derivation chain must show: (a) which substrate quantity saturates and at what scale; (b) whether the acoustic-metric reading from Arc ED-10 holds in the strong-curvature regime or breaks down at some critical curvature; (c) what structural picture replaces the singular point.

**(Q3) How does the information/entanglement distinction behave at a horizon?** ED-06 + ED-10 articulate the information-as-committed-structure / entanglement-as-uncommitted-structure distinction. The substrate-derivation chain must route this through P11 (commitment-irreversibility) + V5 (cross-chain memory) + DCGT machinery.

**(Q4) What substrate structure enforces the area-law entropy and determines the 1/4 factor?** ED-10 §5.6 establishes the area-law form FORCED. The 1/4 factor is genuinely open — substrate-level statistical-mechanics on horizon participation degrees of freedom either produces it exactly (parallel to T19's $G$ derivation) or determines it as INHERITED at value layer with explicit structural reason.

**(Q5) How does the ED-channel picture (ED-I-26) produce the BHPT phase shift?** Substrate-derivation chain showing the high-multiplicity ED-gradient structure of a BH produces the global invariant identified in WQFT/BHPT. Predictions for PM-corrections + Kerr channel-twisting follow.

**(Q6) What are the stability constraints on the acoustic-metric approximation in strong curvature?** Arc ED-10's condition (C5) Lorentzian-non-degeneracy of acoustic metric is structurally well-defined for non-extreme regimes. The strong-curvature regime near a would-be singularity is potentially extreme. Audit whether the framework's machinery extends cleanly or hits a structural-conditional content (acoustic-metric breakdown becomes a load-bearing condition).
