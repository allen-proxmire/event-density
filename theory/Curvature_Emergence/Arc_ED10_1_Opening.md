## 1. Purpose

This memo opens the Curvature Emergence Arc (ED-10), the queued long-horizon arc identified at multiple points in the program (ED-I-06 §5, ED-QFT Overview §10, Arc_SG_6 §5). The arc:

- **Investigates how curvature-like participation structure (Theorem GR1) can become genuine spacetime curvature.** GR1 (Phase-3 closure) supplied the kernel-level gravitational-sector content via Hadamard-parametrix construction lifting V1 to curved spacetime. ED-10 asks whether this kernel-level structure can be extended to the field-equation level — whether ED's substrate-derivation produces a covariant analogue of Einstein-class field equations or stops at GR1's kernel-level content.
- **Extends substrate gravity beyond flat background.** Arc SG closed the flat-background substrate-gravity sector (Newton + MOND-class + BTFR slope-4). ED-10 asks: what curvature-emergent generalization is structurally consistent with the substrate primitives + DCGT + GR1?
- **Identifies the minimal structural requirements for a covariant field equation** that the substrate-derivation could produce. These requirements include (a) recovery of Arc_SG_4's modified Poisson equation in the weak-field flat-background limit, (b) compatibility with Theorem GR1's curved-spacetime kernel structure, (c) preservation of FORCED-form / INHERITED-value methodology, (d) consistency with the program's no-Einstein-equation guardrail (or, if Einstein-class behavior emerges, honest acknowledgment that ED-Phys-10 is being challenged).
- **Must recover the six SG-6 weak-field-limit targets.** Arc_SG_6 §5 identified six checkable structural targets: Newtonian Poisson, modified Poisson with $\mu$ asymptotics, BTFR slope-4, $a_0$ horizon-scale invariance, SG-4 recovery in $g_{\mu\nu}\to\eta_{\mu\nu}$ limit, GR1 generalization. ED-10's deliverable is a curvature-emergent structural framework that meets all six targets simultaneously.

**Speculative-ratio framing.** Every closed ED arc to date has built on closed structural-foundation prerequisites (T17 + T18 + ED-I-06 + DCGT + ED-Phys-10 acoustic-metric guardrails). ED-10 *extends* the methodology beyond the flat-Minkowski-acoustic-metric regime that has been the operative baseline. This is structurally new territory; the arc may close cleanly with a positive structural-suggestive verdict (parallel to YM-6's Intermediate Path C verdict for Clay-YM), close with a stall verdict identifying a load-bearing structural obstruction that ED-10 cannot resolve, or remain open across multiple sub-arcs without producing a closure within the present memo's planning horizon. **Honest expectations:** speculative-ratio comparable to Phase-3 GR1's GR-4A SPECULATIVE status; structural rigor lower than NS / MHD / YM / Arc D / Arc SG closures.

The arc is scoped as a six-memo plan parallel to Arc SG / Arc YM / Arc D in structural form, though with explicit acknowledgment that the speculative ratio is higher and any sub-arc may identify a stall-locus that requires arc-replanning.

**ED10-1 (this memo).** Arc opening; framing, scope, non-goals, inputs, plan.

**ED10-2 — Curvature-Emergent Degrees of Freedom.** Identify the substrate-level structural ingredients that produce curvature-emergent content at continuum scale. Working a-priori: the substrate participation-channel network supplies a metric-emergence mechanism via the cumulative-strain relationships among nearby chains; curvature emerges from the variation of substrate participation density across spacetime. Concretely identify which substrate quantities map to (a) the metric tensor $g_{\mu\nu}$, (b) the Christoffel connection $\Gamma^\rho_{\mu\nu}$, (c) the Riemann curvature tensor $R^\rho_{\sigma\mu\nu}$, and (d) the Ricci tensor / scalar / Einstein tensor combinations. This is the load-bearing consistency check (target 5 of Arc_SG_6 §5). If the weak-field limit fails to match SG-4, ED-10 must identify which structural assumptions break and how the curvature-emergent framework can be revised.

**ED10-4 — Covariant Generalization of the Modified Poisson Equation.** Identify the curvature-emergent covariant analogue of the modified Poisson equation. Working a-priori: the field equation might take a form involving (a) covariant derivatives $\nabla_\mu$ replacing $\partial_\mu$, (b) some covariant generalization $\mu_\mathrm{cov}(\cdot)$ of the interpolation function, (c) a curvature-tensor source term that generalizes $4\pi G\rho_m$ to include both mass-energy density and the curvature-like-field participation density. **ED10-6 — Synthesis + Clay-Relevance-Style Verdict.** Aggregate the four sub-arc results; produce a Clay-relevance-style verdict parallel to NS-Smoothness Intermediate Path C and Arc_YM_6's Clay-YM-relevance statement. Honest framing: ED-10 may produce a structural-positive verdict (curvature emergence works structurally), a structural-conditional verdict (curvature emergence works under specific structural assumptions, identified explicitly), or a stall verdict (a load-bearing obstruction identified that ED-10 cannot resolve within the closed structural-foundation prerequisites).

Even if the curvature-emergent equation has cosmological implications, they are flagged as out-of-scope consequences rather than developed.

Phenomenological metric-dynamics modeling is out of scope.
- **No new primitives.** The arc operates strictly within the closed thirteen-primitive substrate framework; no primitive amendments. If ED-10 hits a load-bearing obstruction that would require a primitive amendment, the obstruction is flagged and ED-10 stalls rather than amending primitives ad hoc.

## 4. Structural Questions to Resolve

Six load-bearing questions for the arc:

**(Q1) How does GR1 generalize SG-4's field equation?** GR1 supplies the V1 kernel structure on curved spacetime via Hadamard parametrix. SG-4's modified Poisson equation operates on flat background. The question is: what is the field-equation generalization (operating on the curved-spacetime kernel structure of GR1) that reduces to SG-4 in the flat-spacetime limit? Working a-priori: the generalization involves replacing $\partial_\mu \to \nabla_\mu$ + lifting the source term to include a curvature-related contribution + verifying that GR1's kernel structure is consistent with the field-equation derivation.

**(Q2) What substrate structure corresponds to curvature tensors?** Riemann curvature $R^\rho_{\sigma\mu\nu}$ is a four-index tensor characterizing the deviation of parallel transport from flatness. The substrate analogue must involve some four-index participation-channel structure. Working a-priori: the substrate cumulative-strain structure between four nearby chain commitments at spacetime points $(x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4)$ supplies a four-index quantity; under DCGT-style multi-scale expansion in curved background, this should produce the Riemann curvature at continuum level. The detailed identification is the substantive ED10-2 work.

**(Q3) How does DCGT operate on curvature-like fields?** DCGT was derived in flat-Minkowski-acoustic-metric regime. Curvature-emergent application requires generalization: hydrodynamic-window scale separation in curved background, multi-scale expansion with non-trivial covariant derivative structure, gradient-expansion truncation under metric variation. Working a-priori: DCGT generalizes cleanly to curved background as long as the curvature radius is much larger than the substrate cutoff $\ell_P$; the structural modifications are technical (covariant-derivative bookkeeping) rather than fundamental.

**(Q4) What replaces the Laplacian in curved background?** The flat-background Laplacian $\nabla^2$ becomes the curved-background Laplace-Beltrami operator $\Box_g = \nabla^\mu\nabla_\mu = (1/\sqrt{|g|})\partial_\mu(\sqrt{|g|}g^{\mu\nu}\partial_\nu)$ plus possible curvature-coupling terms (e.g., $R$ scalar coupling, $R_{\mu\nu}\nabla^\mu\nabla^\nu$ tensor coupling, etc.). The question is which of these couplings ED-10 produces from substrate-derivation; the answer determines the specific covariant generalization of SG-4.

**(Q5) What covariant object plays the role of $\mu(|\nabla\Phi|/a_0)$?** SG-4's interpolation function is FORCED in asymptotic limits but INHERITED in transition-regime detail. The covariant generalization must (a) preserve the asymptotic limits at curvature-emergent level (Newtonian and deep-MOND), (b) produce a covariant object whose argument generalizes $|\nabla\Phi|/a_0$ to curved-spacetime context. Working a-priori: the covariant argument involves the magnitude of a metric-covariant derivative of the metric perturbation or a related curvature-invariant; the specific identification is ED10-4 work.

**(Q6) What conditions ensure the weak-field limit matches SG-6 targets?** ED-10's curvature-emergent framework must reduce to flat-background substrate-gravity in the appropriate limit. The conditions for this matching to work (a) constrain the candidate-form selection in ED10-4, (b) supply checkable consistency conditions, (c) fail if the curvature-emergent framework is structurally inconsistent with the closed-arc substrate-gravity work.

## 5. ED-10 Prerequisites (from SG-6)

The six weak-field-limit targets that ED-10 must reproduce, per Arc_SG_6 §5:

**1. Newtonian Poisson equation** $\nabla^2\Phi = 4\pi G\rho_m$ in the weak-field high-acceleration limit. Standard GR-class consistency check.

**2. Modified Poisson equation with $\mu(x)$ asymptotics** $\nabla\cdot[\mu(|\nabla\Phi|/a_0)\nabla\Phi] = 4\pi G\rho_m$ in the weak-field general-acceleration regime. Substrate-gravity-specific check.

**3. BTFR slope-4** $v^4 = G\,M\,a_0$ in the deep-MOND regime. Empirically-anchored target.

**4. $a_0$ as horizon-scale invariant.** ED-10 must produce $a_0 = c\,H_0/(2\pi)$ as a horizon-scale quantity independent of substrate-cutoff scale, with $H_0$ playing the cosmological-horizon role in both flat and curved regimes.

**5. Recovery of SG-4 equation in $g_{\mu\nu}\to\eta_{\mu\nu}$ limit.** Strongest checkable consistency condition: full curvature-emergent field equation must reduce to Arc_SG_4 modified Poisson equation when metric is flat Minkowski.

**6. Curvature-like participation structure (GR1) must generalize SG-4.** ED-10's curvature-emergent equation must reduce to GR1 at the kernel level + reduce to SG-4 at the field-equation level. The two reductions must be mutually consistent.

These six targets form the structural-consistency framework within which ED-10's deliverables are evaluated.
