## 1. Purpose

This memo addresses the second-most load-bearing technical question of the YM arc: does the substrate cutoff at scale $\ell_P$ produce a mass gap for non-Abelian gauge fields, and does that gap survive the continuum limit?

Specifically, the memo:

- **Analyzes whether the substrate-level gap survives the continuum limit.** The substrate is finite-difference at scale $\ell_P$; lattice-gauge-theory analogs guarantee a gap on lattice. The question is whether the gap survives the limit $\ell_P \to 0$ at fixed macroscopic scale.
- **Identifies the effective mass term generated by the V1 kernel's second moment.** The $\mathcal{O}(\ell_P^2\nabla^2 A)$ correction recorded in YM-2 §3 is the structural ancestor of an effective mass term in the gauge-field action; this memo extracts it explicitly and characterizes its $k$-dependence.
- **Determines the scaling behavior of the gap under coarse-graining.** Bare gap scales as $\ell_P^{-2}$; renormalized physical gap depends on how the V1 kernel profile rescales as $\ell_P \to 0$. The renormalization-scaling condition is the load-bearing technical question.
- **Evaluates whether the ED substrate provides a structurally forced mass gap for non-Abelian gauge fields.** The verdict is conditional: gap exists unconditionally at substrate level (Form-FORCED); gap survives the continuum limit conditionally on the kernel-profile-rescaling condition.

**Important framing note.** This memo is **not** a solution to the Clay YM-existence-and-mass-gap problem. The Clay problem requires constructive proof of YM existence with mass gap on $\mathbb{R}^4$ at the level of Streater-Wightman / Osterwalder-Schrader axioms. The present memo is a *structural analysis* of the ED substrate's mass-gap mechanism, parallel in scope to NS-Smoothness's Intermediate Path C verdict on Clay-NS. ED's contribution is the substrate framing of the gap, not the constructive proof.

## 3. Step 1 — Substrate Origin of the Gap

The V1 kernel has finite spatial width at scale $\ell_P$ (Theorem N1 admissibility class, Theorem 18 forward-cone support). Under the multi-scale expansion of the V1-weighted gauge-field correlator (Arc_YM_2 §3, parallel to Arc_D_4 §4 in the velocity sector), the substrate-level field strength acquires a next-to-leading-order correction at second moment:

$$F_{\mu\nu}^a \;\longmapsto\; F_{\mu\nu}^a \;+\; c_{V1}\,\ell_P^2\,\nabla^2 A_\nu^a \;+\; \cdots,$$

with $c_{V1}$ a dimensionless V1-profile coefficient (proportional to the second-moment integral $\bigl\langle\delta^2\bigr\rangle_{V1}/\ell_P^2$, INHERITED at value layer per the standard form-FORCED / value-INHERITED split).

**Non-Abelian analogue of R1.** This correction is the structural analogue of the R1 hyperviscous term $-\kappa\mu_\mathrm{V1}\ell_P^2\nabla^4\mathbf{v}$ derived in Arc_D_4 / NS-3.01 / NS-Smooth-2. The structural parallel is:

| Velocity sector (NS) | Gauge sector (YM) |
|---|---|
| V1 second-moment + 4th-moment expansion | V1 second-moment expansion |
| R1: $-\kappa\mu_\mathrm{V1}\ell_P^2\nabla^4\mathbf{v}$ | YM-3 mass term: $c_{V1}\ell_P^2\nabla^2 A$ |
| Hyperviscous stabilization at high-$k$ | Mass term suppressing low-$k$ massless modes |
| Lyapunov-decay-stabilizing in 3D NS | Mass-gap-stabilizing in YM |
| Form FORCED by V1 finite width | Form FORCED by V1 finite width |
| Sign FORCED by V1 positive Fourier transform | Sign FORCED by V1 positive Fourier transform |

The structural similarity is exact at the level of the kernel-moment expansion. The difference is the *order* at which the correction enters the equation of motion: in the velocity sector, the leading non-trivial term is the second-moment Laplacian (viscosity) and R1 enters at fourth moment ($\nabla^4$); in the gauge sector, the leading kinetic term $-\tfrac{1}{4}F^2$ already contains derivatives of $A_\mu$ (so the second-moment expansion of $A_\nu$ produces a $\nabla^2 A$ correction one derivative-order lower than the kinetic term, giving an effective mass term).

**Three structural consequences:**

- **Suppresses high-frequency gauge-field modes.** Modes with $k \gtrsim 1/\ell_P$ are suppressed by the second-moment correction, parallel to R1's suppression of high-$k$ velocity modes in NS.
- **Introduces an effective IR scale.** Modes with $k \to 0$ (massless modes that would naively dominate the gauge-field path integral) acquire an effective mass term $\sim \ell_P^{-2}$ via the second-moment correction. This is the mass-gap-mechanism candidate.
- **FORCED by the finite-width kernel.** V1's finite width is FORCED at primitive level by Theorem N1 (substrate event-discreteness + finite proper-time intervals + bounded bandwidth). Hence the second-moment correction is FORCED unconditionally; the mass-gap mechanism is structural, not phenomenological.

## 4. Step 2 — Effective Mass Term from Kernel Expansion

Take the Fourier transform of the substrate-corrected gauge-field equation. With $A_\mu^a(x) = \int d^4k\,\widetilde A_\mu^a(k)\,e^{ikx}$,

$$\nabla^2 A_\mu^a(x) \;\longmapsto\; -k^2\,\widetilde A_\mu^a(k).$$

The second-moment correction becomes

$$c_{V1}\,\ell_P^2\,\nabla^2 A_\mu^a \;\longmapsto\; -c_{V1}\,\ell_P^2\,k^2\,\widetilde A_\mu^a.$$

For modes near the substrate cutoff $k \sim 1/\ell_P$, this is an order-unity correction to the gauge-field amplitude — the correction is non-perturbative at the substrate scale. For modes with $k \to 0$, the correction is small in magnitude but carries the structural seed of a mass term.

**Effective-mass identification.** The standard relation between an inverse-Laplacian shift and a mass term is

$$(-k^2 + m^2)\,\widetilde A_\mu^a \;\Leftrightarrow\; (-\nabla^2 - m^2)\,A_\mu^a.$$

The substrate-induced shift $c_{V1}\ell_P^2\,\nabla^2 A_\mu^a$ contributes additively to the kinetic operator at second order in $\ell_P$. At momentum scale $k \sim 1/\ell_P$, the substrate correction to the dispersion relation is

$$k^2 \;\longmapsto\; k^2 + m_\mathrm{eff}^2(k),$$

with

$$\boxed{\;m_\mathrm{eff}^2(k) \;\sim\; c_{V1}\,\ell_P^{-2} \quad\text{for modes near the cutoff.}\;}$$

This is the **substrate-induced mass scale**. Three structural points:

- **It is a substrate-induced mass scale, not a phenomenological mass term.** It arises from the V1 kernel's finite width, FORCED at primitive level. No symmetry-breaking mechanism is invoked.
- **It is not a Proca mass.** A Proca mass term $-\tfrac{1}{2}m^2 A_\mu A^\mu$ would explicitly break gauge invariance. The substrate-induced mass term arises in the *kinetic* operator (as a $k$-dependent suppression at high $k$) rather than as a quadratic mass term in $A_\mu$ directly. The substrate-corrected gauge-field action remains gauge-invariant; the mass term is a spectral property of the gauge-field propagator, not a mass insertion in the Lagrangian.
- **It is a kinetic suppression scale.** The mass-term-like structure suppresses modes at high $k$ and produces a finite spectral gap below which no gauge-field modes propagate freely. This is structurally similar to lattice-gauge-theory's gap mechanism (the lattice spacing supplies a UV cutoff that translates into a spectral gap in the Hamiltonian formulation).

The structural analogue at the velocity sector is the R1 term's role in NS-Smoothness: R1 supplies a spectral suppression of high-$k$ velocity modes, gradient-norm-Lyapunov-stabilizing in 3D NS. Here the same mechanism supplies a spectral gap for non-Abelian gauge modes, mass-gap-relevant in YM.

## 5. Step 3 — Continuum-Limit Survival Criterion

The substrate-level gap is automatic and FORCED. The structurally non-trivial question is whether it survives the continuum limit.

**Continuum limit.** Define the limit as

$$\ell_P \;\to\; 0, \qquad R_\mathrm{cg} \;\to\; \infty, \qquad \frac{\ell_P}{R_\mathrm{cg}} \;\to\; 0,$$

with the macroscopic flow scale $L_\mathrm{flow}$ held fixed (or, equivalently, with the dimensionful physical mass scale $m_\mathrm{phys}$ held fixed).

**Bare gap divergence.** Naively, the bare substrate-level gap

$$m_\mathrm{eff,\,bare}^2 \;\sim\; c_{V1}\,\ell_P^{-2}$$

diverges as $\ell_P \to 0$. This is the same kind of UV divergence that appears in standard QFT under a momentum-cutoff regularization: bare quantities depend on the cutoff and require renormalization to extract physical content.

**Renormalized gap depends on kernel-profile rescaling.** Under the continuum limit, the V1 kernel profile $K_{V1}(\boldsymbol{\delta}/\ell_P)$ at fixed dimensionless argument structure rescales: as $\ell_P \to 0$, the kernel becomes more sharply peaked at the origin in physical units. The dimensionless coefficient $c_{V1}$ may itself depend on $\ell_P$ — specifically, on the way the kernel profile rescales. Three regimes:

- **(i) $c_{V1}(\ell_P) \to 0$ as $\ell_P^2$:** the renormalized gap $c_{V1}(\ell_P)\ell_P^{-2}$ is finite; gap survives at finite $m_\mathrm{phys}^2 > 0$.
- **(ii) $c_{V1}(\ell_P) \to 0$ slower than $\ell_P^2$:** the renormalized gap $\to \infty$; substrate gap diverges and the continuum theory is gap-divergent (likely non-physical).
- **(iii) $c_{V1}(\ell_P) \to 0$ faster than $\ell_P^2$:** the renormalized gap $\to 0$; substrate gap closes in the continuum limit (gap closure).

**Mass-gap survival condition.** The continuum-limit-survival of the gap is therefore conditional on:

$$\boxed{\;c_{V1}(\ell_P)\,\ell_P^{-2} \;\to\; m_\mathrm{phys}^2 \;>\; 0 \quad\text{as}\quad \ell_P \to 0.\;}$$

This is the **mass-gap survival condition** of the YM arc. It is structurally analogous to the renormalization-flow conditions that determine the existence of a continuum limit in lattice gauge theory: the lattice spacing must be taken to zero with the bare coupling tuned to a fixed point such that the physical mass remains finite. The ED substrate-derivation reframes this from a renormalization-flow technicality to a kernel-profile-rescaling condition: V1 kernel amplitude must rescale as $\ell_P^2$ for the gap to survive at finite physical value.

**ED's structural verdict.** The kernel-profile-rescaling condition is a value-INHERITED constraint: the rescaling is determined by V1's substrate-level physical content (Theorem N1's admissibility class), and ED's structural derivation does not pin the rescaling exponent at value layer. The existence of the gap is FORCED at substrate level (V1 finite width unconditional). The survival of the gap at finite physical value is INHERITED at kernel-rescaling layer.

This is the honest framing parallel to NS-Smoothness's Intermediate Path C: ED supplies a real Clay-relevant mechanism (R1 for NS smoothness; substrate-induced mass scale for YM gap). The Clay-relevance verdict is conditional on a value-INHERITED condition (R1 vs. super-Burnett quantitative competition for NS; kernel-profile-rescaling exponent for YM).

## 6. Step 4 — Non-Abelian Enhancement

The non-Abelian commutator structure $-ig[A_\mu, A_\nu]$ in $F_{\mu\nu}$ produces self-interactions among gauge-field components. These self-interactions amplify the effect of the substrate cutoff in two structurally important ways.

**Quartic self-interaction.** Expanding the YM kinetic term $-\tfrac{1}{4}F_{\mu\nu}^a F^{\mu\nu\,a}$:

$$-\tfrac{1}{4}F_{\mu\nu}^a F^{\mu\nu\,a} \;=\; -\tfrac{1}{4}(\partial_\mu A_\nu^a - \partial_\nu A_\mu^a)^2 \;-\; \tfrac{1}{2}gf^{abc}(\partial_\mu A_\nu^a - \partial_\nu A_\mu^a)A^{\mu\,b}A^{\nu\,c} \;-\; \tfrac{1}{4}g^2(f^{abc}A_\mu^b A_\nu^c)^2.$$

The cubic ($AAA$) and quartic ($AAAA$) self-interaction vertices arise from the non-Abelian commutator structure. Combining with the substrate-induced mass term from Step 2, the effective potential for gauge-field fluctuations near a homogeneous background takes the form

$$V_\mathrm{eff}(A) \;\sim\; m_\mathrm{eff}^2\,A^2 \;+\; g\,A^3 \;+\; g^2\,A^4 \;+\; \cdots$$

at the level of the gauge-field action. The quartic term $g^2 A^4$ is non-perturbative-stabilizing: it bounds the gauge-field amplitude from below at large $A$ and gives the effective potential a stable minimum at $A = 0$ even in the presence of higher-loop fluctuations.

**Two structural consequences.**

- **The quartic term stabilizes the mass term.** Without the non-Abelian self-interaction, the substrate-induced mass term might be unstable under loop corrections (massless gauge fields tend to remix at low energies via radiative corrections). The quartic term supplies a structural stabilization mechanism: any low-energy fluctuation that would attempt to remove the mass term encounters the quartic potential's confinement to small $A$, which preserves the gap.
- **The cutoff-induced mass is non-perturbatively enhanced.** In the Abelian case ($f^{abc} = 0$), the cubic and quartic terms vanish and the substrate-induced mass term is purely perturbative. In the non-Abelian case, the self-interaction terms produce a non-perturbative dressing of the mass term: the bare mass $m_\mathrm{eff}^2$ acquires non-perturbative corrections from gluon self-coupling at low energies. By analogy to non-perturbative gluon-condensate-class arguments in standard YM, this enhancement is structurally toward *increasing* the gap rather than closing it.

The non-Abelian enhancement is therefore a structural argument *in favor* of gap survival: where the Abelian gap might close in the continuum limit (it does, in $U(1)$ massless QED), the non-Abelian gap is structurally stabilized by the self-interaction. This parallels the standard YM-existence intuition: pure non-Abelian gauge theory is expected to have a mass gap precisely *because* of self-interaction; the Abelian theory is expected to be gapless.

## 7. Step 5 — Result: Substrate Mass-Gap Mechanism

**Summary of the substrate mass-gap mechanism.**

The ED substrate provides a natural mass-gap mechanism via four structural elements:

**(1) Finite-width V1 kernel** → $\ell_P^2\nabla^2 A$ correction at second moment of the kernel expansion (Step 1). The correction is FORCED at substrate level by Theorem N1's V1 kernel admissibility class.

**(2) Fourier transform** → effective mass scale $m_\mathrm{eff}^2 \sim c_{V1}\,\ell_P^{-2}$ at modes near the substrate cutoff (Step 2). The mass scale is gauge-invariant (kinetic-class, not Proca-class) and substrate-derived.

**(3) Non-Abelian self-interactions** → stabilization of the mass term against loop-correction remixing; non-perturbative enhancement of the gap (Step 4). Structurally distinguishes the non-Abelian case from the Abelian (gapless) case.

**(4) Continuum-limit survival condition** → $c_{V1}(\ell_P)\,\ell_P^{-2} \to m_\mathrm{phys}^2 > 0$ as $\ell_P \to 0$ (Step 3). Conditional on kernel-profile-rescaling at value layer.

**Structural verdict.**

- **Form-FORCED:** the substrate mass-gap mechanism exists unconditionally. V1 finite-width kernel + non-Abelian self-interaction + multi-scale expansion + Fourier transform jointly produce a substrate-level gap. No phenomenological postulate enters.
- **Sign-FORCED:** the V1 kernel's positive Fourier transform (Theorem N1 admissibility class, Theorem 18 forward-cone support) ensures the mass-term contribution is stabilizing rather than destabilizing. Non-Abelian self-interaction's quartic term reinforces the stabilizing sign at non-perturbative level.
- **Value-INHERITED:** the physical mass-gap value depends on the V1 kernel profile's rescaling under the continuum limit. The kernel-profile-rescaling exponent is a substrate-physics quantity INHERITED at value layer; ED does not predict its numerical value.

**Honest framing.** The verdict on YM mass-gap-from-substrate-cutoff parallels NS-Smoothness's Intermediate Path C verdict on Clay-NS:

- **What ED supplies:** a structurally real, FORCED, sign-FORCED-stabilizing mass-gap mechanism with substrate origin in the V1 kernel finite width and non-Abelian self-interaction enhancement.
- **What ED does not supply:** a numerical value for the physical mass gap, or a rigorous proof that the kernel-profile-rescaling condition is satisfied for any specific compact simple gauge group.
- **Clay-relevance:** the mechanism is structurally relevant to the Clay YM-existence-and-mass-gap problem; it is not a Clay-problem solution. ED's contribution is the substrate framing and the identification of the kernel-profile-rescaling condition as the load-bearing technical question.
