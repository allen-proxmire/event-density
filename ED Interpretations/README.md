# ED Interpretations

The interpretive reach of the Event Density framework across physics.

This folder contains 49 documents that apply the ED ontology to specific
physical phenomena, from quantum mechanics and galactic dynamics to
condensed matter, photonics, particle physics, and cosmological structure.
Each interpretation maps a known physical system or observation onto the
ED architectural language — density fields, gradients, mobility, penalty,
participation, horizons — and identifies the structural features that ED
provides without additional assumptions.

---

## Role in the ED Program

The Architecture defines the principles. The Rigour Paper proves them.
The Experiments folder tests them. This folder asks a different question:
*what does existing physics look like when viewed through the ED lens?*

The interpretations are not derivations. They do not claim to replace
standard treatments. They demonstrate that the ED structural vocabulary —
gradients, channels, mobility collapse, penalty equilibrium, triad
coupling — maps naturally onto phenomena across physics, often revealing
connections that standard frameworks treat as unrelated. Where the mapping
produces a quantitative prediction, it is recorded in the Applications
Paper and the Open Note. Where it produces a structural insight, it remains
here as an interpretation.
